Eco-Friendly Vending RFP Framework

ENERGY STAR, refrigerant compliance, telemetry, and packaging standards

Eco-friendly vending is a procurement-spec exercise, not a marketing label. This RFP framework gives qualifying hosts the technical criteria to require from operators — and the evidence types each criterion should be backed by.

1. ENERGY STAR equipment spec

ENERGY STAR-certified refrigerated vending machines (RVMs) use 30-50% less energy than legacy units, per EPA. Snack vendors are unregulated by ENERGY STAR but can be specified for LED lighting and high-efficiency power supplies.

Refrigerated beverage
ENERGY STAR Version 3.0 certification mandatory. Annual kWh < 1,500 for Class A (8 ft³+) units.
Glass-front coolers
ENERGY STAR Version 4.1 commercial refrigerator with ECM evaporator fans, LED case lighting, anti-condensate controls.
Snack vendors
LED interior lighting (not fluorescent); motion-activated lighting that dims to 20% when idle; ≥ 80 PLUS power supply.
Documentation
ENERGY STAR product certification listing URL; CEC Modernized Appliance Efficiency Database (MAEDbS) record number.

2. Refrigerant compliance — AIM Act + EPA SNAP

The American Innovation and Manufacturing Act (AIM Act) and EPA SNAP rule restrict HFC refrigerants in new vending equipment. Operators procuring 2025+ inventory should be using low-GWP refrigerants.

Approved low-GWP
R-290 (propane, GWP 3), R-600a (isobutane, GWP 3), R-744 (CO2, GWP 1). Permitted under EPA SNAP Rule 23 for self-contained refrigeration.
Phased out / restricted
R-134a (GWP 1430), R-404A (GWP 3922) — restricted in new equipment manufactured after Jan 1, 2025 per AIM Act Subsection (i).
Charge limits
R-290 charge limit 150 g per circuit (UL 60335-2-89 / IEC 60335-2-89).
Documentation
Refrigerant type + charge weight on equipment nameplate; manufacturer compliance attestation.

3. Telemetry — waste-avoidance metric

Cellular telemetry reduces operational waste: fewer truck rolls, less expired product, smaller carbon footprint per transaction.

Truck-roll reduction
Telemetry-driven restocking cuts visits by 30-50% vs fixed schedules. EPA SmartWay program documents the fuel-and-emissions delta.
Expiry waste
Per-SKU velocity tracking targets < 2% expiry waste vs ~8% industry baseline.
Required data fields
Online/offline status, per-SKU velocity, last-restock timestamp, internal temperature, compressor runtime, door-open events.
Audit interface
Web dashboard + monthly CSV export. Host has read-only access to their site portfolio.

5. Operator commitments to require in RFP

  • ENERGY STAR-certified refrigerated inventory ≥ 80% of fleet by year 1; 100% by year 3.
  • Low-GWP refrigerants (R-290 / R-600a / R-744) in all post-2025 procurement.
  • Cellular telemetry on 100% of host placements.
  • Expiry waste < 3% reported monthly; sustainability report annual.
  • Route optimization software (e.g., VendSys Optix, Cantaloupe Seed) in use; SmartWay-registered fleet.
  • Take-back program for retired equipment — refrigerant reclamation per Clean Air Act Section 608 + EPA-certified scrap recycler.

7. Reporting cadence + KPIs

  1. Monthly

    kWh per machine, expiry waste %, truck-roll count, eco-SKU sell-through %.

  2. Quarterly

    Carbon footprint per transaction (kg CO2e), refrigerant inventory by GWP class, take-back tonnage.

  3. Annual

    Sustainability report aligned to GRI 305 (Emissions) and GRI 306 (Waste); third-party verification optional but preferred.
