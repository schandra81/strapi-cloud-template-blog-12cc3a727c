Vending Machine Pre-Install Site Survey Checklist

Dimensional, electrical, clearance, accessibility, and egress verification for facilities teams

Hand this checklist to the facilities team before the operator's installer arrives. Every item maps to a measurable specification — dimensions, electrical capacity, ADA Section 308 reach range, NFPA 13 sprinkler clearance, NFPA 70 (NEC) circuit requirements, OSHA 1910.37 egress widths, or FDA Food Code 3-501.16 cold-chain holding temperature. Capability-framed: operator should attest in writing to compliance with each item.

1. Machine envelope + footprint

Standard full-size combo or beverage machines fall in 39-42 in W × 33-36 in D × 72-79 in H. Glass-front cooler machines run 39-43 in W × 33-37 in D × 72-79 in H. Confirm the specific model's published dimensions on the operator's submittal before install day.

  • Width clearance: machine width + 4 in side ventilation × 2 sides verified at placement (typical 47-50 in clear width for a 39-42 in machine).
  • Depth clearance: machine depth + 6 in rear ventilation verified (typical 39-43 in clear depth).
  • Height clearance: ceiling clear of at least 80 in to allow opening top-access service panels on glass-front coolers.
  • Door swing arc: full door swing unobstructed (typical 36-42 in clear in front).
  • Floor pad: level within ¼ inch over 4 ft span; no expansion joint within machine footprint.
  • Floor load: machine + loaded product (typical 800-1,400 lb) within posted floor capacity.
  • Delivery route: dolly path from receiving to placement verified; door widths ≥ 36 in or removable hinges; elevator capacity ≥ 1,500 lb if upstairs.

2. Electrical specification (NFPA 70 / NEC)

  • Dedicated 120V/15A circuit (non-refrigerated) or 120V/20A (refrigerated / combo) within 6 ft of placement.
  • Circuit not shared with high-draw equipment (microwave, refrigerator, copier).
  • GFCI per NEC Article 422 where within 6 ft of a sink or wash zone.
  • Receptacle within 6 ft to avoid daisy-chained extension cords (not allowed for permanent placements).
  • Cord drape: receptacle position allows cord to drop straight without crossing aisle.
  • Surge protection: facility electrical service has whole-building surge protection or a hospital-grade surge strip dedicated to the machine.

3. ADA Section 308 + 309 accessibility

  • Highest operable part ≤ 48 in above finished floor (308.2.1) — verify selection keypad, payment reader, dispense flap.
  • Lowest operable part ≥ 15 in AFF (308.2.1) — verify card reader and dispense bin opening.
  • Operating force ≤ 5 lbf (309.4) — verify with calibrated push-pull gauge or operator attestation.
  • No tight grasping, pinching, or twisting required (309.4) — verify dispense flap and refund button design.
  • Clear floor space 30 in × 48 in for parallel or forward approach (305.3).
  • Operable parts distinguishable by touch where the interface is visual-only (tactile / contrast).

4. NFPA 13 + fire / egress

  • Sprinkler head clearance per NFPA 13: typical 18 in below deflector kept clear; machine not blocking sprinkler coverage.
  • Egress width per OSHA 1910.37: corridor clear ≥ 28 in for ≤ 50 occupants; ≥ 44 in for > 50 occupants — machine does not narrow below threshold.
  • Machine not placed in a means-of-egress path or in front of any required exit.
  • Smoke / fire detector coverage not blocked; ceiling sensors clear within 36 in radius.
  • Manual pull station access maintained — typical 60 in AFF mounting per local code, unobstructed approach.

5. Telemetry + payment readiness

  • Cellular signal verified at placement ≥ -100 dBm (operator brings test device); OR dedicated Ethernet drop available.
  • Cellular carrier confirmed (operator's modem matches available carrier — Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile).
  • Payment hardware: contactless EMV + Apple Pay/Google Pay supported; PCI-DSS 4.0 AOC and SAQ-B-IP on file.
  • Telemetry portal access granted to host facility coordinator (read-only).

6. Cold-chain + refrigeration (if refrigerated machine)

  • Operating temperature holds ≤ 40°F per FDA Food Code 3-501.16; internal-temp telemetry alert configured.
  • Ambient operating range verified at placement (typical machine spec 50-90°F).
  • Refrigerant disclosed (R-290 propane, R-134a, R-448A); low-GWP refrigerant preferred where commercially available.
  • Defrost / condensate handling — drain or evaporative tray confirmed; no condensate puddle on placement floor.

7. Signage + compliance documentation

  • FALCPA allergen labeling visible on each SKU.
  • FDA menu labeling (calorie display) per 21 CFR 101.11 where operator has ≥ 20 placements.
  • Refund / support phone + QR code visible on machine face.
  • Operator emergency contact + service ticket process posted near machine.
  • Operator's COI on file with host facility coordinator ($2M GL, $1M umbrella, additional-insured endorsement).
  • State weights-and-measures registration where required.
This checklist is informational and not legal advice. Facilities should confirm placement complies with local building code, fire marshal requirements, and any tenant or lease constraints.
