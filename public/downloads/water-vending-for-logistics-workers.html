Logistics Hub Water + Hydration Vending Pack

OSHA heat-illness program framework, placement matrix, subsidy model, and operator selection

Worker hydration at logistics hubs is regulated under OSHA's General Duty Clause and the 2022 Heat-Illness National Emphasis Program (CPL 03-00-024). This pack consolidates the four working documents an EHS, HR, or facility services team needs to scope a hydration-forward vending program: heat-illness program framework, placement matrix, subsidy model, and operator selection.

1. OSHA heat-illness program framework

OSHA does not yet have a final indoor heat standard (proposed rule 89 FR 70698 published August 2024). Until finalized, enforcement runs via the General Duty Clause 29 USC §654(a)(1) and the NEP. Below: what an OSHA-aligned hydration program includes.

  • Cool, potable water available at no cost during all shifts when heat index ≥ 80°F (OSHA NEP recommendation)
  • Water access within reasonable distance of work areas — OSHA guidance ~400 ft or one minute walk from work positions
  • ≥ 1 quart (32 oz) of water per worker per hour of moderate work; ≥ 1.5 quarts/hour for heavy work in heat
  • Electrolyte drinks available where workers sweat extensively (heat index > 95°F or > 2 hours strenuous activity)
  • Acclimatization plan for new + returning workers — gradually increase exposure over 7–14 days
  • Heat-illness training documented annually; signs of heat exhaustion + heat stroke posted in break rooms
  • Emergency response plan: cool location + cold-water immersion / cooling within 5 minutes for heat-stroke signs

3. Subsidy + free-water model

OSHA requires free water at no cost during heat-index trigger conditions. Subsidy mechanic distinguishes 'free' (OSHA-required hydration) from 'subsidized' (everything else).

  1. Free-water mechanic

    Plain bottled water (or filtered fountain) dispensed at no cost during heat-index ≥ 80°F shift days. Mechanic: badge tap auto-issues free water; daily limit per worker (e.g., 6–10 bottles/shift).

  2. Subsidized electrolyte

    Electrolyte SKUs (Liquid IV, sugar-light Gatorade, Pedialyte, Nuun) subsidized at 50–100% during heat-trigger conditions; full retail otherwise. Mechanic: badge ID + heat-index trigger from facility BMS or weather service.

  3. Subsidized hydration year-round

    Subsidy applied to entire hydration category year-round in heat-prone facilities; mechanic stays simple — no trigger needed.

  4. Payroll-deduct fallback

    For workers who exceed free-water limit, residual cost via payroll deduct (bi-weekly Workday / Kronos / UKG file).

  5. Cold-weather equivalent

    Cold-storage and outdoor-yard workers get equivalent hot-beverage free mechanic during heat-index ≤ 32°F equivalents (frost / freeze advisories).

4. Equipment specification

Hydration cabinet
Refrigerated reach-in cabinet, 25–40°F internal; cellular telemetry sub-15-min heartbeat; cold-chain alert > 41°F for > 30 min.
Filtered-water station
NSF/ANSI 42 + 53 + 401 filter chain; flow rate ≥ 1.5 GPM; sanitary fill nozzle (no contact surface); annual filter change documented.
Outdoor hardened cabinet
IP44 + UL-471 rated; ambient operating 0–110°F; condenser shroud; insulated cabinet door; tamper-resistant fasteners.
Bulk water dispenser
Filtered fountain + bottle-fill station (e.g., Elkay EZH2O class) — 1.5 GPM bottle-fill; chiller capacity 8 GPH @ 50°F.
Telemetry + reporting
Per-shift hydration volume report; correlated with heat-index data; flagged for OSHA-aligned access compliance.

7. Quarterly review template

  • Hydration volume per FTE per shift (water + electrolyte) — correlate with heat-index data
  • Free-water utilization rate (% of FTE redeeming on hot days)
  • Subsidized-electrolyte utilization rate
  • Cold-chain excursion log
  • Filtered-water station filter change history + microbial test if facility policy requires
  • Heat-illness incident log + correlation with hydration access (if any incidents)
  • Refill / refund SLA adherence
  • OSHA-aligned access audit — every work area within 400 ft of hydration during heat trigger
