Student-Flow Placement Pack

Flow analysis framework, K-12 + college placement matrix, Smart Snacks checklist, operator selection

Vending machine placement is governed less by aesthetics than by student flow, Smart Snacks scope, ADA Section 308 reach range, and bell-schedule windows. This pack codifies four artifacts — flow analysis framework, K-12 + college placement matrix, Smart Snacks checklist, and operator selection — so a school is equipped to place a machine where it serves students, complies with federal law, and produces the predicted revenue.

1. Flow analysis framework

Flow analysis maps where students physically go in 5-minute windows. A placement that's 30 feet outside a high-flow corridor loses 60-70% of expected revenue (consistent with NAMA placement-velocity benchmarks).

  1. Map bell-schedule corridors

    Identify the 2-3 corridors with highest 5-minute pedestrian volume during passing periods. Walk each corridor during 3 transition windows (morning, midday, afternoon).

  2. Map dwell zones

    Identify dwell zones — cafeteria queue, study commons, athletics waiting area. Dwell zones convert at 2-3x corridor rates for snack vending.

  3. Map after-school zones

    Identify gym lobby, theater entrance, club spaces. These zones drive 15-25% of revenue in after-school event mode.

  4. Score each candidate

    Score 1-5 for: 5-min pedestrian volume, dwell time, ADA reach range, electrical access, custodial access, and after-school activity.

  5. Build the placement plan

    Top-3 scored locations become the placement plan. Map each to equipment type (combo / beverage / fresh-food / AI cooler).

4. ADA Section 308 placement checklist

Every placement must meet ADA reach range and operating force per Section 308 of the 2010 Standards.

  • Highest operable part ≤ 48 inches above finished floor (forward reach unobstructed).
  • Lowest operable part ≥ 15 inches above finished floor.
  • If obstructed forward reach, depth ≤ 20 inches and highest point ≤ 48 inches.
  • Clear floor space 30 inches × 48 inches in front of operable parts.
  • Card reader, contactless tap zone, mobile-wallet target within reach range.
  • Operable parts ≤ 5 lbf operating force; no tight grasping or twisting.
  • Dispense bin opening within reach range; ≤ 5 lbf to open dispense flap.

5. Smart Snacks scope checklist (K-12 only)

Use this checklist before commit. Operator should be able to attest in writing to each item.

  • Per-SKU attestation: every SKU passes the USDA Smart Snacks Product Calculator.
  • Snack standards: ≤ 200 cal, ≤ 35% fat-calories, ≤ 10% saturated fat, ≤ 200 mg sodium.
  • Entrée standards: ≤ 350 cal, ≤ 35% fat-calories, ≤ 480 mg sodium.
  • Beverage standards by grade: HS ≤ 20oz calorie-free / ≤ 12oz milk-juice; MS/ES water + ≤ 8oz milk-juice only.
  • School-day window: midnight to 30 minutes after last bell, per 7 CFR 210.11(b).
  • After-school planogram swap timestamped.
  • Quarterly attestation refresh to wellness committee.

8. Annual placement review

  • Walk each placement during 3 transition windows; confirm flow assumptions still hold.
  • Pull telemetry: top + bottom velocity by location; flag placements at < 50% of expected revenue.
  • ADA Section 308 measurements re-verified; logged with photo.
  • Smart Snacks attestation refreshed for full SKU list.
  • After-school SKU set reviewed with athletics + activities coordinator.
  • Wellness committee review of placement plan.
  • Decision: maintain / relocate / pull each placement.
