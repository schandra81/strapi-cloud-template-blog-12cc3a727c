Snack-and-Drink Vending Configuration Playbook

Combo, paired, and multi-machine bank scoping

The decision is not 'can we have both' — every operator partner equipped through the LetUsVending network can. The decision is what configuration: a single combo machine, a snack-and-drink pair, or a multi-machine bank with fresh food. This playbook codifies the sizing, electrical, and assortment logic referenced against NAMA operator-survey velocity norms, NEC 210.8/210.52 receptacle rules, FDA Food Code 2022 §3-501.16 cold-chain, ADA 308 reach range, and FALCPA + FASTER Act labeling.

2. Combo machine — when it's the right call

Strengths
Single footprint, single power circuit, single MDB / telemetry SIM, single delivery + service visit. Lower install cost. Cleanest fit for small offices and remote satellite locations.
Limits
Snack column count is 60-70% of a dedicated snack machine; drink count is 6-12. SKU breadth + healthy-share + dietary coverage all constrained.
Best fit
10-50 employees per shift, single-purpose breakroom, single-shift schedule, no cold-food need.
Models
Crane National 187 Combo, AMS Sensit Combo, USI 3014A, Federal Machine FSI / FMR. New $5,500-$9,500; refurb $3,500-$6,500.

3. Snack + drink pair — the standard workplace configuration

Two cabinets, two telemetry SIMs, two card readers — but a full 40 snack selections and 9-12 drink selections. This is the configuration ~60% of mid-market workplaces land on.

Snack cabinet
Crane National 187 / AMS 39 / USI 3145 — 40 selections. ~1.5-2 cu ft per selection volume.
Drink cabinet
Dixie Narco 5800 / Royal Vendors RVCC / Vendo V21 — 9-12 selections. 200-380 can capacity.
Coordination
Telemetry on both machines on the same dashboard. Single restock run; planogram coordinated so duplicate beverages don't appear in the snack cabinet's beverage slots.
Refund flow
Member can refund either machine through the same app / SMS / on-cabinet button.

4. Multi-machine bank + cold food

  1. When to step up

    150+ employees per shift, or any shift that doesn't have access to other food (24/7 ops, healthcare, industrial). The cold-food machine is the multiplier on member NPS.

  2. Cold-food specifics

    Crane Merchant / AMS Sensit Cold Food / Avanti Cold Food. ≤41°F continuous per FDA Food Code 2022 §3-501.16. 7-day discard per §3-501.17. Cellular telemetry alerts on temp excursion.

  3. Fresh-food sourcing

    Operator-prepared (commissary kitchen) or branded supply (Hand-Pasta, Smartmeals, FarmerFocus, Tovala-style refrigerated). Daily or 2x/week delivery.

  4. Coffee tie-in

    Most banks of this scale add a single-cup bean-to-cup or commercial pod station adjacent — see the coffee-vending playbook.

5. Site readiness checklist

  • NEC 210.8 GFCI 20A 120V receptacle per machine, within 6 feet of each cabinet
  • Single, level floor surface — combo and drink machines weigh 700-1,000 lb empty
  • ADA 308 reach range — selection buttons + card reader 15-48 in above finished floor; 30 in × 48 in clear floor space
  • Cellular signal verified for each machine's telemetry SIM (or in-building cellular boost)
  • Door clearance + delivery path — 36 in min through every doorway + corner along the install route
  • Room temperature 60-85°F ambient for refrigerated cabinets (per OEM spec)
  • Trash + recycling bin within 10-15 feet
  • Water + drain access if a coffee or water-dispense unit joins the bank

7. Operating SLA across the bank

  • Tier 1 (offline / payment down / temp excursion) <24 hr ack + resolve
  • Tier 2 (single SKU stockout) 24-48 hr ack, 5-7 day resolve
  • Telemetry-driven restock cadence: 1-3x/week base, daily during cold-food onboarding
  • Quarterly business review with the host site: uptime, healthy-share velocity, refund rate, NPS
  • FALCPA + FASTER Act allergen labels on every packaged SKU + cold-food entrée
  • Calorie disclosure on machine front per 21 CFR 101.8 (vending machine calorie rule) for operators with 20+ machines
