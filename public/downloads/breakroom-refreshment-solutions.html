Layered Breakroom Refreshment Program Toolkit

Employee survey template, RFP framework, planogram design, ESG checklist, and quarterly review template

This toolkit gives HR, facilities, and procurement the working documents to scope, RFP, launch, and govern a layered breakroom program (coffee + water + vending + micro-market or AI cooler). References pulled from ADA Section 308, FALCPA allergen labeling, FDA menu labeling, PCI-DSS 4.0, and ENERGY STAR. Capability-framed throughout — operator attestations belong in writing before contract execution.

1. Employee survey template

Pulse the population before scoping. A 7-9 question instrument with ≥ 25% response rate is sufficient for planogram and tier-mix calibration. Run via SurveyMonkey, Microsoft Forms, or Qualtrics; keep open ≤ 7 business days.

Q1 — Frequency
How often do you use the breakroom for food or beverage? (Daily / 3-4x week / 1-2x week / Rarely / Never)
Q2 — Current satisfaction
Rate satisfaction with current breakroom offerings (1-5 scale + open comment).
Q3 — Coffee preference
What coffee format would you most use? (Bean-to-cup / Single-serve pod / Drip carafe / Cold brew on tap / I don't drink coffee)
Q4 — Beverage preferences
Rank top 3 beverages: bottled water, sparkling water, coffee, tea, kombucha, energy drink, soda, juice, dairy, plant milk.
Q5 — Food formats
Rank top 3 formats: vending snacks, vending entrees, micro-market fresh, AI cooler grab-and-go, catered lunch days.
Q6 — Dietary needs
Check all that apply: gluten-free, dairy-free, vegan, halal, kosher, nut-free, low-sugar, high-protein.
Q7 — Price sensitivity
What is your acceptable price for a fresh sandwich? ($5-7 / $7-9 / $9-11 / I'd bring my own)
Q8 — Payment
Preferred payment: contactless card, Apple Pay/Google Pay, payroll deduction, account app, cash.
Q9 — Open suggestion
What single change would make the breakroom better for you?

2. RFP framework — section headings + key requirements

Issue to 3-5 operators. Allow 21-28 days for response. Capability-frame all requirements (operator should attest, not 'will deliver').

  1. Facility profile + scope

    Headcount, shift schedule, sq ft of breakroom, target placements (coffee unit, vending machines, micro-market, AI cooler), survey results summary.

  2. Equipment specification

    ADA Section 308 compliance, ENERGY STAR where applicable, NSF/ANSI 51 food zone for any prep surfaces, NSF/ANSI 4 for refrigerated units. Operator marks comply / not-comply.

  3. Service + uptime SLA

    ≥ 98% uptime, Tier-1 ≤ 24 hr ack/resolve, restock cadence by format (2-3x/wk vending; 3-5x/wk micro-market; daily-to-3x/wk AI cooler).

  4. Planogram framework

    Per Section 3 of this toolkit; healthier-option mix; allergen labeling per FALCPA.

  5. Pricing + commission model

    Subsidy / pass-through / commission tier; total annual cost to host; refund SLA.

  6. Sustainability / ESG

    Per Section 4 of this toolkit; rainforest-alliance or fair-trade coffee, compostable cups, recycling capture.

  7. Implementation + go-live

    T-30 / T-21 / T-14 / T-7 / T-0 plan with owner accountability.

  8. References + capability evidence

    3+ comparable accounts ≥ 12 months in service; sample uptime + QBR.

  9. Compliance attestations

    PCI-DSS 4.0 AOC, ADA attestation, FDA Food Code adherence, insurance COI.

4. ESG / sustainability checklist

Procurement and sustainability teams increasingly require ESG attestations. Use this as the RFP appendix and the contract attestation.

  • Coffee: ≥ 50% of bean volume rainforest-alliance, fair-trade, or USDA organic certified; documented at quarterly QBR.
  • Cups + lids: compostable or recyclable; operator provides BPI certification or recyclability documentation.
  • Refrigeration: ENERGY STAR certified machines where commercially available; R-290 (propane) or R-448A low-GWP refrigerant preferred.
  • Lighting: LED interior; nighttime low-power mode active.
  • Packaging: ≥ 30% of fresh-meal packaging post-consumer recycled or compostable by program year 2.
  • Food waste: micro-market / AI cooler operator donates near-expiration unsold product to local food bank where permitted by state Good Samaritan law.
  • Recycling capture: bottle/can recycling bin co-located with vending; operator reports diversion rate at QBR.
  • Healthier choices: ≥ 30% of snack and beverage facings meet better-for-you criteria (≤ 200 cal, ≤ 230 mg sodium, ≤ 10 g added sugar per CDC guidance).
  • Allergen labeling: per FALCPA; allergen summary signage at machine face.
  • Supplier diversity: operator reports % of product sourced from diverse-owned suppliers (women, minority, veteran, LGBTQ+) where the host requires.

5. Quarterly business review template

  • Uptime report (per-machine, per-month over the quarter) — target ≥ 98%
  • Transaction trend + average basket trend
  • Per-category velocity (snack / beverage / fresh / coffee)
  • Top-20 SKUs + bottom-20 SKUs by velocity
  • Stockout incidents + resolution time vs SLA
  • Refund volume + reason codes (target < 2% of transactions)
  • Coffee bean origin + certification mix
  • ESG metrics report (recycling diversion, BFY share, near-expiration donation)
  • Employee survey snapshot (re-run light-touch survey at quarter end)
  • Planogram refresh proposal for next quarter
  • Action items + owner + due date
