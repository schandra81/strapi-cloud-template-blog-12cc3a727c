Transit Station Vending Framework

Placement-type matrix, FTA procurement scope, planogram weighting by station type

Transit-station vending operates on a distinct profile: 16-24 hour operating windows, commuter peak velocity, public-safety oversight, and federal funding rules that govern many station authorities. This pack codifies a placement-type matrix (heavy-rail, commuter rail, bus, airport landside), FTA-aligned procurement scope, and station-type planogram weighting.

2. FTA procurement scope — federal-funding considerations

Many transit authorities receive Federal Transit Administration (FTA) funds. FTA Circular 4220.1F (third-party procurement) governs how station-related contracts (including concessions) are structured.

Concession vs. service contract
Vending at FTA-funded stations is typically structured as a concession agreement (operator pays commission to authority) — outside FTA procurement scope. Confirm with authority's procurement officer.
If structured as service contract (authority pays operator)
FTA Circular 4220.1F applies — full and open competition, Buy America provisions, DBE goals, debarment + suspension check (SAM.gov), wage rate determination if labor scope triggers Service Contract Act.
Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)
FTA-funded contracts include DBE goal. Vending operator network may include DBE-certified operators — coordinate with authority's DBE liaison officer.
Davis-Bacon / Service Contract Act
Pure vending services typically don't trigger DBA; if installation work is bundled (electrical, anchoring, kiosk fit-out), DBA may apply to the install scope.
Revenue collection + reporting
Concession revenue typically held separate from FTA-funded operating accounts. Confirm with authority finance.

3. Planogram weighting by station type

Per-station planograms differ. Use as starting points; quarterly telemetry review tunes to actual ridership.

Downtown hub (16-24 hr, transfer pattern)
Hydration 35%, coffee + energy 20%, grab-and-go snacks 25%, meal-format 10%, sweet treats 10%. AM coffee push 6-9 AM.
Commuter rail outlying (peak-heavy)
Coffee + hot beverage 25% (AM peak), hydration 25%, breakfast bars 15%, lunch / snack 25%, sweet 10%. Morning planogram differs from evening — operator EMS / merchandising tool should support time-of-day SKU swap.
Bus terminal (long-dwell)
Meal-format 20%, hydration 25%, snacks 30%, coffee 15%, charging-bay drinks 10%. Longer dwell tolerates microwaveable bowls + sandwiches.
Airport landside (premium captive)
Premium snacks 30%, hydration 25%, meal-format 20%, coffee 15%, treats 10%. Higher price tolerance; premium SKU mix appropriate.
Park-and-ride (daylight peaks)
Coffee 30% (AM), hydration 25%, snacks 25%, energy / sports drink 20%. Limited slot count typical — micro-vending or 1-2 small machines.

4. Equipment + telemetry — transit-specific spec

  • Vandalism-resistant cabinet — reinforced glass, tamper-resistant fasteners, e-lock with audit log.
  • Cashless-primary or cashless-only (cash is a theft target at high-traffic public placements).
  • Cellular telemetry — do not rely on transit-authority Wi-Fi (often segregated for ridership / safety systems).
  • External antenna for underground / underdeck placements.
  • Surge protection — transit power circuits experience switching events.
  • Operating-temperature spec covers indoor (concourse) or outdoor (curbside / platform-shelter) per placement.
  • CCTV coverage of placement — coordinate with authority's transit-police / security.
  • Hardened against splash + cleaning chemistry (concourse floor washdown is common).

5. Service cadence + access protocol

Transit stations operate 16-24 hours. Service visits must align with low-ridership windows + security access protocols.

  1. Off-peak service windows

    Restock scheduled outside AM + PM commuter peaks. Typical windows: 10 AM-2 PM and 9 PM-midnight.

  2. Security access program

    Operator route drivers cleared through transit-authority badge program. Background check on file. Coordination with transit police as needed.

  3. Restock dolly / equipment

    Concourse-friendly restock dolly; coordinate with station manager on freight elevator access where applicable.

  4. Telemetry-driven cadence

    Hub stations: 3-5 restocks per week. Outlying stations: 1-2 restocks per week. Adjust by telemetry velocity.

  5. Tier-1 incident SLA

    Offline / payment failure: <24 hour response. Refrigeration excursion: <4 hour response (fresh-food spoilage).

  6. Incident reporting to authority

    Vandalism / theft incidents reported to transit police + authority via standard form within 24 hr.

6. ADA + accessibility (Title II + DOT regulations)

Transit stations meet ADA Title II + DOT ADA regulations (49 CFR Parts 27, 37, 38). Vending in stations falls under Title II requirements with transit-specific considerations.

  • Reach range 15-48 in AFF (Section 308).
  • Clear floor space 30 in × 48 in (Section 305); placement does not block accessible path-of-travel.
  • Operating force ≤ 5 lbf (Section 309.4).
  • Cashless option for users without coin/bill handling.
  • High-contrast pricing display; characters readable in station lighting.
  • Annual ADA recertification on file from operator.
  • Accessible path-of-travel from accessible boarding area / fare gate to placement maintained.

7. Reporting + governance

  • Monthly revenue + transaction report to authority finance (concession commission).
  • Quarterly uptime + service-SLA report.
  • Per-station velocity, stockout, refund metrics.
  • Vandalism / incident log + insurance claim status.
  • DBE participation report (if FTA-funded contract path).
  • ADA + cleaning attestations current.
  • Planogram refresh recommendations from telemetry.
