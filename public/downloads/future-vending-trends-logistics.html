This playbook codifies the capabilities a forward-looking operator should be equipped to deliver and how to write them into your next agreement.

2. Modern RFP language — clauses to add

Copy these clauses into your next RFP. Each is enforceable, measurable, and reflects a 2026-era logistics operator's standard capability.

  1. AI cooler readiness

    'Operator has deployed AI computer-vision coolers (AWM Smart Shelf, Boxie, Foxbox, AiFi, or comparable) at no fewer than one (1) Fortune 500 logistics customer in the past 24 months. Operator will provide a customer reference letter on request.'

  2. Telemetry transparency

    'Operator provides Host real-time access to a property-level dashboard showing per-machine uptime, last-restock timestamp, per-SKU velocity, refund volume, and temperature logs (where refrigerated). Dashboard available via web and mobile.'

  3. Energy + scope 2 reporting

    'Operator provides quarterly scope 2 emissions report covering all machines at Host's facility: kWh consumption per machine × the applicable regional emission factor (eGRID subregion or local-grid factor). Report compatible with CDP and GRI 305-2 disclosure.'

  4. Refrigerant transition

    'Operator attests that no fewer than fifty percent (50%) of refrigerated machines deployed at Host's facility use R-290 hydrocarbon refrigerant or other refrigerant with GWP ≤ 150. Operator commits to one hundred percent (100%) within thirty-six (36) months of contract effective date.'

  5. Workforce-platform integration

    'Operator's kiosk + app supports SSO via SCIM 2.0 or OIDC to Host's HRIS (Workday, UKG, ADP, Ceridian, or comparable). Payroll-deduct integration runs through Host's HRIS API; Operator does not store employee SSN, DOB, or pay-rate data.'

  6. Data privacy + minimization

    'Operator processes transaction metadata only — machine ID, SKU, timestamp, payment method, and the Host-issued loyalty token where applicable. Operator does not collect, store, or share worker PII outside the Host's HRIS-mediated integration channel.'

3. AI computer-vision cooler deployment framework

AI coolers replace coil-and-spiral vending with a refrigerated case where workers tap a card, open the door, take items, and the system charges automatically. Velocity uplift commonly reported at 25-50% vs equivalent vending square footage.

  1. Site survey

    Network: 1 dedicated Ethernet drop per cooler, or 5G/LTE-M cellular with ≥ 10 Mbps sustained. Power: dedicated 20A circuit, no shared loads. Lighting: ambient 50-200 lux at the case (computer vision performance is lighting-sensitive).

  2. Inventory + planogram setup

    Vendor or operator loads SKU master with weight, dimensions, image references. Computer-vision training period 2-4 weeks during which accuracy is monitored at 95%+.

  3. Payment + identity

    EMV chip, contactless, mobile wallet at minimum. Payroll-deduct via HRIS integration where the host elects.

  4. Performance monitoring

    Accuracy ≥ 98% transaction recognition; mis-charge rate < 1%. Vendor SLA covers vision-model recalibration when accuracy slips.

  5. Workforce communication

    Launch huddle for shift-by-shift orientation; refund flow signage prominent; FAQ card at the cooler.

4. Workforce-platform integration

Integration with the host's HRIS turns vending into a managed-benefit touchpoint without compromising worker privacy.

Payroll deduction
Worker enrolls via host SSO. Transactions accumulate over the pay period; total deducted on payday. HRIS API receives the file (Workday, UKG, ADP, Ceridian).
Subsidy + employer-paid programs
Host funds a monthly stipend (e.g., $20/month healthy-food subsidy). Worker uses it at the kiosk or cooler with no out-of-pocket up to the stipend; overage flows to payroll deduct or card.
Shift-recognition tokens
Worker completes shift perfect-attendance milestone; HRIS pushes a one-time credit to the worker's vending account.
Wellness program tie-in
Healthy-SKU purchases counted toward wellness program points (where the host's wellness vendor — Virgin Pulse, Limeade, WellRight — supports an inbound activity feed).

5. ESG + reporting framework

  1. Scope 2 emissions

    Quarterly report: kWh per machine × eGRID subregion factor → tCO2e. Aggregated to facility level for the host's CDP, GRI 305-2, or SASB disclosure.

  2. Refrigerant inventory

    Per-machine refrigerant type, charge weight, and end-of-life reclaim attestation. Aggregated for the host's EPA SNAP and Significant New Alternatives Policy reporting.

  3. Packaging + waste

    Operator pulls dated SKUs for waste audit; donation-where-possible attestation (Bill Emerson Good Samaritan Food Donation Act protections).

  4. Workforce wellness reporting

    Healthy-SKU share of facings; healthy-SKU velocity. Reported to the host wellness program for ROI calculation.
