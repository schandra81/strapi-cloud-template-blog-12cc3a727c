Seasonal Manufacturing Vending Program Pack

Rotation framework, overlay checklists, pre-season equipment verification, promotional calendar

Manufacturing breakrooms run on a different seasonal rhythm than office or retail vending. Summer hydration shifts on factory floors above 85°F, winter comfort foods, and shift-specific overlays through every season. This pack gives you a 4-season rotation framework, equipment readiness checklist, and a 12-month promotional calendar template.

1. Why seasonality matters more in manufacturing

Factory environments amplify seasonal demand. OSHA recommends water intake of ~1 cup every 15-20 minutes for workers in heat above 90°F (OSHA Heat Illness Prevention). Conversely, cold-floor and outdoor-receiving roles in winter shift demand toward hot beverages and calorie-dense snacks. A static planogram leaves 15-25% of velocity on the table across the year.

3. Pre-season equipment verification (run 30 days before each season)

Each season has equipment-specific risks. Verify before the season hits.

Pre-summer (May)
Refrigeration condenser coil cleaning; verify 35-40°F set point; confirm internal temperature sensor calibrated; verify cellular modem coverage for outdoor-rated units; replace door gaskets if compromised. ENERGY STAR Tier 2 units should hold target temp in 90°F ambient.
Pre-fall (Aug)
Hot-beverage equipment service (if applicable); confirm heating coil and dispense mechanism; test hot-cup dispenser; restock hot-beverage SKU base.
Pre-winter (Nov)
Verify breakroom ambient ≥ 50°F for refrigerated units (below this, condensate can freeze); inspect outdoor units for cold-weather kit installation; verify card readers operate in cold (manufacturer typical range 32-122°F).
Pre-spring (Feb)
Annual deep clean; replace water filters on hot-beverage units; recalibrate scale on micro-market dispense bins; review allergen labels for spring SKU rotation.

6. EHS coordination — summer heat illness program

Vending program should coordinate with EHS heat illness prevention plan. OSHA recommends water + electrolyte access in workplaces above 90°F and during heat advisories.

  • Vending operator confirms ≥ 40% hydration share by end of May.
  • Electrolyte SKU (sports drink or electrolyte powder) included in planogram.
  • Water (16 oz minimum) priced at or below cost where corporate policy supports.
  • EHS posted reminder near machine: 1 cup water per 15-20 minutes in heat ≥ 90°F.
  • Telemetry confirms water + electrolyte SKUs maintain ≥ 95% in-stock during heat advisory days.

7. Measurement — what to track each quarter

  • Per-season velocity vs. prior year (same season).
  • Stockout rate on seasonal overlay SKUs (target ≤ 2%).
  • Hydration share % by month during summer.
  • Featured-SKU sell-through rate (target ≥ 70% of forecast).
  • Refund rate during seasonal transitions (target ≤ 2% — spikes indicate SKU mismatch).
  • Worker satisfaction snapshot (3-question intercept survey twice per year).
