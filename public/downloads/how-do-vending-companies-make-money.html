2. Why commissions vary so widely

Revenue volume
A $300/month machine cannot support a 10% commission because service cost is fixed (~$60-$90/visit). Commission scales with revenue, not flat-rate.
Service intensity
Daily-restock sites get less commission room than weekly-restock sites; COGS + labor consume more of the revenue.
Customization
Brand wraps, member-app SDK integration, BI exports add operator cost. Commission tier reflects them.
Contract term
5-year contracts unlock equipment refresh + commission upside; 1-year contracts reflect amortization risk.
Exclusivity
Site-wide exclusivity is worth 2-4 percentage points to the operator's underwriting; reflected in commission tier.

5. Operator selection framework

  1. Telemetry-equipped fleet

    100% telemetry coverage (Cantaloupe / Nayax / 365). No 'we'll add it later' — that is a capability gap.

  2. Cashless 80%+

    Mix of cashless transactions ≥ 80% indicates modern payment platform + member confidence.

  3. Route density

    Operator has 5+ accounts within 10 miles of the placement; service cost is amortized.

  4. Insurance + permits

    $2M general liability + $1M auto + product liability; state retail food permit if fresh.

  5. Reporting

    Monthly host reporting standard, telemetry-backed; not handwritten 'sell sheets.'

  6. References

    3+ comparable hosts (same vertical, similar size) reachable for reference call.

  7. Capital + equipment age

    Average fleet age < 7 years; capex plan for refresh.

6. Commission negotiation template

Use during operator selection. Each clause should be in the executed contract addendum.

Tier definition
Commission tiers defined per Section 4 above, settled monthly, with telemetry-export evidence.
Settlement cadence
Monthly within 15 days of month-end; ACH preferred; commission statement includes per-machine breakdown.
Audit rights
Host can pull telemetry data and reconcile against settlement statement quarterly; 30-day cure period for discrepancy.
Price-change notice
Operator gives host 30-day written notice of consumer price changes; host can decline.
Below-threshold remedy
If gross < $300/machine/month for 3 consecutive months, parties review planogram + placement; option to remove + relocate.
Termination
30-day or 60-day notice without cause after Year 1; material breach 30-day cure; equipment removal at operator cost.

7. Red flags in operator economics

  • Promised commission > 30% on a sub-$1,500/month machine — economically implausible.
  • Refusal to provide telemetry-backed settlement statements.
  • No reference accounts in the host's vertical.
  • Equipment average age > 10 years; no capex refresh plan.
  • Cashless mix < 50%; signals payment-platform gap.
  • Insurance certificate not provided at intake.
  • Sub-contracted route labor with no service SLA pass-through.
