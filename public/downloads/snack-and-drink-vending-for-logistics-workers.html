Logistics Vending Program Pack

Planogram framework, service model, AI cooler decision tree, and subsidy mechanics

Logistics-hub workforces (DC, sort, cross-dock, 3PL) have specific dietary, hydration, and shift-pattern needs that off-the-shelf retail planograms don't serve well. This pack consolidates four working references: a logistics-tuned planogram framework, a service model with SLA tiers, an AI-cooler / micro-market decision tree, and subsidy mechanics aligned to payroll systems.

2. Service model + SLA tiers

  1. Telemetry baseline

    Cellular LTE with sub-15-minute heartbeat. Per-shift velocity reporting. Cold-chain alerts on > 41°F for > 30 minutes (FDA Food Code §3-501.16).

  2. Restock cadence

    High-volume (200+ FTE/shift) 3–4x/week. Medium (100–200 FTE) 2–3x/week. Low (< 100 FTE) 1–2x/week. Telemetry-driven mid-window top-off for cold-chain SKUs.

  3. Tier 1 — revenue/cold/payment

    < 4 hour acknowledge, < 24 hour resolve. After-hours phone line for 24/7 sites.

  4. Tier 2 — stockout / planogram

    24–48 hour acknowledge, 5–7 day resolve.

  5. Tier 3 — cosmetic / signage

    5-day acknowledge, 14-day resolve.

  6. Uptime target

    98% monthly per-machine. Below 95% triggers operator root-cause + corrective action plan.

  7. Refund SLA

    App / SMS / phone refund; auto-credit within 24 hours for cashless. Refund > 2% triggers planogram + machine quality review.

4. Subsidy mechanics

Employer-funded subsidy is the single biggest driver of program uptake. Below: four mechanics commonly used, with payroll-system integration notes.

$/shift credit
Typical $3–$8/shift loaded against badge ID. Activated on shift clock-in via Workday/Kronos/UKG integration; expires at end-of-shift.
$/pay-period credit
Typical $20–$40 per bi-weekly period loaded against badge. Unused balance expires at end-of-period or rolls over per policy.
Category subsidy
Subsidy applied only to selected categories (hydration, real meals, fresh fruit) — indulgent SKUs at full retail. Drives wellness without paying for soda.
Discount-at-PoS
Operator applies fixed % discount (e.g., 15–25%) on all SKUs; employer reimburses operator monthly. Simplest mechanic; least targeting.
Payroll-deduct integration
Bi-weekly SFTP file or API push to Workday / Kronos / UKG. Reconciliation report monthly. Operator carries BAA-equivalent data terms for employee identifier.

5. Heat-illness program tie-in (OSHA NEP-CPL 03-00-024)

Heat illness is a 2022 OSHA National Emphasis Program priority. Vending program can align as a documented water-and-rest support.

  • Free water available at all times during heat index > 80°F (OSHA recommendation)
  • Electrolyte SKUs available in placement-zone cabinets (Liquid IV, Pedialyte, sugar-light Gatorade)
  • Sodium-replenishment snacks available (pretzels, salted nuts, pickle chips)
  • Cooling-zone placements documented; placements within 200 ft of indoor work areas where heat index > 85°F regularly
  • Worker training material — recognize heat illness signs — posted in break rooms
  • Quarterly review of hydration SKU velocity correlates with shift heat index data

7. Pilot rollout — 90-day plan

  1. Days 0–30 — site survey + planogram design

    Site survey, pedometer mapping, worker pulse survey, planogram drafted with EHS + wellness committee input.

  2. Days 30–60 — install + onboarding

    Equipment install on dedicated 20A circuits; signage + onboarding comms ≥ 2 weeks before go-live; payroll-deduct integration tested.

  3. Days 60–90 — first telemetry cycle

    Per-shift velocity baseline; first planogram refresh based on real data; first refund-rate review; first uptime report.

  4. Day 90 — QBR + go/no-go

    Quarterly business review with operator. Decide expansion, contraction, or hold. Document lessons learned.
