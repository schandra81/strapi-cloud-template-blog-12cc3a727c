Multi-Campus District Vending Management Playbook

Master agreement, per-campus addenda, scorecard, audit calendar

A multi-campus K-12 district or multi-campus higher-ed system runs vending most efficiently under a single master agreement with per-campus addenda — not 12 separate contracts. This playbook codifies the master/addendum structure, the district-wide scorecard, and the audit calendar that keeps compliance and program performance visible at the district office.

1. Master agreement structure

The master agreement holds the terms that don't vary by campus — operator entity, insurance, ADA + nutrition compliance, telemetry, audit rights, commission framework. The per-campus addenda hold what varies — machine count, placement, planogram, school-specific access rules.

Parties + term
District (or system) as principal; operator entity (named legal entity, not 'doing-business-as'). 3-5 year primary term with 1-2 year renewals.
Compliance obligations
USDA Smart Snacks (7 CFR §210.11) for K-12; campus-specific dietary requirements for higher-ed; ADA Title III (2010 Standards §305 + §308 reach range); FDA 21 CFR §101.8 calorie labeling; FALCPA allergen labeling.
Insurance
$2M general liability + $1M auto + $1M product liability + statutory workers' comp. District named as additional insured. Certificate filed annually.
Audit rights
District may audit operator books + telemetry + restock logs annually with 30-day notice; semi-annual unannounced site audits on 10% of placements.
Termination
30-day for convenience (district); 90-day for material breach (either party with cure period); immediate for repeated compliance failure.

3. District-wide scorecard

Roll up per-campus metrics into a district scorecard reviewed quarterly by procurement + nutrition services + facilities. Same scorecard structure across all campuses.

  • Uptime per machine, per campus, monthly. District target ≥ 98%
  • Smart Snacks compliance pass rate (K-12) — annual + spot audit results; target 100% per campus
  • ADA compliance pass rate — annual verification + spot; target 100%
  • Stockout SKU-hours per campus, weekly. Target ≤ 2%
  • Service SLA adherence (Tier 1 < 24 hr, Tier 2 < 7 day, Tier 3 < 14 day); target 100%
  • Revenue + commission per campus, monthly; aggregated to district P&L
  • Refund rate per campus, quarterly; target < 2%
  • Allergen + calorie labeling audit; target 100% per FALCPA + FDA 21 CFR §101.8

5. Operational support structure

  1. Restock cadence

    Per-campus telemetry-driven. K-12 cafeteria machines typically 2-3 visits/week during school year, 0-1/week during summer. Higher-ed 3-5 visits/week year-round.

  2. Service SLA — Tier 1

    Offline, cold-chain excursion, payment failure — <24 hr ack + <24 hr resolution. SLA windows respect school hours where security access requires escort.

  3. Service SLA — Tier 2

    Single-SKU stockout, planogram refresh — 24-48 hr ack, 5-7 day resolution.

  4. Service SLA — Tier 3

    Cosmetic, signage, cabinet cleaning — 5 day ack, 14 day resolution.

  5. Refund flow

    Refund via app, on-machine touchpad, or campus front-office hand-off. Quarterly refund report to district nutrition services.

  6. Academic calendar coordination

    Restock cadence flexes around academic calendar — pre-position for back-to-school, scale down for breaks, surge for testing weeks per campus addendum.

6. Quarterly business review — district structure

  • District-level scorecard rollup (uptime, compliance, SLA, revenue, refunds)
  • Per-campus deep-dive — bottom 20% campuses by performance, action plan
  • Smart Snacks + ADA compliance audit results since prior QBR
  • Planogram + nutrition recommendations from district nutrition services
  • Equipment refresh + retirement roadmap (typically 7-10 year cycle per machine)
  • Forward-quarter calendar — events, testing, breaks, summer staffing changes
  • Action items + owners + due dates, carried in QBR tracker
