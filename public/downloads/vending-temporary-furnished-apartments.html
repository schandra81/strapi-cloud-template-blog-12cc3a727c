3. AI-cooler vs traditional decision tree

Corporate-housing operators face a real cabinet-choice question. The decision tree below routes the answer.

  1. Property scale

    < 50 units / cabinet → traditional combo (lower capex). 50-200 units → smart cooler RFID. > 200 units → AI vision cooler (AWM Smart Shelf) for broadest SKU + lowest friction.

  2. Guest density per cabinet

    < 100 transactions/week → traditional. 100-300 → smart cooler. > 300 → AI vision.

  3. SKU breadth target

    20-40 SKUs → traditional combo. 40-80 SKUs → smart cooler. 80+ SKUs → AI vision + micro-market hybrid.

  4. Brand identity

    Hospitality-brand-driven (Sonder, Mint House) → AI vision + brand-immersive cabinet. Workforce-tier corporate housing → traditional combo with branded vinyl.

  5. Capex tolerance

    $3.5-7K → traditional. $12-22K → smart cooler RFID. $18-30K → AI vision.

  6. Guest demographic

    Tech-forward business travelers favor AI vision + tap-and-go. Insurance-displacement guests favor straightforward traditional UI.

6. Service + restock framework

Restock cadence
Daily fresh-SKU restock (yogurt, salad, sandwich, sushi) at high-volume corporate housing; weekly base restock; telemetry top-off for high-velocity SKUs.
After-hours access
Operator dispatch has 24/7 lobby + cabinet access with property-manager approval; spare-key or PIN-code logged.
Telemetry SLA
Sub-15-min heartbeat; alert on offline + cold-chain excursion + payment failure.
Refund SLA
Cashless auto-refund within 24 hr; cash refund (rare in this format) within 7 days.
Cold-chain SLA
Excursion > 41°F for >30 min → 4-hr response; SKU pull + full credit; no resale.

7. Member-app / guest-app integration

  • Guest-app deep-link to operator app — pricing tier + refund + stock visibility.
  • Pay-on-room option — charge to room folio at checkout (advanced; requires PMS integration with Oakwood / Synergy / Blueground PMS).
  • Tap-and-go via guest mobile wallet (Apple Pay / Google Pay) as fallback when guest-app not used.
  • First-night welcome credit ($10-25) for guests on stays > 14 nights.
  • Refund-flow visible in guest welcome packet + lobby signage + on-machine UI.
  • Multi-language UI for international assignment guests (Spanish, Mandarin, Japanese, German, Portuguese).

8. Reporting + commission framework

Monthly scorecard
Per-cabinet uptime, top SKU, refund rate, guest-mix, cashless mix, gross revenue, commission settlement.
Guest-stay segmentation
Operator reports velocity by stay-length cohort (30 / 60 / 90 day) — informs planogram tuning.
Commission tier
$700-$1,500/month → 6-12%; $1,500-$3,000 → 12-18%; > $3,000 → 18-25%. Cold-chain reduces commission room by 3-5 percentage points.
Settlement
Monthly within 15 business days; ACH; per-cabinet detail attached.
Audit rights
Property can pull telemetry-export quarterly and reconcile against settlement.
