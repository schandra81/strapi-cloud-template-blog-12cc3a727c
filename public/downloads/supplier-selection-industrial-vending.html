Industrial Vending Supplier-Selection RFP Framework

Six-dimension scorecard, reference-check template, contract review checklist

Industrial vending supplier selection has different priorities than retail or office. ANSI/ASSE Z244.1 lockout-aware service, ANSI Z535 signage, OSHA 1910.176 + 1910.95 alignment, FDA Food Code 3-501.16 cold-chain, and ADA Section 308 reach range all matter. This RFP framework codifies three artifacts — six-dimension scorecard, reference-check template, contract review checklist — so a warehouse procurement leader is equipped to select a compliant, financially stable operator rather than the lowest commission bid.

1. Six-dimension scorecard

Each bidder scored 1-5 across six dimensions. Minimum 24/30 for award; minimum 4/5 on Compliance + Service SLA dimensions (no exceptions).

2. Reference-check template

Speak with 3 references at facilities comparable in size, shift coverage, and equipment mix. Each reference covers eight questions.

  1. Length of relationship + comparable scope

    How long has the reference used this operator? What's the equipment mix and shift coverage?

  2. SLA adherence

    Tier-1 response time on actual incidents (machine offline, payment failure, cold-chain excursion). Documented adherence rate?

  3. Telemetry visibility

    Does the reference have read access to per-SKU velocity + uptime + cashless mix? Is the data current?

  4. Compliance attestation

    Has operator provided written attestation on Section 308, FDA Food Code, FALCPA, and (if K-12) Smart Snacks?

  5. Refund + dispute handling

    Refund processing time? Dispute resolution experience?

  6. Surge / temporary capacity

    Has operator mobilized temporary machines or surge labor during peak windows? Lead time?

  7. Billing transparency

    Are invoices itemized and reconcilable to telemetry? Any disputes?

  8. Would they re-select

    If RFP'ing today, would the reference re-select this operator? Why or why not?

3. Compliance attestation requirements

Operator must attest in writing to each compliance area before commit. Attestation refreshes annually.

  • ADA Title III + Section 308 — operable parts ≤ 48 inches, ≥ 15 inches; ≤ 5 lbf operating force; clear floor space 30×48 inches.
  • ADA Section 707 (where kiosk applies) — VPAT for digital interface.
  • ANSI Z535 signage — machine-mounted safety placards + wayfinding compliant.
  • ANSI/ASSE Z244.1 — lockout-aware service interface; service door interlock.
  • OSHA 1910.176 — aisle clearance preserved at placement.
  • OSHA 1910.95 — audio confirmation level appropriate where hearing protection used.
  • FDA Food Code 3-501.16 — refrigerated 35-40°F, hot-hold ≥ 135°F.
  • FALCPA — allergen labels on every SKU; allergen summary on machine face.
  • PCI DSS — cashless payment hardware compliance.
  • State weights + measures — calibration documented for any SKU sold by weight.

4. Contract review checklist

Procurement and legal counsel review the contract against this checklist before signature.

  • Scope — number + type of machines, placement, restock cadence, shift coverage.
  • Commission structure — clear rate, payment frequency, any escalator capped at indexed CPI.
  • SLA tiers — Tier-1/2/3 with quantified resolution times and remedies.
  • Compliance attestation refresh cadence — annual minimum.
  • Insurance — GL ≥ $5M, auto, workers' comp, cyber, umbrella where required.
  • Indemnification — product liability per state cap; cross-indemnification.
  • Term — initial 36-60 months; renewal terms; termination for cause + convenience.
  • Data ownership — telemetry data accessible by host with retention ≥ 24 months.
  • Audit rights — annual open-book audit of per-machine revenue + commission.
  • Cost pass-through cap — indexed to CPI-Food-Away-From-Home; no unilateral increases.
  • Force majeure + business continuity — operator's BC plan documented.

5. Financial stability check

Operator financial stability matters because mid-contract operator failure stranded inventory and forces re-bid under duress.

5-year financials
Audited or reviewed financial statements for past 5 years (or as available).
D&B / business credit
D&B Paydex ≥ 80 or equivalent third-party credit signal.
Bankruptcy / litigation history
No bankruptcy in past 7 years; no pending litigation that threatens going concern.
Revenue concentration
No single host > 30% of operator revenue (concentration risk).
Insurance current
All insurance certificates current and meet contract requirements.
Bonding capacity
Operator can provide performance bond if RFP requires (typical for large public sector).

7. Common selection pitfalls

Lowest-commission bid wins
Commission below 8-10% often signals operator can't fund compliance + telemetry. Mitigation: minimum compliance attestation floor.
Lacks K-12 / industrial / cold-chain segment experience
Operator with retail experience but no industrial track record fails on Z244.1 + Z535 + cold-chain. Mitigation: segment-specific reference requirement.
Telemetry afterthought
Operator quotes telemetry as 'available' but not deployed. Mitigation: contract clause requires sub-15-min heartbeat on every machine at install.
No surge / temporary capacity capability
Operator can't mobilize for peak windows. Mitigation: explicit SLA addendum on surge labor + temp capacity.
Refund opacity
Operator doesn't report refund rate. Mitigation: monthly KPI report contractual.
