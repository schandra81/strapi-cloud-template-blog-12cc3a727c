Campus Seasonal Vending Playbook

Calendar pivots, planogram shifts, and route schedules across the academic year

University campus vending has more demand variability than almost any other placement. Move-in surge, finals weeks, breaks, summer minimum-staffing, and event tentpoles each shift velocity 30-60% in days. This playbook codifies the calendar-aligned planogram shifts, route schedules, and equipment-rightsizing actions that keep service quality high without burning operator margin on dead-air weeks. It maps to standard NAMA service KPIs, FALCPA allergen labeling, ADA Section 309.4, and the wellness-program healthy-share floors common in campus dining agreements.

Build the program calendar against the registrar's academic calendar, not a generic fiscal year. Account for quarter vs. semester systems, summer session intensity, and athletic-event tentpoles.

2. Move-in / orientation playbook

  1. Pre-arrival (T-minus 7 days)

    Stock to 120% of normal across residence-hall machines. Add bottled water at every machine. Confirm cashless rails (parents tap-pay).

  2. Move-in day(s)

    Surge route — operator field rep on-site, telemetry-driven 2x/day top-off. Refund/feedback channel publicized at orientation. Bottled water + iced beverage focus.

  3. Orientation week

    Maintain elevated cadence. Survey new students for SKU requests; queue for first quarterly refresh.

  4. Settle to baseline (T+10 days)

    Return to standard cadence. Document velocity in QBR; identify outlier machines for further investigation.

3. Finals / midterm pivots

  • Increase caffeinated allocation 20% at study libraries and 24/7 study lounges T-21 days before finals.
  • Double restock cadence at library + study placements T-14 days.
  • Add large-format (16-20 oz) energy + RTD coffee where slot depth allows T-7 days.
  • Add comfort-food + meal-replacement SKUs at residence-hall machines (clif, RXBAR, instant noodles).
  • Hot-brew machine stocked at 2x normal cadence; stale-product disposal documented.
  • Post-finals (T+3 days): revert to baseline; pull large-format and short-shelf-life SKUs before break.

4. Break-period rightsizing

Winter break (3-4 weeks) and summer (8-10 weeks) carry the program's biggest shrink and dead-air risk. Three options scale operator effort to demand.

Option A — close low-occupancy machines
Power off + signage at residence-hall machines that drop below 20 transactions/week. Restock pause; resume at next school session.
Option B — reduced-cadence service
1x/week cadence; pull short-shelf-life and fresh; maintain core shelf-stable SKUs only. Best for academic buildings that remain partially occupied.
Option C — consolidate to anchor machines
Designate 1-2 machines per zone as 'break service'; communicate to remaining residents/staff. Other machines paused.

5. Event + tentpole calendar

  • Athletic tentpoles (home football, basketball, hockey) — pre-event stock-up at adjacent placements; sports drinks + water + snacks; restock evening-after.
  • Commencement — large-format water + grab-and-go at venue-adjacent machines; refund slip + operator-app channel publicized for guests.
  • Admissions / open-house weekends — full breadth at student-union and tour-route machines; cashless verified.
  • Concerts / performing-arts series — pre-event stock at venue-adjacent machines; clean disposal route after evening events.
  • Conference / summer-program hosting — short-term planogram pivot to general-public preferences (bottled water, classic snack SKUs) for 1-2 weeks.

6. Healthy-share + dietary requirements

  • Healthy-share floor maintained year-round at ≥ 30% (NANA threshold: ≤ 200 cal, ≤ 230 mg sodium, ≤ 35% calories from fat); seasonal pivots cannot displace this floor.
  • Allergen labeling per FALCPA on every SKU; allergen summary signage on machine face; allergen-flag opt-in for app members.
  • Halal / kosher / vegetarian SKUs identified where placement student population includes these preferences. Inventory tracked separately.
  • Sugar-free + low-sugar SKUs at every placement; flagged for diabetic students.
  • Dining-coordination — vending planogram aligns with campus dining wellness policy; no parallel-pricing that undercuts dining wellness offerings.

7. KPIs + QBR cadence

Uptime
≥ 98% per machine, 90-day rolling. Standard NAMA-aligned target.
Stockout per placement type
≤ 4/machine/quarter at study + residence; ≤ 6 at academic buildings (acceptable due to event-traffic spikes).
Velocity per slot, seasonal-indexed
Operator benchmarks against comparable campus placements; indexed for school-session vs. break.
Shrink + waste during breaks
≤ 3% of restock value, measured per break period.
Refund rate
≤ 2% of transactions; higher refund weeks investigated within 14 days.
NPS (student survey)
+30 minimum; surveyed twice yearly (mid-fall, mid-spring).

8. Operator + host QBR template — campus version

  • Per-window velocity (move-in, midterm, finals, break, summer) vs. prior year.
  • Stockout incidents by window + machine.
  • Healthy-share consumption — % of transactions on healthy-flagged SKUs vs. floor.
  • Event-coverage performance — venue-adjacent machine uptime + stockout during events.
  • Shrink / waste during winter + summer breaks.
  • Member feedback themes (residence life, athletics, dining liaisons).
  • Planogram-refresh proposal for next quarter, with expected velocity lift.
  • Action items, owner, due date.
This playbook is informational. Campus beverage exclusives, dining-services contracts, and athletic-sponsor agreements may constrain planogram changes; coordinate with the agreement holder where one is in effect.
