Gym Vending Sponsorship + Advertising Framework

Realistic economics, portfolio packaging, sponsor outreach, attribution + reporting

Vending wraps + on-machine digital screens + member-app placements are the three components of a gym vending advertising program. This pack covers what each tier earns, how to package the portfolio, how to pitch sponsors, and how to attribute results.

1. Three advertising surfaces in a gym vending placement

Each surface has its own ad-tech stack and pricing benchmark. Mix all three to package a credible sponsorship.

  1. Cabinet wrap (out-of-home)

    Vinyl wrap on machine exterior. 3M IJ180Cv3 cast vinyl, 6-year outdoor durability. OOH industry-standard CPM benchmarks from OAAA 2024 reports.

  2. On-machine digital screen (DOOH)

    Cantaloupe Engage, Nayax VPOS Touch, 365 Pico, or AWM Smart Shelf. Plays brand video / static / interactive while machine is idle. Programmatic DOOH per IAB Tech Lab OpenRTB DOOH spec (2024).

  3. Member-app placement

    In-app sponsor tile / featured brand / co-branded challenge. Tied to gym member-app surface via gym software (Mindbody Marketing Suite, Glofox, ClubReady).

3. Portfolio packaging — what to offer sponsors

Sponsors buy clusters, not single machines. Package multiple clubs + multiple surfaces into a tiered media kit.

Bronze
Single club, cabinet wrap only, 3 months. $300-$700 / month. Entry point for local brands.
Silver
Single club, cabinet + DOOH screen, 3 months. $500-$1,200 / month.
Gold
Single club, cabinet + DOOH + member-app, 3 months. $1,500-$3,000 / month.
Platinum
5-10 clubs cluster, all surfaces, 6 months. $7,500-$25,000 / month. Targets regional brands + national brands testing the channel.
Title
Multi-quarter exclusive + co-branded challenge + co-branded SKU. $50K-$250K / season. Targets major CPG (PepsiCo, Coca-Cola, KIND, Quest, Liquid IV).

4. Sponsor outreach playbook

  1. Identify likely advertisers

    Recovery + protein brands (Quest, KIND, RXBAR, Premier Protein, Fairlife, Liquid IV, LMNT), apparel (Lululemon, Vuori, Athleta), wellness apps (Calm, Headspace, Whoop), local health-and-wellness (PT clinics, dermatologists, juice bars).

  2. Pitch deck content

    Member demographics + psychographics, dwell time at machine, monthly impressions, NPS, sample creative templates, audited audience numbers via Geopath OOH measurement or Nielsen + Whoop's gym audience reports.

  3. Audience proof

    Geopath panel-based impression measurement (industry standard for OOH) or operator-side telemetry (transactions per machine × dwell estimate). Brand wants verified numbers, not handwave.

  4. Creative spec sheet

    Provide vinyl wrap dimensions, DOOH screen aspect ratio + resolution (1080p typical), and member-app placement specs in a one-page sheet.

  5. Term sheet

    Pricing + duration + creative-approval process + cancellation + makegood policy.

5. Attribution + reporting

Sponsors will not renew without attribution. Build a reporting cadence that ties spend to outcomes.

Impressions (OOH baseline)
Geopath or operator-telemetry-derived impressions. Per OAAA Eyes-On methodology: visits × dwell × visibility factor.
DOOH playback
Player-side log of impressions delivered (timestamp + duration). Standard IAB Tech Lab OpenRTB DOOH reporting.
Member-app clicks + CTR
Standard in-app analytics (Mixpanel, Amplitude, gym's native tooling).
Sponsor-SKU lift
If sponsor has an SKU in the planogram, A/B test velocity vs. control machines during campaign vs. pre-period.
Member NPS + brand recall survey
Quarterly micro-survey to a sampled cohort: 'Have you seen [Brand] at the gym? Are you more likely to purchase?' Standard recall measurement per Marketing Accountability Standards Board (MASB).

6. Creative + approval workflow

  • Creative spec sheet provided to sponsor in advance (cabinet wrap dimensions, DOOH screen 1920×1080 typical, member-app tile typically 1080×1080 or 1920×1080).
  • Sponsor delivers print-ready PDF or video file ≥ 10 business days before launch.
  • Gym brand team reviews for fit with brand standards (do not co-brand with conflicting categories — e.g., a yoga studio may decline alcohol sponsors).
  • ADA + accessibility review: signage character contrast ≥ 70% per Section 703; closed captions on DOOH video where audio is used (rare in vending, but ADA-relevant).
  • Proofs (cabinet wrap physical Pantone proof, DOOH screen preview) approved by both parties.
  • Install / go-live coordinated with operator service schedule.

7. Legal + contract considerations

  • Right to refuse: gym retains the right to decline creative that violates brand standards or jurisdictional rules (e.g., alcohol in family-club; CBD in some states).
  • Make-good clauses: define remediation if impressions or DOOH playback falls below committed levels.
  • Exclusivity: define whether sponsor has category-exclusivity (e.g., 'only protein bar advertiser') and at what cluster level.
  • Intellectual property: sponsor warrants creative does not infringe; gym retains usage rights for the engagement period only.
  • Termination: 30-day cure for material breach; gym may terminate for brand-safety incidents involving the sponsor (PR-disaster clause).
  • Data sharing: clarify what data flows to sponsor (aggregate impressions vs. member-level — member-level requires explicit member consent per CCPA / state privacy laws).
