Low-Sugar Vending Pack

Threshold framework, SKU catalog, planogram templates, and measurement framework

This pack is a working procurement framework for wellness leaders, benefits managers, and food-service directors operationalizing a low-sugar vending program. It covers the AHA / WHO / FDA threshold stack, a SKU catalog by category, three planogram archetypes, and the velocity + biometric measurement model that keeps the program credible past year one.

1. Threshold framework — what counts as 'low-sugar'

Four operative thresholds are in common use. Pick one as your floor and apply consistently.

AHA Added Sugar RecommendationWomen ≤ 25 g/day, men ≤ 36 g/day added sugar; children 2-18 ≤ 25 g/dayHealth-system + ACO wellness programs

4. Sweetener substitution science

Low-sugar SKUs use alternative sweeteners. Wellness + benefits teams should know which substitutes the program endorses.

Sucralose (Splenda)
FDA-approved 1998; 600× sweeter than sucrose. Widely used in diet sodas, protein bars. Stable in heat. ADI 5 mg/kg body weight.
Aspartame (Equal)
FDA-approved 1981. IARC classified 2B 'possibly carcinogenic' 2023 with intake ≤ 40 mg/kg/day still considered safe per JECFA. Most diet sodas. Avoid for phenylketonuria (PKU) populations.
Stevia (Truvia, PureVia, SweetLeaf)
Plant-derived rebaudioside A. FDA GRAS. No glycemic impact. Bitter aftertaste at high concentration.
Monk fruit (Lakanto)
Plant-derived mogroside. FDA GRAS. No glycemic impact. Often blended with erythritol.
Erythritol
Sugar alcohol; ~70% sweetness of sucrose. FDA GRAS. NEJM 2023 cardiovascular-risk study prompted review; ADI not formally set but typical intake well below study levels. Many wellness programs prefer monk fruit + stevia.
Allulose
Rare sugar; FDA permits exclusion from 'added sugar' label since 2019. No glycemic impact. Premium price point.
Acesulfame potassium (Ace-K)
FDA-approved 1988. Often blended with sucralose or aspartame for taste profile. ADI 15 mg/kg.

7. Measurement framework

Velocity by SKU + tier
Per-SKU vends/day. Trend low-sugar share of total vends monthly. Target: archetype % within 30% band.
Sugar-per-vend metric
Calculate average grams added sugar per vend across the program. Target trajectory: -20% year-1, -35% year-3 vs. baseline.
Stockout rate
Top-3 low-sugar SKUs stockout ≤ 5% of days.
Biometric linkage (optional, with HR collaboration)
Annual biometric screening — fasting glucose, HbA1c, BMI. Correlate trend with vending data over 2-3 years.
Satisfaction
Quarterly 5-point smile-rating or NPS. Target NPS ≥ 30 year-1, ≥ 50 year-3.
Subsidy ROI
Health-plan claims data — diabetes + cardiovascular trend. ACSM + AHA studies suggest 8-15% lower diabetes claims in mature low-sugar workplace programs.

8. Subsidy + pricing

Subsidy options
Free-vend water + sparkling water; flat-rate $1 for any low-sugar SKU; price-match (low-sugar same price as conventional); wellness-app reward credit.
Price elasticity
Low-sugar SKUs at 10-20% premium see 30-50% velocity reduction. Subsidize to parity or below for first 6 months.
Budget guideline
$8-25/employee/month for 50% archetype; $20-45 for 70-100%.

9. Operator selection checklist

  • Catalog of ≥ 40 low-sugar SKUs across 8+ categories
  • Telemetry-verified per-SKU velocity reporting
  • Nutrition + ingredient data file refreshed quarterly
  • Allergen + dietary inclusivity disclosure per SKU
  • Subsidy-flexible billing (line-item per SKU)
  • Wellness-app integration capability (Virgin Pulse, Wellable, Limeade, Castlight)
  • References from 3+ comparable wellness-program hosts with ≥ 24 months tenure
