Hybrid-Work Vending Restructure Pack

Occupancy analysis, equipment rightsizing, and commission renegotiation framework

Hybrid-work occupancy patterns have shifted post-2020 office demand by 30-50% in many markets per published JLL and CBRE occupancy studies. This pack helps facilities + procurement leaders restructure the vending program around actual badge-tap occupancy, rightsize equipment, and renegotiate commission with telemetry-derived velocity data — not 2019 assumptions.

1. Occupancy baseline (the input to everything else)

Before any equipment or commission discussion, document actual occupancy by day-of-week for the trailing 90 days. Most large-floorplate offices show a Tuesday-Wednesday-Thursday peak with Monday/Friday troughs.

Data source
Badge-tap system (Lenel, HID, Brivo, Kastle, etc.) export of unique badges per day for trailing 90 days. Alternative: WiFi association data from network team.
Metrics to compute
Average daily occupancy by day-of-week, peak-day occupancy, peak-hour occupancy, trough-day occupancy. Compare to 2019 baseline if available.
Reported pattern
Kastle Systems' Back to Work Barometer reports peak-day occupancy frequently 50-65% of pre-pandemic; trough-day (Friday) often 25-40%.

2. Velocity reality check from operator telemetry

Pull 90-day per-machine, per-day-of-week transaction data from the operator. Compare to occupancy. Velocity-per-occupant should be roughly flat across days; if it isn't, planogram is the issue, not occupancy.

3. Equipment rightsizing options

  1. Reduce machine count per floor

    Pull underutilized machines (sub-2,000 USD trailing-12-month gross). Consolidate to floors with peak occupancy. Operator typically waives removal fee on commission-bearing accounts.

  2. Swap full-line to compact / smart-cooler

    Compact smart-coolers (24-36 inch footprint) carry 50-80 SKUs with telemetry; appropriate for floors averaging under 40 occupants/day.

  3. Convert to micro-market on the highest-occupancy floor

    Where one floor concentrates 60%+ of in-office occupants on peak days, a micro-market (kiosk + open shelving + cooler bank) outperforms multi-floor vending. Telemetry, planogram breadth, and 24/7 access improve velocity per occupant 30-60% per published case studies.

  4. Shift to scheduled-service / fill-only

    For sub-30-occupant-day floors, replace operator route with scheduled-service (operator visits 1x/week) and a smaller equipment footprint.

  5. Consolidate to a single anchor + delivery

    One main breakroom (food + coffee + cold) plus operator-fulfilled office-coffee delivery to satellite floors. Eliminates underutilized machines entirely.

4. Commission renegotiation framework

Commission was originally set at 2019 volume assumptions. Lower volume changes operator unit economics: route labor, telemetry SaaS, and cost-of-goods are partly fixed. Operator margin compresses faster than revenue declines.

Open the conversation with telemetry data
Share the occupancy + velocity analysis. Acknowledge operator margin compression. Aim for a structure both sides sustain.
Tiered commission by gross threshold
Below 15,000 USD/year: 0% commission; 15,000-30,000: 5%; 30,000-60,000: 10%; 60,000+: 15-20%. Aligns operator economics to volume reality.
Flat-fee service alternative
Where annual gross < 12,000 USD per machine, a flat monthly service fee (e.g., 75-200 USD/machine/month) can keep service intact.
Equipment-removal pricing
Negotiate zero or capped removal fee on each machine pulled as part of rightsize. Most operators accept on commission-bearing accounts.
Subsidy lever
If maintaining current footprint is a retention/recruiting priority, host subsidy (price-down on selected SKUs) preserves member perception without raising commission.

5. Planogram updates for hybrid patterns

  • Reduce shelf-stable SKU breadth (less stockpiling); increase fresh + grab-and-go for peak days.
  • Add coffee-companion SKUs (creamer cups, sweetener, snack bars) — coffee velocity holds even when full-meal velocity drops.
  • Add same-day-fresh on Tue/Wed/Thu peak; pull fresh from Friday route to avoid shrink.
  • Healthy-share floor maintained; wellness expectations have not dropped with hybrid.
  • Allergen labeling per FALCPA on each SKU; allergen summary signage on machine face.

7. Member-communication template

  • Send program-update note 14 days before any equipment change.
  • Explain rationale: 'We've reviewed actual usage and made changes to keep the program sustainable.'
  • Direct to feedback channel (Slack, intranet form, in-app survey).
  • Specify what's not changing (price points, healthy-share, refund process).
  • Re-survey at 60 days post-change; document member NPS.

8. Pre-signing red flags on the new contract

  • Operator refuses to share telemetry-derived per-day-of-week velocity.
  • Operator proposes longer term in exchange for equipment swap — exit terms must remain symmetric.
  • Removal fee on pulled machines exceeds 250 USD/machine on commission-bearing accounts.
  • Planogram changes locked to operator price list with no escalator cap.
  • Commission tiers ratcheted only one direction (up on volume increase, no symmetric step-down).
