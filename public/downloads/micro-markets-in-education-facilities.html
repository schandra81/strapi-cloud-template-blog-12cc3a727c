Education Micro-Market Framework

K-12 Smart Snacks compliance overlay, higher-ed meal-plan integration, RFP template

Micro-markets in education facilities split into two regulatory worlds. K-12 must apply USDA Smart Snacks in School standards (7 CFR §210.11) to every student-access SKU during school day; higher-ed has no federal nutrition mandate but typically integrates with the meal-plan system + campus card. This playbook codifies both worlds in one framework, with an RFP template at the end.

2. K-12 Smart Snacks compliance overlay

7 CFR §210.11 sets numeric limits per SKU. Every student-access micro-market SKU must pass during school day (midnight before school until 30 minutes after dismissal).

  • Snack: ≤ 200 calories AND ≤ 200 mg sodium AND ≤ 35% calories from fat AND ≤ 10% calories from saturated fat AND 0g trans fat
  • Snack: ≤ 35% by weight sugar; first ingredient is whole grain or first ingredient is fruit/vegetable/dairy/protein OR contains ≥ 10% DV of calcium/potassium/vit D/fiber
  • Entree: ≤ 350 calories AND ≤ 480 mg sodium AND same fat/sugar limits
  • Beverage K-5: water (no size limit); milk ≤ 8 oz unflavored or flavored nonfat ≤ 8 oz; 100% juice ≤ 8 oz
  • Beverage 6-8: water; milk ≤ 12 oz; 100% juice ≤ 12 oz
  • Beverage 9-12: water; milk ≤ 12 oz; 100% juice ≤ 12 oz; low/no-calorie ≤ 20 oz (≤ 5 cal/8 oz or ≤ 10 cal/20 oz); other ≤ 12 oz with ≤ 40 cal/8 oz
  • Caffeine — none in elementary or middle; allowed in high school
  • Verification — operator maintains Alliance for a Healthier Generation Smart Snacks Product Calculator results or USDA-aligned vendor attestation per SKU

3. Higher-ed meal-plan integration patterns

  1. Campus card swipe

    Mag-stripe or contactless campus card (Blackboard Transact, CBORD Odyssey, Atrium, TouchNet) integrates with micro-market POS (365 Retail Markets, Avanti). Stored value or meal-plan-funded debit.

  2. Meal-plan equivalency

    $5-$10 'meal swipe' converts at micro-market POS; remainder forfeited or returned to plan. Most common in late-night dorm + library placements.

  3. Dining dollars / flex dollars

    Stored-value funded by meal plan, used at vending + micro-market + dining locations. Operates as closed-loop currency through campus card vendor.

  4. Subsidy + access

    Wellness office or student activities may subsidize access — e.g., free coffee at exam week. Funded by sponsor account, drawn via promo code or campus card flag.

  5. Open POS / app pay

    Standard contactless + app pay (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Stripe). Used where campus card integration is not warranted.

4. Equipment + placement

Equipment platform
365 Retail Markets Pico/Stockwell, Avanti Markets MicroMarket, or Three Square Markets — self-checkout open-shelf with cooler + freezer + ambient + kiosk POS.
Placement footprint
100-300 sq ft typical; cooler (40-80 SKUs) + freezer (20-40 SKUs) + ambient shelving (60-120 SKUs) + 1-2 POS kiosks. Some K-12 placements use compact 50-100 sq ft configurations.
K-12 placement
High school cafeteria or media center; elementary + middle typically not warranted. Locked overnight; vended only during instructional hours + after-school program window.
Higher-ed placement
Dorm common areas, library, student union, fitness center, late-night anchors. 24/7 access via campus card after-hours.
Surveillance + loss prevention
Camera + AI analytics (365 RM 'Smart Eye', Avanti 'Eye on the Cooler') for inventory + loss-prevention. Typical shrink 1-3% well-managed.

5. RFP template — section structure

Use this section structure verbatim in the district / system RFP. Includes scoring rubric placeholder for evaluation committee.

  • Section 1 — Scope: campuses, machine + market counts, hours, target install date
  • Section 2 — Smart Snacks compliance (K-12) or wellness policy alignment (higher-ed); per-SKU attestation required
  • Section 3 — Equipment specification: platform, refrigeration, ENERGY STAR, kiosk POS, loss-prevention
  • Section 4 — Payment integration: campus card + meal plan + cashless; PCI DSS v4 attestation
  • Section 5 — Operations: restock cadence, service SLA tiers, refund flow, academic calendar coordination
  • Section 6 — Reporting: telemetry + sales + nutrition + ADA audit reports; reporting cadence
  • Section 7 — Commission + pricing: commission %, price ceilings, subsidy structure if any
  • Section 8 — Insurance + compliance: $2M GL + $1M auto + $1M product; certificate filed annually
  • Section 9 — Scoring rubric: nutrition 25 / price 20 / operations 20 / reporting 15 / experience 10 / sustainability 10
  • Section 10 — Submission: format, deadline, contact, Q&A window

6. Operational support structure

Restock cadence
K-12: 2-3 visits/week during school year, suspended during breaks. Higher-ed: 4-7 visits/week year-round with surge during exam + move-in/out weeks.
Service SLA — Tier 1
Offline, cold-chain excursion, payment system failure — <24 hr ack + <24 hr resolution.
Service SLA — Tier 2
SKU stockout, planogram refresh — 24-48 hr ack, 5-7 day resolution.
Refund flow
Refund via kiosk POS, app, or campus front-office hand-off. Auto-credit within 24 hr for campus card + cashless.
Academic calendar coordination
Pre-position for back-to-school + welcome week; scale down for breaks; surge for finals + move-in/out; planogram refresh quarterly.
