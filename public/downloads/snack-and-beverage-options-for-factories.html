Manufacturing Planogram Pack

Meal-format coverage, hydration share, shift-tuned SKU velocity, healthy share alignment

A factory planogram is different from an office planogram. Long shifts, heavy physical work, limited break windows, and three-shift coverage push the SKU mix toward meal-format options, real hydration share, and shift-tuned velocity. This pack codifies the planogram dimensions that matter, with benchmark splits and an RFP-ready specification.

3. Shift-tuned SKU velocity

Each shift weights different categories. Telemetry should drive shift-specific top-10 every quarter.

Shift-1 (6 AM – 2 PM)
Breakfast bars (granola, oat), fresh fruit, yogurt, coffee, water, balanced lunch combo. ~20% breakfast SKUs early in shift; transitions to lunch by 11 AM.
Shift-2 (2 PM – 10 PM)
Lunch + snack mix. Higher salty-snack share; sports drinks for evening hydration; coffee push 2-4 PM and 7-9 PM.
Shift-3 (10 PM – 6 AM)
Caffeine-heavy (energy drink, coffee, cold brew); comfort + calorie-dense snacks; hot-beverage demand in cold months; minimal fresh-meal velocity (consider longer-shelf alternatives).

4. Hydration share — OSHA-aligned

Hydration share isn't just a wellness number — it's an EHS coordination point. OSHA Heat Illness Prevention guidance recommends 1 cup of water every 15-20 minutes for workers in heat ≥ 90°F.

  • Water (16 oz / 1 L bottle) present in ≥ 4 slots.
  • Electrolyte beverage (sports drink or electrolyte powder) present in ≥ 2 slots.
  • Total hydration share ≥ 40% during heat-advisory months (May-September in most US climates).
  • Water priced at or below cost where corporate policy supports. Subsidy model documented in operator contract.
  • Telemetry alerts on water stockout — same Tier-1 SLA as payment failure.

5. Healthy share — corporate wellness alignment

Frame healthy share against an external standard. Most large employers use a traffic-light system or a published nutrition framework.

FDA / USDA dietary guidelines reference
Better-for-you criteria: ≤ 200 mg sodium per serving (snacks), ≤ 10 g added sugar, ≥ 3 g fiber or ≥ 5 g protein. Document the specific criteria your program uses.
Traffic-light labeling (UK FSA model, adapted)
Green / amber / red shelf tags. Green ≥ 30% of slots.
Partnership for a Healthier America (PHA) framework
Public framework many large employers adopt. Operator should be able to certify per-SKU PHA alignment.

6. Allergen + dietary labeling — FALCPA / FASTER Act

  • All 9 major allergens labeled per FALCPA (1985 / 2004) and FASTER Act (2021 — sesame): milk, egg, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soy, sesame.
  • Allergen summary card on the machine face listing SKUs with each major allergen.
  • Gluten-free, vegan, kosher, and halal SKU indicators where applicable.
  • Calorie display on machine face for SKUs with packaging (most US large-employer machines now disclose calories at point of selection).
  • Operator provides quarterly nutrition report (sodium, sugar, fiber, protein per SKU).

7. RFP-ready planogram specification

Lift directly into your RFP. Operator must commit to meet or exceed.

  1. SKU count + rotation cadence

    Minimum 36 SKUs (combo machine). Quarterly planogram refresh based on telemetry. New SKUs trialed every 6 months.

  2. Meal-format coverage

    ≥ 15% of slots meal-format (sandwich, wrap, microwaveable bowl).

  3. Hydration share

    ≥ 30% baseline; ≥ 40% during heat-advisory months.

  4. Better-for-you share

    ≥ 30% of slots meet documented better-for-you criteria.

  5. Shift coverage

    Per-shift top-10 reviewed quarterly. ≥ 20% of slots tuned for shift-3 demand at sites with overnight shifts.

  6. Allergen + nutrition disclosure

    100% FALCPA labeling; calorie display on machine face; quarterly nutrition report.

  7. Special-diet certification

    Operator certifies which SKUs are gluten-free, vegan, kosher, halal.
