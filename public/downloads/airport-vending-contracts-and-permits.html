Airport Vending Permit + RFP Playbook

Five layers, master concessionaire framework, and TSA badging timeline

Airport vending is not a workplace placement with a SIDA badge stapled on. It's a five-layer regulatory + commercial stack — airport sponsor (FAA AC 150/5190-6), master concessionaire framework (49 CFR Part 23 ACDBE), TSA badging (49 CFR 1542 / 1544), local health department, and concession-specific RFP terms. This playbook codifies the operating practices LetUsVending operator partners are equipped to support in pursuing or operating an airport vending placement.

2. Master concessionaire framework

Most U.S. medium- and large-hub airports run concessions through a master concessionaire (HMSHost / Areas / Paradies Lagardère / SSP America / OTG / Hudson) or a small set of prime concessionaires. Vending often sits as a subconcession under the master, not as a direct airport contract.

  1. Direct lease (rare)

    Operator contracts directly with the airport for vending space. Usually only at non-hub or small-hub facilities. Lowest layer count but full burden on the operator.

  2. Subconcession under master

    Operator contracts with the master concessionaire who already holds the head-lease. Master handles airport-side reporting; operator handles vending economics. Most common path.

  3. Joint-venture / ACDBE arrangement

    ACDBE-certified operator partners with a non-ACDBE prime to meet 49 CFR Part 23 utilization goals (typically 10-30%). Documented utilization plan filed with airport DBE office.

  4. Direct airport RFP (mixed)

    Some airports (mid-hubs, government / international) issue stand-alone vending RFPs. Same five layers but operator owns the concession-agreement layer directly.

4. ACDBE participation

Authority
49 CFR Part 23 (Participation by Disadvantaged Business Enterprises in Airport Concessions). Goal-setting by airport per ACDBE program.
Typical goal %
Goals range 5-30% of concession revenue depending on airport size + opportunity mix. Public goal published annually in the airport's ACDBE program update.
Certification path
Operator certified through the state UCP (Unified Certification Program). Cross-airport reciprocal recognition.
Utilization plan
Prime + ACDBE submit utilization plan with bid; tracked through commercially useful function (CUF) reviews.
Compliance audit
Airport DBE office audits annually; civil-penalty exposure for misrepresented utilization.

5. Concession-agreement economics

  1. Minimum annual guarantee (MAG)

    Floor revenue paid to the airport regardless of sales. Typically 70-85% of pro forma year-1 percentage rent.

  2. Percentage rent

    10-20% of gross at large hubs; 8-15% at mid-hubs; 6-12% at small hubs. Categorical (snack vs. beverage vs. fresh) sometimes differentiated.

  3. Capex contribution

    Operator carries machine + telemetry + branding capex; airport may carry electrical + space build-out depending on the agreement.

  4. Pricing parity / 'street + 10'

    Many airports require menu pricing within X% of local off-airport retail (typically + 10-20%). Pricing must be filed and approved.

  5. Hours of operation

    24/7 expected at hub facilities. Pre-opening + post-closing landside vending often allowed but airside follows concession-program hours.

6. Health + food safety overlay

  • Local health-department food permit for fresh/perishable + commissary kitchen if used
  • FDA Food Code 2022 cold-chain ≤41°F continuous (§3-501.16) with telemetry alert + corrective-action log
  • FDA Food Code 2022 §3-501.17 date-marking + 7-day discard for ready-to-eat items prepared off-site
  • FALCPA + FASTER Act top-9 allergen labeling on every packaged SKU
  • Calorie disclosure per 21 CFR 101.8 (vending calorie rule)
  • ServSafe-trained route personnel where commissary handling occurs
  • Sanitation + pest-control logs aligned to local health-department inspection cadence

7. Operating SLA expectations at airports

  • Tier 1 (offline / payment down / cold-chain) <24 hr ack + resolve — airports often require <12 hr or <4 hr at hubs
  • Restock cadence telemetry-driven; high-velocity gate-area machines may visit 2-3x/day at peak
  • Refund flow per the airport's customer-experience program (often 100% money-back guaranteed)
  • Monthly + quarterly reporting to airport concessions office: gross, MAG vs. actual, refund rate, uptime, ACDBE utilization
  • Annual ACDBE utilization audit + civil-penalty exposure if misrepresented
  • Sustainability + ESG metrics increasingly required at large hubs (water, energy, recyclable packaging)
