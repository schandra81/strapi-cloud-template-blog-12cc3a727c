2. ENERGY STAR + low-GWP refrigerant specification

ENERGY STAR 4.0 for refrigerated beverage vending machines is effective 2024. Specify R-290 (propane, GWP 3) or R-600a (isobutane, GWP 3) per EPA SNAP. Avoid R-404A (GWP 3,922) and R-134a (GWP 1,430).

  • ENERGY STAR 4.0 certified — verify on EPA certified-products list before contract
  • R-290 or R-600a refrigerant; certificate from manufacturer on file
  • LED interior + display lighting; auto-dim or off after 30 min idle
  • Smart sleep mode — temperature setback overnight, weekend, holiday
  • EnergyGuide yellow label on every machine bid (DOE 10 CFR 431)
  • Door-heater controller adjusts based on dew point (Anti-Sweat Heater Control)
  • Annual emission factor per machine supplied for Scope 2 reporting

3. HACCP-equivalent food-safety framework

FDA Food Code + state retail food regulations apply to fresh-food + refrigerated vending. HACCP is not strictly mandatory for unattended sales below thresholds, but operators serving fresh + dairy + refrigerated meat should operate a HACCP-equivalent plan.

Critical control points (CCPs)
Temperature (33-40°F refrigerated, 0-32°F frozen), time-out-of-temperature, expiration date, allergen cross-contact.
Temperature monitoring
Telemetry-monitored interior temperature with ≥ 15-minute sample rate. Excursion alert > 41°F triggers operator response within 4 hours per FDA Food Code.
Health Department interface
Operator registers with state + local health department where required (CA, TX, NY, FL, IL, WA — registration thresholds vary). Annual inspection in most jurisdictions for fresh-food machines.
Time/Temperature Control for Safety (TCS) foods
Fresh sandwiches, wraps, salads, dairy, cut fruit, refrigerated meats. Cumulative time > 41°F: ≤ 4 hours = destroy; > 4 hours = legal liability.
FSMA + supplier validation
Operator validates supplier under Food Safety Modernization Act Preventive Controls Rule. Records on file for 2 years per 21 CFR 117.
Allergen + FALCPA compliance
Top-9 allergen disclosure per SKU; cross-contact attestation; nut-aware planogram option.
Food handler certification
Operator restock + service staff hold ANSI-accredited food handler certificate (ServSafe, Learn2Serve, StateFoodSafety) where state requires.

4. Glass-front beverage (CRBV) detail spec

Capacity sizing
Tall (72 in) holds 330-720 12-oz cans or 200-450 20-oz bottles. Half-height (53 in) holds half. Match to vends-per-day forecast (target restock cadence 1-3×/week).
Door + glass
Tempered safety glass per ANSI Z97.1. Optional anti-fog coating + heated glass for high-humidity environments.
Lighting
LED interior 6500 K white (color-accurate for product). Brightness 800-1,200 lumens.
Payment + telemetry
Card reader (Nayax Onyx, Cantaloupe ePort, Crane Pay) with NFC + mobile wallet. Cellular telemetry standard.
Footprint + clearance
Typical 37 × 33 × 72 in (W × D × H). Clear floor space 30 × 48 in per ADA Section 305.
Electrical
120V/15A dedicated circuit, GFCI per NEC 210.8(B). Inrush current up to 40A momentary on compressor start.

5. Fresh-food vending detail spec

Equipment design
Refrigerated carousel or wedge dispenser. Conveyor-style for sandwich + salad SKUs. Anti-tip + anti-vibration mounts.
Temperature zones
Single-zone 33-40°F most common. Dual-zone (frozen + refrigerated) on Fastcorp Z400 + select AMS models.
Cold-chain integrity
Pre-conditioned product loaded; never warm-loaded. Operator-side temperature log at load + restock.
Telemetry-monitored temperature
≥ 15-minute heartbeat. Excursion > 41°F triggers automatic product purge + alert; cumulative > 4 hr triggers destroy-and-replace.
SKU shelf life
Sandwich + wrap: 3-5 days from production. Salad: 3-5 days. Parfait + yogurt: 14-21 days. Cut fruit: 3-5 days. Operator's HACCP plan specifies.
Allergen + nutrition labeling
FDA menu-labeling rule applies to operators with ≥ 20 vending machines selling foods with calorie disclosure. Front-of-pack calorie + allergen disclosure per SKU.

6. AI camera cooler detail spec

Technology
Computer-vision SKU recognition (AWM, 365 Pico, Cantaloupe Go Mini, Aifi) + weight sensors. Member taps badge or app to unlock; cameras detect SKU removed; automatic checkout.
Loss prevention
Typical 1-3% shrinkage acceptable; > 5% triggers review. Operator's AI accuracy ≥ 97-99% for trained SKUs.
SKU training
Each new SKU requires camera training (~30-60 sec per SKU). Operator handles at restock.
Member experience
Open-front grab-and-go feel; no buttons, no waiting. Higher velocity vs. traditional vending (10-20% in measured deployments).
Network
Wi-Fi or cellular; bandwidth higher than traditional vending (200-500 MB/month per cooler) due to image upload.
Best for
Mature workplaces transitioning from vending to micro-market without the staffing model.

7. Placement + ADA compliance

  • ADA Section 308 reach range: 15-48 in operable parts
  • ADA Section 305 clear floor space: 30 × 48 in for parallel or forward approach
  • ADA Section 309.4: operating force ≤ 5 lbf, one-hand operation, no tight grasping
  • Anti-tip bracket or wall-mount per UL 541
  • GFCI-protected dedicated circuit per NEC 210.8(B)
  • Floor weight rating: glass-front beverage ~800-1,200 lb full; concrete or properly-rated raised floor
  • Ventilation clearance per manufacturer (typically 4-6 in rear + 2 in sides)
  • Drainage where condensate management requires; floor drain or condensate evaporator

9. Operator selection checklist

  • 100% ENERGY STAR + R-290 / R-600a equipment standard
  • Telemetry with temperature monitoring on every refrigerated machine
  • HACCP-equivalent food-safety plan for fresh-food machines
  • State + local health department registration where required
  • ANSI-accredited food handler certification for restock staff
  • FALCPA + FDA menu-labeling compliance
  • Insurance: $2M GL, $1M product, $1M cyber; certificate naming host as additional insured
  • References from 3+ comparable hosts (workplace + fresh-food experience) with ≥ 24 months tenure
