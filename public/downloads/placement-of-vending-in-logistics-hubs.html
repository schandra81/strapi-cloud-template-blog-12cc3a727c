Logistics Hub Vending Placement Framework

5-zone placement map, survey methodology, safety walk-through, and equipment spec

Placement is the single biggest driver of utilization and uptime in logistics-hub vending. This pack consolidates a five-zone placement map, a worker-survey methodology, a safety walk-through, and an equipment spec — the working documents a facility services team typically needs before scoping an operator RFP or recompete.

1. The five zones

Most hubs break down into five functional zones. Each has different worker flow, dwell time, ambient conditions, and security posture. Vending suitability differs accordingly.

2. Worker-survey methodology

Run a 7-day survey before placement to ground placement decisions in observed flow, not guesswork. Combine pedometer-style observations with a one-question pulse survey.

  1. Headcount + shift map

    Confirm FTE per shift; identify the 5 highest-traffic worker paths during shift start, mid-shift break, and shift end. Walk the path with stopwatch; record dwell points.

  2. Existing food / drink utilization

    Audit existing coolers, coffee stations, water fountains. Record peak queue times and stockout reports.

  3. One-question pulse survey

    'If we could place a vending machine on your shift, where would you use it most?' — 5 options matched to candidate zones. Capture department + shift. Target ≥ 30% response rate.

  4. Sightline + safety overlay

    Walk candidate placements with EHS lead; check egress, sprinkler coverage, forklift path, dock-leveler exclusion zones.

  5. Climate measurement

    Spot-check temperature + humidity at candidate placements across shifts; refrigerated cabinets need 50–90°F ambient per typical NSF-7 spec.

3. Safety walk-through checklist

  • Egress corridor retains ≥ 44 in clear width per NFPA 101 §7.1 (or ≥ 36 in if occupant load < 50)
  • No placement within 36 in of fire extinguisher, eyewash, or AED
  • Sprinkler coverage not obstructed; clearance per NFPA 13 typically ≥ 18 in below deflector
  • Outside forklift travel paths and marked aisle yellow-line offset
  • ≥ 25 ft from any dock leveler edge or fall hazard (OSHA 29 CFR 1910.28)
  • Dedicated 20A circuit available; outlet GFCI where within 6 ft of water
  • Lighting in placement zone ≥ 20 fc at machine face (recommended)
  • No placement directly under overhead conveyor, sprinkler riser, or HVAC condensate line
  • ADA reach + clear floor space verified (§308, §305)
  • Network: cellular signal ≥ −90 dBm or facility VLAN drop available with PCI segmentation attestation

4. Equipment spec by zone

Front office / admin
Standard refrigerated cabinet or compact micro-market kiosk; ENERGY STAR; ADA-compliant.
Break room / canteen
Full micro-market (smart cooler + traditional pair) + coffee. AWM Smart Shelf, 365 Retail Markets, Cantaloupe Yoke, or Nayax VPOS Touch class.
Floor-adjacent break station
Hardened traditional cabinet — reinforced glass, vandal-resistant keypad, tamper-resistant fasteners. Cellular telemetry preferred.
Dock + sortation
Hydration-only cabinets; cabinets rated for 40–95°F ambient extreme; cellular telemetry; cashless-only.
Driver / yard area
Outdoor-rated hardened cabinets (IP44 + UL-471 outdoor listed); canopy or vestibule preferred; cellular telemetry mandatory; sub-zero rated where winter lows < 20°F.

6. Integration with facility systems

  1. Badge access (optional)

    HID iCLASS / Prox / Seos; integrates with payroll-deduct via Workday / Kronos / UKG bi-weekly file.

  2. Network

    Operator cellular LTE preferred (PCI segmentation). If facility WiFi, dedicated SSID + VLAN with attestation from facility IT.

  3. Power

    Dedicated 20A circuits; UL-listed equipment; not on shared circuit with shop tools or air handlers.

  4. Signage

    Worker onboarding signage in break rooms ≥ 2 weeks before go-live; QR-link to refund flow.

  5. Telemetry roll-up

    Operator dashboard accessible to facility services + EHS; per-shift velocity, uptime, refund rate, temperature excursions.

8. Placement decision tree

  1. If pedometer shows < 30 workers/shift passing in 100 ft radius

    Skip the placement; consolidate into a nearby higher-traffic zone.

  2. If ambient temperature exceeds 90°F at peak

    Hydration-only cabinet; refrigerated condiment-snack cabinets fail rapidly outside NSF-7 spec.

  3. If shift workforce > 50% non-English-primary speakers

    Multilingual on-machine signage + multilingual operator app for refunds.

  4. If site is 24/7 with no on-site management overnight

    Cashless-only; cellular telemetry; remote refund (no on-site cash handling).

  5. If site has > 5 candidate zones

    Phase deployment — 2 zones at pilot, expand based on first 90 days of telemetry.
