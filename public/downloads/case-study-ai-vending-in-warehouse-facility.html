Warehouse AI Cooler Deployment Pack

Site survey, planogram template, and operator SLA framework for 24/7 industrial placements

Warehouse + distribution-center AI coolers serve three shifts of associates who can't leave the facility for breaks. This pack bundles a site-survey template, a shift-aware planogram, and an SLA framework an operations + EHS team can use to evaluate and govern the placement.

1. Site-survey template

Walk the site with the operator before signing. Each item maps to a placement-fit or compliance constraint. Cross every item or note the remediation plan.

  • Headcount per shift: shift-1 / shift-2 / shift-3 documented; total ≥ 200 associates per 24 hr across one cooler.
  • Cooler location: break room, mezzanine, or production-floor adjacency that does not interfere with material-handling traffic; OSHA-compliant 36-in clear aisle around cooler.
  • Electrical: dedicated 120V/20A circuit, GFCI; emergency power if facility has 24/7-critical load category.
  • Connectivity: cellular ≥ -95 dBm at cooler face OR dedicated Ethernet drop on segmented VLAN.
  • Ambient: 60-85°F operating range, < 75% RH, no direct sunlight, away from forklift travel zones.
  • Floor + anchoring: level concrete floor; cooler bolted per OEM spec; tamper-evident hardware.
  • Safety + EHS: cooler does not block emergency egress, fire extinguisher, AED, or eyewash; clearance from electrical panels ≥ 36 in per NEC 110.26.
  • Cold-chain (if refrigerated): temperature 35-40°F, alert threshold ± 5°F triggers immediate operator alert + EHS notification.

4. Shift-aware restock cadence

Restock during a low-traffic window for each shift, not against forklift + material-handling traffic. Operator should coordinate route with the facility shift supervisor.

High-volume (300+ associates per 24 hr)
4x/week visits, two visits before shift-1 start (5-6 AM), one mid-day for shift-2, one before shift-3 (9-10 PM). Telemetry-triggered top-offs as needed.
Medium-volume (150-300 associates per 24 hr)
3x/week visits, one before each shift transition. Telemetry-triggered top-off for hot-selling SKUs.
Lower-volume (75-150 associates per 24 hr)
2x/week visits, scheduled around shift-1 + shift-3 starts. On-demand based on telemetry.
Specialty cold-chain
Daily cold-chain check via telemetry; physical visit if temperature trend > 38°F sustained for 30 min.

5. EHS + safety coordination

  • Cooler placement reviewed by facility EHS officer before install; documented in EHS file.
  • Cooler does not block emergency egress, fire panel, AED, eyewash station, or first-aid kit.
  • Forklift traffic study: cooler ≥ 6 ft offset from designated forklift travel lanes; bollards installed if forklift-lane adjacency unavoidable.
  • Heat-exposure season: free-water-bottle program coordinated with EHS during high-heat days (per OSHA heat-illness guidance).
  • Cleaning + sanitation: cooler interior + dispense bin sanitized at each restock visit; cabinet exterior wiped daily by facility staff.
  • Sharps + glass: SKU mix avoids glass bottles in production-adjacent placements; glass restricted to break-room placements only.
  • Emergency contact: operator dispatch number + facility EHS phone tree posted on cooler face.

7. Quarterly business review checklist

  • Per-shift transactions + basket (shift-1 vs shift-2 vs shift-3)
  • Top-15 and bottom-15 SKUs by velocity per shift
  • Uptime by month per cooler
  • Cold-chain incidents + resolution
  • Refund rate; flags > 2% threshold
  • EHS incidents involving cooler (none expected)
  • Planogram refresh proposal for next quarter
  • Subsidy program reconciliation (if HR-funded)

8. Install + launch plan

  1. T-30: site survey + LOI

    Operator + facility ops + EHS walk site, document placement, electrical, connectivity, egress. Sign LOI specifying placement + scope.

  2. T-21: facility prep

    Facility runs electrical + Ethernet (if needed); EHS confirms placement signage + bollards; operator confirms cabinet dimensions.

  3. T-14: planogram + signage approval

    Planogram reviewed by HR + EHS for allergen labeling + Smart-Snacks/wellness compliance (where applicable). Cabinet wrap brand-approved.

  4. T-7: install + commissioning

    Cooler delivered, anchored, calibrated; 200+ vision-system training transactions per SKU; cold-chain validated for 48 hr before launch.

  5. T-0: launch + shift-by-shift demo

    On-site associate demo at each shift change for first week; HR newsletter announcement; free-first-purchase code.

  6. T+30: planogram refresh

    30-day velocity report; bottom-10 SKUs replaced; HR confirms allergen panel + signage.
