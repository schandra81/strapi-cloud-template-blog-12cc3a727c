High-Traffic vs Low-Traffic Vending Playbook

Equipment, telemetry, service cadence, commission structure, portfolio discipline

Vending portfolios that treat every placement identically underperform on both ends — they over-spec low-traffic placements and under-serve high-traffic ones. This playbook codifies the two operating envelopes (high-traffic / low-traffic) across equipment, telemetry, service cadence, commission, and the portfolio-mix discipline that keeps the route profitable.

2. Equipment specification by tier

Over-spec on T3-T4 placements destroys the route economics; under-spec on T1 placements destroys the customer experience. Equipment selection follows traffic.

T1 — High-traffic
Premium glass-front (USelect VM850, Crane Merchant Media), wide selection 40-60 SKUs, 21-32 in. screen, contactless + app pay, dispense camera. Or micro-market (365 Retail Markets, Avanti).
T2 — Medium-traffic
Standard glass-front (Crane Merchant Combo, AMS 39, Royal 660). Branded wrap. Cellular telemetry.
T3 — Low-traffic
Standard glass-front, possibly used + refurbished cabinet. Reliable cellular telemetry mandatory to avoid wasted truck rolls.
T4 — Marginal
Retain only if strategic (account relationship, future growth, brand obligation). Otherwise relocate to higher-traffic site.

3. Telemetry baseline — all tiers

Cellular-modem-enabled telemetry (Cantaloupe Seed, Nayax, 365 Retail Markets, Crane Streamware) is mandatory across all tiers — the low-traffic placements need it most to avoid wasted truck rolls.

  • Online status + heartbeat sub-15-min on T1-T2; sub-30-min on T3-T4
  • Per-SKU sell-through + cash/cashless mix + payment-failure rate
  • Refrigeration temperature monitoring on every refrigerated unit (33-41°F / 0°F frozen)
  • Route-optimization software (Vendsoft, VendingMetrics, VendMAX) consumes telemetry to schedule restock by actual depletion, not fixed days
  • Alert routing — Tier 1 alerts to dispatch within 5 min; Tier 2-3 batched into route morning briefing

5. Commission structure by tier

Commission rates flex with placement economics. T1 high-traffic placements can support higher host commission while still generating route margin; T3-T4 cannot.

  1. T1 commission

    10-25% of gross sales typical; 30%+ for marquee placements (university student union, airport food court). Some T1 placements bid as flat monthly rent + commission floor.

  2. T2 commission

    10-15% of gross sales typical. Tiered escalators per revenue band reward host for growing the placement.

  3. T3 commission

    0-10% of gross sales typical. Many T3 placements run zero-commission to fund the logistics premium.

  4. T4 commission

    Zero commission + relocation consideration. Or operator subsidy reversed (host pays a per-employee subsidy to retain access).

  5. Equipment investment recovery

    Premium equipment on T1 placements amortizes over 5-7 years against transaction volume; T3-T4 placements use existing or refurbished equipment to avoid capex sink.

6. Portfolio mix discipline

Healthy vending routes carry a portfolio mix — not all T1, not all T3. The mix balances revenue (T1-T2 carries), profitability (T3-T4 fills route windows), and relationship (T3-T4 doors open T1-T2 referrals).

  • Target mix — 20-30% T1, 40-50% T2, 20-30% T3, < 10% T4 by placement count
  • Revenue contribution — T1 should carry 50-70% of route revenue; T2 30-40%; T3 5-15%; T4 < 5%
  • Route windows — T3-T4 stops fill the geography between T1-T2 anchors; net incremental drive-time minimized
  • Annual portfolio review — every placement reviewed for retention, repositioning, or removal based on 12-month trailing economics
  • T4 removal threshold — placements that miss revenue floor 2 quarters in a row + show no growth trajectory removed unless strategic
  • T1 prospect pipeline — 3-5 active T1 prospects in pipeline at any time to backfill churn or replace T4 capacity
