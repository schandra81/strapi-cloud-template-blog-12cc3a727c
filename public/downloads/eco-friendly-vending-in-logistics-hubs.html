Eco-Friendly Logistics Hub Vending Pack

RFP language, equipment specification, supplier verification framework, and ESG metrics template

This pack consolidates the working documents a logistics operator's facility, ESG, and procurement teams need to scope a vending program aligned to corporate sustainability targets (SBTi, CDP, ENERGY STAR). It includes RFP language, an equipment specification, a supplier verification framework, and an ESG metrics reporting template. Built around operator-attestable capability.

1. Equipment specification — sustainability layer

ENERGY STAR refrigerated vending
Per current ENERGY STAR Refrigerated Beverage Vending Machine spec — typical Class A baseline 1,500–2,500 kWh/year vs ~3,500–4,500 kWh/year for non-ENERGY STAR comparable. Operator confirms model + version on EPA list.
LED lighting
Full LED interior + exterior; no fluorescent retrofits. ~70–80% reduction in lighting load vs T8 fluorescent.
Refrigerant
Natural refrigerant (R-290 propane or R-744 CO₂) preferred; HFC R-134a only if LED + ENERGY STAR otherwise. GWP < 1500 target consistent with AIM Act (40 CFR Part 84) HFC phase-down.
Sleep / setback modes
Cabinet enters low-power state during measured low-traffic windows. Telemetry verifies state engagement; sleep mode saves ~15–25% kWh.
Compressor + cabinet insulation
Variable-speed or ECM motor; cabinet U-value per manufacturer spec; vacuum-insulated panels where rated.
Lifecycle
Cabinet life 10–15 years; refurbishment program preferred at mid-life over replacement. End-of-life disposal via EPA RAD or certified refrigerant-recovery.

3. RFP language — drop-in clauses

Use these clauses verbatim or adapt into your operator RFP. Each clause is operator-attestable.

  1. ENERGY STAR attestation

    All refrigerated cabinets installed at Site shall meet the current ENERGY STAR Refrigerated Beverage Vending Machines specification at install date. Operator shall provide model + version per EPA list at install and any material change.

  2. Refrigerant disclosure

    Operator shall disclose refrigerant type + GWP per cabinet at install. Operator shall maintain a transition plan to GWP < 1500 refrigerants consistent with AIM Act 40 CFR Part 84 timelines and provide annual progress.

  3. Packaging waste reduction

    Operator shall maintain ≥ 60% of planogram slots in recyclable/compostable packaging (How2Recycle or equivalent) and report quarterly on slot composition.

  4. Local sourcing

    Operator shall maintain ≥ 30% of slots from suppliers located within 250 miles of Site, measured by SKU count, reported quarterly.

  5. Telemetry-driven route

    Operator shall use telemetry-driven dispatch to reduce route miles; report quarterly on miles-per-restock against prior-year baseline.

  6. End-of-life

    Operator shall remove cabinets at end-of-life via EPA RAD-recognized refrigerant recovery and shall recycle steel cabinet ≥ 80% by weight. Documentation provided to Site within 60 days.

  7. ESG reporting

    Operator shall provide quarterly ESG dashboard: kWh consumed, refrigerant GWP, plant-based slot %, local-supplier slot %, recyclable-packaging slot %, route miles, food waste % by units.

4. Supplier verification framework

  • Operator submits supplier list with certification copies (B Corp, USDA Organic, Fair Trade USA, Rainforest Alliance, MSC, RSPO, Fairtrade International)
  • Certifications dated within last 12 months; renewals tracked
  • Supplier code of conduct on file aligned to ETI Base Code or SA8000
  • Conflict-mineral attestation if any electronic component SKUs (cosmetics, OTC excluded categories)
  • FSMA Sec. 204 traceability data available for high-risk foods if applicable
  • Carbon disclosure — Scope 1 + 2 reported by major suppliers (CDP or equivalent)
