School Vending Approval Packet

Smart Snacks documentation, parent communication, and wellness committee workflow

Parent and staff approval for K-12 vending isn't a single meeting — it's a documented packet covering federal Smart Snacks (7 CFR 210.11), state competitive-food rules, district wellness policy (Healthy, Hunger-Free Kids Act of 2010 § 204), and a wellness committee workflow. This pack codifies the packet so a district is equipped to walk into a board meeting, parent forum, or wellness committee review with documentation in hand.

1. Smart Snacks documentation

Every SKU on a student-accessible machine during the school day must pass the USDA Smart Snacks Product Calculator. The packet includes a per-SKU attestation from the operator.

SKU list with Smart Snacks attestation
Per-SKU: name, brand, calories, fat %, saturated fat %, sodium, sugar, and 'pass' attestation from the USDA Smart Snacks Product Calculator.
Entrée standards
≤ 350 cal, ≤ 35% fat-calories, ≤ 10% saturated fat, ≤ 480 mg sodium per 7 CFR 210.11.
Snack standards
≤ 200 cal, ≤ 35% fat-calories, ≤ 10% saturated fat, ≤ 200 mg sodium.
Beverage standards (HS)
Plain water, flavored water ≤ 5 cal/8oz, ≤ 12oz low-fat milk, ≤ 12oz 100% juice, ≤ 20oz calorie-free/low-calorie beverages.
Beverage standards (MS/ES)
Plain water, ≤ 8oz low-fat milk, ≤ 8oz 100% juice — calorie-free/low-calorie NOT permitted.

3. Parent communication template

Parent communication has three touchpoints: pre-launch announcement, first-90-day check-in, and annual update. Each uses district-standard channels (ParentSquare, Bloomz, Remind, district email, board agenda).

  1. Pre-launch announcement (60 days before)

    Letter from principal + wellness coordinator. Covers Smart Snacks compliance, SKU list, payment options, refund process. Includes parent feedback form (open 30 days).

  2. Wellness committee meeting (45 days before)

    Parent representative + nutrition director + operator present SKU list, compliance attestation, and placement plan. Committee approval documented.

  3. Board agenda (30 days before)

    Vending placement + contract on board consent agenda; documentation packet attached.

  4. First 90-day check-in

    Wellness committee reviews telemetry — top sellers, refund rate, Smart Snacks compliance attestation. Findings communicated to parents through district channels.

  5. Annual update

    Annual wellness report includes vending KPIs, SKU updates, parent feedback summary.

4. Wellness committee workflow

Per the Healthy, Hunger-Free Kids Act § 204, every district must have a wellness committee. The committee owns vending program oversight.

  • Committee membership includes parent, teacher, school nutrition director, student representative, administrator.
  • Committee meets ≥ 2x/year per HHFKA requirements; vending is on each agenda.
  • Committee reviews SKU list and Smart Snacks attestation each quarter.
  • Committee approves any planogram change ahead of operator deployment.
  • Committee reviews refund rate, stockout rate, and parent feedback annually.
  • Committee documents decisions in minutes posted to the district website.

5. Staff approval workflow

Staff approval is the second track — separate from the wellness committee. Staff-lounge vending falls outside Smart Snacks if locked to students, but the district has wellness signaling.

Staff-lounge equipment
AI smart-cooler or combo machine, staff-card-locked outside staff hours.
Staff SKU input
Staff council provides input on SKU mix once per year; operator commits to a planogram refresh per the input.
Staff-only payment
Payroll-deduct or staff-ID payment integration where the district uses MySchoolBucks staff side or comparable.
Staff feedback
Annual staff survey reports satisfaction, refund issues, and SKU requests.

6. FAQ for parent communication

These are the seven questions that surface most often in parent forums and the documented response for each.

Q: Is vending allowed during the school day?
A: Yes, with Smart Snacks compliance per 7 CFR 210.11. Every SKU passes the USDA Smart Snacks Product Calculator before placement on a student-accessible machine.
Q: What about allergies?
A: Each SKU carries FALCPA-compliant allergen labels. Allergen summary is on the machine face. Peanut/tree-nut SKUs are segregated and labeled prominently.
Q: Can my child use a debit card / phone payment?
A: Yes. EMV chip, contactless tap, and mobile wallet (Apple Pay / Google Pay) are supported. Where the district uses MySchoolBucks / Titan / LINQ Connect, student-ID payment is integrated.
Q: How do I report an allergen mistake or expired product?
A: Operator app or SMS-to-refund number. Refund processed within 24 hours for cashless transactions. Operator log captures the incident.
Q: Who controls what's in the machine?
A: District wellness committee approves the SKU list and the operator's quarterly planogram refresh; parents are represented on the committee.
Q: What happens to expired food?
A: 2-day pull rule; pulled SKUs are donated to a Feeding America partner where the cold chain permits, otherwise discarded. Documented in the operator log.
Q: Does the school make money?
A: Yes, through commission revenue. Revenue funds athletic programs, student activities, or wellness initiatives per district policy and PTA agreement.

7. Approval packet contents checklist

Bring this packet to the wellness committee, parent forum, and board meeting. Operator should provide each item before the meeting.

  • Per-SKU Smart Snacks attestation report.
  • Equipment specification sheet with ADA Section 308 attestation.
  • Operator certificate of insurance (GL + auto + workers' comp + cyber).
  • Operator references — 3 comparable K-12 placements.
  • Planogram refresh schedule.
  • Refund policy + parent communication channels.
  • Donation pathway documentation (Bill Emerson MOU).
  • Telemetry capability sheet (Cantaloupe / Nayax / 365 Retail Markets).
This packet is a working approval reference. Localize to district policy and state competitive-food rules. Confirm wellness committee meeting cadence and board approval workflow with the district superintendent's office.
