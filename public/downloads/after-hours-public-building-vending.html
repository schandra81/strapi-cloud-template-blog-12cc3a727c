After-Hours Public-Building Vending Pack

Circuit checklist, cellular telemetry spec, shift-change service protocol

Public buildings — courthouses, libraries, transit stations, recreation centers — serve workers and the public outside business hours. After-hours vending requires a different operating model than business-hours office vending: dedicated circuits, cellular telemetry independent of building Wi-Fi, and service protocols timed to building staffing windows. This pack codifies the install + service framework.

1. After-hours operating profile

After-hours demand differs from business-hours demand on three dimensions: volume (typically 20-40% of business-hours velocity), product mix (more beverages, meal replacements, fewer fresh items), and service availability (building staffing is reduced or absent).

2. Electrical circuit checklist

Vending equipment requires a dedicated 20A circuit per machine (manufacturer typical). After-hours placements add specific risks — circuit cycling during janitorial shifts, breaker resets only available when staff are present.

  • Dedicated 20A circuit per refrigerated machine (NEC 210 — branch circuit sizing).
  • GFCI protection where required by NEC 210.8 (within 6 ft of sinks, outdoor placement, basement / unfinished space).
  • Surge protection on circuit — power events in older public buildings are common.
  • Circuit clearly labeled in panel with placement reference (e.g., 'Vending — North Lobby').
  • Building-automation / lighting circuits do not share with vending (avoids scheduled shutoff after hours).
  • Emergency / generator backup decision — typically NO (vending is not life-safety); document so facility knows machine goes dark on outage.
  • After-hours breaker reset protocol documented — who has access, response time, operator notification path.

3. Cellular telemetry specification (do NOT rely on building Wi-Fi)

Building Wi-Fi rarely covers vending placements at usable signal, often goes down or restarts overnight, and adds an IT-coordination burden. Cellular modem on the machine is the standard.

Cellular modem
4G LTE Cat-M1 or LTE-M / NB-IoT preferred for low power + indoor penetration. CAT-1 acceptable. Avoid 3G (sunsetted by US carriers 2022).
Carrier coverage
Verify carrier signal ≥ -100 dBm RSSI at the placement before install. Modem with multi-carrier eSIM preferred for thick-wall buildings (basement courthouses, transit underground).
Heartbeat cadence
≤ 15 minutes. Alert threshold: 30 minutes offline triggers operator NOC alert.
Data plan
Operator's commercial IoT plan; not on facility account. ~$3-8 per machine per month typical.
Antenna placement
External antenna for basement or steel-cabinet placements where signal is degraded.
PCI scope
Cellular path keeps cashless transactions off the building network — PCI scope stays with operator.

4. Shift-change + after-hours service protocol

Operator service visits must align with building access windows. The protocol below covers the common after-hours coordination patterns.

  1. Building access registration

    Operator route drivers cleared through facility security badge / contractor-access program. Background check + ID on file per facility policy. Federal buildings: HSPD-12 / PIV-I where required.

  2. Service window definition

    Routine restock during business hours where building staff are present. Emergency service (offline, temperature) outside business hours via after-hours coordinator.

  3. Custodial coordination

    Operator schedules visits to avoid conflict with floor-cleaning / waxing cycles. Custodial supervisor notified day-of for surface coordination.

  4. Security escort policy

    Some facilities require escort for any contractor work after hours. Document policy in contract; cost passes through transparently.

  5. Offline / breaker-trip response

    If machine goes offline outside business hours, operator notifies facility next business day. Tier-1 SLA window starts at building re-opening.

  6. Cold-chain excursion (refrigerated)

    Temperature excursion triggers immediate alert. Facility's after-hours coordinator (security desk) notified. If unrecoverable, fresh-food product loss documented and credited.

5. Public-building procurement framing

Public buildings procure under state / local contract code. Vending typically falls under either a small-procurement threshold (no bid required) or a competitive RFP path.

State-contract path
Most states have a vending services line on the master contract schedule. Procuring from this schedule streamlines the path; pricing is pre-negotiated.
Local RFP path
Many city / county purchasing departments issue their own RFP for buildings outside state-schedule scope. Standard RFP language applies.
GSA Schedule (federal)
Federal buildings procure via GSA Schedule 73 (Food Service / Vending) where applicable. Coordinate with facility GSA representative.
Concession / no-cost contract
Many public-building vending contracts are concession agreements — operator pays a commission percentage to the building / agency. No facility outlay; revenue share documented.

6. Accessibility (ADA Title II)

Public buildings fall under ADA Title II (state + local government services). After-hours placements still meet full ADA requirements — reach range, clear floor space, operating force.

  • Highest operable part ≤ 48 in AFF (ADA Section 308.2.1).
  • Lowest operable part ≥ 15 in AFF.
  • 30 in × 48 in clear floor space (Section 305).
  • Operating force ≤ 5 lbf, no tight grasping / pinching / twisting (Section 309.4).
  • Cashless option present so users without coin/bill handling capability can transact.
  • ADA attestation on file from operator; annual recertification.

7. Implementation checklist

  • Facility site survey — placement, circuit, signal, ADA clearance.
  • Cellular signal test at placement (-100 dBm or better).
  • Dedicated 20A circuit confirmed; surge protection installed.
  • Building security badge program enrollment for operator route drivers.
  • After-hours coordinator contact captured (security desk number).
  • Custodial supervisor briefed on visit schedule.
  • Telemetry dashboard access provisioned for facility manager.
  • Quarterly review cadence scheduled.
