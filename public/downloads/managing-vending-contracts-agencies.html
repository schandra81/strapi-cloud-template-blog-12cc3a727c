Public Agency Vending Contract Management Toolkit

Quarterly agenda, commission reconciliation template, healthy-vending audit checklist, and renewal decision framework

This toolkit gives contracting officers, facility coordinators, and agency wellness leads the working documents to govern an existing vending contract at a federal, state, county, or municipal site. Standards referenced: ADA Section 308, GSA SIN 478-1 vending services context, AbilityOne 41 CFR Part 51 where the placement is Randolph-Sheppard or AbilityOne, HHS/CDC food-service guidelines, FALCPA allergen labeling, and PCI-DSS 4.0. Capability-framed — operator attestations belong in the contract file.

1. Quarterly business review agenda

Schedule the QBR 30 days in advance. Required attendees: contracting officer (or COR), facility coordinator, agency wellness lead (where applicable), operator account manager, operator route supervisor. Block 75 minutes.

  1. Uptime + service SLA review (15 min)

    Operator presents per-machine, per-month uptime over the quarter. Target ≥ 98%. Review Tier-1 incident log (machine offline > 24 hr, cold-chain excursion, payment system down) and resolution times against SLA.

  2. Commission reconciliation (10 min)

    Cross-check operator's revenue report against telemetry transaction count. Variance ≤ 1% expected; > 2% triggers reconciliation per Section 2.

  3. Planogram + velocity (15 min)

    Top-20 / bottom-20 SKUs; operator proposes drops + adds for next quarter. Confirm healthier-vending mix per Section 3 audit.

  4. Compliance attestation refresh (10 min)

    ADA Section 308 attestation, PCI-DSS 4.0 currency, FDA menu labeling adherence (where > 20 placements), state weights-and-measures registration, Randolph-Sheppard / AbilityOne flow-down where applicable.

  5. End-user feedback (10 min)

    Refund volume + reason codes (target < 2% of transactions). Sample of any employee or visitor complaints since last QBR.

  6. Action items + accountabilities (15 min)

    Each open item gets an owner and a due date. Action items roll to the next QBR agenda.

3. Healthy-vending audit checklist

Federal and many state placements require a healthier-vending standard. Audit at every QBR. The checklist below references the HHS/CDC Food Service Guidelines for Federal Facilities and the GSA healthier-food provisions where in effect.

  • ≥ 50% of snack facings meet the HHS/CDC Food Service Guidelines: ≤ 200 cal, ≤ 230 mg sodium, ≤ 10 g added sugar, ≤ 35% calories from total fat (with seafood/seeds/nuts exception).
  • ≥ 50% of beverage facings meet beverage criteria: water, ≤ 40 cal per 8 oz juice, low-fat dairy, unsweetened tea/coffee.
  • Calorie display per FDA menu labeling rule (≥ 20 placements operator-wide).
  • Allergen labeling per FALCPA on each SKU; allergen summary signage on the machine face.
  • ADA Section 308 reach range verified at audit (15-48 in operable parts; ≤ 5 lbf operating force).
  • Healthier-option facings are at eye level (24-60 in shelf) where possible.
  • Pricing parity: healthier options priced ≤ 10% above conventional comparator.
  • Signage promotes healthier options (point-of-decision prompts per CDC guidance).
  • Cold-chain attestation: refrigerated units hold ≤ 40°F per FDA Food Code 3-501.16; telemetry log on file.
  • Quarterly photo documentation of planogram retained in contract file.

4. End-user feedback channels + refund flow

Public-building visitors and staff need an accessible refund and feedback path. Channels and SLAs should be in the contract.

Channel 1 — On-machine QR / NFC
QR or NFC tap on machine face links to operator refund form; auto-credit to cashless transactions within 24 hr.
Channel 2 — Text-to-refund
SMS short code or 10-digit number printed on machine; auto-credit cashless within 24 hr; cash refund mailed within 7 business days.
Channel 3 — Phone
Toll-free number printed on machine; staffed during business hours; voicemail with same-business-day callback.
Channel 4 — Facility coordinator
Local facility contact accepts complaints and forwards to operator; closes loop with end user within 5 business days.
Reporting
Operator delivers monthly refund report by reason code; QBR reviews trend and triggers planogram or machine quality review at > 2%.

6. Annual compliance file checklist

  • Operator's current insurance COI ($2M GL, $1M umbrella, additional-insured endorsement)
  • PCI-DSS 4.0 AOC / SAQ-B-IP
  • ADA Section 308 written attestation (annual)
  • FDA menu labeling attestation where applicable (≥ 20 placements operator-wide)
  • Healthier-vending policy attestation per HHS/CDC Food Service Guidelines
  • Randolph-Sheppard priority attestation where federal cafeteria/vending placement
  • AbilityOne 41 CFR Part 51 flow-down where applicable
  • State weights-and-measures registration where required
  • Quarterly QBR records (last 4 quarters retained)
  • Monthly commission reconciliation records (last 12 months)
  • Refund + complaint log (last 12 months)
