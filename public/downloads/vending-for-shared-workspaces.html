Shared Workspace Vending Pack

Operator-neutral + tenant-management integration + multi-language + dietary inclusivity + hospitality-grade aesthetic

Shared workspaces — coworking, executive suites, business incubators, and mixed-use commercial — have requirements that traditional single-tenant office vending models cannot meet. This pack covers the five specifications that distinguish a coworking-ready vending program: operator neutrality, tenant-management-system integration, multi-language UX, dietary inclusivity for diverse populations, and hospitality-grade aesthetic.

1. Operator-neutral payment + access

Coworking members + their guests + day-pass holders + drop-in users + tenant employees all need access without forced enrollment in a single operator's app. The program must accept any reasonable payment method.

  • Tap-to-pay contactless (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay) without app enrollment
  • Credit + debit card swipe / tap (Visa, MC, Amex, Discover)
  • QR code Pay-with-Phone (PayPal, Venmo, CashApp via vendor like Cantaloupe Pay or Crane Pay)
  • Building access badge tap (HID, LEGIC, Mifare) integrated with tenant-management system
  • Member-app option (optional, never required); guest checkout always available
  • Cash + bill acceptor on at least one machine per floor (residual demand 5-15%)
  • Refund self-serve via QR + SMS (no app install required)

2. Tenant-management system (TMS) integration

Modern coworking + flex-office operators run on Nexudus, Optix, Cobot, OfficeRnD, Andcards, or Yardi Elevate. Vending should integrate so members + tenants can pay via account credits + receive monthly invoicing.

3. Multi-language UX

Coworking member populations skew international + multilingual. Machine UX should support at minimum English + Spanish + simplified Chinese; add languages by membership composition.

Machine UI language
Touchscreen UI supports language toggle (icon-based + at least 3 languages). Crane Merchant Media + AMS Sensit III + AWM Smart Shelf support 8-12 languages.
Signage
Photo-realistic SKU images > text labels. Where text required, dual-language (English + most-common second language at the site).
Allergen + ingredient labeling
Top-9 FALCPA labeling visible per SKU. Bilingual allergen card available on request.
Member app + push
Operator app supports member-language preference; push notifications in member's preferred language.
Support + refund
Multi-language SMS-to-refund + multilingual support phone line. Auto-translate web chat for written support.

5. Hospitality-grade aesthetic

Coworking + flex-office competes on member experience. Vending must read as hospitality + retail, not industrial.

  • Glass-front + open-front coolers (not pushbutton legacy)
  • LED interior lighting at color-accurate 6500 K
  • Wood-grain or brass-trim wrap; coordinated with workspace branding
  • Quiet operation — compressor sound level < 50 dB at 3 ft
  • AI camera cooler (AWM Smart Shelf, 365 Pico, Cantaloupe Go Mini) where budget allows — true grab-and-go feel
  • Integrated planters + neighboring lounge seating turn the area into a micro-pantry destination
  • Telemetry-driven planogram refresh keeps it looking 'always-stocked, never-stale'
  • Recyclable + compostable packaging dominant — visible sustainability matters to member retention

7. Revenue + cost model

Revenue share to landlord / operator
Coworking + flex-office hosts typically take 10-25% commission on transactions. Some prefer flat-rate equipment fee + 100% revenue to vending operator.
Member-account credit
Where TMS integrated, members receive monthly account credit (e.g., $20-50/month for premium membership tier). Credit redeems against vending; landlord receives invoice from operator.
Free-vend programming for member retention
Quarterly free-coffee day, monthly featured-snack giveaway, member-appreciation week. Cost: $4-10/member/month subsidized.
Drop-in / day-pass surcharge
Day-pass holders pay full retail (vs. members at credit-discounted rate). Differentiates membership value.

8. Operator selection — coworking-specific criteria

  • Operator integrates with at least 2 of: Nexudus, Optix, Cobot, OfficeRnD, Andcards, Yardi Elevate
  • Multi-language machine UX (≥ 3 languages standard)
  • Operator-neutral payment stack (tap + card + QR + cash all accepted; no forced app)
  • AI camera cooler experience demonstrated (or premium glass-front grab-and-go)
  • Diverse SKU catalog with international + dietary segment coverage
  • ENERGY STAR + R-290 equipment standard
  • Hospitality-grade aesthetic samples on site visit
  • References from 3+ comparable coworking + flex-office hosts (WeWork, Industrious, Convene, Mindspace, regional brands) with ≥ 18 months tenure

9. Service SLA + cadence

  1. Restock cadence

    Telemetry-driven 2-4×/week; daily for flagship. AI camera cooler refilled daily to avoid empty visual.

  2. Tier 1 SLA — offline, payment, temperature

    <4 hr acknowledgement, <24 hr resolution. Loaner equipment within 24 hr for any AI cooler outage.

  3. Tier 2 SLA — stockout, planogram refresh

    <24 hr acknowledgement, <48 hr resolution.

  4. QBR cadence

    Quarterly with landlord + community-manager; reviews uptime, velocity, member feedback, planogram refresh.
