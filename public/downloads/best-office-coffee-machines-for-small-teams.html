2. Equipment shortlist — small team (5-25 person)

Equipment cost spans $500 (single-serve) to $15K (bean-to-cup). Reliability + service uptime matter more than absolute cost for small teams.

Keurig K-3500 / K-2500 (commercial single-serve)
$1,500-$2,500. K-Cup standard; reservoir + plumbed options; supports 5-15 person teams. Pros: low learning curve, reliable. Cons: pod waste; cost per cup $0.65-$1.10.
Nespresso Professional Aguila 220 / Momento
$3,500-$8,000. Capsule-based; premium espresso + milk; supports 10-30 person teams. Pros: barista-quality espresso. Cons: capsule waste; ongoing capsule cost.
Jura X8 / X10
$5,500-$8,500. Bean-to-cup; bean hopper; supports 10-40 person teams. Pros: fresh beans, no waste, dual-bean hopper. Cons: requires daily cleaning, $400-$800/year maintenance.
Saeco PerfectBrew / Royal OTC
$3,500-$6,500. Bean-to-cup; supports 10-30 person teams. Pros: cost per cup $0.18-$0.30, no pod waste. Cons: requires daily cleaning, programming.
Bunn iMix-3 / iMix-5 (instant + filter combo)
$2,500-$4,500. Multi-station; supports 15-40 person teams. Pros: rapid serve, multiple drink options. Cons: less premium experience.
Pour-over + grinder station (Fellow Aiden + Baratza Vario)
$700-$1,200. Manual pour-over + grinder; supports 5-15 person teams. Pros: lowest cost, premium experience. Cons: labor-intensive, inconsistent for higher headcount.
Filter brewer (Curtis Gemini, Fetco CBS-2131)
$2,500-$5,500. Batch brew; supports 25+ person teams. Pros: high capacity, low cost per cup. Cons: dated experience; quality drops after 30-60 min.

3. OCS provider RFP template — required clauses

Office coffee service (OCS) providers handle equipment + supply + service. Use the clauses below in any OCS RFP.

Equipment + lease model
Provider proposes equipment options; lease ($X/month, no capital), purchase ($X capital + $Y/year service), or capital-included-in-supply model.
Coffee + supply scope
Per-pound or per-cup pricing; minimum order quantity; lead time for orders; substitutions for back-order; private-label option.
Service SLA
Tier-1 (machine down): < 24 hr response, < 24 hr resolve. Tier-2 (calibration, descaling): < 5 days. Routine maintenance: monthly.
Preventive maintenance
Quarterly PM visit; descaling, brew-group inspection, milk-system clean (if applicable), water-filter replacement.
Water filtration
Provider supplies + maintains water filter (Everpure, 3M, or comparable); replacement cadence aligned to gallons or 6-month interval.
Specialty coffee + alternative milk support
Whole-bean roasts (3-5 SKUs minimum); ground coffee; flavored options; alternative milk (oat, almond, soy) where machine supports.
Cup, lid, sleeve, stirrer supply
Provider supplies + manages inventory; recyclable + compostable options where available.
Reporting
Monthly consumption report; quarterly business review; sustainability metrics (recycled content, packaging, fair-trade percentage).
Sustainability + certifications
Provider commits to Fair Trade / Rainforest Alliance / B Corp coffee SKUs ≥ 25% of mix; reusable mug program supported.
Insurance + indemnity
$1M GL, additional-insured to office; food product liability $500K; equipment insurance carried by provider.

5. Water + plumbing + electrical checklist

Coffee equipment lives or dies by water quality. Confirm each item below before install.

  • Water hardness ≤ 50 ppm (3 grains/gal) preferred for espresso; ≤ 100 ppm acceptable with filtration
  • Water filter installed (Everpure, 3M, OmniFilter or comparable); replacement cadence per provider spec or every 6-12 months
  • TDS (total dissolved solids) 75-250 ppm for optimal coffee extraction
  • Plumbed connection: 3/8 in cold-water line; 1/2 in drain line within 5 ft of machine
  • Dedicated 120V/15A circuit for single-serve; dedicated 120V/20A or 240V for commercial bean-to-cup
  • Counter depth + height: confirm machine fits with 6 in clearance behind for ventilation + service access
  • Drainage: trap or floor drain near install; spill containment + drip tray for high-volume placements
  • GFCI outlet within reach; surge protection recommended

6. Operations + maintenance cadence

Daily (office manager / staff)
Empty drip tray + grounds container; rinse milk system (if applicable); refill bean hopper; wipe down exterior.
Weekly
Deep-clean brew group; descale (if hard water); wipe down full machine + counter; restock cups + supplies.
Monthly (provider)
Site visit: full clean cycle, descale, milk-system deep-clean, water-filter check, bean hopper inspection.
Quarterly (provider PM)
Full preventive maintenance: brew-group inspection, gasket replacement if needed, water-filter replacement, calibration.
Annual (provider)
Full machine refurb + capacitor + heating-element inspection; OCS contract review.
On-demand (provider service ticket)
Tier-1 (machine down): 24 hr response. Tier-2 (calibration, software): 5 days. Tier-3 (cosmetic, signage): 14 days.

7. Sustainability + waste-reduction framework

  • Single-serve waste audit: K-Cup recycling program enrolled (Keurig recycling); compostable pods where machine supports
  • Bean-to-cup grounds composting: office composting program or municipal pickup
  • Reusable mug program: provide branded mugs at hire + amenity; discount or incentive for reusable use
  • Cup + lid: shift to PLA / fiber-based compostable + recyclable; FSC-certified paper cup
  • Coffee certifications: ≥ 25% Fair Trade / Rainforest Alliance / B Corp / Organic mix
  • Water-filter cartridge recycling: provider takes back or recycle program
  • Alternative-milk shelf-stable cartons recyclable; on-demand option to reduce waste
  • Annual sustainability report from provider: kg of waste diverted, recycled content %, kWh per cup
