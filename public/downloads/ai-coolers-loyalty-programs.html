AI Cooler Loyalty Integration Playbook

SSO, accrual, tier-based pricing, and behavior triggers

AI coolers (camera-vision smart fridges with frictionless checkout) create the cleanest place to run a loyalty program inside vending. Identity is already established at the door tap, transactions are per-SKU, and the member experience supports tier-aware pricing without slowing throughput. This playbook codifies the operational practices that keep loyalty programs at adoption parity with the operator app and at SSO-friction parity with corporate single-sign-on. It maps to PCI DSS v4.0, ADA Section 309.4, NIST IR 8259 (IoT baseline), and standard FTC unfair/deceptive-practice guidance.

1. Identity + SSO baseline

Loyalty value compounds with identity persistence. Every transaction must resolve to a stable member ID without member action.

Identity source
Corporate IdP (Okta, Azure AD, Ping) or host badge system (HID iCLASS, Lenel, Brivo). Operator integrates via SAML 2.0, OIDC, or badge tap (MIFARE, iCLASS).
SSO friction target
Door-tap to identity-resolved state in ≤ 2 seconds. Manual login fallback retained for app-only members.
Joiner/leaver
IdP joiner provisions member account within 24 hours; leaver de-provisions within 24 hours of HR ticket. Service-account credentials rotated annually.
MFA
Phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2 / WebAuthn) on operator admin console; member identity inherits from corporate SSO MFA, not duplicated at the cooler.

2. Accrual mechanics

  1. Base accrual

    1 point per 1 USD spent (or per host-defined unit). Posted to member balance within 24 hours of transaction settlement. Refunded transactions reverse points within 48 hours.

  2. Healthy-share bonus

    1.5x or 2x accrual on healthy-flagged SKUs (NANA threshold: ≤ 200 cal, ≤ 230 mg sodium, ≤ 35% calories from fat). Drives wellness committee KPI without changing planogram.

  3. Streak bonus

    Bonus on 5+ consecutive weekdays of activity. Capped at one bonus per member per month to prevent gaming.

  4. Referral / activation bonus

    One-time bonus on first transaction (activation) and on referred member's first transaction (referral). 90-day expiry to avoid drift.

  5. Negative signals

    Refund flag reverses accrual. Excessive refund pattern (> 5% of transactions for a single member over 30 days) triggers review, not auto-block.

4. Behavior triggers + notifications

Re-engagement
Member inactive 14 days → push notification with a single-use bonus. Send rate-limited to 1 per 30 days.
Healthy-share nudge
Member viewed healthy SKU but selected non-healthy → next-visit prompt suggests a comparable healthy alternative (opt-in).
Stock alert
Member's frequently-purchased SKU back in stock after stockout → notification with SKU.
Birthday / anniversary
Optional, opt-in, single-use bonus. No demographic collection beyond what member voluntarily provides.
Notification cadence cap
Maximum 3 marketing notifications per member per week; transactional (refund, receipt) unlimited.

5. Privacy + data architecture

  • Member identity + balance + transaction history stored; no compensation, no benefits-eligibility, no protected-class data.
  • Camera-vision raw frames not retained — feature vectors only; deletion within 24 hours of cart finalization.
  • Member dashboard exposes data collected, purpose, retention, and 'delete my data' button. Deletion within 30 days.
  • Consent log retained for the duration of the relationship plus 3 years.
  • Sub-processor list disclosed; DPA signed; data residency stated (US-based for US placements).
  • Camera-vision model card available under NDA; bias-testing methodology documented.

6. Accessibility + safety guardrails

  • ADA Section 309.4 — door, screen, and any input within reach range and ≤ 5 lbf operating force.
  • Allergen labeling per FALCPA on every SKU; member allergen list (opt-in) surfaced at door tap.
  • Healthy-share floor enforced — loyalty mechanics cannot displace healthy planogram allocation.
  • No exclusionary pricing on essentials (water, basic snacks) — must remain at base tier for all members.
  • Tier-eligibility rules reviewed for protected-class neutrality with HR + legal before launch.

8. Pre-signing red flags

  • Tier eligibility tied to demographic / protected-class factors.
  • Raw camera frames retained past 24 hours by default.
  • Notification cadence uncapped — risk of member fatigue + spam reports.
  • Operator declines to publish model card or DPA.
  • Refund flow does not reverse loyalty accrual — accounting drift risk.
This playbook is informational and not legal, privacy, or HR advice. Tier-pricing programs that intersect with employee benefits or compensation should be reviewed by HR, payroll, and counsel before launch.
