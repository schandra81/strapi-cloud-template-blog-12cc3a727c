Government Building and Office Vending Lifecycle Pack

Contract scope, installation checklist, quarterly review template

Government buildings and offices — federal agency offices, state buildings, county admin, city hall — share operating characteristics distinct from private workplaces: structured procurement, accountability under public-records and open-meeting laws, federal-funding implications, and a mixed audience of employees + visiting public. This pack covers the contract scope, installation framework, and the quarterly review cadence that keeps the program performing across the full term.

1. Procurement-path decision

Identify the procurement path early — it determines the contract template, the scoring rubric, and the timeline.

GSA Schedule 73 (federal)
Food Service / Vending. Federal-agency offices procure via GSA Schedule. Streamlined ordering; pre-negotiated terms. Coordinate with agency's GSA representative.
Federal concession (Randolph-Sheppard Act — 20 U.S.C. § 107)
For federal-building vending in some locations, the Randolph-Sheppard Act gives priority to blind licensees through the state's Business Enterprise Program. Verify priority status with state BEP coordinator before issuing competitive RFP.
State master contract
Most states maintain a master vending contract. State agencies + offices can ride the master without independent solicitation in most cases.
Cooperative procurement (NASPO ValuePoint / OMNIA / Sourcewell)
Used by many counties + cities. Streamlined path leveraging pre-competed contracts.
Local RFP / concession agreement
City + county admin offices typically issue independent RFPs or concession agreements. Operator pays commission (8-25%) to the agency.

2. Contract scope — clauses that matter

A government vending contract is more than pricing. Include the scope clauses below.

  • Term + renewal — 3-5 year base; optional 1-year renewals; clear termination-for-convenience clause.
  • Commission schedule (concession path) — % of gross revenue; reporting cadence; payment schedule.
  • Performance standards — uptime %, restock cadence, refund SLA, telemetry availability.
  • Equipment refresh — minimum equipment age at install; refresh trigger (5-7 year typical).
  • ENERGY STAR + sustainability — all refrigerated equipment ENERGY STAR; refrigerants ≤ 150 GWP.
  • ADA attestation — Title II + Section 504 (federal); annual recertification.
  • PCI DSS v4.0 — operator AOC on file; reviewed annually.
  • Background check + access — operator route drivers cleared through agency contractor program; HSPD-12 / PIV-I for federal placements where required.
  • Insurance — commercial general liability ≥ $1M each occurrence / $2M aggregate; auto; workers' comp per state.
  • Open-records compliance — operator acknowledges agency must disclose contract under state public-records / FOIA per applicable law.
  • Federal Funding Accountability — operator's USAspending / SAM.gov registration current (federal-funded path).

3. Installation checklist

Run this checklist before each placement goes live.

  1. Site survey

    Facility manager + operator walk-through. Confirm placement, dimensions, clearance, circuit, signal, ADA path-of-travel.

  2. Electrical

    Dedicated 20A circuit per refrigerated machine; GFCI per NEC 210.8 where applicable; surge protection; circuit labeled in panel.

  3. Telemetry

    Cellular signal test (≥ -100 dBm RSSI); external antenna if marginal. PCI scope kept on operator side via cellular path.

  4. Equipment placement

    Anchored / leveled; clearance per manufacturer (typically 4-6 in rear, 2 in side, 12 in top); ADA clear-floor verified (30 × 48 in).

  5. Software + signage

    Operator portal access provisioned for facility manager; on-machine signage installed (refund process, allergen summary, ADA accommodation contact, language-access copy where required).

  6. Background + badge

    Operator route drivers enrolled in agency contractor-access program; background check on file.

  7. Live test

    Cashless transaction tested end-to-end; refund flow tested; telemetry heartbeat verified; first-day stocking confirmed against planogram.

  8. Go-live communication

    Building-wide announcement (email + lobby signage); refund + customer-service contact published.

5. Quarterly review template (QBR)

Operator + facility manager review quarterly. Public-records implications — keep meeting notes structured and factual.

  • Per-machine uptime % (rolling 3 months).
  • Service-SLA adherence by tier (T1 / T2 / T3).
  • Stockouts: count, MTTR, top-5 SKUs by stockout rate.
  • Revenue + transaction count + average ticket (concession path).
  • Commission paid to date vs. budget.
  • Refund rate + reason codes.
  • Planogram refresh recommendations from telemetry.
  • Energy + Scope 2 metrics (if program-integrated).
  • ADA + PCI + cleaning attestations current.
  • Background-check + badge roster current.
  • User-feedback channel summary (refund inquiries, SKU requests).
  • Action items + owner + due date.

6. Federal-building extras

Federal-building placements add a few specific requirements.

  • HSPD-12 / PIV-I credentials for operator route drivers where required (typically GSA-managed buildings).
  • Randolph-Sheppard priority verified before competitive solicitation.
  • FAR clauses incorporated by reference (if direct federal contract path).
  • Section 508 — digital accessibility for any operator portal exposed to federal users.
  • Buy American Act / Build America, Buy America (BABA) considered where equipment falls in scope.

7. Open-records + transparency

Government vending contracts are subject to public-records / FOIA / state Sunshine laws. Plan for it.

  • Contract drafted with the assumption it may be disclosed in full under public-records request.
  • Commission revenue accounted in the agency's general ledger; visible in budget documents.
  • Performance reports retained per agency records-retention schedule (typically 3-7 years).
  • Operator selection memo + scoring sheet retained per procurement records-retention schedule.
