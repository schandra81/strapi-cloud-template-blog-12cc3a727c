2. PMS integration checklist

AI cooler should post each transaction to the PMS within 60 seconds and reconcile nightly. Confirm operator can attest to integration with the hotel's specific PMS version.

  • PMS family supported: Oracle Opera Cloud, Mews, Cloudbeds, Stayntouch, infor HMS, Maestro, Agilysys, RoomKeyPMS, or proprietary.
  • Integration method: OXI (Opera Exchange Interface) v2.x, Mews Connector API, HTNG charge-posting standard, or HFTP guest-folio-charge spec.
  • Posting cadence: transaction posts to folio within 60 seconds of completion; failure queue retries with operator + front-desk alert.
  • Identity binding: room number + last name verification at first purchase, or app-bound account on check-in; tokenized at cooler, no PAN at PMS.
  • Settlement: nightly reconciliation file (CSV or PMS posting report) to hotel A/R; dispute window 7 days.
  • PCI scope: cooler stays out of PMS PCI scope; AOC + SAQ-B-IP on file from operator.
  • Tax + service charge: cooler applies hotel-specific sales tax + room-service charge (if applicable) per state and city code.

3. Brand-finish + design guide

Hotel AI coolers should match the property brand standard. Most platforms offer custom cabinet wrap, top header, lighting tone, and digital signage on the door.

Cabinet wrap
3M or Avery vinyl, full-color, replaceable; hotel supplies brand-aligned art (4-5 color minimum); typical lead time 14 days.
Header / topper
Backlit graphic 24-36 in wide; hotel logo + 'For Guests' or pantry name; LED color temperature matched to lobby (2700K-3000K typical).
Door digital signage
10-15 in screen displays brand reel, daily specials, weather, and current promotions; CMS controlled by operator with hotel-approved content.
Interior lighting
Dimmable LED 4000-5000K product-presentation lighting; auto-dim during overnight hours to lobby spec.
Glass front + frame
Brushed stainless, matte black, brass-tone, or wood-grain laminate frame options; tempered low-iron glass for accurate product color.
Materials + ADA
Anti-fingerprint coatings, accessible operating parts 15-48 in AFF, ≤ 5 lbf operating force, accessible reach for card reader + dispense bin.

5. Front-desk SOP

  1. Check-in script

    'There's a 24/7 pantry in the lobby/floor X. It posts to your room with a tap of your phone or key. Free first purchase with the QR card in your welcome packet.' (15 sec)

  2. First-purchase friction handling

    Guest can't get cooler to open: front desk confirms room status + folio active, calls operator support, offers complimentary first item from front desk while resolved.

  3. Refund / dispute

    Guest disputes charge: front desk pulls cooler transaction screenshot from operator portal, removes charge from folio immediately, files dispute with operator for credit-back to hotel.

  4. Stockout report

    Front desk or housekeeping spots empty shelves: photo + slot ID texted to operator dispatch number; operator confirms next-visit ETA.

  5. Daily reconciliation

    Night audit reviews cooler posting report against PMS folio postings; flags exceptions for AM follow-up with operator.

6. Operator selection criteria

  • Hospitality reference accounts: 3+ active hotel placements, including same brand family or comparable ADR tier.
  • PMS integration certification with hotel's specific PMS family.
  • Commission tier negotiated: 8-15% of gross revenue typical, scaled by volume; lower tier if operator funds cabinet + cabinet wrap.
  • Telemetry + uptime SLA: 98%+, ≤ 30 min offline alert, ≤ 4 hr Tier-1 response for hospitality placements.
  • Insurance: $2M GL + $1M umbrella, additional-insured to hotel + ownership + management.
  • Brand-approval process: operator commits to design-review cycle with hotel brand standards team.
  • STR/Smith Travel reporting compatibility: F&B revenue reportable to hotel asset manager under appropriate category.

7. Install + launch plan

  1. T-45: brand-approval submission

    Operator submits cabinet wrap + signage to hotel brand standards; typical brand-approval cycle 14-21 days.

  2. T-30: PMS integration test

    Operator + PMS vendor + hotel IT run integration test in staging environment; charge-posting end-to-end test.

  3. T-14: install + commissioning

    Cooler delivered, installed, vision-system calibrated with 200+ training transactions per SKU, network + PMS posting validated.

  4. T-7: front-desk + housekeeping training

    60-min training, FAQ printed, escalation phone numbers, free-first-purchase QR cards stocked at desk.

  5. T-0: launch

    Lobby signage live, in-room collateral live, first-week monitoring with operator on-call.

  6. T+30: planogram review

    First-month velocity report; bottom-15 SKUs replaced; brand-approved new SKUs added.

8. Quarterly business review checklist

  • Per-cooler revenue, basket, transaction count
  • PMS posting accuracy (target ≥ 99.5%); dispute count
  • Uptime by month; vision-system accuracy
  • Local + curated SKU rotation by season
  • Guest-satisfaction signal (TripAdvisor / Google review mentions, in-app feedback)
  • Commission tier review based on volume thresholds
  • Brand-standard compliance audit (cabinet wrap condition, lighting, signage)
