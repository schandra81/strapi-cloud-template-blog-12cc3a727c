Custom Office Coffee Service (OCS) Package Pack

Seven-dimension framework, matrix-mapped bundles, equipment specs, and reference-check guidance

This pack helps procurement, facilities, and workplace-experience teams scope a custom OCS program rather than accept a vendor's off-the-shelf bundle. It covers the seven decision dimensions, three reference bundle archetypes, equipment shortlists by tier, KPI targets, and the questions to ask an operator's existing references before signing.

1. Seven-dimension OCS scoping framework

Most OCS bids fail because the host scopes on price alone. The seven dimensions below let two operators bid on a like-for-like scope.

Headcount + density
Daily on-site headcount, peak occupancy, machines per 100 employees (target: 1 brewer per 50-75 employees for bean-to-cup; 1 per 25-35 for single-cup). Dwell time per drink (45-90 seconds for bean-to-cup at peak).
Drink profile
Mix of brewed coffee, espresso-based drinks, cold brew, tea, hot chocolate, flavored milks. Hybrid sites typically target 60% bean-to-cup espresso menu / 30% drip / 10% specialty.
Equipment tier
Pour-over + airpot (entry), single-cup pod (transitional), bean-to-cup superautomatic (mid), full barista-style (premium). See section 4.
Service model
Full-service (operator restocks + maintains + cleans), micro-market hybrid, self-managed (host buys product, operator maintains). Full-service is standard above 75 employees.
Subsidy structure
Free-vend (host pays 100%), subsidized (host covers 50-100% of base SKUs), pay-per-cup (employees pay $0.50-$1.50). Free-vend is the dominant model for high-retention employers.
Sustainability + sourcing
Fair Trade, Rainforest Alliance, Organic, Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) grade ≥ 80 cupping score, compostable cups, Smart-Way-certified freight, low-GWP refrigerants on cold equipment.
Telemetry + reporting
Cellular telemetry on every brewer (heartbeat, drinks/day, water/grounds flow). Monthly utilization report, quarterly business review (QBR), satisfaction-survey embed (NPS or 5-point smile-rating).

3. Bean + sourcing specification

Grade
Specialty grade per SCA (≥ 80 cupping score). Reject below 78. Operator submits Q-grader scoresheet per origin.
Origin transparency
Single-origin or named-region blends. Lot ID + harvest year traceable to producer co-op or estate.
Roast date freshness
Beans delivered within 21 days of roast. Site rotation FIFO with 60-day max bean shelf life.
Certification stack
Fair Trade USA or Fair Trade International, Rainforest Alliance, USDA Organic where employer ESG mandate applies. Bird Friendly (Smithsonian) is a niche premium tier.
Decaf option
Swiss Water Process or CO2-decaffeinated (chemical-free). Standard offering, not premium upcharge.

5. Water + dairy + ancillary spec

Water filtration
Carbon block + softener (target hardness 50-80 ppm CaCO3, TDS 100-200 ppm per SCA water standard). Reverse osmosis with remineralization for premium tier. Filter replacement every 6 months or 6,000 gallons.
Dairy
Refrigerated milk reservoir (NSF-rated), 2% + whole + oat (Oatly Barista, Califia) + almond options. Telemetry-monitored temp (33-40°F).
Cups + lids
PFAS-free compostable cups (BPI-certified or Cedar Grove accepted), PLA lids, or fiber lids. Optional ceramic mug program with employee-owned cups.
Condiments
Cane sugar, raw sugar, stevia (Truvia or SweetLeaf), erythritol blend. Honey + agave packets. Avoid HFCS-based syrups in premium tier.

6. KPI targets + measurement

Uptime
≥ 98% per machine per month (telemetry-verified). Sub-95% triggers root-cause review at QBR.
Drinks per employee per day
Bean-to-cup mid-tier baseline: 1.4-1.8 drinks/employee/day. Hybrid cafe: 1.8-2.4.
Satisfaction (NPS)
Target NPS ≥ 40 within 90 days of launch, ≥ 50 by month 12. Survey embedded in payment confirmation or mobile app.
Waste
Spent grounds composted or partnered with municipal compost stream. Cup-diversion rate ≥ 60% where compost infrastructure exists.
Cost variance
Monthly cost-per-cup tracking with ≤ 5% variance to bid. Variance > 10% triggers contract clause review.

7. Reference-check guidance

Ask each operator for three references at similar headcount + equipment tier. Use these questions on a 20-minute call.

  • How long has the operator served you? (target: 24+ months for tier confidence)
  • What was the actual uptime over the last 12 months vs. contract SLA?
  • How quickly does the operator resolve a payment-system or brewer outage in practice (not contract SLA)?
  • How does the operator handle planogram or menu refresh requests? Cadence + responsiveness.
  • What was the QBR cadence and quality? Show me a redacted QBR deck.
  • Has cost-per-cup tracked to bid, or have there been pass-through increases? What was the largest increase and notice period?
  • How did the operator handle the first major equipment failure? Loaner machine within 24 hours?
  • Would you re-select this operator at next renewal? Why or why not?

8. Contract clause checklist

  • Uptime SLA with credit mechanism (e.g., 1% monthly fee credit per 1% uptime below 98%)
  • Cost-pass-through formula tied to published commodity index (ICE coffee C, dairy) with cap
  • Equipment replacement cycle (5-7 years bean-to-cup, 3-5 years pod, end-of-life refurb vs. replace decision)
  • Telemetry data ownership — host retains right to export raw data on contract termination
  • Termination for convenience with 60-90 day notice, no early-termination fee after month 24
  • Insurance: $2M general liability, $1M product liability, certificate naming host as additional insured
  • Health-department + food-handler certification on file (where local code applies to coffee service)
  • ADA compliance attestation for brewer height, reach, and operating force per 2010 ADA Standards Section 308 + 309
