Dorm Vending Program Design Pack

Per-placement planograms, sizing model, and campus-card integration checklist for laundry + snack vending in residence halls

Dorm vending pairs two adjacent placements: a laundry-room cooler (detergent, fabric softener, sheets, small household) and a snack/beverage cooler (grab-and-go meal, late-night comfort, hydration). This pack bundles the planograms for each placement, a sizing model for a residence-hall portfolio, and the campus-card integration checklist.

1. Laundry-room vending planogram (40 SKUs)

Laundry rooms are high-frequency, low-basket placements. Residents arrive every 7-10 days, need quick detergent + softener + minor household. Cooler can be a hybrid AI cooler + small-format vending mix.

Detergent + softener — 35%
Tide single-pods, Gain pods, All Free + Clear pods, Persil pods, fabric softener sheets (Bounce, Snuggle), dryer balls. $2.50-$6.00.
Specialty laundry — 18%
Stain stick (Tide-to-Go), color catcher, delicates wash bag, lint roller, mesh wash bag. $3.50-$8.00.
Household + dorm supply — 18%
Single-roll toilet paper, paper towel, dish soap (single-serve), sponges, trash bag 5-pack, all-purpose wipes. $2.00-$6.50.
Personal care — 15%
Single-use shampoo, conditioner, body wash, deodorant, razor 2-pack, toothbrush + paste travel pack. $2.50-$5.50.
OTC + emergency — 7%
Pain reliever 2-pack, allergy 2-pack, antacid, anti-itch, band-aid 5-pack. $2.50-$5.00.
Snack + beverage (small share) — 7%
Bottled water, snack bar, gum — bridge SKUs for laundry-day visits. $1.50-$3.00.

4. Campus-card integration checklist

Dorm vending should accept campus card (dining dollars, declining balance, flex), payroll deduction (RA + staff), and Apple Pay/Google Pay. Confirm each item below.

  • Campus card vendor (Atrium / Blackboard / CBORD / TouchNet) SDK certified
  • Tender mapping: dining dollars, declining balance, flex, meal exchange
  • Settlement: daily file to campus card vendor; reconciliation to auxiliary services finance
  • FERPA DPA signed by operator; SOC 2 Type II on file
  • Apple Pay + Google Pay alongside campus card tap target
  • Refund flow: QR + SMS refund flow to original tender; ≤ 24 hr credit-back
  • Loyalty / gamification (optional): healthy-share double-points, low-volume incentive

5. Restock + operations cadence

Snack/beverage cooler — high-traffic hall
3x/week visits; telemetry-triggered top-off; refresh peak Sunday/Wednesday evening; fresh/perishable rotation per SKU shelf life.
Snack/beverage cooler — mid-traffic hall
2x/week visits; telemetry-triggered top-off.
Laundry vending unit
1-2x/week visits; restock by day-of-week traffic pattern (Sunday + Wednesday typical peak); slower SKUs trimmed quarterly.
Cabinet cleaning + signage check
Weekly per restock visit; deep-clean monthly; signage check at every visit.
Refund + service tickets
Tier-1 (offline, cold-chain): ≤ 24 hr. Tier-2 (single SKU stockout): ≤ 48 hr. Tier-3 (cosmetic): ≤ 7 days.

6. Late-night safety + access considerations

  • Cooler placement in residence-hall main lounge or amenity room with 24/7 lighting + camera coverage
  • Cooler does not narrow corridor below 44 in clear width per egress code
  • Resident-staff (RA + desk) trained on refund + escalation tree
  • Laundry-room placement near laundry-card reload (or campus card auto-replenish) to bundle traffic
  • Cooler tilt + door-open sensors tied to building safety desk + operator dispatch
  • After-hours service-call number visible on cooler face for residents
  • Refund flow accessible without app login (QR + SMS works) for late-night usability

7. Communications + onboarding kit for residence life

Move-in welcome packet
Includes free-first-purchase QR card for snack/beverage cooler + laundry vending; 'how it works' graphic.
Floor signage
11x17 lounge panel: payment options, refund QR, allergen summary, late-night support number.
RA training packet
30-min training: payment, refund, allergen, escalation, refund-dispute flow.
Newsletter copy
150-word piece for residence-life newsletter at start of each semester; planogram refresh announcement.
Refund + dispute resolution
Resident → RA or desk → operator dispatch → auxiliary services if unresolved within 7 days.
Accessibility note
Plain-language note on residence-life site explaining ADA features, alternative refund flow, and accommodation-request channel.

8. Operator selection criteria — dorm placements

  • Higher-ed residence-hall reference accounts ≥ 2 active ≥ 12 months
  • Campus card SDK + FERPA DPA in place
  • Laundry-vending experience (some operators do snack only; confirm laundry-SKU experience)
  • Late-night service SLA (24/7 dispatch; on-call route)
  • Planogram capability for HHS-aligned share + allergen labeling
  • Refund automation + dashboard accessible to residence-life staff
  • Insurance: $2M GL, additional-insured to university; food product liability $1M
This pack is informational. Residence life + auxiliary services + facilities + campus IT should jointly review operator capability claims and attestations before contract execution. Late-night placements should confirm service SLAs and resident-safety coordination with campus police.
