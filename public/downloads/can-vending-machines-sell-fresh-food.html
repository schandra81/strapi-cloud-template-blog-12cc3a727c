Fresh-Food Vending Evaluation Pack

Site qualification, operator rubric, supply-chain interview script

Fresh-food vending (TCS/PHF — Time/Temperature Control for Safety / Potentially Hazardous Food) is regulated under FDA Food Code Chapter 3 and state retail food code. This pack qualifies sites and evaluates operators against the supply-chain and HACCP capabilities required.

1. What 'fresh food' means in vending

Fresh-food vending covers TCS items — refrigerated salads, sandwiches, fruit cups, parfaits, hot entrees (in heated micro-markets), sushi, and dairy. Hold temperatures are 41°F or below (cold) per FDA Food Code 3-501.16, or 135°F or above (hot).

Cold fresh
Salads, wraps, sandwiches, sushi, yogurt, fresh fruit cups, hummus packs — hold 35-41°F.
Hot fresh
Heated micro-market entrees, paninis — hold ≥ 135°F or via fast-reheat tech with consumer microwave.
Out of scope
Shelf-stable snacks, packaged baked goods, candy — not regulated as TCS.

2. Site qualification checklist

  • Daily foot-traffic ≥ 100 unique visitors for break-even on fresh program.
  • Dedicated 208V/20A or 115V/15A circuit for refrigerated cabinet; surge protection.
  • Ambient temperature controlled (no outdoor / non-conditioned placement).
  • Floor load > 800 lb concentrated; service clearance 36 in front, 6 in sides + rear.
  • Cellular signal > -95 dBm or guest Wi-Fi for telemetry + temperature monitoring.
  • Trash + recycling within 25 ft for packaging disposal.
  • Site permit allowance — confirm with facility for TCS food sales (state retail food permit + sales tax registration).

5. Supply-chain interview script

Use this script during operator selection. Each question should yield a documentable answer.

  1. Commissary

    Where is the commissary? Who owns it? When was the last health-department inspection and what was the score?

  2. Co-pack relationships

    Which co-packers supply prepped salads / wraps / sandwiches? Are co-packers FSMA-PCQI certified?

  3. Transport

    Refrigerated transport — own fleet or 3PL? Average dock-to-machine time? Temperature data logger on every trip?

  4. Restock cadence

    Daily or 2x daily for high-turn sites? Pull-and-rotate schedule documented?

  5. Date marking

    How is the consume-by date determined? Is it per FDA 3-501.17 (≤ 7 days at 41°F)? Operator-side discard SOP at expiry?

  6. Recall capability

    If a supplier issues a recall, how fast can the operator pull SKUs from every machine in the portfolio? Documented < 4 hours?

  7. Waste metric

    What is the current waste % on fresh? Industry baseline 8-12%; capable operators run 4-6%.

  8. Cold-chain audit rights

    Can host pull cold-chain logs and audit randomly?

6. Cold-chain SLA template

Monitoring
Cellular telemetry probe, 5-minute sampling, dashboard visible to operator + host.
Tier 1 alert
Internal temp > 41°F for ≥ 30 min → immediate operator dispatch, 4-hour on-site SLA, SKU pull + full credit.
Tier 2 alert
Compressor short-cycle, door ajar > 5 min → 24-hour response.
Door-open audit
Every dispense event timestamped; sustained door-open beyond authorized restock flagged.
Product loss
Operator-borne discard at excursion or expiry; never passed to host or sold post-excursion.

7. Reporting + governance

  • Monthly cold-chain log export.
  • Per-SKU waste % and discard log.
  • Pull-and-rotate event log.
  • Recall drill record (annual).
  • Quarterly business review with planogram refresh.
  • Annual operator audit (random — host or third party).
