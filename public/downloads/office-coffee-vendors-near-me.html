Office Coffee Vendor Selection Framework

Category matrix, capability rubric, service radius, and SLA expectations

This framework gives offices a structured way to evaluate local + regional + national office coffee vendors. References to NSF/ANSI 4 + 18 commercial equipment standards, UL 197, ENERGY STAR for commercial brewers, ADA Section 308 + 309 for accessible operable parts, and PCI-DSS 4.0 where the program includes account-linked OCS. Capability-framed throughout — every vendor claim should be attestable in writing.

2. Capability + service-radius rubric

Score each shortlisted vendor 1-5. Operator should attest in writing.

Service radius + technician coverage
5 = dispatchable technician within 1 hr drive; 3 = within 2 hr; 1 = > 2 hr or partner-dispatched. Verify by ZIP-code lookup with operator.
Reference accounts in your metro
5 = ≥ 5 in matching size band within metro; 3 = ≥ 3; 1 = none in metro. Reference call required.
Equipment specification + ENERGY STAR / NSF
Operator should provide NSF/ANSI 4 + 18 certified brewers + ENERGY STAR where commercially available. UL 197 for cooking-style equipment.
Tier-1 service SLA (machine down)
5 = ≤ 24 hr ack + resolution; 3 = ≤ 48 hr; 1 = no contractual SLA.
Tier-2 service SLA (descale, filter, calibration)
5 = scheduled cadence + on-demand within 5 days; 3 = scheduled cadence only; 1 = on-call.
Water filtration program
5 = under-counter cartridge with documented replacement; 3 = scheduled; 1 = none.
Bean origin + certification
5 = ≥ 50% fair-trade / rainforest-alliance / organic + named roasters; 3 = ≥ 30%; 1 = no certification commitment.
Plant-milk + condiment range
5 = oat / almond / soy + multiple sweetener tiers; 3 = oat + 1 alternative; 1 = dairy only.
ADA Section 308 + 309 compliance
5 = written attestation + annual verification; 3 = written attestation; 1 = none.
Insurance + liability
$2M GL + $1M umbrella + additional-insured endorsement; food product liability ≥ $1M.
