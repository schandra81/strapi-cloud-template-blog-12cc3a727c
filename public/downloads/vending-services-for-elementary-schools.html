Elementary School Vending Pack

Placement options, planogram by audience, Smart Snacks compliance checklist, allergen protocol, PTA partnership template

Elementary school vending operates under the strictest USDA Smart Snacks beverage rules: at K-5 schools, plain water, ≤ 8oz low-fat milk, and ≤ 8oz 100% juice are the only beverages permitted during the school day per 7 CFR 210.11. This pack codifies five artifacts — placement options, planogram by audience, Smart Snacks checklist, allergen protocol, and PTA partnership template — so an elementary school is equipped to run a wellness-aligned program rather than over-fit a HS template.

1. Placement options

Most elementary schools do NOT place student-accessible snack vending during the school day. Beverage coolers (water + milk), staff-lounge AI smart-coolers, and after-school PTA event vending are the three viable placements.

2. Planogram by audience

Audience drives planogram. Student-accessible during the school day is the tightest planogram; after-school event mode is the most flexible.

Student daytime
Plain water (any size); ≤ 8oz low-fat or fat-free milk (including flavored); ≤ 8oz 100% juice. No snack vending. No calorie-free or low-calorie beverages.
Staff lounge
Coffee, tea, water, snack and beverage mix per staff council; not required to meet Smart Snacks if locked to staff.
After-school event
Concession-style mix — water, juice, milk, fruit, granola bars, baked snacks; aligns with district wellness policy and PTA partnership.
Family / parent center
Adult-targeted mix — coffee, water, healthy snacks; not required to meet Smart Snacks if outside student access window.

3. Smart Snacks compliance checklist

  • Per-SKU attestation: every student-accessible SKU passes the USDA Smart Snacks Product Calculator.
  • Beverage rules: water (any size); ≤ 8oz milk; ≤ 8oz juice — NO calorie-free or low-calorie beverages at ES.
  • School-day window: midnight to 30 minutes after last bell, per 7 CFR 210.11(b).
  • After-school SKU swap timestamped in operator log.
  • Wellness committee approves SKU list quarterly.
  • Operator delivers per-quarter attestation in writing.
  • State competitive-food overlay confirmed with school nutrition director.

4. Allergen protocol

Elementary populations have the highest concentration of severe food allergies. Allergen protocol is the most important operational element of an ES vending program.

  1. FALCPA labeling on every SKU

    Big-9 allergens (milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soy, sesame) declared in plain language per FALCPA.

  2. Allergen summary on machine face

    Summary card lists each SKU and its allergen profile. Card is visible from the clear floor space (Section 305).

  3. Peanut/tree-nut policy

    If the school has a nut-free policy, operator excludes any SKU containing peanut or tree-nut allergen including 'may contain' SKUs.

  4. Cross-contact disclosure

    Operator attests in writing to manufacturing-site cross-contact disclosures for any SKU sold.

  5. Refund + epinephrine protocol

    Refund flow includes an allergen incident report; school nurse on the operator's incident escalation list.

  6. Annual allergen review

    Annual review with school nurse + nutrition director; updates planogram for any new policy changes.

5. PTA partnership template

Most ES vending revenue routes through the PTA / PTO. The partnership template covers approval, revenue split, and event coordination.

Approval workflow
PTA board approves the placement + commission split; wellness committee approves the SKU list; school board approves the contract.
Commission split
Common splits: 50/50 PTA/school, or 100% to PTA with school covering electrical. Document in MOU.
Revenue use
Typically funds family events, classroom supplies, field trips, or playground equipment per PTA policy.
Event coordination
PTA coordinates after-school event scheduling with operator — Open House, Curriculum Night, Spring Fling — 2-week lead time.
Annual review
PTA + administration review revenue, KPIs, and SKU updates annually.

6. Equipment specification

Water + bottle-filler
Combination unit per ASHRAE / plumbing code; filter cartridge replacement cadence documented.
Beverage cooler (milk + juice)
Royal Vendors / Dixie-Narco / AMS Sensit; 35-40°F; per-row velocity telemetry + temperature alarm.
Staff AI smart-cooler
AWM Smart Shelf, 365 PicoCooler, Selecta; staff-card-locked; loss rate target < 2%.
Event combo machine (PTA storage)
Mobile combo for after-school events; ADA Section 308 compliant; cashless EMV + NFC.
Telemetry
Cellular telemetry on every machine; sub-15-minute heartbeat; temperature alarm 4-hour SLA.

7. Parent communication template

  • Pre-launch letter from principal + PTA president + wellness coordinator.
  • Smart Snacks compliance explanation (what students can/cannot get during school day).
  • Allergen protocol explanation (FALCPA labels + nut-free policy if applicable).
  • After-school event-mode SKU set posted on PTA page.
  • Refund channel published on PTA + operator app.
  • Annual update with revenue + KPIs + planned use of funds.

8. Operator selection checklist

  • Operator has 24+ months of K-5 placements (NOT just HS) with references.
  • Operator's allergen protocol is documented and operator can attest to cross-contact disclosures.
  • Operator can attest to ES-specific Smart Snacks beverage rules (water + ≤ 8oz milk + ≤ 8oz juice).
  • Operator's telemetry and cashless capabilities meet contract spec.
  • Operator carries insurance (GL ≥ $2M / $5M aggregate, auto, workers' comp, cyber).
  • Operator has executed a wellness-policy review with the district.
  • Operator's restock cadence + after-school event flexibility are contractual.
This pack is a working ES program reference. Localize to PTA charter and district wellness policy; confirm allergen protocol with the school nurse and state competitive-food rules with the school nutrition director.
