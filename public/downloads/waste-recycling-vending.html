Office Vending Waste + Recycling Pack

Packaging + bins + supplier + telemetry + ESG QBR framework

Waste + recycling is where most office vending programs either earn their sustainability credentials or quietly contradict them. This pack covers the five-layer framework: packaging shift, bin + diversion infrastructure, supplier criteria, telemetry-verified waste data, and the ESG QBR documentation needed for CDP / SBTi / CSRD reporting.

1. Packaging shift (upstream)

Diversion downstream is only as good as recyclability upstream. Apply these standards to every SKU bid.

  • Aluminum cans ≥ 70% recycled content (industry avg ~73%; leaders ≥ 90%)
  • PET bottles ≥ 50% rPET; targeting ≥ 25% per EPR-state floor (CA SB 54, OR SB 582, ME LD 1541, CO HB 22-1355)
  • Snack films: mono-material PE preferred; How2Recycle Store Drop-Off labeling on every applicable SKU
  • Paperboard cartons: FSC- or SFI-certified, ≥ 70% recycled content, recyclable curbside
  • Compostable cups (coffee): BPI-certified, PFAS-free (per FDA 2024 voluntary phase-out)
  • No PVC, PVdC, EPS, expanded polypropylene in primary packaging
  • Plastic stirrers + cutlery eliminated (birchwood or FSC paperboard alternatives)
  • Supplier disclosure of How2Recycle labeling per SKU; refreshed annually

2. Bin + diversion infrastructure (point of disposal)

Three-stream sorting at every vending machine. Bin signage drives 80% of contamination prevention.

Bin count + type
Three bins per machine site (or shared across machines on the same floor): landfill / commingled recycle / compost. Toter + Rubbermaid + ClearStream commercial-grade options.
Bin colors
Recycle Across America standardized labels OR municipal-required colors (Seattle SPU, NYC DSNY, San Francisco DPW, Portland Metro each differ).
Signage
Photo-realistic SKU images at user eye-level. Generic icons cut contamination 30% less effectively than photo signage.
Placement
Within 10 ft of machine; not blocked by chairs or partitions; ADA-compliant approach 30 × 48 in clear floor space.
Liner type
Recycle bin: clear liner (helps janitorial team verify; signals transparency to users). Landfill: opaque. Compost: BPI-certified compostable liner.
Service cadence
Recycle + compost bins emptied separately by janitorial team. Coordinate with facilities to avoid commingling at janitor closet (program-killer).

3. Supplier + downstream MRF criteria

Sustainable vending is meaningless if the recycle bin actually goes to landfill (a common failure). Verify the downstream chain.

  • Recycling vendor provides annual MRF destination report — specific MRF name + address
  • Compost vendor provides annual destination report — specific composter (Cedar Grove, Recology, NYC Composting, similar)
  • MRF accepts the materials your packaging stream contains; verify aluminum + rPET + paperboard at minimum
  • Snack film (multilayer) takeback: Store Drop-Off (How2Recycle) partnership — TerraCycle programs as supplementary not primary
  • Contamination policy: > 10% contamination triggers municipal landfill rejection in most jurisdictions
  • End-of-life equipment: refrigerant reclamation per EPA Section 608 + scrap-metal recycling at every machine refresh

4. Telemetry + waste data

Per-SKU velocity data
Operator supplies monthly per-SKU velocity → multiplied by packaging mass per SKU → packaging mass generated per month.
Diversion-rate calculation
Recycle weight + compost weight / total waste weight = diversion rate. Quarterly waste audit verifies.
Contamination rate
Percentage of recycle bin contents that are landfill-bound. Target ≤ 10%; > 15% triggers signage refresh + program intervention.
Emission-factor reporting
Annual kg CO2e per vend split by electricity + refrigerant + freight + packaging. Operator supplies for Scope 3 reporting.
Quarterly waste-audit cadence
Operator + janitorial + sustainability lead conduct quarterly bin audit. Document with photos + weight + composition.

5. ESG QBR documentation

Operator brings this package to every QBR. Sustainability lead + facilities sign-off.

  • Per-SKU packaging-material disclosure (aluminum %, rPET %, mono-PE %, paperboard %, compostable %)
  • Annual emission factor per vend (kg CO2e/vend); methodology + verification body
  • Quarterly diversion rate + contamination rate per site
  • MRF + composter destination receipts + annual destination report
  • Equipment refresh log — ENERGY STAR + R-290 transition % for the host's fleet
  • Telemetry-verified route mileage allocation per host (for Scope 3 freight allocation)
  • Supplier transition roadmap for non-recyclable SKUs; date-certain milestones
  • EPR-compliance attestation for applicable states (CA SB 54, OR SB 582, ME LD 1541, CO HB 22-1355, WA + NY pending)

7. Behavior change + employee engagement

  • Earth Day or quarterly waste-audit volunteer day with sustainability ERG
  • Signage refresh every 6 months — kept fresh prevents 'background noise' effect
  • Bin-audit data shared at all-hands or sustainability newsletter (transparency drives behavior)
  • Compete + reward: floor-level diversion-rate contest with small reward
  • New-hire orientation includes 1-minute on the recycle / compost / landfill sort
  • Visiting-guest signage near machine — 'how to sort' for non-employees

8. Equipment-level reductions

Operator's machine fleet emissions allocate to host Scope 3. Push for fleet transition.

ENERGY STAR fleet share
Target 100% on host's account at next refresh cycle. Year-over-year trajectory reported at QBR.
R-290 / R-600a fleet share
Target 100% at next refresh. R-404A out by 2027 across host's fleet.
Telemetry fleet share
100% telemetry by year 1. Enables dynamic restock = 30-50% vehicle-miles reduction.
EV route vehicle share
Operator commits to EV transition trajectory (e.g., 50% by 2028, 100% by 2032 in metros with grid + infrastructure).
End-of-life recycling
Refrigerant reclamation per EPA Section 608 + scrap metal recycling at every retirement. Certificate filed.

9. Operator selection checklist

  • Three-stream bin infrastructure included in operator scope or partnered with host janitorial
  • Quarterly waste audit + diversion-rate report
  • Annual per-vend emission factor with methodology
  • ENERGY STAR + R-290 equipment standard
  • Telemetry on every machine with operator-supplied raw data export
  • Supplier transition roadmap for non-recyclable SKUs
  • EPR-compliance attestation for applicable states
  • References from 3+ ESG-mandated hosts (Fortune 500 sustainability programs) with ≥ 24 months tenure
