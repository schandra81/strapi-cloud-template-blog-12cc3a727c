2. Creative brief template — per campaign

Use the brief structure below for each monthly campaign. Operator marketing team + auxiliary services + student-affairs comms collaborate.

Campaign name + dates
Working title, launch date, end date, hero week.
Audience
Primary (e.g., freshmen, athletes, late-night studiers), secondary (parents, alumni, sponsors).
Insight + hook
One-sentence audience truth driving the campaign (e.g., 'midterms peak demand for healthy fuel + caffeine').
Key messages
3 bullets max — what the audience should remember.
Creative + assets
TikTok (3-5 videos), Instagram Reels (2-3), Stories (5-7 frames), poster art, RA email graphic.
Distribution
Channels + partner accounts + paid amplification (if any).
KPIs + measurement
Engagement, transactions per cooler, redemption codes, healthy-share share if applicable.
Compliance + brand
Campus brand standards approval; allergen + dietary attestation language; refund + accessibility reference linked.

3. Sponsor pitch deck — outline + key sections

Local businesses, supplier brands, and athletic boosters can sponsor cooler placements, planogram features, or campaign weeks. Use the deck outline below.

1. Cover + ask
Sponsor logo + campus brand + one-line ask ($X for [campaign / placement / SKU spotlight]).
2. Audience + reach
Enrollment, foot traffic at top cooler placements (union, library, athletics), follower counts on student-life social, anticipated impressions.
3. Cooler placement + brand integration
Branded cooler-wrap option, sponsored shelf, sponsored SKU spotlight, sponsored campaign theme.
4. Activation calendar
Where the sponsorship lights up: cooler-face, social, RA blast, on-site activation, athletic event tie-in.
5. Sustainability + community impact
Local sourcing tie-in, AASHE STARS angle, minority-owned business angle, alumni/community resonance.
6. Measurement + reporting
What sponsor receives: impressions, transactions per spotlighted SKU, redemption codes, social engagement, optional case study.
7. Pricing tiers
Bronze ($X / single campaign), Silver ($XX / quarter), Gold ($XXX / academic year + cooler wrap), Title sponsor (capital share + multi-year).
8. Compliance + terms
Campus brand standards approval, advertising-content standards, exclusivity clauses if applicable, termination + refund language.
9. Next steps
Decision deadline, sponsorship agreement template, contact.

5. Compliance + content standards

  • Campus brand standards: logo, color, fonts, and tone approved before publication
  • FERPA: no student-identifiable data in campaign creative (no student names, IDs, or photos without signed release)
  • FALCPA: allergen labeling visible in any food-SKU creative
  • Dietary attestation accuracy: vegan/GF/nut-free claims verified by operator + dietitian before promotion
  • Sustainability claims: any 'local,' 'plant-forward,' or 'organic' framing tied to operator's signed STARS report
  • Accessibility: campaign assets include alt-text + captions; cooler-face signage maintains ADA standards
  • Sponsor exclusivity: confirm no conflict with existing dining-services or athletics sponsor contracts

6. Measurement framework — KPI dictionary

Top-funnel
Reach, impressions, engagement rate, UGC mentions, hashtag usage.
Mid-funnel
QR redemptions, app installs, loyalty signups.
Bottom-funnel
Transactions per cooler per week, basket size, repeat purchase rate, spotlighted SKU sell-through.
Brand + experience
Survey-measured satisfaction, refund rate, complaint volume, dietitian audit score.
Sustainability + STARS
Local SKU sell-through, plant-forward share trend, packaging-recyclability progress.
Sponsor reporting
Per-sponsor: impressions, transactions, redemption codes, social engagement on co-tagged content.

7. Operator + campus comms workflow

  1. Monthly editorial meeting

    Operator marketing + auxiliary services + student-affairs comms + sustainability office review next-month campaign brief; approve assets + sponsor tie-ins.

  2. Creative production

    Operator funds creator + production; student-affairs comms reviews for brand + compliance. Final approval ≤ 7 days before launch.

  3. Launch + amplification

    Cross-channel post + RA blast + parent email; operator-funded paid amplification for top campaigns (Earth month, finals).

  4. Mid-campaign check-in

    Day 7 mid-campaign data review; pivot creative or amplification if KPIs lagging.

  5. End-of-campaign reporting

    Within 7 days of campaign end: KPI report, learnings, sponsor reporting if applicable.

  6. Quarterly campaign review

    Joint review of 3-month campaign mix; planogram + creative + sponsor tier adjustments for next quarter.

This pack is informational. Student-affairs comms + brand standards + sustainability office + dining services should jointly review campaign briefs and sponsor agreements before publication. Operator-funded campaigns should align with campus content standards and avoid student-identifiable content without signed release.
