Industrial Vending Planogram Playbook

High-calorie, familiar-brand, shift-aligned planogram design

Industrial workforces — warehouse, manufacturing, distribution, oilfield service, logistics — show measurable preferences that diverge from office or retail mixes. Higher calorie demand, stronger preference for familiar mainstream brands, higher hydration share, sharp shift-3 caffeine + hot-snack tilt. This playbook codifies the planogram patterns + ACOEM hydration framing + measurement structure to converge on a high-performing mix.

1. Four behavioral observations from industrial telemetry

Operator-side telemetry (Cantaloupe Seed Pro, Nayax, 365 Retail Markets) aggregated across industrial placements shows four consistent patterns. Each maps to a planogram lever.

Calorie-density preference
Industrial workforce sell-through skews toward 200-400 cal SKUs (meal-replacement, calorie-dense salty/sweet) vs office sell-through at 100-250 cal.
Familiar-brand preference
Top-10 SKUs in industrial placements are typically Frito-Lay + Mondelez + Hershey + Coca-Cola + Pepsi mainstream. Premium + specialty brands underperform vs office.
Hydration peak
Hot-environment + heavy-labor placements show beverage facings 35-50% water + electrolyte (ACOEM industrial workforce + ACGIH heat-stress framing).
Shift-3 caffeine + hot-snack tilt
Shift-3 placements show 40-50% beverage facings caffeinated (coffee + energy + caffeinated soft drink) and meal-replacement + comfort snack weighting.

3. Planogram lever — familiar-brand preference

  1. Top-10 anchor SKUs

    10 SKUs in every industrial machine are mainstream anchors — Doritos Nacho Cheese, Lay's Classic, Cheez-It, Snickers, Reese's, M&M Peanut, Hershey Milk Chocolate, Cheetos Crunchy, Pringles Original, Honey Bun. These earn their facing through velocity, not novelty.

  2. Premium + specialty share

    10-15% of facings can carry premium + specialty (KIND, RXBAR, Lärabar, premium jerky) for the workforce subset that wants it. Beyond 15%, sell-through drops sharply on industrial placements.

  3. Local + regional brands

    Where a regional brand has strong familiarity (e.g., Goldfish in northeast, Takis in southwest, BBQ Lays in southeast), 2-3 facings for the regional tilt.

  4. Wellness-program signaled

    NAMA FitPick mark on better-for-you SKUs serves the worker who's looking; doesn't substitute for mainstream tonnage.

4. Planogram lever — hydration share

ACOEM industrial-workforce guidance + ACGIH 2024 TLV for heat stress frame hydration as a safety control. Industrial beverage planograms reflect that by elevating water + electrolyte share.

  • Water — bottled water (Aquafina, Dasani, generic) 15-20% of facings
  • Electrolyte — Gatorade, Body Armor, Liquid IV, Sqwincher 15-20% of facings
  • Carbonated soft drink — Coke, Pepsi, Dr Pepper, Mountain Dew 30-40% of facings
  • Energy drink — Red Bull, Monster, Bang, Celsius 10-15% of facings (higher on shift-3)
  • Coffee / RTD coffee — Starbucks Doubleshot, Java Monster, cold brew 5-10% of facings
  • Other — tea, juice, milk-based 5-10% of facings
  • Hot-environment placement — water + electrolyte share elevated to 50%+ during summer + in foundry/forge/hot-work breakrooms

6. Compliance + labeling overlay

  • FDA 21 CFR §101.8 — calorie disclosure visible on machine face per SKU; updated on planogram change
  • FALCPA — allergen disclosure visible at planogram or via QR; updated on SKU change
  • ACOEM industrial workforce guidance — hydration share ≥ 30% as baseline; ≥ 50% in heat-stress placements
  • NAMA FitPick / FitPick Select — better-for-you marking optional but consistent across the network
  • ADA 2010 §305 + §308 — reach range + clear-floor-space preserved (not a planogram issue per se but verified on every install)
  • ANSI/NAMA 2-2011 — equipment service clearance + hot-surface labels preserved
