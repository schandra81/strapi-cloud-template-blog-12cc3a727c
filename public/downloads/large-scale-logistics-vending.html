Large-Scale Logistics Vending Operations Playbook

Master agreement, account team model, fleet management cadence

A 3,000-worker fulfillment center or a multi-site logistics network is a different operator-management problem than a single break room. This playbook codifies the master-agreement structure, account-team model, and fleet-management cadence a national-scale operator should be equipped to deliver.

1. Master agreement architecture

At national or multi-site scale, the contract sits in three layers: master agreement at the corporate level, site addendum at each property, and quarterly SLA review.

  1. Master agreement (corporate level)

    Pricing framework (commission or fixed-fee schedule), SLA tiers, insurance + indemnity, data + privacy posture, ESG attestations, change-management process. Term 3-5 years with 90-day renegotiation window.

  2. Site addendum (per facility)

    Headcount + shift mix, equipment list, planogram baseline, restock cadence, on-site coordinator, EHS PPE requirements, badging/access procedure, local sales-tax permit number.

  3. Quarterly review

    Per-site KPI report rolled up to corporate. Issues escalated to the operator account team within 5 business days; resolution tracked against master-agreement SLA.

  4. Termination + transition

    180-day notice typical at multi-site scale. Transition plan includes asset removal, equipment-buyout option (where the host elects), planogram + SKU handoff, and final-month reconciliation.

3. Fleet management cadence

Equipment fleets at large scale rotate on a documented capital cycle. Operator should be able to attest to fleet age, maintenance status, and refresh roadmap.

Asset register
Per-machine: model, serial, install date, refrigerant type, last PM service, current uptime, planogram capacity. Updated at every PM visit.
Capital cycle
Refrigerated equipment 7-10 year useful life; non-refrigerated 10-15 years; payment readers + telemetry modules 5-7 years. Operator funds replacement under master agreement at no incremental charge to host.
PM schedule — weekly
Route supervisor visual inspection: cleanliness, signage, payment reader function, dispense mechanism.
PM schedule — monthly
Technician PM: refrigerant pressure check, fan/condenser cleaning, payment reader firmware, telemetry heartbeat verification.
PM schedule — quarterly
Comprehensive: door gaskets, coil cleaning, electrical inspection, deep clean of dispense mechanism, payment terminal recertification.
PM schedule — annual
Compressor + refrigerant cycle inspection (EPA Section 608 certified technician); ENERGY STAR verification; ADA recertification site visit; insurance + license verification.

4. Telemetry + dashboard

  1. Real-time visibility

    Cellular sub-15-minute heartbeat from every machine. Dashboard rolls up to facility, region, and corporate views. Host receives access for facility, regional, and corporate users.

  2. Alert routing

    Tier 1 alerts (offline > 30 min, temperature excursion, payment down) route to operator Regional Operations Manager + Site Lead; host on-site coordinator copied where the contract elects.

  3. Reporting suite

    Monthly: uptime, velocity, refund volume, scope 2 kWh. Quarterly: SLA adherence, planogram refresh recommendations, ESG roll-up. Annual: total cost of program + benchmark vs prior year.

  4. Data export

    API or SFTP export to host data lake / BI environment. Supports the host's procurement + sustainability reporting needs.

5. Insurance + indemnity at scale

  • General liability $5M per occurrence / $10M aggregate
  • Umbrella / excess liability $25M
  • Product liability (food-borne illness coverage) $5M minimum
  • Auto liability $2M for route vehicles
  • Workers' compensation per state statute
  • Cyber liability $5M (covering payment data + telemetry breach)
  • Host listed as additional insured + waiver of subrogation
  • Mutual indemnity with carve-outs reviewed by host General Counsel
  • Annual certificate-of-insurance refresh + 30-day cancellation notice

6. ESG + reporting framework

Scope 2 emissions report
Quarterly per-machine kWh × eGRID subregion factor = tCO2e. Aggregated to facility, region, corporate.
Refrigerant inventory + transition
Per-machine refrigerant type + charge weight; replacement path to R-290 within 36 months of contract effective date.
Equipment end-of-life
EPA Section 608 refrigerant reclaim attestation; scrap metal recycling; e-waste handling for payment + telemetry modules.
Workforce health metrics
Healthy-SKU share of facings + velocity. Reported to host wellness or sustainability team for inclusion in CDP / GRI / SASB disclosures.
Operator scope 3 contribution
Operator route emissions reported where the host's scope 3 disclosure includes vendor transport.

  • Uptime + SLA breach rate by site + region + total
  • Per-site velocity vs planogram-target ratio
  • Scope 2 emissions report
  • Fleet age + refresh roadmap status

  • Open Tier 1/Tier 2 escalations
  • Action items + owner + due date
