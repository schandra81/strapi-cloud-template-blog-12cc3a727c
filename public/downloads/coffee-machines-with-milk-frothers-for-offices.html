3. OCS contract scope — milk-frother-specific clauses

Beyond standard OCS clauses, milk-frother programs require specific scope around milk supply, alternative-milk support, milk-system cleaning, and dairy-handling SLA.

Equipment lease + capex model
Provider proposes lease ($X/month, no capex), capital-buy + service contract, or capital-included-in-supply pricing.
Milk supply scope
Fresh dairy delivery cadence (daily / 2x/week); refrigerated storage included; cold-chain maintained at 35-40°F.
Alternative milk support
Oat (Oatly Barista, Minor Figures), almond (Califia, Pacific Foods Barista), soy (Silk Original Soy), coconut. Machine compatibility attested.
Milk-system cleaning cadence
Daily auto-clean cycle by user; weekly deep-clean by provider; quarterly milk-system replacement (gaskets, tubing) per HACCP.
Dairy-handling SLA
Provider attests to milk-supply temperature monitoring + delivery within 24 hr of order; back-up dairy on-site for surge.
Service SLA
Tier-1 (machine down): < 12 hr response, < 24 hr resolve. Tier-2 (milk-system fault): < 6 hr response, < 24 hr resolve (critical for office service).
Preventive maintenance
Monthly site visit; quarterly major PM; annual full refurb; milk-system replacement on cycle.
Water filtration
Provider supplies + maintains water filter (Everpure, 3M H2 Series); replacement every 6-12 months or per gallon spec.
Reporting + sustainability
Monthly drink consumption report; quarterly business review; sustainability metrics (Fair Trade %, recycled cup share, milk waste reduction).
Insurance + indemnity
$1M GL, $1M food product liability, additional-insured to office; equipment insurance provider-side.

4. Water filtration spec — milk-frother machines

Milk-frother + espresso machines are far more sensitive to water quality than drip brewers. Specify each parameter below.

Total hardness (CaCO3)
≤ 50 ppm (3 grains/gal) preferred; ≤ 100 ppm acceptable with regular descaling.
TDS (total dissolved solids)
75-250 ppm for optimal extraction; below 75 ppm produces flat-tasting espresso, above 250 ppm risks scale.
pH
6.5-7.5 neutral; soft + acidic water (< 6.0) corrodes brass boiler components.
Chlorine + chloramine
≤ 0.1 ppm; activated-carbon filter removes; protects gaskets + machine internals.
Filter system
Everpure 4FC-S + 7CB-5 (combo carbon + softening) or 3M HF Series; replacement every 6 months or 9,000 gal whichever first.
Backflow prevention
Required per local plumbing code (typically RPZ or AVB).
Drain
1/2 in indirect drain within 5 ft; drip-tray drain hose for full-plumbed install.
Monitoring
Annual water test by provider; replace filters based on actual gallons (smart-meter recommended for high-volume sites).

6. Operations + maintenance cadence

Daily (office manager)
Run auto-milk-system clean cycle; empty drip tray; replenish bean hopper; check refrigerated milk supply.
Weekly (provider or trained staff)
Manual milk-system deep-clean (gasket, tubing); descale (if water hard); wipe down brew group; restock alternative-milk cartons.
Monthly (provider)
Site visit: full milk-system inspection, descaling, water-filter check, bean hopper inspection, software update if applicable.
Quarterly (provider PM)
Milk-system gasket/tubing replacement, full clean cycle, brew-group inspection, water filter replacement.
Annual (provider)
Full refurb; heating-element + capacitor inspection; OCS contract review; sustainability + consumption report.
Service ticket SLA
Tier-1 (machine down): < 12 hr response, < 24 hr resolve. Tier-2 (milk-system fault): < 6 hr response, < 24 hr resolve. Tier-3 (cosmetic): < 5 days.
