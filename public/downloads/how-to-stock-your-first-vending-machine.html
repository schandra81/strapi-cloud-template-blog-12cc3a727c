First Vending Machine Stocking Playbook

Planogram templates and sourcing reference for new operators

The first 30 days of stocking a machine sets margin, refund rate, and shrink for the next 12 months. This playbook codifies the planogram templates and sourcing reference LetUsVending operator-network onboarding uses — referenced against NAMA operator-survey planogram norms, FDA FALCPA + FASTER Act allergen labeling, FDA Food Code 2022 cold-chain, and the 2020-2025 Dietary Guidelines healthy-share framework.

1. Planogram templates by machine type

Each template is a starting baseline. Tune week-by-week with telemetry velocity data; planogram v2 ships at the 4-week mark.

Snack machine (Crane National 187 / AMS 39 / USI 3145 — ~40 selections)
Salty (Chex Mix, Cheez-It, Doritos, Cheetos, Lays, Pretzels) — 12 slots; Sweet baked (Pop-Tarts, Cookies, Brownies) — 6; Candy (Snickers, M&M, KitKat, Reese's, Twix) — 6; Protein/healthy-share (KIND, RXBAR, Quest, almonds, jerky, fruit) — 8; Pastry (Pop-Tart, Honey Bun, Danishes) — 4; Gum/mint — 2; Reserve — 2.
Drink machine (Royal Vendors RVCC / Dixie Narco — ~9-12 selections)
Water + sparkling — 2; Coke + Diet Coke — 2; Pepsi + Diet Pepsi — 2; Mountain Dew or Dr Pepper — 1; Sprite — 1; Gatorade — 1; Red Bull or Monster — 1; Iced coffee or tea — 1.
Combo machine (snack + drink, ~30-38 selections)
Snacks 60% / drinks 30% / healthy-share endcap 10%. Reserve at least one column for water and one for protein bar.
Fresh-food machine (Crane Merchant / AMS Sensit / Avanti)
Salads, wraps, sandwiches, parfaits, fruit cups, hummus + veg, hard-boiled eggs, yogurt. ≤41°F continuous per FDA Food Code 2022 §3-501.16. Date-marked discard ≤7 days §3-501.17.
Coffee machine (single-cup bean-to-cup)
Drip + decaf + espresso + flavored option + non-dairy + sweetener; cup/lid/stir/napkin par stations on countertop adjacent.

4. Healthy-share design

  1. Definition

    Better-for-you = ≤200 cal/pack, ≤10g added sugar, ≤480mg sodium, OR ≥5g protein/whole-grain. Aligns with FDA 21 CFR 101.65(d)(2) framework where 'healthy' is claimed.

  2. Share target

    Start at 25% facings; expand to 35-40% once velocity tells you the workforce will buy it. Forced healthy-share without telemetry signal increases shrink.

  3. Placement

    Eye-level + reach-zone, top two rows for an adult workforce. Thorndike et al. (Harvard 2014/2020) — 10-30% velocity lift from prominent placement alone.

  4. Signage

    Green-leaf or 'better-for-you' sticker on selection card. FALCPA allergen statement visible on every package.

5. Pricing + margin math

Margin target
Aim for 50-55% gross margin blended across snacks + drinks + healthy-share. Snacks typically 55-65%; sodas 35-45%; energy 40-50%; healthy-share 45-55%.
Pricing reference
Match local convenience-store pricing for branded SKUs (Cheez-It $1.75-$2.25, Snickers $2-$2.50, 12 oz Coke $1.50-$2, Red Bull $3.50-$4.50, KIND $2.50-$3).
Round to $0.25
$1.50, $1.75, $2.00, $2.25 — fewer pricing tiers reduces refund disputes and matches kiosk/credit-card displays.
Premium-placement uplift
Airports, hotels, and 24-hour facilities support 25-50% pricing uplift over office baseline; reflect in your contract.

6. Labeling + compliance

  • FALCPA + FASTER Act top-9 allergen labels on every SKU (milk, egg, fish, crustacean shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soybeans, sesame)
  • FDA Nutrition Facts panel (21 CFR 101.9) including added-sugars line on every packaged SKU
  • Calorie disclosure on machine front per 21 CFR 101.8 (vending machine calorie rule) for operators with 20+ machines
  • Selection-card health/nutrient claims limited to those in 21 CFR 101.13 + 101.65
  • Allergen summary card on the machine or in the alongside-signage
  • Refrigerated machines temperature-monitored continuously per FDA Food Code 2022 §3-501.16

7. Quality + refund controls

  1. Refund flow

    Cashless: refund within 24 hours via app, SMS, or kiosk. Cash: refund slip honored at the next visit (max 7 days). Refund rate >2% triggers planogram + machine quality review.

  2. FIFO rotation

    Each restock pulls expiring product forward. Visible 'best by' check on every SKU placed.

  3. Pre-stock checks

    Inspect machine cleanliness, dispense flap, cooler temperature, payment reader operation. Photograph planogram for QBR documentation.

  4. Telemetry feedback loop

    Weekly velocity report drives the next restock. Top 20% of SKUs gets 60% of refacing budget; bottom 10% rotates out at the 4-week mark.
