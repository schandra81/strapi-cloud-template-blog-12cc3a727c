Vending Business Launch Playbook

Parallel-track execution and anchor account development for the first 6 months

Most vending business startups fail not on the equipment but on running the four launch workstreams in series instead of parallel. This playbook codifies the parallel-track execution model — entity + tax, equipment + telemetry, sourcing + planogram, and anchor-account sales — that gets a new operator to 8-12 placed machines in 5-7 months. Referenced against IRS Pub 583 (starting a business), NAMA (National Automatic Merchandising Association) operator practice, FDA FALCPA + FASTER Act labeling, FDA Food Code 2022 cold-chain, NEC 210.8 GFCI, and PCI-DSS 4.0 cashless requirements.

2. Entity, tax, and compliance setup

  1. Entity formation

    LLC for most solo operators; S-corp election at ~$50-80k+ net income for SE-tax planning. File with the Secretary of State; cost $50-$500 depending on jurisdiction.

  2. Federal + state registrations

    EIN from IRS (free, online), state sales-tax permit, local business license, FDA food-facility registration if applicable (21 CFR 1.225), some states require a 'merchandise vendor' permit.

  3. Insurance

    General liability $1M/$2M typical; product liability rider; commercial auto for the service vehicle; workers' comp where required by state once an employee is hired.

  4. Cashless / payments

    PCI-DSS 4.0 scope is reduced when telemetry/card readers tokenize at the point of swipe (Nayax, Cantaloupe, Worldnet, USAT). Attest to SAQ-B-IP at minimum.

  5. Recordkeeping

    IRS Pub 583 — keep receipts, mileage logs, service-call logs, and sales-tax detail for 4-7 years. Quickbooks Online or Wave for bookkeeping.

3. Equipment + telemetry scope

Machine mix (starter pack)
2 combo (snack + drink) or 1 snack + 1 drink. Newer used (Crane, AMS, USI, National) 5-10 years old runs $2,000-$4,500; refurbished $4,000-$7,000; new $5,500-$9,500.
Telemetry
Cellular modem on every machine — Cantaloupe Seed, Nayax VPOS Touch, 365 Retail Markets Smart Series, or comparable. Cost $30-$60/machine/month. Non-negotiable for route economics.
Card reader / cashless
EMV + NFC + mobile-wallet. Coverage rate at workplace placements typically 60-80% cashless; airports 80-95%.
Electrical
NEC 210.8 GFCI 20A 120V receptacle within 6 feet of each machine; coordinate with site facilities.
Refrigerated machines
FDA Food Code 2022 §3-501.16 — ≤41°F for TCS foods; thermometer in cold zone.

4. Sourcing + planogram

  • Distributor accounts: Vistar (national vending wholesale), McLane (broadline), Eby-Brown (convenience), local C&S, Costco Business
  • Planogram v1: 60% top-velocity SKUs (Cheez-It, Doritos, Pop-Tarts, Snickers, water, Coke, Pepsi, Red Bull), 25% mid-velocity, 15% healthy-share endcap
  • Healthy-share definition documented (≤200 cal, ≤10g added sugar, ≤480mg sodium) so anchor accounts can reference it
  • Allergen labels on every SKU per FALCPA + FASTER Act (top-9)
  • Margin target: 50-55% gross margin on combined product mix; less is a planogram problem, more is a pricing problem
  • Refresh cadence: quarterly drops/adds based on telemetry velocity

5. Anchor-account development

Anchor accounts are 50-300 employee sites where one win unlocks two-machine economics. Targeting beats canvassing.

  1. Target profile (anchor)

    50-300 employees, 24/5 or 24/7 schedule, single decision-maker (HR director / facilities manager), existing vending vendor underperforming.

  2. Outreach sequence

    LinkedIn + email + warm referral. Mention specific operating signal — telemetry uptime, refund-flow SLA, healthy-share %. Avoid generic 'we offer vending' pitches.

  3. Discovery questions

    Headcount, shift mix, existing vendor + frustration points, healthy-share + dietary-coverage requirements, commission expectation, contract length willingness.

  4. Pilot terms

    30-90 day pilot at single location, telemetry-reported KPIs (uptime, healthy-share velocity, refund rate, NPS), break clause.

  5. Commission framework

    Tiered by gross — 0% under $X, 5-10% on the next tier, 10-15% above. NAMA operator-survey median 5-8%.

6. Operational SLA the new operator must publish

  • Tier 1 (offline / payment down / temperature excursion) <24 hr acknowledgement, <24 hr resolution
  • Tier 2 (single SKU stockout) 24-48 hr ack, 5-7 day resolution
  • Tier 3 (cosmetic / signage) 5-day ack, 14-day resolution
  • Refund flow within 24 hours of member request (app / SMS / on-machine)
  • Monthly route audit + quarterly business review with the anchor
  • Insurance + sales-tax + ADA / FALCPA attestation provided in writing on request
