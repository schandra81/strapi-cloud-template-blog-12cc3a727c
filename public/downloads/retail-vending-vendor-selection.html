Retail Vending Vendor Selection Framework

7-dimension RFP scoring, reference check protocol, contract template

Retail vending vendor selection is a procurement exercise with merchandising, IT, finance, and operations stakeholders. Without a structured scoring framework, the cheapest bid wins and the program underperforms within 12 months. This pack codifies a 7-dimension RFP rubric, a reference check protocol that surfaces real performance, and a contract template anchored on measurable SLAs.

1. 7-dimension RFP scoring rubric

Score 1-5 per dimension; weight by retailer priority. Operators below 3.5 weighted average rarely deliver on stated commitments.

2. RFP scoring template — per dimension detail

  1. Equipment + telemetry

    Score 5 = ENERGY STAR Tier 2 on 100% of placements + LTE Cat-M1 telemetry + EMS standard + ≤ 5 year equipment age. Score 1 = legacy mixed-age fleet, partial telemetry, no EMS.

  2. Payment + PCI posture

    Score 5 = current PCI DSS v4.0 AOC + EMVCo + Apple Pay/Google Pay on every machine + cellular-isolated CHD path + transparent interchange-plus pricing. Score 1 = magstripe-primary, dated AOC, opaque fee schedule.

  3. Merchandising + planogram

    Score 5 = direct supplier relationships at 3+ major manufacturers, ≥ 30% healthy share, quarterly planogram refresh from telemetry, supports promotional + sampling campaigns. Score 1 = single-distributor SKU mix, static planogram, no campaign support.

  4. Service + SLA

    Score 5 = ≥ 98% uptime SLA + telemetry-driven cadence + Tier-1 SLA <24 hr + dedicated route driver per region + auditable performance reports. Score 1 = no SLA, scheduled-only restock, no telemetry-driven service.

  5. Reporting + integration

    Score 5 = REST API documented + daily settlement + monthly per-SKU reports + loyalty SDK integration + 24-month online history. Score 1 = portal-only access, manual exports, no API.

  6. Financial terms

    Score 5 = transparent commission + fee schedule + flexible subsidy basket + reasonable termination terms (90-day for cause / 180-day for convenience). Score 1 = opaque pricing, multi-year lock-in, expensive termination.

  7. Account team + references

    Score 5 = dedicated account manager + ≥ 3 retailer references at comparable scale + named route-driver continuity + escalation path to operator executive. Score 1 = shared account team, junior references, no named escalation path.

3. Reference check protocol

References supplied by the operator self-select for positive feedback. Use the protocol below to get past the boilerplate.

  1. Ask for 5 references; call 4

    Including 1 retailer at similar scale, 1 retailer in a similar geography, 1 customer who switched away (operator may not provide; ask), 1 multi-year customer.

  2. Specific service questions

    What is the actual MTTR on Tier-1 incidents? When did the operator last miss a restock? What was the root cause? What change did they make?

  3. Account-team continuity

    How many account managers have you had in the past 3 years? Has the route driver changed? How was the transition handled?

  4. Reporting quality

    Do you trust the operator's data? Have you found discrepancies between portal data and your own observations?

  5. Contract experience

    Has the operator ever invoked a price-change or fee-change clause? How was it communicated? What about contract renewal — were original terms honored?

  6. Real value-add

    What does the operator do that you wouldn't get from a commodity vendor? Anything specific?

4. Operator background + due diligence

  • Years in business (≥ 5 years typical minimum; younger operators acceptable with strong recent references).
  • Geographic coverage — operator's own routes vs. subcontracted routes. Subcontracted is acceptable but adds an SLA pass-through risk.
  • Insurance — commercial GL ≥ $1M each occurrence / $2M aggregate; auto; workers' comp per state. Certificates of insurance current.
  • PCI DSS v4.0 AOC current within 12 months.
  • SOC 2 Type II or ISO 27001 for portal / API.
  • Litigation search — any active or recent litigation involving the operator (PACER, state court records). Disclose findings to legal.
  • Bankruptcy / financial stability — operator's audited financials or D&B report (private operators) or recent investor disclosures (PE-backed operators).
  • Workforce — operator's route-driver retention rate (high turnover is a service-quality red flag); E-Verify participation.
  • DBE / MBE / WBE certifications where retailer's program supports diverse-supplier goals.

5. Contract template — anchor clauses

Standard contract structure. Build from these anchor clauses; legal owns final language.

Term
3-year initial; up to two 1-year renewals at retailer's option. Termination for cause: 30-day cure + 60-day exit. Termination for convenience: 180-day notice (retailer); 90-day notice (operator, with equipment-removal coordination).
Commission / fee schedule
Commission % of gross revenue or fixed-fee placement model. Interchange + processor + operator markup transparently separated. Annual price-adjustment formula (CPI-indexed typical).
SLA + performance
Uptime ≥ 98% (rolling 3 months); Tier-1 SLA <24 hr; restock cadence per placement plan; refund process documented. Performance below threshold triggers operator action plan; sustained underperformance is grounds for termination for cause.
Equipment + refresh
All refrigerated equipment ENERGY STAR + low-GWP refrigerant; refresh trigger 5-7 years or earlier on energy / reliability flags. Refresh capital from operator.
Data + reporting
Portal access for retailer; REST API documented; daily settlement; monthly per-SKU report; quarterly QBR.
Insurance + indemnity
Operator names retailer as additional insured; indemnity for operator-caused incidents.
Confidentiality + data
Loyalty / member data is retailer's property; operator processes per documented DPA. Cardholder data per PCI DSS.
Compliance
Operator complies with ADA, FALCPA, PCI DSS, applicable cashless-discrimination law, applicable open-records / consumer-disclosure law.

6. RFP timeline — typical 60-90 day path

  1. Days 0-14 — pre-RFP scoping

    Stakeholders (procurement, IT, merchandising, finance, ops) align on scope. Draft RFP + scoring rubric.

  2. Days 14-21 — RFP issued

    RFP to 4-6 operators. Mandatory pre-bid call. Site walk-through at representative stores.

  3. Days 21-45 — responses + scoring

    Operators respond. Internal scoring against rubric. Shortlist 2-3 for interviews.

  4. Days 45-60 — interviews + references

    Operator presentations to full committee. Reference calls. Contract negotiation begins.

  5. Days 60-75 — contract sign

    Final scoring + selection memo. Contract finalized. Transition plan with incumbent operator (if applicable).

  6. Days 75-90 — install / cutover

    Equipment install at pilot stores; cutover from incumbent; full rollout per agreed schedule.

7. Post-selection — first 90 days

  • Pilot stores live and reporting via portal within 30 days of contract sign.
  • Telemetry dashboard available to retailer team.
  • First monthly settlement reconciles cleanly to operator's portal data.
  • Day-60 operational review — uptime, SLA adherence, refund rate, planogram velocity.
  • Day-90 first QBR — full performance review + adjustments for full-rollout.
  • Any incumbent transition completed; no service gaps.
