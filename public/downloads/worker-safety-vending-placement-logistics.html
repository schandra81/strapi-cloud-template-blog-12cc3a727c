Logistics Hub Safety-Integrated Placement Pack

Placement matrix, safety review framework, PPE overlay, and fire-code checklist

Vending placement in logistics hubs is a worker-safety design decision before it's a hospitality decision. Forklift paths, sprinkler coverage, NFPA 101 egress, and OSHA aisle marking all dictate where machines can and cannot sit. This pack consolidates four working references — placement matrix, safety review framework, PPE overlay, fire-code checklist — for EHS leads, facility services, and operators.

2. Safety review framework — walk-through

EHS lead + operator + facility services walk every candidate placement before install. Below: standard agenda + sign-off framework.

  1. Egress audit

    Walk path with tape measure. Confirm corridor width retained ≥ 44 in (or ≥ 36 in occupant load < 50). Photograph at install + each year.

  2. Sprinkler audit

    Confirm ≥ 18 in below sprinkler deflector. Confirm no obstruction of coverage pattern (NFPA 13 §8.6.5). If micro-market shelving extends > 4 ft above floor, additional analysis.

  3. Forklift / MHE path audit

    Confirm placement outside marked aisle. Confirm no blind corner. Consider bollard or guard rail if forklift speed > 5 mph in proximity.

  4. Dock + fall-hazard audit

    Confirm ≥ 25 ft from any dock-leveler edge or unprotected fall ≥ 4 ft. Confirm placement not in dock-leveler swing arc.

  5. Emergency equipment audit

    Confirm placement does not obscure or restrict access to fire extinguisher, eyewash, AED, fire alarm pull, sprinkler control valve.

  6. Lighting + visibility audit

    Lighting ≥ 20 fc at machine face. No blind corner that could harbor a worker injury or theft incident.

  7. Sign-off

    EHS, facility services, and operator sign-off recorded with date and photographs. Retained in facility records.

3. PPE overlay — where vending intersects PPE policy

Workers in active PPE zones may need to remove PPE to transact. Plan the placement so transactions happen in PPE-doffing or hybrid zones, not active hazard zones.

Hard hat zone (cold storage racking)
Vending placement in adjacent break room only; not under racking. Worker doffs hat upon entry to break room, not at machine.
Hi-vis vest zone (forklift adjacency)
Acceptable if placement is outside marked forklift aisle and worker dwell at machine ≤ 30 seconds.
Eye + face protection zone (sortation laser, induction)
Vending in break room only; never at the work cell.
Hearing protection zone (> 85 dBA)
Vending in adjacent quiet area (< 70 dBA at machine). Worker keeps protection on at machine; ensure tactile / visual interface (ADA §309) does not require speech.
Cold-storage gloves (freezer)
Vending in pre-cool break room only; ensure card reader operable with insulated glove (some readers require glove-doffing — operator confirms spec).
Cleanroom / food-safety zone
Vending excluded entirely; designate hand-hygiene station + visible vending outside controlled zone.

4. Fire-code checklist

  • Egress corridor width retained ≥ 44 in per NFPA 101 §7.1 (or ≥ 36 in if occupant load < 50)
  • Sprinkler deflector clearance ≥ 18 in per NFPA 13 §8.6 — vertical clearance measured
  • Equipment UL-listed; nameplate visible
  • Dedicated 20A circuit per NFPA 70 §210; not shared with shop tools or air handlers
  • GFCI protection where placement within 6 ft of water source (NFPA 70 §210.8)
  • Combustible cardboard restock packaging not stored on top of cabinet
  • No placement under sprinkler riser, FDC, or fire alarm pull station
  • No placement obstructing alarm-bell sightline or strobe coverage
  • Travel distance from any point in alcove to exit ≤ NFPA 101 occupancy max (typically 200–250 ft for industrial)
  • Annual AHJ walk-through coordinated; vending alcove included in facility fire-safety plan

5. OSHA 300 + incident-log integration

Vending placements interact with OSHA 300 incident logs in two ways: (1) injuries at the vending alcove (slip/trip, ergonomic, struck-by MHE), and (2) hydration-related incidents (heat illness). Plan the integration formally.

  • Vending alcove included in facility slip/trip incident review
  • Ergonomic review — reach range, weight of dispense, repetitive-motion exposure for restock route staff (operator-side)
  • Hydration access mapped against any OSHA 300 heat-illness entries — closes loop between vending design + heat-illness prevention
  • Struck-by MHE near-miss reports reviewed for vending-alcove location pattern
  • Operator route-staff incidents reported to facility EHS within 24 hours; mutual-aid + reporting flow documented
