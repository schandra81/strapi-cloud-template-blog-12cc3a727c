Campus AI Cooler Design Pack — Healthy Vending

Planogram framework, RFP language, and payment integration scope for HHS-aligned campus AI coolers

Healthy-vending on campus is no longer a marketing line — it's enforceable through HHS Food Service Guidelines (FSG), Smart Snacks (where K-12 framing applies), AASHE STARS alignment, and the campus dietitian's quarterly audit. This pack bundles the three working documents (planogram framework, RFP language, payment integration scope) a campus team needs to launch a healthy-vending AI cooler program.

1. HHS-aligned planogram framework

HHS Food Service Guidelines (FSG) define healthy-share thresholds for federally connected food service. Universities adopting the FSG floor (typically 50-75% HHS-aligned facings) can use the framework below.

Beverage — HHS Standard
Water (still + sparkling) ≥ 35% of beverage facings. No-cal or low-cal (< 40 cal/8 oz) ≥ 50%. Caloric beverages ≤ 25 cal/8 oz on standard sizes. Allowed: 100% juice, plain milk, low-fat dairy, unsweetened tea/coffee.
Snack — HHS Standard
Per serving: ≤ 200 cal, ≤ 230 mg sodium, ≤ 35% calories from total fat (with exemptions), ≤ 10% saturated fat, ≤ 35% sugar by weight (with exemptions for fruit + dairy).
Fresh + perishable — HHS preferred
Whole fruit, hard-boiled egg cup, Greek yogurt, hummus + veg, salad bowl, sushi tray, charcuterie cup. ≥ 25% of cooler facings recommended.
Dietary attestation per SKU
Vegan, vegetarian, gluten-free, nut-free, dairy-free, kosher, halal — labeled at SKU and aggregated on cooler face.
Allergen labeling
FALCPA on every pack; cooler face displays allergen summary with cross-contamination note.
Local + regional share
Operator targets ≥ 15% local/regional SKUs (within 250 miles); supports AASHE STARS OP-8 sub-credit.
Dietitian audit
Semi-annual audit by campus dietitian; pass = ≥ 50% HHS-aligned facings, allergen labeling complete, no expired SKUs.

2. Planogram template — 70 SKU HHS-aligned mix

Target ≥ 50% HHS-aligned. Substitute by campus dietitian preference. Allergen labels per FALCPA on every SKU.

Water + unsweetened — 22%
Still water, sparkling water (Spindrift, LaCroix), unsweetened tea/coffee, sparkling unsweetened. $1.50-$3.50.
Low-cal beverage — 15%
Coconut water, electrolyte (no/low cal), low-cal sparkling, plain kombucha. $2.50-$4.50.
Fresh + perishable — 18%
Greek yogurt, whole fruit, hard-boiled egg cup, hummus + veg, salad bowl, sushi tray. $3.50-$8.50.
Protein + smart bar — 12%
RXBAR, KIND Protein, Built, Larabar, Aloha. $2.50-$4.00.
HHS-aligned snack — 13%
Baked chips, Skinny Pop popcorn, almond pack, trail mix, rice cakes, whole-grain crackers. $1.50-$3.50.
Plain milk + dairy — 5%
Plain milk (low-fat), Greek yogurt, cottage cheese. $2.00-$3.50.
Treat / non-HHS — 15%
Bottom-half tier; non-HHS allowed but not promoted; transparent labeling. $1.50-$4.00.

3. RFP language — healthy-vending clauses

Drop the following clauses into any healthy-vending RFP. Each requires written operator commitment.

HHS-alignment commitment
Operator commits to ≥ [50/65/75]% of cooler facings meeting HHS FSG snack + beverage standards as documented quarterly.
Smart-Snacks compatibility (where K-12 framing applies)
Operator commits to ≥ 50% Smart-Snacks-compliant facings; nutrition-fact-panel attestation available per SKU.
Allergen labeling
FALCPA labeling on every SKU; cooler-face allergen summary; ≥ 25% nut-free options labeled.
Dietitian audit
Operator supports semi-annual campus dietitian audit; remediation plan due within 30 days for any non-compliance.
Local + regional sourcing
Operator commits to ≥ 15% local/regional SKUs (within 250 miles); annual sustainability report aligned to AASHE STARS OP-8.
Sugar-sweetened beverage cap
Operator commits to ≤ 25% of beverage facings being sugar-sweetened (> 25 cal/8 oz).
Dietary attestation labeling
Vegan, vegetarian, gluten-free, nut-free, dairy-free, kosher, halal — visible at SKU; summary on cooler face.
Refresh cadence
Operator refreshes planogram quarterly based on velocity + dietitian input; underperforming SKUs replaced within 60 days.

4. Payment + identity integration scope

Healthy-vending coolers benefit from incentive-payment integration (free-water program, healthy-share subsidies). Operator should support each component below.

Campus card SDK
Atrium, Blackboard Transact, CBORD, TouchNet OneCard, or comparable; certified integration; FERPA DPA signed.
Healthy-share subsidy
Operator app supports campus-funded credit ($X/student/visit) for HHS-aligned SKUs; expires at end of academic term; usage report monthly.
Free-water program
Operator supports unlimited-water voucher for residence-hall placements on heat-illness days (coordinated with student health).
Apple Pay + Google Pay
Contactless EMV, mobile wallet at cooler face; tokenized; PCI-DSS 4.0 attestation.
Loyalty + healthy-share gamification
Optional: app loyalty program where HHS-aligned purchases earn double points; gamification toward wellness rewards (campus rec credit, dining-dollar boost).
Refund + dispute
QR + SMS refund flow ≤ 24 hr credit-back; campus dietitian + auxiliary services see refund dashboard.

5. Communications + signage kit

  • Cooler-face panel: 'How it works' + payment options + refund QR + allergen summary
  • Healthy-share badge: visible at cooler face explaining HHS standard + percent compliance
  • Dietary attestation legend (vegan, GF, etc.) at cooler face
  • Per-SKU shelf-talker tag with allergen + dietary attestation icons
  • Free-water + healthy-share subsidy explainer card if program funded
  • Newsletter copy: 200-word explainer for residence-life or student-health newsletter
  • Refund + escalation 4x6 lobby card with QR + SMS + dietitian email contact for nutrition concerns

6. Dietitian audit checklist (semi-annual)

  • Count HHS-aligned facings; target ≥ 50% (or campus-defined floor)
  • Sugar-sweetened beverage facings ≤ 25%
  • Allergen labeling complete + accurate
  • Fresh/perishable rotation: no expired or near-expiry SKUs visible
  • Dietary attestation icons consistent and accurate
  • Refresh cycle: bottom-15 SKU replacement plan from operator
  • Subsidy/incentive program reconciliation
  • Student-feedback survey results review

7. Operator selection criteria — healthy vending

  • HHS / Smart-Snacks planogram capability attested + demonstrated
  • Campus dietitian audit history at peer institution placements
  • Local + regional sourcing capability (≥ 15% within 250 miles)
  • AASHE STARS reporting capability (OP-8 + OP-9)
  • Campus card SDK + FERPA DPA on file
  • Insurance + food product liability $1M + cyber $5M
  • Reference calls with campus dietitian + auxiliary services at 2+ peer institutions

8. Annual review + improvement process

  • Q1: prior-year HHS-alignment trend; dietitian summary report
  • Q1: student wellness survey results; planogram input requests
  • Q2: pilot 10 new SKUs targeting wellness goals; measure velocity vs displaced SKUs
  • Q3: finalize annual planogram + HHS-alignment target adjustment
  • Q4: AASHE STARS reporting + sustainability annual report from operator
This pack is informational. Campus dietitian, auxiliary services, student health, and the sustainability office should jointly review operator capability claims and HHS-alignment attestations before contract execution. HHS Food Service Guidelines are voluntary at universities but increasingly required by state systems and AASHE STARS-pursuing institutions.
