Wheelchair-Accessible Vending Audit Pack

ADA reach range, clear floor space, operable parts force testing, and VPAT framework

This pack consolidates the working documents a facility accessibility coordinator, procurement officer, or vending operator can use to audit a placement against the 2010 ADA Standards for Accessible Design (Section 308 reach range, Section 305 clear floor space, Section 309 operable parts) and request a VPAT for digital interfaces. Use as a capability reference when scoping a new program or recertifying an existing fleet.

1. Reach-range audit — Section 308

Reach range applies to operable parts: selection buttons, card readers, contactless tap zone, dispense doors, refund buttons, and any user-facing touchscreen. Verify both unobstructed and obstructed cases.

Unobstructed forward / side reach — high
≤ 48 inches above finished floor (§308.2.1, §308.3.1).
Unobstructed forward / side reach — low
≥ 15 inches above finished floor (§308.2.1, §308.3.1).
Obstructed forward reach — high
If obstruction ≤ 20 in deep, max reach is 48 in; if obstruction > 20 in and ≤ 25 in deep, max reach drops to 44 in (§308.2.2).
Obstructed side reach — high
If obstruction ≤ 10 in deep, max reach is 48 in; if obstruction > 10 in and ≤ 24 in deep, max reach drops to 46 in (§308.3.2).
Verification method
Field tape measure at install and at annual recertification. Photographs taken at each measurement point; retained in procurement file 5 years.

2. Clear-floor-space audit — Section 305

  • 30 in × 48 in minimum clear floor space provided at the operable parts (§305.3).
  • Clear floor space allows either forward or parallel approach (machine documented for which approach it supports).
  • Knee + toe clearance under any front-projecting trim is ≥ 9 in toe / ≥ 27 in knee if forward approach is required (§306).
  • Floor surface stable, firm, slip-resistant per §302.1; carpet pile ≤ ½ in with secured edges (§302.2).
  • Clear floor space does not overlap a major circulation path that creates conflict — accessible route width retained at ≥ 36 in (§403.5.1).
  • Threshold at entry to alcove ≤ ½ in beveled (§303.3) if machine sits in a recessed alcove.

3. Operable-parts audit — Section 309

  • Operable parts function with one hand (§309.4).
  • No tight grasping, pinching, or twisting of the wrist required (§309.4) — paddle handles or recessed buttons preferred over round knobs.
  • Operating force ≤ 5 lbf (§309.4) — verified with force gauge at each selection button, card reader, contactless tap, and dispense flap.
  • Selection method usable without speech (machine does not require voice command to complete a purchase).
  • Cashless payment available so users without coin/bill handling capability can transact.
  • Refund flow accessible via app, SMS, or phone — not solely through a wall-mounted touchscreen out of reach range.

4. Tactile + visual interface audit — Sections 703, 707

Where the device interface is visual-only, additional tactile cues are required. Where a kiosk includes a touchscreen, Section 707 (and DOJ 2024 Title II web/kiosk rule for public entities) applies.

Tactile selection
Raised characters, recessed buttons, or tactile selection numbers where visual-only labels are used (§703.3 references).
Character height + contrast
Visual character height ≥ 5/8 in; contrast ratio ≥ 70% per §703.5.1 for high-traffic public placements.
Braille pricing labels
Provided at federal placements (Randolph-Sheppard) and where state code requires (e.g., several state procurement codes for state buildings).
Audio output
Touchscreen kiosks with audio output via standard 3.5mm headphone jack or wireless pairing; navigable without visual reliance (§707.5, WCAG 2.1 AA alignment).
Privacy
Audio mode shields visual display so a user wearing headphones can navigate privately (§707.5.2).

5. VPAT framework (Voluntary Product Accessibility Template)

Operator-side digital interfaces (card reader, mobile-wallet flow, kiosk OS, operator app for refunds) should each have a VPAT 2.5-Rev508 (or current revision) on file. Reviewer scopes the VPAT against the specific deployment, not the manufacturer's marketing.

  1. Scope

    VPAT covers the exact firmware/software version deployed at the site, not a generic product family. Version + release date recorded.

  2. Section 508 chapters

    Reviewer confirms Chapter 4 (Hardware), Chapter 5 (Software), Chapter 6 (Support Documentation), and Chapter 7 (Support Services) are each addressed with 'Supports / Partially Supports / Does Not Support / Not Applicable' and a remarks column.

  3. Critical conformance points

    302.1 Without Vision, 302.3 Without Use of Hands, 302.5 Without Hearing, 302.7 With Limited Manipulation, 402.2 Speech-Output Enabled, 407.6 Operable Parts — Discernible, 407.7 Operable Parts — Tactilely Discernible.

  4. Remediation timeline

    Any 'Does Not Support' or 'Partially Supports' line item carries a documented remediation plan + date.

7. Annual recertification checklist

  • Reach range re-measured at every operable part (§308)
  • Clear floor space re-measured (§305)
  • Operating force re-tested with calibrated force gauge (§309.4)
  • Photos taken at install date matched against current photos — no creep into compliance edge
  • VPAT version on file matches firmware / software currently deployed
  • Refund flow tested via app + SMS + phone — at least one method available without on-machine touchscreen reach
  • Operator signs annual ADA attestation; facility accessibility coordinator counter-signs
  • Documentation retained in procurement file for contract duration + 5 years
