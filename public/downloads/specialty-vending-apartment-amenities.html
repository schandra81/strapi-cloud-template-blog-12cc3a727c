2. Demographic match heuristics

Resident demographic predicts specialty-category fit. Use property data + NMHC benchmarks.

Family-heavy (3-bedroom share > 25%)
Ice cream, kid snacks, pet treats. Avoid CBD / wellness adult-only specialty unless physically segregated.
Young-professional / single-occupant (studio + 1-BR > 60%)
Wellness, electronics, fitness recovery, beauty. Premium price-point support.
Senior / 55+
Wellness (electrolyte, supplements), pharmacy-adjacent OTC, accessibility-spec equipment mandatory.
Student / co-living
Office supplies, electronics, fitness recovery, snack specialty (energy bars, late-night).
Luxury / lifestyle
Beauty, wellness, premium electronics, ice cream premium-tier. Brand-immersive cabinet finish.
Pet-friendly (pet-policy properties)
Pet treat + waste-bag dispenser at dog-wash + dog-park amenity.

4. Operator capability checklist per specialty

Not every multifamily operator is equipped for every specialty category. Use the checklist below to gate operator qualification.

  1. Ice cream / frozen

    Cold-chain SLA contract, cellular temperature monitoring, ENERGY STAR Tier 2 freezer fleet, FDA Food Code 3-501.16 attestation, 4-hr excursion response SLA, seasonal cadence.

  2. Pet treats + waste-bags

    Pet-property references, FDA AAFCO-compliant treats supplier, outdoor-rated cabinet capability if dog-park placement.

  3. Beauty + personal care

    FDA-registered cosmetic supplier, brand-licensing where applicable, FALCPA-style allergen labeling, secure cabinet experience.

  4. Wellness

    FDA-DSHEA-compliant supplement supplier; state-specific CBD/cannabis-adjacent compliance where applicable; mid-tier inventory insurance.

  5. Electronics

    $25-$200K floor-inventory insurance, hardened-cabinet experience, RFID inventory, lost-prevention controls, MSRP-discipline.

  6. Fitness recovery

    Protein + supplement compliance (FDA-DSHEA), refrigerated + ambient cabinet experience, near-fitness placement experience.

  7. Office / co-work supplies

    Mixed-format inventory, compact cabinet experience, B2B SKU supplier relationships (Amazon Business or comparable).

5. Compliance + regulatory footnotes

  • Food safety — FDA Food Code Chapter 3 for ice cream, fresh, beverage.
  • Cosmetics — FDA Color Additives + Cosmetic Labeling per FDCA Section 601-602.
  • Supplements — DSHEA labeling + structure-function claims compliance.
  • CBD / cannabis-adjacent — state-specific; Farm Bill THC threshold compliance; some states (FL, TX) require additional licensure.
  • Electronics — California Prop 65 chemical warning; FCC + UL certification labeling.
  • Pet treats — AAFCO labeling; jurisdiction-specific feed-control compliance.
  • ADA accessibility — Title III + Section 308 reach-range for all specialty cabinets at public-amenity placements.

7. Placement-evaluation worksheet

  • Property resident demographic confirmed against specialty match in Section 2.
  • Zone identified (lobby / lounge / fitness / pool / dog-wash / corridor) with foot-traffic estimate.
  • Power + signal verified (circuit, surge protection, cellular > -95 dBm or Wi-Fi).
  • Compliance footprint reviewed for the chosen specialty (food / cosmetic / supplement / electronics).
  • ADA reach-range pre-check (48 in upper / 15 in lower).
  • Operator capability checklist passed for the specialty.
  • Resident-communication plan drafted (portal announcement, lobby signage, member-app deep-link).
