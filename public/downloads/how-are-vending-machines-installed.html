28-Point Pre-Install Site Survey Checklist

Hand to facilities before the operator arrives — dimensional, electrical, accessibility, telemetry, and compliance verification

Hand this checklist to the facilities team before the operator's installer arrives on site. Every item references a measurable specification — NFPA 70 (NEC) for electrical, NFPA 13 for sprinkler clearance, OSHA 1910.37 for egress, ADA Section 308 + 309 for accessibility, FDA Food Code 3-501.16 for cold-chain, and PCI-DSS 4.0 for payment compliance. Capability-framed: operator should attest in writing to each item before install day.

1. Delivery + access route (points 1-4)

  • 1. Delivery route confirmed: receiving dock or front entry to placement; doorways ≥ 36 in clear; tight-corner radius noted.
  • 2. Elevator capacity ≥ 1,500 lb if upstairs; elevator car ≥ 80 in tall for an upright machine.
  • 3. Floor protection plan: dolly skid, blankets, or floor runners for path through finished spaces.
  • 4. Loading dock available + scheduled OR curbside drop with stair-climbing dolly arranged.

2. Placement envelope + clearances (points 5-9)

  • 5. Machine footprint + 4 in side / 6 in rear ventilation clearance fits at placement.
  • 6. Floor level within ¼ inch over 4 ft span; no expansion joint within footprint.
  • 7. Floor load: machine + loaded product (typical 800-1,400 lb) within posted capacity.
  • 8. Door swing arc clear in front (typical 36-42 in).
  • 9. Service-side access: front-load servicing preferred; if rear-access required, ≥ 24 in clearance behind unit.

3. Electrical (points 10-13) — NFPA 70 / NEC

  • 10. Dedicated 120V/15A (non-refrigerated) or 120V/20A (refrigerated/combo) circuit within 6 ft of placement.
  • 11. GFCI per NEC Article 422 where within 6 ft of sink or wash zone.
  • 12. Circuit not shared with high-draw equipment; cord drops straight to receptacle (no daisy-chain).
  • 13. Surge protection at the machine OR whole-building surge protection per NEC 285.

4. ADA + accessibility (points 14-17) — Section 308 + 309

  • 14. Highest operable part ≤ 48 in AFF (selection keypad, payment reader, dispense flap).
  • 15. Lowest operable part ≥ 15 in AFF (card reader, dispense bin opening).
  • 16. Operating force ≤ 5 lbf (309.4); no tight grasping, pinching, or twisting required.
  • 17. 30 in × 48 in clear floor space for parallel or forward approach (305.3).

5. Fire + egress (points 18-20) — NFPA 13 + OSHA 1910.37

  • 18. NFPA 13 sprinkler clearance: typical 18 in below deflector kept clear.
  • 19. Egress corridor width preserved (≥ 28 in for ≤ 50 occupants; ≥ 44 in for > 50).
  • 20. Smoke / fire detector coverage not blocked; manual pull station access maintained.

6. Telemetry + payment (points 21-23) — PCI-DSS 4.0

  • 21. Cellular signal verified at placement ≥ -100 dBm OR dedicated Ethernet drop available.
  • 22. Contactless EMV + Apple Pay / Google Pay supported; operator's PCI-DSS 4.0 AOC + SAQ-B-IP on file.
  • 23. Host facility coordinator granted read-only access to telemetry portal (Cantaloupe / Nayax / 365 / AWM).

7. Cold-chain (points 24-25) — FDA Food Code 3-501.16

  • 24. Refrigerated unit holds ≤ 40°F; internal-temp telemetry alert configured (immediate alert with 4-hr resolution SLA).
  • 25. Ambient operating range at placement within machine spec (typical 50-90°F); placement is not in unconditioned space without high-ambient-rated unit.

8. Compliance + documentation (points 26-28)

  • 26. FALCPA allergen labeling on each SKU; allergen summary signage at machine face; FDA menu labeling per 21 CFR 101.11 where operator has ≥ 20 placements.
  • 27. Operator COI on file ($2M GL, $1M umbrella, additional-insured endorsement, product liability ≥ $1M).
  • 28. State weights-and-measures registration where required; local health permit where applicable; ADA Section 308 written attestation on file.
This checklist is informational and not legal advice. Facilities should confirm placement complies with local code, fire marshal, and tenant constraints.
