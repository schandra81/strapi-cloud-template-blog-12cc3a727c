Vending Machine Financing Decision Framework

Cash vs equipment financing vs SBA vs lease — credit-tier APR matrix + total cost of capital calculator

This framework helps prospective vending operators decide how to finance their first or expansion fleet. References reflect typical 2024-2026 SBA program terms, Section 179 + bonus depreciation under current IRS guidance, and standard equipment-finance industry tiering. Tax outcomes depend on individual circumstances — consult a CPA. Capability-framed — every financing option should be verified with the actual lender's current terms before commitment.

3. Decision rubric — how to choose

If you have ≥ $20K liquid + plan to scale fast
Use cash for first 2-3 machines to build operating history; then pivot to equipment financing or SBA 7(a) for accelerated fleet build. Cash deployment preserves equipment financing capacity for expansion.
If you have $5-20K liquid + want to start
Equipment financing at A or A+ credit tier; 10% down; 48-month term. Reserve cash for working capital (initial inventory, fuel, payment processing, insurance).
If you need working capital + equipment
SBA 7(a) general loan covers equipment + working capital + initial inventory in a single facility; longer term lowers monthly burden in early operating period.
If you're acquiring an existing route + buying real estate / warehouse
SBA 504 for the real estate + SBA 7(a) for the route acquisition + working capital; structure with SBA-approved lender.
If you want equipment off balance sheet
Operating lease (true lease); end-of-term return option preserves flexibility; trade-off is no Section 179 benefit + you don't own at end of term.
If OEM offers a 0% / promotional rate
Vendor financing on specific models can beat equipment financing for term length; confirm there's no markup on equipment price baked into the promo.
If credit is B-tier and machine is < $7K
SBA Microloan covers up to $50K; lower credit threshold; longer underwriting cycle.
