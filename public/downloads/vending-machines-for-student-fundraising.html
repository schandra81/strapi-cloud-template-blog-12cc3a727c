AI Cooler Student Fundraising Pack

Commission framework, Smart Snacks checklist, placement matrix, and operator selection criteria for student-organization fundraising vending

This pack bundles four working documents.

2. Smart Snacks + HHS-alignment checklist

Smart Snacks rules apply to K-12; on university campuses, HHS Food Service Guidelines or campus dietitian standards apply. If your campus requires Smart-Snacks-aligned vending for student-organization fundraising, the checklist below applies.

  • Per snack: ≤ 200 cal, ≤ 230 mg sodium, ≤ 35% calories from total fat, ≤ 10% saturated fat, ≤ 35% sugar by weight
  • Fruit, vegetable, dairy, protein, whole grain — at least one as the first ingredient or 10%+ DV per serving
  • Beverages: water (any size), low-fat plain milk (≤ 12 oz), unflavored low-fat milk (≤ 8 oz), 100% juice (≤ 12 oz), flavored milk (≤ 8 oz, ≤ 100 cal)
  • Caffeinated beverages restricted to coffee/tea (no energy drinks) where Smart Snacks framework adopted
  • Sugar-sweetened beverages capped per Smart Snacks: ≤ 60 cal/12 oz (high school) or stricter
  • Allergen labeling per FALCPA on every SKU; cooler-face allergen summary
  • Dietitian audit semi-annual; remediation plan within 30 days for non-compliance
  • Healthy-share floor: ≥ 50% of cooler facings meeting Smart Snacks or HHS standards

4. Operator selection criteria — fundraising context

  • Higher-ed reference accounts ≥ 2 active student-organization placements at peer institutions
  • Smart-Snacks / HHS / FITPICK planogram capability attested and demonstrable
  • Campus card SDK + FERPA DPA on file
  • Commission accounting: per-cooler, per-organization tracked; quarterly statements to each org
  • Refund automation + dashboard accessible to student-org + auxiliary services
  • Insurance: $2M GL, $1M umbrella, food product liability $1M, additional-insured to university + student-org
  • Sustainability + STARS-aligned reporting capability for org-aligned planogram (sustainability club, etc.)
  • Brand-flexibility: cabinet wrap supports student-org branding + university brand standards co-design

5. Operating workflow — student-org governance

  1. Org sponsorship + brand approval

    Student-org advisor + auxiliary services confirm placement + brand standards; org submits cabinet-wrap design + planogram preferences.

  2. Cooler install + launch

    Operator installs cooler with org-branded wrap; soft-launch event with org members; cooler-face signage explains 'proceeds support [org]'.

  3. Restock + service

    Operator runs standard restock cadence + service SLA; org has no operational responsibility (no labor commitment).

  4. Quarterly commission statement

    Operator issues quarterly statement to org treasurer; commission payment within 30 days of quarter close; auxiliary services CCs statement for audit.

  5. Planogram refresh

    Semi-annual: org input on planogram aligned to org identity (e.g., athletics → recovery protein focus); dietitian + auxiliary services co-approve.

  6. Promotion calendar

    Org-aligned promotions (home-game day discount, awareness-week SKU spotlight); operator funds creative; org promotes via channels.

  7. Annual review

    Year-end performance review with org leadership + auxiliary services + operator; renewal decision + new placement consideration.

8. Risk + compliance considerations

  • Smart-Snacks / HHS-alignment audited semi-annually; failure triggers cure plan within 30 days
  • FERPA DPA covers campus card SDK if cooler accepts campus-card tender
  • Insurance: operator carries $2M GL + food product liability; student-org named as additional-insured
  • Tax: commission revenue to student-org may have unrelated business income tax (UBIT) implications for nonprofit affiliates; tax counsel review recommended
  • Contract: org-operator memorandum of agreement signed by org advisor + auxiliary services
  • Brand standards: org cabinet-wrap must respect university brand standards + trademark policies
  • Termination: org can request placement removal with 60-day notice; operator transitions cooler to standard cabinet wrap
This pack is informational. Student-org advisor + auxiliary services + dietitian + brand standards + tax counsel should jointly review program design before launch. Smart Snacks alignment + commission accounting + FERPA DPA are core compliance components.
