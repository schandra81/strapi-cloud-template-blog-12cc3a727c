Airport Vending Placement Playbook

Zones, telemetry-driven sizing, and concession coordination

Airport placement is the difference between a $40k/yr machine and a $250k/yr machine. The driver is zone classification + dwell-time + competitor concession adjacency — not square footage. This playbook codifies the zone framework, telemetry-driven sizing, and concession-coordination practices LetUsVending operator partners are equipped to apply, referenced against FAA AC 150/5360-13 (Airport Terminal Planning), ACI-NA terminal benchmarking, TSA 49 CFR 1542 zone definitions, FDA Food Code 2022 cold-chain, and FAA Part 139 (Certification of Airports).

2. Zone-by-zone sizing

  1. Pre-security landside

    1 combo or 1 snack + 1 drink per 800-1,500 daily enplanements + deplanements through the touchpoint. Most volume on bottled water, light snack, charger/headphone essentials.

  2. Post-security concourse

    1 multi-machine bank (snack + drink + cold-food + coffee) per 1,500-2,500 enplanements / day passing the bank. Add a healthy-share endcap (≥40% facings) — TSA + ACI-NA benchmarking shows airside travelers buy higher healthy-share than retail averages.

  3. Gate hold area

    1 drink + 1 snack machine per 3-5 gates at hubs, 1 per 1-2 gates at peak overnight or red-eye gates. Telemetry must be cellular (Wi-Fi gates over-subscribed).

  4. Airline club / lounge

    1 sub-machine bank — typically operator-supplied refrigerated case + coffee + snack rack as part of the lounge's amenity program. Lounge brand standards govern visuals.

  5. Crew + employee lounge

    1 multi-machine bank with hot-food / microwaveable entrée coverage + 24/7 telemetry-driven restock. Captive workforce — peak shift-change windows drive volume.

  6. Parking / shuttle / rental car

    1 combo machine per shuttle stop or 1 vending bank per garage level. Bottled water dominates; sizing follows passenger throughput, not square footage.

4. Concession coordination

Adjacent retail (cobranded vs. competitor)
Avoid placing high-velocity SKUs adjacent to a Hudson / Paradies / Brookstone storefront unless concession agreement permits — most master agreements include category-exclusivity language.
Food and beverage exclusion zones
Some agreements exclude vending within X feet of a Starbucks, restaurant, or grab-and-go. Identify exclusion in the RFP review.
Concession program coordination
Concession office assigns a coordinator at signing — quarterly walk-down reviews placement, signage, and program alignment.
ACDBE utilization
DOT 49 CFR Part 23 utilization tracked per concession; placement decisions filed in the utilization plan.
Sponsorship + signage
Brand-system + signage must match the airport's environmental graphics manual — coordinate with airport experience / customer-experience office.

5. Operational SLA at airport placement

  • Tier 1 SLA <12 hr resolution at hubs (large airports often demand <4 hr at airside)
  • Telemetry-driven restock cadence: gate-area can hit 2-3 visits/day at peak
  • Refund flow per airport customer-experience program — often money-back-guaranteed
  • SIDA-badged service personnel only on airside (49 CFR 1542)
  • Vehicle access permit (AVOP) if route requires apron driving
  • Fresh-food cold-chain ≤41°F monitored continuously (FDA Food Code 2022 §3-501.16)
  • Sustainability + waste reporting per airport's sustainability program (ACI-ACA accreditation often referenced)
