24/7 Hotel Vending + Amenity Playbook

Placement portfolio, RFP framework, SLA matrix, planogram by placement type, guest-communication kit

Hotel vending is a guest-amenity decision, not a snack-merchandising decision. This playbook codifies how to design a 24/7 amenity program against the property's brand standard, AAA Diamond / Forbes inspection criteria, and ADA Title III. Each section maps to a measurable SLA or a brand-standard reference your operator should be equipped to attest to.

2. RFP framework — operator selection

The RFP must let you compare operators on brand-standard alignment, not just commission. Anchor to these eight capability areas.

  1. Brand-standard attestation

    Operator can attest in writing to the property's brand standard (Marriott BSA, Hilton OS&E, IHG Brand Hallmarks, Hyatt Brand Standards, Choice Brand Standards). Photo proof of equipment + signage at comparable properties.

  2. Cashless + folio integration

    Operator supports EMV chip, contactless tap, mobile wallet, and PMS folio post (where the brand supports it — Opera, OnQ, Marsha, FOSSE). Demo environment available for testing.

  3. SLA matrix

    Tier 1 ≤ 4-hour on-site; Tier 2 24-48 hour acknowledgement; Tier 3 5-day. Penalty structure for SLA breach written into the agreement.

  4. Telemetry + reporting

    Cellular sub-15-minute heartbeat. Monthly uptime and per-SKU velocity report. Property-level dashboard access.

  5. ADA + accessibility

    ADA Title III + Section 308/309/703 attestation; VPAT for digital interfaces.

  6. Insurance + indemnity

    $2M general liability + $5M umbrella minimum; property listed as additional insured; mutual indemnity with carve-outs reviewed by property GM.

  7. Energy compliance

    ENERGY STAR equipment + R-290 refrigerant attestation. Scope 2 reporting where the property tracks emissions for brand sustainability program.

  8. Guest-communication kit

    Signage, in-room TV channel slide, in-room compendium copy, app-deeplink to vending locations.

4. Planogram by placement type

Pantry market
Grab-and-go meals (salads, wraps, parfaits) refreshed daily; beverages weighted 50/50 still vs sparkling; sundries (toothpaste, deodorant, charger cables) 12-15 SKUs; alcohol category if state law + brand standard permit.
Floor vending alcove
Beverage tower: water 30%, soda 25%, energy/sports 20%, juice/tea 15%, premium 10%. Snack tower: chips 25%, candy 25%, healthy 20%, salty 15%, sweet 15%. Sundry: contraceptives, OTC pain reliever, toothpaste, deodorant, phone charger.
Pool / fitness
Water dominant (50%); protein bars + recovery beverages; no carbonated sodas at brand-standard wellness properties.
Meeting-floor fallback
Bottled beverages + sealed snack-pack format; aligned with banquet F&B standards.

5. Guest-communication kit

Guests cannot use what they cannot find. The communication kit is a brand-standard amenity in itself.

  1. Wayfinding signage

    Elevator-lobby map showing alcove locations. Lobby pantry signage at check-in line of sight. ADA Section 703 visual character + raised character + braille.

  2. In-room compendium

    Vending + pantry section in the in-room compendium (digital or print) listing locations, hours, payment methods, and the property phone extension for refunds.

  3. In-room TV slide

    Brand-template TV welcome channel slide pointing to amenity locations.

  4. App + WiFi splash

    Brand app deep-link or property captive-portal mention of the amenity. Refund flow accessible from the app.

  5. Front-desk script

    Trained answer to 'where can I get water at 2 AM?' that points the guest to the nearest alcove or pantry.

6. Brand-standard + regulatory references

AAA Diamond Inspection Guidelines
Amenity availability + cleanliness scoring affects Diamond tier (One-Five). 24-hour beverage availability is a Four-Diamond expectation at most flag properties.
Forbes Travel Guide standards
Five-Star properties typically substitute curated mini-bar + 24-hour in-room dining; Four-Star tier permits well-designed 24/7 amenity programs.
ADA Title III + Section 308/309/703
Reach range, operating force, contrast/character requirements for all guest-accessible machines.
FDA Food Code (state-adopted)
TCS foods at 41°F or below refrigerated, 135°F or above hot. Temperature logs retained 12 months.
Brand alcohol policy + state ABC
Alcohol-vending placements (where permitted) operate under state Alcoholic Beverage Control licensure; age-verification at point of sale; brand-specific approval letter.

7. Quarterly business review

  • Uptime report by machine and floor
  • Per-SKU velocity vs planogram-target ratio
  • Refund volume + reason codes + folio-charge dispute summary
  • Brand-inspection readiness checklist (AAA / Forbes / brand BSA)
  • ADA recertification status
  • Temperature-log audit summary (refrigerated)
  • Guest-feedback review (front-desk log + brand survey verbatims)
  • Action items + owner + due date
