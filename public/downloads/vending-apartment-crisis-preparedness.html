Apartment Vending Crisis-Preparedness Pack

Staples planogram, backup power spec, continuity SOP template

Vending is the only resident-accessible food source when a property loses access to delivery, retail, or staff coverage. This pack defines the crisis-mode planogram, backup-power spec, and SOP template to keep vending operational through power events, weather, and supply disruption.

1. Crisis-mode taxonomy

Different crisis types require different responses. Build the SOP against the four classes below.

2. Staples planogram (crisis-mode override)

When property crisis-mode is activated, operator switches affected cabinets to staples planogram. SKU breadth narrows to longest-shelf-life essentials.

Hydration (35-40% of capacity)
Bottled water 16oz + 24oz, electrolyte drinks (Liquid IV, Gatorade), shelf-stable juice.
Calorie-dense shelf-stable (25-30%)
Nut bars, jerky, trail mix, peanut butter packs, pretzels, crackers, granola bars.
Comfort + morale (15-20%)
Chocolate, packaged baked goods, coffee RTD, hot-drink mix packets.
Kid + family (10-15%)
Kid juice pouch, kid snack pack, fruit pouch (room-temp), animal crackers.
PPE / essentials (5-10%)
Disposable mask, hand sanitizer pack, OTC pain reliever, AA/AAA batteries, phone-charge cable.
Allergen-aware staples
Clearly-labeled allergen-free options in each category; FALCPA-compliant labels.

3. Backup power specification

Not every cabinet needs UPS. Prioritize cold-chain + payment + telemetry on UPS; ambient-snack cabinets can ride out short outages.

Cold-chain cabinets
Online UPS 1500-3000VA per cabinet (APC Smart-UPS, Eaton 9PX); 30-60 min compressor runtime; auto-transfer to property backup-generator circuit if equipped.
Payment + telemetry only
Battery-backed cellular telemetry (built-in on Cantaloupe Seed Pro, Nayax VPOS Touch) survives 4-12 hr outage.
Ambient snack cabinets
No UPS needed; equipment auto-restarts at power return.
Generator-circuit priority
Property generator (if installed) should include lobby + amenity-zone vending circuits for cold-chain + lobby essentials.
Surge protection
Whole-circuit surge-protective device (Type 2 SPD per NEC Article 285) on every vending circuit, regardless of UPS.

4. Pre-event restock SOP

When a forecast event (hurricane, named storm, blizzard, public-health declaration) lands within 72 hours, operator triggers pre-event restock.

  1. T-72 hr — staples activation

    Operator switches affected cabinets to crisis planogram; route truck dispatched to property; SKU swap initiated.

  2. T-48 hr — full restock

    All affected cabinets restocked to 100% capacity with staples planogram; surge SKU pool (water, batteries, kid snack) loaded.

  3. T-24 hr — confirmation

    Telemetry confirms full stock + cold-chain stable; property POC notified; resident-portal pre-event announcement.

  4. T-0 (event)

    Cabinets operate on UPS / generator if outage; cashless via cellular telemetry remains operational.

  5. T+24 hr (post-event)

    Telemetry health check; first post-event restock dispatched within 24-72 hr depending on road access.

  6. T+72 hr (recovery)

    Restore standard planogram; resident-portal recovery announcement; commission floor waived for crisis week.

5. Continuity SOP template

Codify in the operator contract addendum. Each clause should be answered Yes/No + responsible-party.

  • Crisis-mode trigger — National Weather Service watch/warning, property-issued emergency declaration, or local public-health order.
  • Operator's crisis dispatch contact (name, mobile, 24/7 line).
  • Pre-event restock SLA — T-72 staples activation; T-48 full restock; T-24 confirmation.
  • Post-event restock SLA — within 72 hr of road access restoration.
  • UPS / generator circuit list — which cabinets are protected.
  • Telemetry continuity — cellular fallback path; battery-backed heartbeat duration.
  • Resident communication channel — property POC pushes notices to resident portal + lobby signage.
  • Refund-flow continuity — member-app refund processing not dependent on property network.
  • Commission floor waiver clause for crisis weeks (uptime < 90% pro-rata).

6. Cold-chain crisis protocol

Excursion at outage
If cold-chain cabinet not on UPS / generator and internal temp > 41°F (refrigerated) or > 10°F (frozen) for >2 hours: SKU pull on power return; full credit; no resale.
Excursion-event log
Telemetry timestamp + duration + temp profile; operator + property POC both alerted.
Recovery restock
Operator dispatches refrigerated truck; fresh SKUs restocked within 72 hr of road access; commission credit issued.
Member transparency
Resident-portal notice that fresh SKUs were pulled and replaced; member-app shows updated stock; refunds processed for in-flight cashless transactions on pulled SKUs.

7. Crisis-preparedness annual drill

  • Annual tabletop drill with property POC + operator on-call lead.
  • Verify backup-power circuits at annual fire-protection / life-safety inspection.
  • Refresh UPS batteries every 3-5 years per manufacturer spec.
  • Refresh staples planogram SKU list annually (shelf-life rotation).
  • Resident-portal emergency vending FAQ refreshed annually.
  • Post-drill action items + owner + due-date logged.
  • Post-real-event after-action review within 30 days; SOP updated as needed.

8. Resident communication during crisis

Pre-event (T-72 to T-0)
Resident-portal notice with cabinet locations, staples-mode notice, payment-method options, and refund process. Lobby signage + member-app banner.
During event
If cabinets remain operational, member-app shows live stock; if cabinets offline, portal notice gives ETA for restoration.
Post-event recovery
Resident-portal recovery notice; commission floor waiver acknowledgment; resident-feedback survey within 14 days.
Special-population accommodations
Property staff coordinates delivery from cabinet to mobility-restricted residents during crisis-mode; this is property-side, not operator-side.
