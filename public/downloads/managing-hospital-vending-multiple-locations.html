Multi-Location Hospital Vending Pack

RFP template, contract structure, SLA framework, audit checklist

Health systems with 3+ hospitals plus outpatient + ambulatory locations need a different vending strategy than a single hospital. This pack covers multi-location RFP design, contract structures, SLA framework, and the audit checklist to keep performance consistent across sites.

1. Multi-location strategy — three structures

Choose the structure that fits the system's scale, geographic footprint, and centralization preference.

StructureMechanicsBest fit
Single national operatorOne operator covers all sites under one master MSALarge IDN with footprint in multiple states; national operators like Compass Group / Canteen, Aramark Refreshments, Five Star Food Service
Regional operator clusters2-4 regional operators each cover a region under aligned MSAsMid IDN spanning 2-3 regions; balances local service with central governance
Per-site operator + central governanceEach site selects its own operator under a system-wide templateSmaller IDN or recently-merged systems with legacy local relationships

2. Master Services Agreement (MSA) framework

The MSA is the legal + commercial anchor. Use a single MSA across the system with site-specific Statements of Work (SOWs).

  1. MSA scope

    Definitions, term, mutual representations, insurance, indemnification, limit of liability, IP, confidentiality, termination — items common to every site.

  2. SOW per site

    Equipment count + models, planogram, commission schedule, SLA tier per zone (ED, lobby, staff break, outpatient), zone-specific stakeholders.

  3. Master commission schedule

    Standard categories + rates; per-site overrides documented in SOW only with VP-level approval.

  4. Central governance

    Quarterly QBR at system level + per-site QBR; named system account exec at operator.

  5. Single billing point

    Operator invoices system materials management; system disburses to site cost centers.

3. RFP template — multi-location-specific items

Beyond the standard hospital RFP, multi-location adds operational + governance items that single-site RFPs miss.

  • Geographic coverage proof: operator demonstrates direct or partner coverage in every site's metro market with named technician(s).
  • Cross-site reporting: operator provides system-rollup dashboards (system → region → site → machine).
  • Standardized planogram framework with per-zone variants (ED, lobby, staff, outpatient) deployable system-wide.
  • Standardized SLA tier definitions applicable system-wide with site-specific resolution adjustments.
  • EOC documentation pack delivered consistently per Joint Commission EOC.02.06.05 across all sites.
  • Cross-site refund + member-experience policy (member at site A redeems at site B).
  • Vendor management: operator's subcontractor + partner policy if regional partners are required.
  • Commission audit at system level: monthly with quarterly true-up.
  • Telemetry rollup: site-level + system-level dashboards; data access for materials management.
  • References from at least 2 prior multi-site IDN deployments at comparable scale.

5. Audit + governance checklist

Without governance, multi-site contracts drift within 12 months. Build quarterly + annual audit cadence.

  • Monthly: telemetry + commission report aggregation; flag sites > 1 standard deviation from system average on uptime, stockout, or commission %.
  • Quarterly: System QBR with operator senior leadership; per-site QBR with materials mgmt + facility ops.
  • Quarterly: Random site visits (2-4 sites per quarter); ADA + IP + planogram audit.
  • Semi-annual: Healthy-beverage / healthy-food framework compliance audit.
  • Annual: Full EOC documentation review per Joint Commission EOC.02.06.01 + EOC.02.06.05.
  • Annual: Insurance certificate refresh; verify aggregate limits remain adequate.
  • Annual: Operator financial health review (D&B, recent financial statements where contract allows).
  • Annual: Contract performance scorecard published to system leadership.

6. Commission + finance structure

Multi-site commission structures benefit from blended rates that smooth per-site variance and allow the operator to invest in lower-volume sites.

Blended commission
Single % across all sites; lifts low-volume sites that would otherwise be uneconomic for the operator.
Tiered by site role
Different rates for ED, lobby, staff, outpatient; reflects revenue + cost-to-serve differences.
Category-tiered
Lower commission on premium / recovery SKUs (higher COGS); higher on ambient snacks.
Foundation share
% of commission routed to system or local foundation per site (IRS Form 990 Schedule H community benefit alignment).
Cashless interchange handling
Pass-through specified at MSA; standard 2.9-4.0%.
Sales tax
Operator manages multi-state sales tax collection + remittance per state DOR rules.

7. Planogram + brand consistency

Members visiting multiple system sites should see consistent quality + framework even if individual SKUs vary by region.

  • System-wide planogram template: per-zone category share + healthy-tier minimums.
  • Top-50 system SKUs: required at every site for visitor familiarity.
  • Regional SKU tail: operator may swap up to 25% of facings for regional preferences without system approval.
  • Quarterly planogram refresh aligned with system marketing calendar.
  • Branded signage + tier-color labels per the chosen healthy framework — identical across all sites.

8. Operator capabilities required

  • Demonstrated multi-site IDN deployment at ≥ 5 hospitals; references provided.
  • National or multi-region service coverage with named technician(s) per market.
  • System + site rollup telemetry + commission dashboards.
  • Standardized EOC documentation pack delivered consistently across all sites.
  • Named system account exec + per-region account managers.
  • PCI-DSS attestation maintained; cashless handled via operator merchant ID, not system.
  • Insurance with aggregate limits scaling to system footprint (typically GL $5M/$10M aggregate for system-wide deployments).
  • Financial stability — D&B credit score acceptable to system finance; 3 years of audited or reviewed statements.
