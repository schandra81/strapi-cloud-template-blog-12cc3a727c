Apartment AI Cooler Evaluation Pack

Property-fit checklist, operator shortlist criteria, planogram template, and resident-amenity revenue model

This pack bundles four working documents a multifamily property manager needs to evaluate, place, and operate an AI cooler in a residential building. Use it during RFP, install, and quarterly business reviews. Each section is capability-framed — confirm with operator in writing before contract execution.

1. Property-fit checklist

Use this checklist on a walk-through before signing a placement letter. Property should clear all items or note the remediation plan.

  • Unit count: building or community has ≥ 150 doors served by a single amenity area, OR multiple buildings clustered within a single management zone totaling ≥ 250 doors.
  • Resident density: foot traffic through amenity area ≥ 40 unique residents/day measured by access-control log or amenity calendar.
  • Placement: dry, climate-controlled (60-80°F ambient), within 10 ft of dedicated 120V/20A circuit, within 25 ft of building cellular signal ≥ -100 dBm or hardwired Ethernet drop.
  • ADA Section 308: 30x48 in clear floor space, operable parts 15-48 in above finished floor, ≤ 5 lbf operating force.
  • Security: 24/7 lobby/amenity camera coverage, fob/keycard access control, after-hours lighting ≥ 10 fc at the cooler face.
  • Egress + fire code: cooler does not narrow corridor below 44 in clear width; not in a means-of-egress path.
  • WiFi or cellular: cooler operator can attest to ≥ -95 dBm cellular signal or dedicated VLAN/SSID with ≥ 5 Mbps up/down.

2. Operator shortlist criteria

Score each candidate operator on the criteria below. Require operator to attest in writing and provide references at three comparable multifamily placements.

Multifamily reference accounts
Operator should provide 3+ active multifamily placements ≥ 12 months in service; reference call with on-site property manager required.
Telemetry + uptime SLA
Operator should attest to 98% uptime, cellular or VLAN telemetry, ≤ 30 min offline alert threshold, ≤ 24 hr Tier-1 service response.
Payment + identity stack
Apple Pay, Google Pay, contactless EMV, optional app/account. PCI-DSS 4.0 attestation, no PAN storage at cooler, AOC and SAQ-B-IP on file.
Computer-vision platform
AiFi, Trigo, Standard AI, Grabango, or comparable; vendor-neutral. Operator should attest to vision-system accuracy ≥ 99% by SKU.
Restock cadence + margin share
Standard 2x/week restock with telemetry-triggered top-offs; resident-amenity commission tier (typically 5-12% of gross revenue depending on volume + planogram constraints).
Insurance + liability
$2M general liability, $1M umbrella, additional-insured endorsement to property owner + management company; food product liability ≥ $1M.

5. Install + go-live SOP

  1. T-30: site survey + LOI

    Property manager + operator walk site, confirm placement, take measurements, sign letter of intent specifying placement location, electrical work owner, signage rules.

  2. T-21: electrical + connectivity prep

    Property facilities team confirms dedicated 120V/20A circuit, GFCI, cellular signal test or runs network drop. Operator confirms cooler dimensions vs corridor + door clearances.

  3. T-14: planogram + signage approval

    Operator submits planogram + cooler-face signage for property branding review. Property confirms FALCPA allergen labeling visibility and pricing display.

  4. T-7: install + commissioning

    Operator delivers, installs, levels, connects, performs vision-system calibration (typical 90-min calibration walk-through with 200+ training transactions per SKU).

  5. T-0: soft launch

    Free-first-purchase code for residents, on-site demo, lobby signage, resident newsletter announcement.

  6. T+14: planogram review

    Operator pulls 2-week velocity report, drops bottom-5 SKUs, adds 5 substitutes based on actual demand.

6. Quarterly business review template

  • Uptime by month over the quarter (per machine)
  • Transactions per week trend + basket size trend
  • Top-20 and bottom-20 SKUs by velocity
  • Stockout incidents + resolution time vs SLA
  • Refund rate (target < 2% of transactions)
  • Vision-system accuracy report (mis-charge rate)
  • Planogram refresh proposal for next quarter
