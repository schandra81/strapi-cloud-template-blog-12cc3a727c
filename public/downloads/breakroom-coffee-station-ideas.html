Breakroom Coffee Station Playbook

Equipment tier, accompaniments, water source, layout, cleaning discipline, and subsidy structure

A breakroom coffee station is a 6-decision system: equipment, accompaniments, water source, layout, cleaning, and subsidy. This playbook codifies each decision against office size and use pattern. It maps to NSF 4 / NSF 169 sanitation standards, FDA food-contact equipment standards (21 CFR 175-178), ADA Section 309.4 for operating force, and standard breakroom electrical + plumbing requirements.

2. Accompaniments — what to stock

Milk + alternatives
Whole + 2% (refrigerated quart or half-gallon) at every station. Oat + almond at offices over 30. Soy where requested. Cream + half-and-half at offices serving older / traditional preferences.
Sweeteners
Sugar (white + raw), Splenda, Stevia, Equal. Avoid offering only artificial sweeteners — generation-mix preferences vary.
Flavorings
Vanilla + hazelnut syrup pump bottles at offices over 60 (where bean-to-cup machine doesn't auto-flavor). Cocoa for hot chocolate option in winter.
Cups + lids
Hot cup + lid + sleeve in two sizes (10 + 16 oz) at offices over 30. Compostable / recyclable where placement supports the waste stream.
Stir sticks
Wood preferred for sustainability; plastic alternative for environments where wood splinters matter.
Cleaning + supplies
Counter spray, microfiber cloth, paper towel. Operator-supplied at managed-station tiers.

3. Water source

  1. Plumbed-in (filtered)

    Best for bean-to-cup + drip + pod machines used daily by 20+ employees. Operator installs 3M / Everpure / BWT filter sized to machine + water hardness. Filter replaced per spec (6-12 months). Eliminates jug-handling labor + plastic waste.

  2. Bottled water cooler (5-gallon)

    Where plumbing isn't feasible. Operator-managed swap cadence (typically 4-8 jugs per week per cooler at 60-employee office). Document jug source water quality + delivery cadence.

  3. Bottleless water cooler (filtered)

    Plumbed cooler with multi-stage filtration. Replaces jug handling. Hot + cold + sparkling options on premium models. Annual sanitation service.

  4. Water-quality testing

    Hardness + chlorine + pH tested at install + annually. Hard-water sites require additional descaling cadence or softener install.

4. Layout + ergonomics

  • Counter depth ≥ 24 inches for bean-to-cup; ≥ 18 inches for pod-only. Machine + cup-staging + spillover zone.
  • Electrical — dedicated 20A circuit for commercial bean-to-cup; standard 15A for pod + office-prosumer. Plumbed machines require water + drain access.
  • ADA reach range — Section 308.2.1: cup-dispense, machine controls, sweetener / milk station within 15-48 inches above finished floor.
  • Operating force — Section 309.4: machine buttons, pump bottles, milk dispensers operable with one hand and ≤ 5 lbf.
  • Trash + recycling within 6 feet of cup-disposal point; cup-rinse sink within 12 feet at managed-cup stations.
  • Drip-tray + drain — bean-to-cup drip tray accessible for daily emptying; plumbed-in drain at commercial tier.
  • Signage — drink-mode quick-reference card at machine; allergen / dietary placard visible.

5. Cleaning discipline

Daily
Empty + rinse drip tray. Wipe counter, machine exterior, sweetener / cream caddy. Refill cups + sweeteners. Empty trash. Quick visual quality check.
Weekly
Deep clean machine exterior. Clean milk frother / steam wand. Descale check (machine-prompted or scheduled). Restock paper goods. Wipe inside cabinet doors.
Monthly
Descale per machine schedule (or per machine prompt). Replace milk supply tubing where machine spec calls. Audit pump-bottle freshness; replace expired syrup.
Quarterly
Operator field-rep deep clean — disassemble + sanitize milk system, descale, inspect seals, calibrate dispense volumes. NSF 4 / NSF 169 alignment.
Annual
Water filter replacement (or per spec); preventive maintenance per OEM schedule.

6. Subsidy + cost structure

  • Most common: host-subsidized, free to employees. Per-employee monthly cost 8-25 USD at semi-commercial tier; 15-40 USD at commercial tier.
  • Cost-recovery (drinks priced 1.00-2.00 USD) where visitor / contractor traffic shares the station.
  • Tier-pricing (free for employees; charged to non-employees via badge / app rail) at offices with substantial visitor traffic.
  • Bundle option — combine coffee with snack + cold-beverage program for single-invoice management at 50+ employee offices.
  • Annual escalator capped at CPI or 5%, whichever lower.

8. Pre-signing red flags

  • Equipment tier mismatched to peak-hour drink count — under-spec drives morning queues + complaints.
  • Bottled water cooler proposed where plumbing is feasible — higher recurring labor + plastic waste.
  • No documented cleaning compliance log — milk-system off-taste within weeks.
  • Water-filtration program absent or 'as needed'.
  • SLA proposed without per-tier acknowledgment + resolution targets.
  • Subsidy structure unspecified — per-employee cost surprises at first invoice.
This playbook is informational. Equipment selection benefits from a 90-day pilot at the proposed tier matched to actual office drink-mix.
