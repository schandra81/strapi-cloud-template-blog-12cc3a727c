College + University Vending Cost Pack

Contract structures, commission negotiation, AI cooler vs combo evaluation, campus card SDK framework, operator selection checklist

Vending program economics on a college campus depend on contract structure (commission vs revenue-share vs full-line), placement mix (traditional combo vs AI cooler), and integration depth (campus card SDK, payroll deduction for staff). This pack bundles the five working documents a procurement + auxiliary services team needs to negotiate and operate a financially sound vending program.

2. Commission negotiation framework

Commission tiers are negotiable. Operators expect higher commissions at higher volumes and at premium placements. Use the framework below to anchor your ask.

Volume tier 1 — < $25K gross/year per placement
Commission 5-10% of gross. Typical for residence-hall floors with lower traffic.
Volume tier 2 — $25K-$75K gross/year per placement
Commission 10-18% of gross. Mid-density residence + academic-building placements.
Volume tier 3 — $75K-$150K gross/year per placement
Commission 15-22% of gross. High-traffic union + library + athletics placements.
Volume tier 4 — > $150K gross/year per placement
Commission 18-28% of gross. Hub-anchor placements at union, recreation center, or 24/7 library.
Minimum guarantee
Operator commits to a per-placement floor (e.g., $1,500/year); shields campus from underperforming placements during ramp.
Cabinet + signage capital
If operator funds custom-wrap + brand-aligned cabinet, expect commission tier to drop 2-3%. If campus funds, commission tier improves.

4. Campus card SDK + payment-integration framework

Most universities require operator support for the campus card (one-card) tender. Confirm each item below in the operator's response.

Campus card vendor support
Operator supports Atrium, Blackboard Transact, CBORD, TouchNet OneCard, Heartland Campus Solutions, or comparable.
Tender types
Dining dollars, declining balance, flex dollars, meal exchange (where applicable). Operator should attest to each tender's transaction-mapping.
Integration method
SDK or direct API; certified by the campus card vendor; tested in dev environment before production.
Tap + dip flow
Card-side ≤ 3 seconds end-to-end; declines surface a clear reason code; no PII leakage to operator beyond signed-DPA scope.
Settlement + reconciliation
Daily settlement file from operator to campus card vendor; reconciliation to auxiliary-services finance within 7 days.
FERPA DPA
Operator-signed FERPA DPA covering student-ID data traversal; SOC 2 Type II in good standing.
Cashless + mobile
Apple Pay, Google Pay, contactless EMV at cooler face alongside campus card tap-target.

5. Operator selection checklist

  • Higher-ed reference accounts ≥ 3 active placements at peer institutions ≥ 12 months in service
  • Campus card SDK integration certified and demonstrated in pilot environment
  • FERPA DPA + GLBA acknowledgment + SOC 2 Type II on file
  • PCI-DSS 4.0 AOC + SAQ-B-IP on file
  • Insurance: $2M GL, $1M umbrella, cyber-liability $5M, food product liability $1M, additional-insured to university
  • Refund automation: QR + SMS refund flow ≤ 24 hr credit-back
  • Reporting: per-placement, per-SKU, per-shift velocity dashboards accessible to auxiliary services
  • Sustainability + STARS-aligned reporting capability
  • Smart-Snacks / HHS / FITPICK planogram capability attested
  • Insurance + workforce: route-staff living-wage attestation if campus policy requires

6. Total-cost-of-ownership model — typical 30-placement program

Model assumes mid-density campus, 30 placements split 20 combo + 10 AI cooler, mixed traffic.

Operator capital (typical)
Operator funds equipment; campus capital = $0 for hardware. Optional: campus funds cabinet wrap + signage refresh, $500-$2,500/placement.
Campus operating cost
Auxiliary services PM time ~ 0.25 FTE for vendor management; finance reconciliation ~ 0.10 FTE.
Campus revenue (gross commission)
Combo placements: 20 x $30K/yr x 12% commission = $72K/yr. AI cooler placements: 10 x $90K/yr x 15% commission = $135K/yr. Total ~ $207K/yr.
Campus revenue (net)
Less PM + finance staffing allocation ~ $40K-$60K (campus salary + benefits); net $145K-$165K/yr.
Subsidy / equity programs
If campus underwrites SKU subsidies (recovery protein, healthy-share floor), expect $5K-$20K/yr offset to gross commission.
Sustainability capex (optional)
If campus brand requires custom wraps + ENERGY STAR refresh on capital cycle: $50K-$100K every 5-7 years.

7. Contract clauses to require

  • Commission tier table specifying per-volume tier and quarterly true-up
  • Minimum guarantee per placement
  • Termination for cause: uptime SLA breach, security incident, brand-standard breach
  • Termination for convenience: 90 days written notice from campus, 180 days from operator
  • Uptime SLA: 98%+ per machine per month; service credit for breach
  • Refund SLA: ≤ 24 hr credit-back to original tender
  • Sustainability + STARS reporting: quarterly report obligation
  • Brand-standard compliance: cabinet wrap + signage approval cycle
  • Insurance + indemnity language
  • Data + privacy: FERPA DPA + GLBA exhibit + PCI AOC schedule

8. Annual review framework

  • Q1: prior-year revenue + commission true-up; planogram annual refresh
  • Q2: mid-year operations review; refund + uptime audit
  • Q3: contract-anniversary commission-tier review + scope-change negotiation
  • Q4: STARS / sustainability annual report from operator
  • Annually: SOC 2 + PCI + FERPA DPA refresh
