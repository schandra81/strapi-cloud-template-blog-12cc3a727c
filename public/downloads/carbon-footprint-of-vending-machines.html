Vending Machine Carbon Footprint Analysis Pack

Electricity + refrigerant + vehicle + packaging emissions framework with a reduction stack

This pack is a working carbon-accounting framework for a single vending machine, a fleet, or a corporate program. It separates Scope 1 (refrigerant + service vehicle), Scope 2 (machine electricity), and Scope 3 (packaging + product upstream), gives reasonable emission factors, and lays out a stacked reduction roadmap that targets 40-70% reduction vs. a 2018-era baseline.

1. Boundary + scope

Use the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard for the boundary. Most vending carbon analyses cover the operator's Scope 1 + 2 + a partial Scope 3 (packaging, freight). The host's Category 1 (purchased goods + services) covers the vending operator's full footprint.

Scope 1 (operator)
Refrigerant leakage from refrigerated machines + delivery vehicle tailpipe emissions.
Scope 2 (host)
Grid electricity drawn by machines at the host site. Reported under the host's Scope 2 unless operator pays utility directly.
Scope 3 (both)
Packaging (cans, bottles, films, cartons), product upstream emissions, end-of-life of containers, employee commute to operate (negligible).
Functional unit
kg CO2e per machine per year and per vend (gram CO2e/vend). Per-vend lets you compare machines of different size + utilization.

3. Refrigerant emissions (Scope 1)

Refrigerant leakage is small in mass but high in GWP. Legacy HFC R-404A has GWP 3,922; modern hydrocarbon R-290 has GWP 3. Switching a fleet from R-404A to R-290 cuts refrigerant-leak emissions ~99.9%.

4. Vehicle + service emissions (Scope 1 operator)

Telemetry-driven restock cuts route miles 30-50% by skipping low-velocity stops. Electrification of the route van drives Scope 1 to ~zero (allocated against host's Scope 3 grid factor).

Diesel route van baseline
12-16 mpg, 15,000-25,000 mi/yr, 10.21 kg CO2e/gallon → 9,600-21,300 kg CO2e/yr per van. Divide across ~40-80 machines served per van = 120-530 kg CO2e/machine/yr.
Telemetry-optimized routing
Cantaloupe Seed Pro, Vagabond, Nayax dynamic routing cut visits by 30-50%. Direct kg CO2e/machine cut: 35-260 kg/yr.
Electric step-van
Ford E-Transit, Workhorse, Lightning eMotors — 0.6-1.0 kWh/mi. At 0.371 kg/kWh = 0.22-0.37 kg CO2e/mi. 15,000 mi × 0.37 = 5,550 kg CO2e/yr per van. Cut vs. diesel: 50-75%.

6. Reduction stack (highest-impact first)

Apply in order. Each layer typically delivers measurable reduction without dependency on the next.

  1. ENERGY STAR replacement at end-of-life

    Replace any pre-2014 refrigerated machine with ENERGY STAR 3.0 / 4.0 at end-of-life. Cut: 40-70% of machine electricity, 60-80% reduction in refrigerant CO2e if paired with R-290.

  2. Low-GWP refrigerant retrofit or replacement

    Specify R-290 or R-600a on every refrigerated machine bid. Cut: 95-99% of refrigerant emissions per machine.

  3. Telemetry-driven dynamic restock

    Cantaloupe / Nayax / Vagabond + route optimizer. Cut: 30-50% of service-vehicle miles.

  4. LED lighting + smart sleep mode

    Most modern refrigerated machines ship with LED + sleep mode that cuts overnight energy 30-50%. Verify enablement at install + each QBR.

  5. Packaging shift to rPET + 95% recycled aluminum + paper/fiber where category allows

    Cut packaging Scope 3 by 40-60% per SKU on rPET + 70-80% on high-recycled aluminum.

  6. Electric route vehicles + dispatch consolidation

    Cut Scope 1 service emissions 50-100% (offset by grid factor on EV).

  7. On-site renewable electricity / RECs

    Host-side green-power purchase or operator commitment to renewable PPAs covers residual Scope 2. Cut: up to 100% of Scope 2 with verified RECs.

  8. End-of-life machine recycling

    Refrigerant reclamation per EPA Section 608 + scrap-metal recycling. Avoid landfill disposal.

8. ESG reporting + standards alignment

GHG Protocol
Corporate Accounting + Reporting Standard for Scope 1+2+3. Scope 3 Category 1 (purchased goods + services) covers vending Scope 3 from the host side.
ENERGY STAR for vending
ENERGY STAR 4.0 for refrigerated beverage vending machines (effective 2024). Verify yellow EnergyGuide label on every machine bid.
EPA SNAP
Significant New Alternatives Policy lists approved low-GWP refrigerants. R-290 + R-600a approved for stand-alone retail refrigeration.
CDP
Climate Change disclosure C6.3 (Scope 3 emissions). Vending vendors should provide annual emission factor per vend.
SBTi
Science-Based Targets initiative — vending operator should be on a 1.5°C pathway (4.2% absolute reduction per year).
SASB
Food + Beverage standards for packaging + food-waste disclosure.

9. Operator selection — questions to ask

  • What is your published emission factor per vend? Last calculated date + methodology?
  • What percentage of your fleet is ENERGY STAR + low-GWP refrigerant? Year-by-year trajectory?
  • Can you provide telemetry-verified route mileage to support host Scope 3 Category 1?
  • What is your packaging mix by recycled content and recyclability?
  • Do you have a published net-zero or SBTi commitment? Verified by what body?
  • Can you supply a CDP response or equivalent third-party-verified disclosure annually?
