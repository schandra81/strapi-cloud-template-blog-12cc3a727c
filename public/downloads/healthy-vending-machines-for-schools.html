School Healthy Vending Pack

Smart Snacks compliance framework, SKU catalog, audit documentation template, and operator selection checklist

This pack is a working compliance + procurement framework for K-12 districts, individual schools, and higher-ed campuses operating vending under USDA Smart Snacks in School. It supplements district nutrition services' own policy with the operational details — SKU catalog, planogram, audit documentation, and operator-selection criteria — needed to keep a program audit-ready.

1. Smart Snacks in School — regulatory framework

USDA Smart Snacks Final Rule (7 CFR 210 + 220) applies to all foods + beverages sold to students on the school campus during the school day (midnight to 30 minutes after the last bell). Includes vending, school stores, fundraisers, and à la carte.

Snack standards (all grade levels)
≤ 200 cal/portion, ≤ 35% calories from total fat, ≤ 10% calories from saturated fat, 0 g trans fat, ≤ 200 mg sodium, ≤ 35% sugar by weight.
First-ingredient rule
Whole-grain-rich (≥ 50% whole-grain ingredient by weight) OR fruit/vegetable/dairy/protein-food as first ingredient OR ≥ ¼ cup fruit/veg per portion.
Beverage limits (elementary K-5)
Water (any size), low-fat milk ≤ 8 oz, 100% juice ≤ 8 oz only. No caffeinated beverages.
Beverage limits (middle 6-8)
Water (any size), low-fat milk ≤ 12 oz, 100% juice ≤ 12 oz only. No caffeinated beverages.
Beverage limits (high 9-12)
Water (any size), low-fat milk ≤ 12 oz, 100% juice ≤ 12 oz, other beverages ≤ 60 cal/12 oz or ≤ 40 cal/8 oz. Caffeinated permitted.
Entrée standards
≤ 350 cal, ≤ 35% calories from total fat, ≤ 10% calories from saturated fat, 0 g trans fat, ≤ 480 mg sodium.

4. Allergen + cross-contact framework

FALCPA top-9 disclosure
Every SKU labeled for milk, egg, fish, shellfish, tree nut, peanut, wheat, soy, sesame. Operator supplies CSV of allergen data per SKU.
Nut-free zone (K-5 default)
Dedicated machine or planogram row with no tree-nut or peanut SKUs. Clear signage at student eye-level.
Gluten-free options
≥ 15% of healthier SKUs gluten-free (KIND Healthy Grains certain SKUs, RXBAR, Larabar, Chomps).
Dietary accommodation request
Process for parent / student to request specific dietary SKU additions; food-service director reviews monthly.
Cross-contact attestation
Operator attests to manufacturer cross-contact statements on each SKU (e.g., 'may contain' peanut traces).

5. Audit documentation template

Operator provides this documentation at install, annually, and at any USDA or state audit. Filed with food-service director.

  • Smart Snacks Product Calculator output per SKU, dated within last 90 days
  • Manufacturer nutrition label + ingredient list per SKU (PDF or operator-portal export)
  • Operator attestation letter: all SKUs compliant with Smart Snacks Final Rule
  • Allergen disclosure CSV per SKU (top-9 + cross-contact)
  • Quarterly compliance refresh: confirm no SKU formulation change broke compliance
  • Planogram diagram + photograph per machine showing current SKU placement
  • Vending operator background-check + fingerprint attestation per state code (CA AB 1610, FL 1012.32, TX 22.083)
  • Telemetry-verified restock log with date, technician, SKUs (for HACCP-equivalent food-safety record where applicable)

6. Equipment + safety specification

Equipment type
Glass-front refrigerated snack/food for fresh + dairy + parfait; CRBV for beverages; ambient snack for shelf-stable. ENERGY STAR-certified preferred.
ADA compliance
2010 ADA Standards Section 308 (reach range 15-48 in) + 309 (≤ 5 lbf operating force). Required in all public + federally-funded facilities including schools.
Anti-tip + anti-rock
All machines secured to wall or anti-tip bracket per UL 541 + NAMA safety guidance.
Glass safety
Tempered safety glass per ANSI Z97.1 on glass-front machines.
Electrical
GFCI-protected 120V/15A outlet per NEC 210.8(B). Dedicated circuit preferred.
Temperature monitoring
Refrigerated machines maintain 33-40°F per FDA Food Code. Telemetry-verified with excursion alerts.

7. Subsidy + pricing strategy

Price points (K-12)
Most district policies cap vending at $1.00-$2.00 per snack and $1.50-$3.00 per beverage. Operator supplies tiered pricing per SKU.
Subsidy from district / activity fund
Free-vend water + fruit; subsidize whole-grain bars to parity with conventional. Activity-fund-supported water on game days.
Title I + free + reduced
Where district policy allows, vending purchase via student meal account; aligned with USDA Community Eligibility Provision (CEP) where applicable.
Revenue share to school
Typical 10-20% commission to school activity fund. Earmarked for wellness + nutrition programs per CDC Whole School, Whole Community, Whole Child (WSCC) model.

8. Operator selection checklist

  • Operator demonstrates Smart Snacks compliance database + Product Calculator workflow
  • Background-checked + fingerprinted restock staff per state code; copies on file with district HR
  • ENERGY STAR + low-GWP-refrigerant equipment standard
  • Allergen + cross-contact disclosure CSV per SKU
  • Telemetry on every machine with monthly utilization + compliance report
  • References from 3+ comparable districts with ≥ 24 months tenure
  • Insurance: $2M general liability, $1M product liability, $1M cyber, certificate naming district as additional insured
  • Capacity to support audit (USDA or state) within 5 business days
  • Local + district-of-attendance preference where applicable (Buy California, Buy Florida-Grown, similar)

9. Wellness + curriculum integration

  • Vending data shared with school wellness council per CDC WSCC framework
  • Nutrition curriculum integration — vending datasets shared with health + science classes (NGSS HS-LS1 + MS-LS1)
  • Annual student survey on healthier SKU preferences feeds quarterly planogram refresh
  • Parent + PTO communication on vending changes via newsletter + parent portal
  • Coordination with athletic department on game-day hydration + post-practice protein options
