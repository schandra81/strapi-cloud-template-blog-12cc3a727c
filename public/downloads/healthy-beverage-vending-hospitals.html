4. Contract language — clauses to require

Without explicit contract language, planogram drift takes a 'healthy launch' back to baseline within 6-12 months. Lock the framework in.

  1. Framework election

    Contract names the specific framework (e.g., 'Partnership for a Healthier America Healthier Vending Standard'). Operator binds to deliver.

  2. Minimum share + max share

    Per-machine minimum Tier-1 share + max Tier-3 share. Verified by quarterly audit.

  3. Quarterly audit + remediation

    Operator submits planogram inventory each quarter; failure to meet thresholds triggers 30-day cure + commission penalty.

  4. Right to substitute

    Hospital nutrition lead has right to require swap of any SKU within 30 days.

  5. Per-SKU labeling

    Operator labels each facing with tier color (green / yellow / red) per the chosen framework so members can see compliance.

  6. Termination for sustained non-compliance

    ≥ 2 consecutive failed audits triggers right to terminate with 60-day notice.

  7. Reporting

    Operator provides annual healthy-beverage compliance report aligned with hospital community-benefit reporting (IRS Form 990 Schedule H).

6. Pricing + behavioral nudges

Compliance + price + placement together drive behavior. Cleveland Clinic, Boston Children's, and Kaiser case studies all combine all three.

Pricing differential
Tier 1 priced 10-20% below Tier 3. Multiple peer-reviewed studies (Block et al. 2010 NEJM; French et al. 2010 AJPH) show 10-25% Tier 3 consumption reduction with price + placement combined.
Placement
Tier 1 at eye level (rows 3-4 of 6); Tier 3 at top + bottom (less visible).
Tier color labeling
Per-facing tier-color sticker per the chosen framework; high-contrast labels per Section 703 (≥ 70%).
Front-page kiosk message
Where the machine has a touchscreen, default landing screen highlights Tier 1 SKUs + a wellness message.
Refill stations
Pair the machine with a hospital-branded water refill station within 50 feet; supports Tier 1 substitution + ASHE / FGI 2022 hospital water-quality guidance.

7. Operator capabilities required

  • Refrigerated fleet with ENERGY STAR 4.0 Class A merchandisers (MDEC ≤ 8.84 + 0.0252 × V kWh/day).
  • Telemetry-driven planogram audit; can produce per-machine tier breakdown on demand.
  • Established cold-chain logistics for Tier 1 fresh milk + Fairlife SKUs (FDA Food Code §3-202 sourcing).
  • Cashless-enabled with PCI-DSS attestation.
  • Quarterly QBR with hospital wellness + nutrition leadership.
  • References from at least 2 prior hospital healthy-beverage deployments (Kaiser, Cleveland Clinic, Boston Children's, NYC H+H, or comparable IDN).
  • Insurance + liability appropriate for hospital placements (GL ≥ $2M/$4M, product liability ≥ $2M).
