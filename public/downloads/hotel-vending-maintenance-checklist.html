Hotel Vending Maintenance Pack

Daily housekeeping, weekly operator, monthly preventive maintenance, and quarterly deep-service checklists

Hotel vending alcoves live in a high-visibility, guest-facing environment with brand-standard cleanliness expectations layered on top of operator obligations. This pack consolidates four working checklists (daily housekeeping, weekly operator visit, monthly PM, quarterly deep service) that an engineering director, GM, or operator can adopt as the operational baseline.

1. Daily housekeeping checklist (front-of-house team)

Housekeeping or night audit completes during a fixed lobby/corridor sweep. Time-on-task ≤ 5 minutes per alcove.

  • Front glass + machine face wiped with neutral cleaner; no smudges or fingerprints
  • Dispense bin emptied of trash, wrappers, or product debris
  • Floor mopped or vacuumed in alcove footprint; no spills, sticky residue, or trip hazards
  • Ice machine drip pan emptied (if co-located); no standing water (ASHRAE 188 Legionella consideration)
  • Card reader / contactless tap zone visibly free of damage, tape residue, or skimmer overlays
  • 'Out-of-order' or service tag noted and reported to engineering before 11:00 AM
  • Lighting in alcove operational; no flickering or burned-out bulbs

2. Weekly operator visit checklist

  • Restock against telemetry-driven planogram; FIFO rotation; pull any expired SKUs
  • All TCS / refrigerated SKUs at ≤ 41°F per FDA Food Code §3-501.16; cooler thermometer verified
  • Date marking on TCS SKUs ≤ 7 days from prep (FDA Food Code §3-501.17)
  • Coin/bill mechanism cleaned, jam tested with calibration coins and bills
  • Card reader EMV chip + contactless tap test transaction; receipt confirms PCI-DSS v4.0 reader online
  • Telemetry heartbeat confirmed at < 15-minute interval; offline status cleared
  • Allergen labeling card on machine face current; Top-9 declared per FALCPA
  • Service log signed + dated; left with engineering or at front desk for retention

3. Monthly preventive-maintenance checklist (operator technician)

  1. Refrigeration system

    Condenser coil cleaned of dust + lint (manufacturer-recommended interval typically 30–90 days). Compressor amp-draw measured against nameplate. Defrost cycle observed and timed against spec.

  2. Door seals + gaskets

    Visual inspection for cracks; dollar-bill test on gasket seal. Replace if bill slides out without resistance. Common life: 24–36 months under hotel duty cycle.

  3. Electrical + grounding

    Cord, plug, and dedicated 20A receptacle inspected; no scorching or wear. Ground continuity verified per NFPA 70 §250 with handheld tester.

  4. Lighting

    Cabinet LED panel inspected; replace any LED with < 80% rated lumens or visible discoloration.

  5. Coin + bill mechanism

    Vacuum debris from acceptor path; lubricate per OEM spec (typically dry PTFE, never WD-40).

  6. Card reader + telemetry

    Firmware updates applied to OEM minor version. EMV terminal certificate validity > 90 days. Cellular signal strength logged.

  7. Cabinet interior sanitization

    EPA-registered food-contact-safe sanitizer; contact time observed; rinsed where required. Log entry retained 12 months.

4. Quarterly deep-service checklist

Deeper service window typically 60–90 minutes per machine. Coordinate with engineering for any temporary alcove signage.

  • Compressor + condenser full clean + capacitor health test
  • Evaporator coil inspection; defrost drain line flushed (microbial / Legionella consideration per ASHRAE 188 §7)
  • Door alignment + hinge torque to OEM spec
  • ADA recertification — reach range (§308), operating force (§309.4), clear floor space (§305) re-measured
  • Cabinet finish inspected for chip, scratch, or rust; touch-up where applicable
  • Card reader replaced if EMV cert nearing expiry or PCI-DSS v4.0 reader generation lifecycle indicates
  • Telemetry battery / backup tested; sub-15-minute heartbeat verified after pull-the-plug test
  • Operator + facility joint review of planogram, refund rate, uptime, and complaint log

6. SLA + escalation

  1. Tier 1 — guest-facing failure

    Machine offline, payment system down, refrigeration failure with TCS product loss. < 24 hour acknowledge, < 24 hour resolve. After-hours escalation phone line provided.

  2. Tier 2 — stockout / planogram

    Single SKU stockout, planogram refresh request. 24–48 hour acknowledge, 5–7 day resolve.

  3. Tier 3 — cosmetic / signage

    Wrap refresh, light bulb, label refresh. 5-day acknowledge, 14-day resolve.

  4. Guest refund SLA

    App, SMS, or phone refund — auto-credit within 24 hours for cashless. Cash refund issued by front desk on operator's behalf and reconciled next visit (≤ 7 days).
