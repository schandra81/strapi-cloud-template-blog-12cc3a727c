Campus Wellness Vending Implementation Pack

Planogram, signage, and health-services partnership template for AI cooler wellness vending

Campus mental-wellness vending pairs AI coolers with student health services to make wellness resources (hydration, recovery, sleep aids, comfort SKUs) and crisis-line signage easy to access at 2 AM during finals. This pack bundles the three working documents (planogram, signage kit, partnership template) a student-affairs + counseling-services + auxiliary-services team needs.

2. Cooler-face signage + wellness messaging kit

Wellness coolers double as quiet wellness touchpoints. Cooler-face signage carries crisis-line numbers + counseling resource QR + accessibility info — not in a clinical way, but always available.

  • Cooler-face panel (11x17 inside-glass): 'Take care of you' — payment options + refund QR + allergen summary + 988 / campus crisis-line + counseling-services QR
  • Dietary attestation icons + healthy-share badge visible at cooler face
  • Wellness-prompts strip (along top header): rotating message ('hydrate today,' 'rest is productive,' 'reach out — counseling services [QR]')
  • 988 + crisis-line number printed prominently on every cooler face (state-specific number where applicable)
  • Counseling-services + basic-needs hub QR — links to plain-language web page
  • Accessibility note: alternative refund flow + accommodation request channel
  • Emergency contact: 24/7 student health + after-hours counseling number
  • FALCPA + dietary signage compliant with campus + state code

3. Health-services partnership template

Wellness vending succeeds when student health services + counseling services + auxiliary services are co-owners. The template below defines the partnership.

Sponsoring offices
Student health services + counseling services + auxiliary services + dean of students; signed memorandum of understanding (MoU) defines roles.
Wellness planogram review
Semi-annual: student health dietitian + counseling-services rep review planogram for wellness alignment; auxiliary services + operator coordinate.
Subsidy program (optional)
Student health funds a 'wellness boost' credit for eligible students (high-stress weeks, mental-health awareness month); operator dispenses against the credit.
Crisis-line signage coordination
Counseling services owns crisis-line signage content + cadence; operator commits to displaying current campus + 988 numbers on every cooler face.
Awareness-week activation
Mental-health awareness month (May), suicide-prevention week (September), stress-awareness week — coordinated SKU spotlight + signage + social campaign.
Data + privacy
Aggregated cooler utilization shared with student health for trend analysis (peak stress periods, healthy-share trends); no student-identifiable data; FERPA DPA covers any campus-card-tender-tied data.
Quarterly review
Auxiliary services + student health + counseling + operator account manager review planogram, signage, awareness-week activations, sponsorship reconciliation.
Annual review
Outcomes assessment: utilization, planogram impact, healthy-share trend; aligned to student-wellness strategic plan.

5. Placement criteria — wellness coolers

  • Foot traffic ≥ 300 unique students/day during academic term
  • Placement near student health center, counseling services, or basic-needs hub
  • Operating hours: ≥ 18 hr/day during term; 24/7 during high-stress weeks where building access supports
  • Acoustic + lighting: low-noise compressor; dimmed overnight lighting; not in clinical waiting room
  • ADA Section 308 compliance: 30x48 in clear floor space, operable parts 15-48 in AFF, ≤ 5 lbf operating force
  • Privacy: cooler placement allows discreet purchase (e.g., not in front of receptionist) for stigma-sensitive SKUs (sleep aid, OTC, comfort)
  • Trash + recycling within 25 ft
  • Cellular ≥ -95 dBm or Ethernet drop on segmented VLAN

6. Stigma + dignity framework

Wellness coolers carry sleep aid + OTC + recovery SKUs that some students avoid in stigmatizing environments. The framework below ensures dignified access.

Privacy at point of purchase
Cooler dispenses without visible-to-others packaging; refund flow is private (QR / SMS, not in-person).
No 'mental-health-only' branding
Cooler cabinet wrap matches campus brand standards; wellness messaging is one of several rotating themes.
Crisis-line visible always
988 + campus crisis line on every cooler face, every day — not as a special-event call-out.
Refund + complaint without identification
Refund flow does not require student ID; QR + SMS allow anonymous refund routing.
Sleep aid + OTC at scale
Sleep aid + OTC are normal SKUs alongside snack + beverage — destigmatized by inclusion in standard wellness mix.
Dietary attestation accuracy
Vegan, GF, halal, kosher, dairy-free icons visible per SKU; reduces friction for diet-restrictive students.
Cultural relevance
Curated SKU mix matching campus demographics; refreshed quarterly with student-affairs input.
