Industrial Facility Vending Equipment & Operations Playbook

Equipment specification, environmental ratings, and operations discipline for industrial sites

Industrial sites — heavy manufacturing, foundries, distribution centers, refineries, mining services — punish consumer-grade vending equipment. This playbook specifies the equipment, placement discipline, and service operations that survive the environment and serve the workforce.

1. Environmental classification

Before specifying equipment, classify each placement against the actual exposure. The same plant can have three different classes within 500 ft of each other.

Class A — Climate-controlled office / control room
60-78°F, 30-60% RH, low dust. Standard glass-front equipment acceptable.
Class B — Conditioned breakroom on production floor
65-85°F, dust intrusion possible, vibration low. Standard equipment plus dust filters and sealed bezels.
Class C — Production floor, semi-conditioned
55-95°F, high dust, vibration moderate. Industrial-spec cabinet (gauge-thicker steel, sealed gaskets), filtered intake, NEMA 12-rated electronics enclosure.
Class D — Outdoor / loading dock / weather-exposed
Outdoor temperature range, weather, UV. Outdoor-rated cabinet (NEMA 4 enclosure, weather hood, freeze-protected condensate). Crane 167 Outdoor or AMS Outdoor-spec.

3. Placement discipline

  1. Worker-time math

    Workers should not lose more than 5 min of a 15-min break walking to/from a machine. Place clusters within 200 ft of any work station; satellites within 100 ft of high-density work cells.

  2. Travel-path safety

    Placements off forklift lanes per OSHA 1910.176(a); 36 in. service clearance per ANSI/NAMA 2-2011 §4; ADA 30 in. x 48 in. approach per 2010 ADA Standards §305.

  3. Power + cooling load

    Aggregate cluster load < 80% of dedicated 20A 120V branch per NEC 210.23. Refrigerated units on dedicated circuits. Heat rejection routed away from break seating.

  4. Sanitation perimeter

    Food consumption areas separated from production per OSHA 1910.141(g)(2). Vending placement aligns with this perimeter, not inside production zone.

  5. Lockout coordination

    Vending power circuits documented in the site electrical drawings so they remain energized during equipment lockout/tagout (LOTO) per OSHA 1910.147.

4. Operational support structure

Restock cadence
2-5 visits/week depending on size. Pre-shift-1 restock window 5-6 AM; mid-shift telemetry-driven top-off for high-velocity machines.
Service SLA — Tier 1
Offline, cold-chain excursion, payment system down — <24 hr acknowledgement, <24 hr resolution.
Service SLA — Tier 2
SKU stockout, planogram refresh — 24-48 hr ack, 5-7 day resolution.
Service SLA — Tier 3
Cosmetic, signage, cleaning — 5 day ack, 14 day resolution.
Preventive maintenance
Quarterly PM visit: filter change, gasket inspection, refrigeration coil clean, payment reader firmware check, telemetry battery check.

5. Telemetry baseline

Industrial sites cannot rely on visual restock checks — the breakroom is often unstaffed for shifts at a time. Telemetry is the operational nervous system.

Required telemetry
Cantaloupe Seed, Nayax, 365 Retail Markets, or Crane Streamware — cellular-modem-enabled with sub-15-min heartbeat. No host LAN dependency.
Monitored metrics
Online/offline, last-restock timestamp, per-SKU velocity, cash/cashless mix, payment-failure rate, refrigeration temperature, door-open events.
Alert thresholds
Offline >30 min triggers operator alert. Temperature excursion outside 33-41°F (refrigerated) or above 0°F (frozen) triggers immediate alert with 4-hour SLA.

6. Planogram for industrial workforce

Industrial workforces show higher calorie demand, higher hydration demand, and stronger preference for familiar mainstream brands. Per ACOEM industrial-workforce guidance, hydration share should be elevated.

  • Snack: 60-70% mainstream salty/sweet (Frito-Lay, Mondelez, Hershey); 20-30% protein/meal-replacement; 10-20% better-for-you SKUs per NAMA FitPick
  • Beverage: 30%+ water + electrolyte + unsweetened (ACOEM hydration guidance); 40% carbonated; 20% energy; 10% other
  • Food/fresh: meal-replacement weight (sandwiches, wraps, protein bowls) over snack-only mix
  • Shift-3 surge: caffeine + hot-snack + comfort food weighting per shift-3 velocity data
