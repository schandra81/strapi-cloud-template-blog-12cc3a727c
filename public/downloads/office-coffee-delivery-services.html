Office Coffee Delivery Playbook

Provider tier selection, equipment scoping, supply cadence, and service-SLA structure

Office coffee delivery services (OCS) operate on a four-decision frame: provider tier, equipment, supply cadence, and SLA. The wrong cut on any one of those drives stockouts, off-taste, or recurring over-spend within two quarters. This playbook codifies each. It maps to NSF 4 / NSF 169 sanitation standards, FDA food-contact equipment standards (21 CFR 175-178), ADA Section 309.4 operating force, and standard NAMA OCS service KPIs.

1. Provider tier selection

Office coffee providers split into three operating models. Tier selection is set by office size + service expectation, not pricing alone.

2. Equipment scoping

  • Match equipment tier to peak-hour drink count: pod under 10/hr, office-prosumer 10-25/hr, semi-commercial 25-60/hr, commercial 60+/hr.
  • Plan for 1.2-1.6 drinks per employee per workday at full in-office occupancy. Hybrid offices: use actual badge-tap occupancy, not headcount.
  • Dual-rail option (pod + drip, or pod + bean-to-cup) at 30+ employee offices accommodates both fast-cup and meeting-pot use cases.
  • Plumbed-in water vs. jug — plumbed always preferred where feasible. Saves recurring jug labor + plastic waste; supports filter program.
  • Alt-milk capability — confirm at machine selection; expect secondary milk hopper or counter-side carafe at commercial-grade.
  • ADA Section 309.4 — cup-dispense, machine controls, sweetener station within 15-48 inches AFF and ≤ 5 lbf operating force.

3. Supply cadence

  1. Auto-replenishment baseline

    Provider sets a baseline cadence based on first-90-day consumption data. Standard pattern: weekly route visit + restock of fast-mover SKUs, biweekly route for slower SKUs.

  2. Telemetry-driven adjustments

    Where the machine is telemetry-enabled (Cantaloupe, Nayax, AWM, OEM-native), per-SKU consumption triggers between-route top-offs. Reduces stockouts 40-60% per published case studies.

  3. Seasonal pivots

    Iced / cold-brew SKUs weighted up in spring/summer; hot-brew + dark-roast in fall/winter. Hot-chocolate added October-March.

  4. Special-request adds

    Member-requested SKUs evaluated quarterly; added with 30-day velocity check before locking into regular cadence.

  5. Bean freshness

    Roast date stamped on bean bag; opened within 30 days of roast; consumed within 14 days of opening. Operator field rep rotates hopper FIFO.

  6. Refrigerated supply

    Fresh milk delivered weekly; refrigerated case temperature logged 35-40°F; excursion triggers same-day swap.

4. Cleaning + maintenance schedule

Daily (host or operator)
Empty + rinse drip tray. Wipe counter, machine exterior, sweetener caddy. Refill cups + sweeteners. Empty trash.
Weekly (operator field rep)
Deep clean machine exterior. Clean milk frother / steam wand. Descale check. Restock paper goods. Wipe inside cabinet doors.
Monthly (operator)
Descale per machine schedule. Replace milk supply tubing per spec. Audit syrup / pump-bottle freshness.
Quarterly (operator)
Disassemble + sanitize milk system, descale, inspect seals, calibrate dispense volumes. NSF 4 / NSF 169 alignment.
Annual (operator)
Water filter replacement; preventive maintenance per OEM schedule. Equipment-condition audit.

6. Pricing + invoicing

  • Pricing structure: per-employee per-month (bundled), per-pound + per-cup (à la carte), or hybrid (base fee + consumption overage). Per-employee preferred at 50+ employee offices for predictability.
  • What's included: equipment, supply (beans / pods, milk, sugar, cups, lids, stir sticks), maintenance, restock labor, route truck.
  • What's excluded: paper goods beyond cups (host-supplied typically), kitchen appliances (microwave, dishwasher — host), water plumbing install (one-time, scoped separately).
  • Quarterly true-up — if headcount changes ± 15%, monthly fee adjusts. Annual full review at QBR.
  • Consumption-overage clause — if drink-count exceeds bundle threshold by > 20%, operator can upgrade tier or charge per-unit overage. Negotiate cap (15% premium max).
  • Annual escalator capped at CPI or 5%, whichever lower.

7. Quarterly business review

  • Drink-count per machine per month over the quarter.
  • Drink-mix — espresso vs. drip vs. milk-based vs. specialty.
  • Stockout incidents by SKU + machine + week.
  • Service-ticket SLA adherence by tier (%).
  • Milk-system cleaning compliance log review.
  • Bean freshness audit — sampled bags' roast date vs. consumption date.
  • Headcount vs. bundle-tier alignment; overage status.
  • Member feedback themes + refund-reason codes.
  • Planogram refresh — SKU swap proposals for next quarter.
  • Action items, owner, due date.

8. Pre-signing red flags

  • Provider declines telemetry on coffee machine — opaque consumption data.
  • No documented milk-system cleaning cadence or compliance log.
  • SLA proposed without per-tier acknowledgment + resolution targets.
  • Water filter program absent or 'as needed'.
  • Equipment tier mismatched to peak-hour drink count.
  • Per-pound + per-cup pricing without consumption cap — variable invoicing surprises.
  • No quarterly business review — program drifts within 2 quarters.
This playbook is informational. Final provider selection benefits from a written 90-day pilot at the proposed equipment tier matched to actual office drink-mix.
