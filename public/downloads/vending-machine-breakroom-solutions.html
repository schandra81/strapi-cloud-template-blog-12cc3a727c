Manufacturing Breakroom Vending Solution Playbook

Equipment ensemble, site survey, layout, and operational support structure

This playbook codifies the breakroom vending design decisions that keep a manufacturing plant of 200-1,500 hourly workers fed across three shifts at 98%+ uptime. Each section is operator-attestable and maps to a measurable SLA or specification.

1. Pre-install site survey

Run the site survey 30-45 days before equipment delivery. Survey output drives the equipment ensemble, electrical scope, and the planogram baseline.

Headcount + shift map
Hourly headcount per shift, break/lunch windows, peak transition timing. Manufacturing plants typically run 3 shifts (6-2, 2-10, 10-6) with 200-600 workers per shift.
Floor space + clearance
Minimum 36 in. service clearance per ANSI/NAMA 2-2011 §4. Verify 6 ft x 8 ft slab footprint per cluster, level within 1/4 in., 30 in. x 48 in. ADA-compliant approach per 2010 ADA Standards §305.
Electrical scope
Dedicated 20A 120V circuit per refrigerated unit (NEC 210.23). GFCI within 6 ft of any sink per NEC 210.8(B). Snack units share circuits within 1.8 A nameplate load.
Network + telemetry
Cellular-modem-enabled telemetry (Cantaloupe, Nayax, 365 Retail Markets, or Crane Streamware) with sub-15-min heartbeat. No host LAN dependency.
Water + drain
Cold-water tap with backflow preventer (ASSE 1022) for coffee/water lines. Floor drain or condensate pump for refrigerated equipment.

3. Breakroom layout — six placement zones

  1. Primary breakroom cluster

    Snack + cold beverage + food in one bank; coffee tower or micro-market at end. Sized for 60-70% of headcount visiting during peak 30-min window.

  2. Floor-side satellite

    1-2 machines (snack + beverage) within 200 ft of every work station so workers don't lose break time walking. OSHA 1910.141 sets sanitation distance for food consumption areas.

  3. Shift-3 / overnight bank

    Higher caffeine + hot-snack mix; locked access via shift badge if main cafeteria closes.

  4. Loading dock / drivers' lounge

    1 snack + 1 beverage + small coffee unit, 24/7 access for inbound drivers.

  5. Office annex

    Higher-end planogram (premium snacks, RTD coffee, kombucha) for admin/engineering staff.

  6. Wellness option

    Optional fresh-food cooler or NAMA FitPick-aligned mini-market within 50 ft of the primary cluster.

4. Operational support structure

Operators capable of multi-shift manufacturing service typically commit to the following in writing. Verify each in the contract.

Restock cadence
2-4 visits/week depending on plant size, with pre-shift-1 restock window 5-6 AM. Per-machine velocity drives schedule, not fixed days.
Service SLA — Tier 1
Machine offline, cold-chain excursion, or payment failure — <24 hr acknowledgement, <24 hr resolution.
Service SLA — Tier 2
Single-SKU stockout, planogram refresh — 24-48 hr ack, 5-7 day resolution.
Service SLA — Tier 3
Cosmetic / signage / cabinet cleaning — 5 day ack, 14 day resolution.
Refund flow
App, SMS-to-refund, or on-machine touchpad; auto-credit within 24 hr for cashless. Quarterly refund report to facility.

5. Wellness + labeling overlay

Manufacturing plants increasingly align breakroom vending to wellness program incentives. FDA 21 CFR §101.8 already mandates calorie labeling on glass-front machines; NAMA FitPick provides an optional better-for-you mark.

  • Calorie disclosure visible on machine face for every front-of-pack SKU (FDA 21 CFR §101.8, effective 2018).
  • Allergen statement per FALCPA visible at the planogram or via app/QR.
  • 30-40% better-for-you SKUs (NAMA FitPick / FitPick Select criteria) where wellness program participation is incentivized.
  • Hydration emphasis — water, electrolyte, and unsweetened beverage SKUs ≥ 30% of beverage facings (per ACOEM guidance for industrial workforce).

7. Quarterly business review checklist

  • Per-machine uptime + service SLA adherence
  • Per-shift velocity report (shift-1 vs shift-2 vs shift-3)
  • Top-20 and bottom-20 SKUs with planogram action items
  • Refund volume + reason codes
  • Wellness mix (FitPick %, calorie labeling audit, hydration share)
  • Equipment health + scheduled preventive maintenance plan
  • Roadmap items: micro-market expansion, satellite placements, shift-3 surge coverage
