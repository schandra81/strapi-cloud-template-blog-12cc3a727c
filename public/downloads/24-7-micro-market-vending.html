24/7 Micro-Market Vending Playbook

Kiosk, refrigerated case bank, healthy-share, shrinkage, telemetry, and subsidy integration

A 24/7 micro-market is an unstaffed retail footprint inside the host's facility: an open shelving footprint, a refrigerated case bank, a self-checkout kiosk, and a payment + identity rail. Per published industry studies, micro-markets outperform comparable vending placements 30-60% on per-occupant revenue when the six operational levers in this playbook are managed. This playbook codifies each. It maps to PCI DSS v4.0, EMV + NFC, ADA Section 309.4 + 308, FALCPA allergen labeling, NIST IR 8259 (IoT baseline), and standard NAMA service KPIs.

1. Kiosk + payment rails

  • Self-checkout kiosk with EMV chip + contactless + mobile wallet + closed-loop (badge / SSO) rails. PCI DSS v4.0 AOC current within 12 months.
  • Camera-vision second-check (where supported) for loss-mitigation: kiosk validates scanned items against camera-detected items before completing transaction.
  • ADA Section 309.4 — kiosk screen, payment reader, and bagging area within reach range (15-48 inches AFF) and ≤ 5 lbf operating force.
  • Audio + accessibility — Section 707 audio output for visually impaired members; high-contrast UI mode.
  • Receipt — printed + emailed (member's opt-in); transaction history available in operator app.
  • Refund flow — member-initiated via app, SMS, or on-kiosk; cashless auto-credit within 24 hours.

2. Refrigerated case bank

Cold-chain is the most failure-prone subsystem in a micro-market. Per FDA Food Code and NSF/ANSI 7 standards, refrigerated SKUs require continuous temperature control with auditable logging.

Temperature target
Refrigerated case 35-40°F continuously; frozen case 0°F or below. Logged via telemetry; excursion outside range triggers immediate alert with 4-hour SLA.
Case capacity
Plan for 50-80 SKUs per linear case foot at 24 inch depth. Mix: 40% beverage, 25% fresh meal / sandwich, 20% snack-prep / dairy / fruit, 15% breakfast / yogurt.
Door sensor
Door-open / door-ajar sensor with telemetry alert at > 5 minute ajar threshold (excursion + theft risk).
Cold-chain documentation
Operator field-rep logs at every restock; auditable per HACCP-aligned principles. Annual sanitation per NSF 4.
Frozen / hot-hold (where in scope)
Frozen case (Häagen-Dazs, frozen meals) at 0°F or below; hot-hold (where present) ≥ 135°F per FDA Food Code.

3. Open-shelving + planogram

  • Open-shelving for shelf-stable SKUs (chips, bars, crackers, candy, dry pantry). Plan 70-120 SKUs per linear shelf-foot.
  • Healthy-share floor — at minimum 30% of SKUs meet NANA thresholds (≤ 200 cal, ≤ 230 mg sodium, ≤ 35% calories from fat); at wellness-focused placements 50%+. Healthy SKUs at eye-level + entry zone; not relegated to bottom shelf.
  • Allergen labeling per FALCPA on every SKU; allergen summary signage at kiosk + each shelving section; allergen-flag opt-in for app members.
  • Dietary-preference SKUs (vegetarian, vegan, halal, kosher, gluten-free) identified where demographics warrant; flagged with shelf tag.
  • Local-source SKUs (regional snack vendor, local bakery) included where requested; documented separately.
  • Quarterly telemetry-driven planogram refresh — bottom-decile SKUs swapped for member-requested + tested winners.

4. Shrinkage management

Shrinkage (theft + spoilage + miscounted) is the single biggest cost variable in unstaffed micro-markets. Published industry data suggests 1.5-4% shrink as typical; > 4% triggers intervention.

  1. Camera coverage

    Two cameras minimum at kiosk + entry; high-shelf cameras over open shelving. 14-30 day retention, sufficient to support incident review. Privacy notice posted at entrance.

  2. Identity at entry (high-trust placements)

    Badge / SSO tap at entry; only identified members enter. Eliminates anonymous shrink. Best at corporate + residential + healthcare staff-only placements.

  3. Camera-vision second-check at kiosk

    Real-time validation of scanned items against camera-detected items. Discrepancy prompts member to re-scan. Reduces honest miscounts + opportunistic skips.

  4. Audit cadence

    Operator inventory audit monthly; high-shrink-rate placements bi-weekly. Variance reconciled to telemetry sales + supply receipts.

  5. Member-facing comms

    Welcome signage emphasizes 'unstaffed, community-trust.' Refund channel publicized. Member feedback channel for honest miscount correction.

  6. Shrink-triggered action

    Shrinkage > 4% over 90 days triggers (a) camera coverage review, (b) identity-rail review (move to badge-tap entry), (c) planogram review (high-shrink SKUs relocated near kiosk), (d) host comms.

5. Telemetry + reporting

Connectivity
Cellular modem + facility WiFi failover. Sub-15-minute heartbeat. Per-SKU velocity, per-kiosk transaction count, temperature for cold-chain.
Alert thresholds
Kiosk offline > 30 min → operator alert. Refrigeration excursion → immediate alert + 4-hour SLA. Payment rail failure > 1 hour → operator alert.
Member-facing app
Member sees catalog + price + healthy-flag + allergen + balance + transaction history. Refund initiation in-app.
Host reporting
Weekly transaction-level export to host SFTP / API by 09:00 Monday. Schema published. Data ownership host; operator licensed for service + de-identified benchmarking only.
PCI scope
P2PE or PCI-listed terminal; no PAN persistence on operator infrastructure. SOC 2 Type II for back-office.

6. Subsidy + payment integration

  1. Full cost-recovery (default)

    Member pays full retail at kiosk. EMV + contactless + wallet rails. Standard for non-subsidized placements.

  2. Subsidized pricing

    Host subsidizes selected SKUs (typically healthy + breakfast); member pays subsidized price at kiosk. Subsidy reconciled monthly via operator settlement.

  3. Closed-loop / payroll deduct

    Member uses badge / SSO at kiosk; transaction posts to member's payroll-deduct account or pre-paid balance. Operator settles monthly to host AP. SOC 2 Type II on settlement environment.

  4. Tier-based pricing

    Loyalty tiers (active member, wellness-program enrollee, role-based) receive discounted pricing on selected SKUs. Reviewed for protected-class neutrality with HR + legal before launch.

  5. Visitor / contractor rail

    Non-employee visitors pay full retail via standard EMV / contactless; do not access subsidized or closed-loop rails.

7. Member experience + 24/7 operations

  • 24/7 access — host badge / lock controls; micro-market remains open even when adjacent facilities close (night shift, weekends, holidays).
  • Lighting + safety — adequate lighting at all hours; security camera coverage; emergency phone or panic button at kiosk for after-hours incidents.
  • Cleaning cadence — operator field rep daily during business days; weekly deep clean (case sanitation, kiosk sanitation, signage refresh).
  • Trash + recycling — visible bins; bin emptying coordinated with facility cleaning crew or operator.
  • Member feedback channel — operator app, SMS, kiosk touchpoint, email. Response within 24 hours for refund; 5 business days for non-refund feedback.
  • Comms — welcome signage explaining checkout, refund, and accessibility options; planogram refresh notices; healthy-share campaign signage.

9. Pre-signing red flags

  • Operator declines telemetry on kiosk + refrigerated cases — opaque velocity + cold-chain data.
  • Shrinkage SLA absent — host bears full cost variance with no operator accountability.
  • PCI DSS v4.0 AOC not current or scope unclear.
  • Camera retention beyond 30 days by default without privacy notice.
  • Healthy-share floor not in writing — depends on operator goodwill quarter-to-quarter.
  • Closed-loop / payroll-deduct integration promised without SOC 2 Type II on settlement environment.
  • No quarterly business review — program drifts within 2 quarters.
