Courthouse Vending Pack

Glass-free planogram template, security pre-clearance checklist, meal-replacement weighting

Courthouse vending serves a captive audience — jurors waiting through voir dire, members of the public waiting on docket, attorneys + clerks across long days. Glass containers, alcohol, certain energy drinks, and any item that could be repurposed as a weapon are typically prohibited inside the security perimeter. This pack codifies the courthouse-specific planogram, pre-clearance checklist, and operating protocols.

1. Why courthouse vending is its own category

Three constraints make courthouse vending unlike any other public-building placement: (1) security perimeter rules restrict packaging materials, (2) captive-audience dwell times (4-8 hours for jurors, often without ability to leave) push the planogram toward meal replacements, (3) building access for operator route drivers requires court-side security pre-clearance + sometimes individual case-day coordination.

2. Prohibited / restricted packaging — security perimeter

Inside the courthouse security perimeter, US Marshals Service / court security typically prohibit:

  • Glass containers (bottles, jars) — risk as projectile / weapon.
  • Metal cans with full-cut tops (some jurisdictions); pull-tab aluminum acceptable in most.
  • Items with metal foil seals heavier than standard yogurt-cup foil (case-by-case).
  • Alcohol (always).
  • Energy drinks above [varies by jurisdiction caffeine threshold] — some courts restrict; check local policy.
  • Heated / pressurized aerosol packaging.
  • Anything with an internal heating element (some self-heating beverage packaging).

4. Meal-replacement weighting — captive-audience dwell

Jurors and waiting public often cannot leave the courthouse during a session. Meal-format and protein-dense options matter more here than in any other public-building category.

Refrigerated meal-format
Turkey + cheese sandwich, hummus + veg wrap, chicken Caesar wrap, grain bowl. 3-4 slots minimum.
Microwaveable shelf-stable
Soup cup, mac-and-cheese cup, chicken-rice bowl, oatmeal. 2-3 slots; requires microwave in waiting area.
Protein-dense snacks
Jerky, mixed nuts, hard-boiled eggs (if refrigerated), protein bars. 3-4 slots.
Fresh fruit + yogurt
Apple slices, grape cup, yogurt, hummus + pretzel pack. 2-3 slots.

5. Pre-clearance checklist (operator + facility)

Run before contract sign + before any equipment delivery.

  • Court administrator + facility chief security officer review proposed planogram for packaging compliance.
  • Operator route driver background check meets court's contractor-access standard (state / federal varies).
  • Operator route driver enrolled in courthouse contractor-badge program.
  • Operator's restock cart / dolly meets security screening protocol (X-ray + visual inspection at entry).
  • Restock visit schedule coordinated with court calendar — avoid high-security days (high-profile trials) unless cleared.
  • Equipment delivery scheduled with facility (typically before court opens or after close).
  • Cash collection: most courts prefer cashless-only; if cash is included, collection scheduled with security present.
  • Operator's commercial GL + auto insurance certificates on file (typically $1M minimum).

6. Equipment + telemetry — courthouse-specific

  • Cashless-primary or cashless-only — reduces cash-handling security overhead.
  • Cellular telemetry — courthouse Wi-Fi is restricted; cellular is standard.
  • External antenna for thick-wall historic courthouses.
  • E-lock with audit log — every door-open event logged (deters insider theft + supports incident investigation).
  • Tamper-resistant fasteners.
  • Glass-front reinforced (laminated / tempered) where required by AHJ / facility security.
  • Surge protection.
  • Dedicated 20A circuit; documented in facility electrical drawings.

7. Service cadence + court-calendar coordination

Court calendars drive demand. High-volume days (jury selection, multi-defendant docket, civil-motion day) push 2-3× normal-day velocity.

  1. Baseline cadence

    Restock 2-3× per week for active courthouses; 1× per week for smaller / part-time facilities.

  2. Court-calendar overlay

    Court administrator shares 4-week calendar with operator. Operator overlays jury / docket peak days and adjusts restock to land morning-of.

  3. Tier-1 SLA

    Offline / payment failure: <24 hr response. Refrigeration excursion (fresh-meal stock): <4 hr response.

  4. Refund / dispense-failure

    Refund process accessible to jurors without leaving floor — QR code on machine to operator's mobile refund form. Refund credited within 24 hr (cashless) or via clerk's office (cash).
