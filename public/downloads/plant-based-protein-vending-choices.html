Plant-Based Protein Vending Pack

Planogram framework — six categories, demographic matching, and velocity tracking

Plant-based protein is no longer a niche category; the global plant-based food market is projected to reach $77B by 2025 (GFI + Bloomberg Intelligence). This pack gives wellness leaders, food-service managers, and benefits teams a six-category planogram framework, demographic matching guidance, and a velocity-tracking model to grow the program from 15% to 30%+ of vends over 18-24 months.

1. Six plant-based protein categories

A balanced plant-based planogram covers all six categories. Most failed programs over-index on bars and under-stock the other five.

1. Plant-based protein bars
GoMacro (10-12g pea + brown rice + sprouted), No Cow (20g pea), MacroBar Mini, Aloha (14g pea + pumpkin), Vega Sport (20g pea + sunflower + alfalfa)
2. Nuts, seeds, butters
Wonderful Pistachios (6g per oz), Justin's Almond Butter pack (7g), Sahale Glazed Cashews (5g), pumpkin seeds 1.5 oz pouch (10g), KIND Nuts & Spices (5-6g)
3. Plant-based jerky + chickpea snacks
Louisville Vegan Jerky (10g per oz), Pan's Mushroom Jerky (3-5g + umami), Biena Roasted Chickpeas (6g per oz), Saffron Road Crunchy Chickpeas (5g)
4. Plant-based RTD shakes
Orgain Organic Protein (21g pea + brown rice + chia), OWYN (20g pea + flax + pumpkin), Vega Protein+ (20g pea + hemp), Koia Protein (18g pea + brown rice + chickpea)
5. Plant-based yogurt + dairy alt (refrigerated)
Kite Hill Almond Yogurt (4g), Forager Project Cashew (6g), Silk Almond Protein (10g), Califia Farms Plant Protein, Oatly Barista (3g but high-volume use)
6. Plant-forward snacks (lower-protein but plant-based)
Bare Apple Chips, Stretch Island Fruit Strips, Skinny Pop, Late July Tortilla, Popcorners, edamame snack packs (Seapoint Farms 8g)

3. Protein quality + complete-protein labeling

A complete protein contains all nine essential amino acids in proportions matching human need. Most single-source plant proteins are incomplete; well-formulated blends are complete.

Complete plant proteins
Soy (edamame, soy isolate), quinoa, buckwheat, hemp seed, chia, amaranth. Pea-protein-plus-brown-rice blends used by Vega, Orgain, OWYN are complete.
PDCAAS scores
Soy isolate 1.00 (matches casein + whey); pea isolate 0.93; whey 1.00 (animal reference). Use as quality proxy on plant-based RTD shake spec.
Branding language for the program
'Complete plant protein' OK on bars + RTDs with verified blend. 'Plant-based protein source' for single-source.
Iron + B12 + vitamin D
Vegan-leaning planogram should include fortified RTD shakes + plant milks to mitigate B12 + iron + vitamin D gaps.

5. Dietary inclusivity overlap

  • Vegan-certified SKUs marked (Vegan Action 'Certified Vegan' or PETA-approved)
  • Gluten-free options ≥ 50% of plant-based facings (GoMacro, RXBAR, Larabar, Bare, Skinny Pop)
  • Kosher (OU, OK, Star-K) where employee population requires
  • Halal-certified options where employee population requires (IFANCA, HMA)
  • Nut-free section adjacent for K-5 + nut-allergy-aware workplaces
  • Top-9 allergen labeling per FALCPA on every SKU

6. Velocity tracking + course-correction

Per-SKU velocity by category
Track all six categories monthly. Plant-based bars usually lead; chickpea + jerky alt lag in year-1.
Plant-based share of total vends
Target trajectory: 15% baseline → 20% by month 6 → 25-30% by month 18 → 30-40% by month 36.
Stockout rate
Top-3 plant-based SKUs stockout ≤ 5% of days. Plant-based stockouts kill program credibility — flexitarian customers do not return.
New-SKU introduction cadence
Quarterly: introduce 2-4 new SKUs in lagging categories; retire bottom-10% velocity SKUs.
Employee survey
Annual survey: dietary preference (vegan / vegetarian / flexitarian / omnivore), satisfaction with plant-based selection, requested SKUs.

7. Subsidy + pricing

Price premium
Plant-based SKUs typically 10-30% premium over conventional. Pea isolate + soy isolate + chickpea are now cost-competitive; almond + cashew yogurt + RTD shakes remain premium.
Subsidy strategy
Price-match to conventional within category (bar to bar, RTD to RTD) for first 6 months.
Budget guideline
$5-15/employee/month incremental for 30% archetype.
Wellness app reward credit
Steps-to-protein-bar or hydration-to-RTD-shake reward via Virgin Pulse, Wellable, Limeade.

8. Operator selection checklist

  • Catalog of ≥ 30 plant-based protein SKUs across all six categories
  • Telemetry per-SKU velocity reporting
  • Allergen + dietary inclusivity disclosure (vegan, GF, kosher, halal) per SKU
  • Refrigerated equipment for category 5 (yogurt, RTD shake) — glass-front + temperature telemetry
  • Quarterly new-SKU introduction process demonstrated
  • References from 3+ comparable hosts (tech, healthcare, university) with ≥ 24 months tenure
  • Subsidy-flexible billing
