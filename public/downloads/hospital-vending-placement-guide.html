Hospital Vending Placement Pack

Zone-by-zone planogram, RFP template, infection control checklist, ADA audit framework

Hospital vending placement is a multi-stakeholder decision. This pack covers zone-by-zone planogram guidance, an RFP template, an infection-control checklist, and the ADA audit framework needed for placement, contract, and ongoing operation.

2. RFP template — what every operator must respond to

  • Operator company profile: years in healthcare, healthcare clients (named or anonymized), fleet size, geographic coverage.
  • Equipment: per-location proposal with make + model + ENERGY STAR 4.0 + AHRI 810-2016 / SNAP refrigerant attestation.
  • Telemetry stack: vendor, heartbeat interval, temperature alerting, dashboard access for hospital materials management.
  • Per-zone planogram proposal aligned with zone framework above + FALCPA per-SKU allergen labeling.
  • Healthy-beverage framework attestation (AHA, PHA, NEMS-V, or Kaiser equivalent).
  • Service SLA proposal with tier definitions + acknowledgement + resolution targets + credit / penalty for misses.
  • Infection-prevention compatibility: surface materials, disinfectant compatibility (CDC EPA List N), cleaning workflow.
  • ADA Section 305 + 308 attestation per unit (clear floor space, reach range, operating force ≤ 5 lbf).
  • Joint Commission EOC documentation pack — sample provided.
  • Commission schedule + foundation / auxiliary share.
  • Insurance: GL $2M/$4M, product liability $2M, auto $1M, workers comp; certificate naming hospital as additional insured.
  • Term + renewal + termination terms.
  • References from at least 3 hospital deployments.

3. Infection control checklist

Infection prevention (IP) reviews every food and beverage placement. The checklist below maps to CDC, Joint Commission EOC.02.06.05, and AIA / FGI Guidelines for Hospital Design (2022).

  • Cabinet exterior materials compatible with EPA List N disinfectants (effective 2024).
  • No fabric, upholstery, or porous surfaces on cabinet exterior.
  • Base raised ≥ 4 inches off the floor or fully sealed to floor for cleanability per AIA / FGI 2022.
  • Card reader + touchscreen surfaces wipeable without damage; oleophobic / antimicrobial coating preferred.
  • Dispense mechanism stainless or polymer with no horizontal dust-collection ledges.
  • Refrigerated cabinet self-defrost drain piped to floor drain or evaporator pan with anti-microbial liner.
  • Air-cooled condenser intake ≥ 6 inches from any wall; cleanable monthly per OEM spec.
  • EVS daily wipe-down high-touch surfaces (selection pad, card reader, dispense door) per IP protocol.
  • Outbreak protocol: terminal clean of cabinet + 6-foot perimeter during communicable-disease event.
  • No live or fresh-cut flowers in any vending placement (CDC ICU + IP guidance for immunocompromised patient adjacencies).

Hospital placements require approval from multiple roles. Map the approvals before submitting the placement proposal.

Risk management
Insurance + indemnification review.

Sight lines, duress alarm, after-hours access.
Legal
Contract terms, BAA-applicability review (typically not — vending isn't HIPAA-scoped unless integrated with patient records).

6. Power + electrical spec

Refrigerated cabinet
Dedicated 120V/20A circuit per NEC Article 422. GFCI per NEC 210.8 in damp / public areas.
Ambient snack
Dedicated 120V/15A circuit.
Charging station adjacency
Separate 120V/20A circuit; UL 2738 multi-port charger; tamper-resistant outlets per NEC 406.12.
Emergency power
Verify whether the placement is on emergency power feed (typically only ED / critical-care). Most break-room and lobby vending runs on normal power per NFPA 99 §6.3.
UL listing
Vending: UL 541 (refrigerated commercial) or UL 751 (vending machine, dry); micro-market cabinets per UL 471 (commercial refrigerators).

7. Fire egress + life safety

  • Cabinet does not reduce egress corridor below 44 in min width per IBC §1018.2.
  • Cabinet does not block sight lines to exit signage or duress alarms.
  • Cabinet anchored to floor or wall per NAMA + manufacturer + OSHA 1910.176 storage stability.
  • Combustible loading + smoke detector spacing: do not place vending under sprinkler-deflector shadow that would obstruct spray pattern (NFPA 13 §10.2.7.4).
  • Annual fire-marshal walk-through; documented in EOC compliance file.
