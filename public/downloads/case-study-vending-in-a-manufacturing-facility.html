2. Deployment workflow — 90-day timeline

  1. Days 0–14 — discovery + RFP

    Site survey by 2–3 operators; pedometer mapping of break-room flow; wellness committee draft planogram principles; payroll-deduct path identified (Workday / Kronos / UKG).

  2. Days 15–30 — operator selection

    Scored RFP responses against criteria below. Reference checks (3 minimum), insurance verification ($1M / $2M GL + product liability), AoC (PCI-DSS v4.0) confirmed.

  3. Days 31–60 — install + integration

    Dedicated 20A circuits run; equipment delivered + installed; cellular telemetry verified ≥ −90 dBm at all placements; badge integration tested in staging; planogram loaded.

  4. Days 60–75 — onboarding + go-live

    Workforce comms 2 weeks pre-launch; signage at break-room entry; QR-coded refund flow; soft-launch shift 1 first; full 3-shift go-live day 75.

  5. Days 75–90 — first telemetry cycle

    Per-shift velocity baseline; first planogram refresh; refund rate review; uptime report; first quarterly review scheduled day 90.

6. Retention-impact reporting framework

Vending is one of many touch-points in a retention equation. Use a directional framework — never claim a causal retention lift without controlling for other variables.

  1. Workforce pulse — pre/post

    Single pulse-survey question pre-deployment and at 90 days, 6 months, 12 months: 'Rate break-room food + drink options 1–5.' Capture department + shift. Pre-post delta is directional only.

  2. Exit-interview text mining

    Reverse-coded mentions of break-room food / drink in voluntary exit interviews. Trend over 12-month rolling window post-deployment.

  3. Internal-referral rate (if HR tracks)

    Internal-referral hires often correlate with workplace satisfaction proxies. Trend pre-post, accounting for other initiatives.

  4. Absenteeism + tardiness (caution)

    Vending availability can reduce 'left site for lunch' patterns; track minutes-on-site as a proxy. Causal attribution to vending alone is not supportable; report as directional with confounders called out.

  5. Subsidy ROI framing

    If subsidy budget is $120k/year and replacement-cost-per-turnover is $4,000–$8,000 (BLS / SHRM benchmark ranges), prevent ~30 voluntary departures to break even on subsidy. Not a guarantee; framework only.
