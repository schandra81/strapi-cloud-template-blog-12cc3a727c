Warehouse Healthy Vending Pack

Heat-illness planogram, protein-forward catalog, hot-meal vending playbook, safety + HR coordination

Healthy vending for warehouse employees is governed less by trend than by physiology: heat exposure (OSHA NEP 2022, ACGIH WBGT > 80°F), shift-3 metabolic demand, single-hand operability in PPE, and the FDA Food Code 35-40°F cold-chain. This pack codifies four artifacts — heat-illness planogram, protein-forward catalog, hot-meal playbook, safety + HR coordination — so a warehouse is equipped to run a wellness-aligned program rather than a generic snack lineup.

1. Heat-illness planogram

OSHA's 2022 National Emphasis Program on Heat Hazards focuses on indoor and outdoor facilities ≥ 80°F. ACGIH TLVs for WBGT trigger work-rest cycles. Vending supports hydration + electrolytes as part of the heat-illness prevention plan.

Hydration baseline
Plain water in multiple sizes; rotate at least 3 SKU types (still water + sparkling + flavored).
Electrolyte mix
Sports drink (Gatorade, Powerade, BodyArmor) + zero-sugar electrolyte (Liquid IV, Pedialyte, Nuun); per ACGIH guidance for ≥ 4-hour heat exposure.
Sodium-replenishment snacks
Pretzels, salted nuts, trail mix; OSHA NEP-aligned with sodium loss during sustained heat exposure.
Cold-only zone
All hydration SKUs in refrigerated unit at 35-40°F; not in ambient cabinet.
Free-vend integration
Where employer subsidizes during heat-advisory days, free-vend hydration SKUs via badge auth.

3. Hot-meal vending playbook

Hot-meal vending replaces a cafeteria meal at shift-3 and weekend shifts when food service is offline. Hot-meal equipment must meet FDA Food Code 3-501.16 (hot-hold ≥ 135°F) and ANSI/ASSE Z244.1 lockout-aware service.

  1. Equipment selection

    Smart-fridge with built-in microwave (e.g., Yo-Kai Express ramen, Aramark Avenue C hot-meal, Selecta hot kiosk); separate hot-hold compartment if used.

  2. FDA Food Code temperature

    Cold-hold 35-40°F (3-501.16); hot-hold ≥ 135°F. Temperature log with 4-hour SLA alarm.

  3. HACCP-style monitoring

    Per-SKU shelf-life tracked via telemetry; expired-SKU auto-flag.

  4. Restock cadence

    Daily restock for fresh-food SKUs; pull-and-discard at 2 days from best-by.

  5. Allergen handling

    FALCPA labels on every SKU; allergen segregation if peanut/tree-nut SKUs deployed.

  6. Cashless payment

    EMV + NFC + mobile wallet; meal subsidy via badge auth where employer covers.

4. Safety + HR coordination

Healthy vending program coordinates across safety, HR, and benefits. Each function owns a piece of the program.

EHS coordinator
Owns heat-illness planogram, hot-food cold-chain attestation, ANSI Z535 signage, lockout-aware placement.
HR / benefits
Owns wellness subsidy structure, free-vend on heat-advisory days, NPS survey, refund flow.
Operations manager
Owns shift-aware restock cadence, machine uptime, SLA enforcement.
Operator (external)
Owns planogram refresh, telemetry, compliance attestation, refund processing.
Wellness committee
Reviews program annually; aligns with broader corporate wellness goals.

5. Shift-aware planogram

Shift workers have different metabolic patterns. Shift-1 (day) skews breakfast + hydration; shift-3 (overnight) skews protein + caffeine + comfort food.

7. Annual wellness review

  • Heat-illness planogram review with EHS coordinator.
  • Protein-forward catalog review with HR + wellness committee.
  • Hot-meal equipment FDA Food Code attestation refreshed.
  • Allergen labeling refreshed; cross-contact attestation reviewed.
  • Worker NPS survey distributed; results shared with operator.
  • QBR with operator covers KPI dashboard + planogram refresh recommendations.
