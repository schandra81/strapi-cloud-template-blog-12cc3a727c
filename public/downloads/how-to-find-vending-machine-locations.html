Vending Location Acquisition Playbook

Six channels, location criteria scorecard, site survey, and scam avoidance

This playbook codifies the six acquisition channels operators use to build a 30+ machine route, the criteria that separate a profitable stop from a money loser, and the red flags that distinguish a real placement opportunity from a locator scam. Numbers are drawn from NAMA Industry FactBook reporting and Automatic Merchandiser State of the Industry data.

1. The six acquisition channels

Most operators discover that 60-70% of route growth comes from referrals and direct outreach, not paid locators. Build a balanced channel mix so no single source dictates your pipeline.

  1. Direct outreach to facility managers

    Cold-call or in-person visits to office parks (25-150 employees), light manufacturing, auto dealerships, and self-storage offices. Target stops with 40+ daily foot-traffic and no existing operator.

  2. Referrals from existing accounts

    After 90 days of clean service, ask the facility contact for a referral to a sister site, vendor in the supply chain, or peer property manager. Operators report a 35-45% close rate on warm referrals vs 3-5% on cold.

  3. Property managers + commercial brokers

    Multi-tenant office buildings and industrial parks centralize amenity decisions through the property manager. Bring a one-page capability sheet and a sample QBR report.

  4. Local trade associations

    Chamber of commerce, BOMA (Building Owners and Managers Association), IFMA (International Facility Management Association) chapter meetings, and SHRM HR roundtables surface decision-makers.

  5. Paid locators (use with caution)

    A locator typically charges $300-$1,200 per placed account. Vet aggressively (see scam section below). Useful for accelerating a new operator's first 5-10 placements; expensive for established routes.

  6. Digital lead capture + SEO

    Most facility managers Google 'vending services near [city]' before issuing an RFP. A local landing page with case studies and an RFP intake form converts at 2-4% of organic traffic.

3. Site survey checklist

  • Measured floor space — 40 inch × 36 inch minimum for a snack machine; 42 inch × 32 inch for a beverage machine. ADA 305 clear floor space 30 inch × 48 inch in front.
  • Confirmed 120V/20A dedicated circuit within 6 feet (or 240V if multi-machine bank). No shared circuits with HVAC or microwaves.
  • Cellular signal test — minimum -85 dBm on the operator's carrier; otherwise plan wired ethernet or a Wi-Fi MOU with the host.
  • Photographed install location from 3 angles plus a wide-frame of the breakroom.
  • Measured doorway widths along the delivery path — minimum 36 inch clearance, 80 inch height.
  • Verified delivery dock or front-door access hours; coordinated with facility security for badged access.
  • Confirmed sales tax jurisdiction (state + local) and any prepared-food tax applicability.
  • Confirmed COI requirements — typical hosts request $1M / $2M general liability with host as additional insured.

4. The acquisition conversation

  1. Open with capability, not product

    'We operate a vending route serving facilities like yours within 30 minutes. Telemetry-driven restocks, cashless on every machine, ADA-compliant signage, and a 24-hour service SLA.' Skip the brand pitch — the host cares about uptime and zero hassle.

  2. Surface the host's current pain

    Ask: 'How often is the current machine out of the items people actually buy?' and 'When something breaks, how long does it take?' Stockout rate >5% and service response >48 hours are the universal pain points.

  3. Propose a 60-90 day pilot

    Pilot with telemetry, weekly stockout reporting, and a planogram review at day 45. Pilot terms convert at 70-80% to multi-year MSAs because the host has data, not a sales pitch.

  4. Document host benefits in writing

    Commission structure, refund policy, planogram refresh cadence, and service SLA in the proposal — not verbal. Hosts who've been burned by prior operators need the paper trail.

5. Commission + revenue-share benchmarks

Commission norms vary by audience size and location difficulty. Higher commissions are not always best — a 25% commission on a high-volume stop nets the host more than 35% on a low-volume stop. The benchmarks below reflect Automatic Merchandiser 2024 State of the Industry reporting.

Small office (under 50 employees)0-10%Many small stops are commission-free in exchange for service guarantees.

6. Scam + bad-locator avoidance

The FTC has prosecuted multiple vending business-opportunity and locator scams under the Business Opportunity Rule (16 CFR Part 437). Red flags below correlate with the FTC and state AG settlements published since 2010.

  • Locator promises 'guaranteed' high-traffic placements but won't name specific facilities until after payment — universal scam pattern.
  • Locator collects payment up front for placements they haven't secured. Legitimate locators tie payment to confirmed placement and a host signature.
  • Equipment-plus-locations bundle from the same vendor — classic biz-op fraud structure. Buy equipment and acquire locations through separate, independent channels.
  • Locator refuses to provide an FTC-required disclosure document if total payment exceeds the threshold ($500-$1,000 depending on state). Cite 16 CFR 437.3.
  • Locator can't provide a list of past placements you can call directly. Always call 5+ references at random.
  • Promises of $X per month per machine without specifying audience size, foot-traffic, or commission structure. Real numbers come from real telemetry data.
  • Pressure to sign within 24 hours, 'limited inventory of premium territories,' or any high-pressure close. Walk away.

7. KPIs after placement

Week 1-4 baseline
Capture per-SKU velocity; establish stockout-free planogram.
Day 45 review
Planogram refresh based on actual sales. Drop bottom-quartile SKUs; expand top-quartile facings.
Day 90 health check
Uptime ≥ 98%, stockout incidents < 3 per machine, service SLA adherence ≥ 95%, refund rate < 2%.
Quarterly business review
Per-machine revenue trend, host satisfaction score, planogram changes, action items.
12-month renewal posture
Stops underperforming the criteria scorecard by month 9 should be repositioned, reformulated, or pulled before renewal.
