Industrial Facility Vending Equipment Selection Guide

Heavy-duty machine specs, site survey checklist, and service contract structure for warehouses, plants, and DCs

This guide walks plant managers, EHS leads, and procurement through the equipment selection, site survey, and service contract structure required for vending in industrial settings. Standards referenced include NEMA enclosure ratings, NFPA 70 (NEC) for electrical, OSHA 1910 for general industry, ADA Section 308 for accessible operable parts, and ANSI/NAMA Z245 conventions used by industrial operators. Capability-framed throughout — operator should attest to each specification in writing.

1. Site environment classification

Before specifying equipment, classify the placement environment. Industrial sites range from climate-controlled QC labs (light-duty machines acceptable) to unconditioned warehouse fringe (heavy-duty required) to wash-down food processing zones (sealed enclosures required).

2. Equipment specification — heavy-duty checklist

Operator should attest to each specification in writing. Reject bids that downgrade specs without explicit waiver from the facility manager.

  • Cabinet: 18-22 gauge steel cabinet with powder-coat finish ≥ 60 micron dry film thickness; reinforced corner brackets.
  • Door: gasketed perimeter seal; double-pane glass on glass-front machines; tamper-resistant T-handle lock or e-lock.
  • Operating temperature range: ambient 5-110°F; refrigeration compressor rated for high-ambient (R-290 hydrocarbon or R-448A preferred for energy).
  • Electrical: 120V/15A or 208V/20A dedicated circuit per NFPA 70 Article 422; GFCI required where within 6 ft of a sink or wash zone.
  • Payment hardware: sealed/gasketed bezel; cellular telemetry (Cantaloupe, Nayax, 365, or comparable); contactless EMV; supports Apple Pay/Google Pay.
  • Vending head: Crane National, AMS, Royal Vendors, Vendo, Dixie Narco, or comparable; industrial-grade product helix or guaranteed-delivery shelf.
  • ADA compliance: highest operable part ≤ 48 in AFF; operable parts ≤ 5 lbf; tactile/contrast pricing labels (Section 308 + 309.4).
  • Energy: ENERGY STAR rated machine where the placement is a coil/refrigerated unit; LED interior lighting; nighttime low-power mode.
  • Service access: front-load servicing where possible; no operator-side clearance > 24 in required behind the unit.

3. Site survey checklist (28-point)

The operator's site-survey lead should walk the placement with the EHS lead and document each point with photos. Survey output becomes an appendix to the operating contract.

  • Floor: concrete with ≥ 3,000 psi compressive strength; level within ¼ inch over 4 ft span; no expansion joint within machine footprint.
  • Floor load: machine + product load (typical 1,000-1,400 lb loaded) within posted floor capacity.
  • Electrical: dedicated 120V/15A or 208V/20A circuit ≤ 6 ft from placement; GFCI per NEC where required; no shared circuit with high-draw equipment.
  • Telemetry: cellular signal ≥ -100 dBm (verify with operator's device) or hardwired Ethernet drop available.
  • Clearances: 4 in side clearance for ventilation; 6 in rear clearance; full door swing arc unobstructed.
  • Egress: machine does not narrow corridor below OSHA 1910.37 minimum (28 in clear for ≤ 50 occupants, 44 in for > 50).
  • Fire suppression: machine not blocking sprinkler head coverage per NFPA 13 (typical 18 in clearance below deflector).
  • Lockout/tagout: operator's restock SOP includes LOTO verification at facilities with active LOTO program (OSHA 1910.147).
  • Forklift traffic: machine protected by bollards (4 in OD steel, concrete-filled) where within 10 ft of forklift travel path.
  • PPE zone: machine outside any zone requiring eye protection, hearing protection, or hard hat per facility EHS map; or signage covers requirement.
  • Wash-down: in wet processing zones, machine specified NEMA 4X with documented IP66 rating.
  • Temperature: ambient measured at placement during shift; verified within machine's operating range.
  • Humidity: documented during shift; verified within machine's operating range (typical ≤ 85% RH).
  • Dust: airborne particulate level documented; filter/sealing requirements specified where elevated.
  • Vibration: machine not placed within 10 ft of stamping press, hammer mill, or other high-vibration equipment without isolation pads.
  • Access hours: shift schedule documented; 24/7 access if any shift operates around the clock.
  • Restroom proximity: within 200 ft per typical industrial breakroom planning guidance.
  • Lighting: ≥ 30 footcandles at machine face during all operating shifts (IES guidance for circulation areas).
  • Allergen + nutritional labeling: per FALCPA + FDA menu labeling where applicable; signage visible from clear floor space.
  • Cold chain: refrigerated unit holds ≤ 40°F per FDA Food Code 3-501.16; operator attests with internal-temp telemetry log.
  • Cleaning + sanitation: weekly cleaning included in service contract; sanitation log retained ≥ 1 year.
  • Pest control: machine integrated into facility IPM program; sealed gaskets verified at install.
  • Security: camera coverage from facility surveillance; or operator-supplied camera with retention ≥ 30 days.
  • Signage: emergency contact, refund instructions, allergen summary, calorie display per FDA menu labeling if > 20 placements.
  • Insurance: operator's COI on file; additional-insured endorsement; product liability ≥ $1M.
  • Permits: state weights-and-measures registration where required; local health permit for prepared food where applicable.
  • Removal SOP: contract specifies machine removal procedure at end of agreement (notice period, restoration of placement area).
  • Photo documentation: 8+ site photos retained in the procurement file.

4. Service contract structure

Industrial vending contracts diverge from office contracts on cadence, SLA, and revenue share. Use the structure below as a starting frame for the RFP and the final agreement.

5. Operator capability rubric (score 1-5)

Heavy-duty equipment fleet
Operator should provide model list with NEMA ratings and operating temperature range; reject if standard commercial fleet only.
Industrial reference accounts
3+ active industrial placements ≥ 12 months in service; reference call with on-site EHS or plant manager required.
Telemetry + uptime track record
Operator provides 90-day uptime report from comparable industrial accounts (anonymized); should show ≥ 98%.
Service technician coverage
Operator has dispatchable technician within 1-hour drive of placement during all shifts; back-up arrangement for off-hours coverage.
Wash-down + harsh-environment experience
Where applicable, operator provides photos and references from food-processing or wash-down placements.
Cold chain compliance
Refrigerated units include internal-temp telemetry with FDA Food Code-aligned alerts; operator retains logs ≥ 1 year.
Payment compliance
PCI-DSS 4.0 AOC and SAQ-B-IP on file; contactless EMV; mobile-wallet support; no PAN storage at the cooler.

6. RFP template — section headings

  1. Scope + placement summary

    Facility profile, headcount per shift, square footage, placement count, zone classifications (per Section 1 of this guide).

  2. Equipment specification requirements

    Reference Section 2 specs as the minimum standard; operator marks comply / not-comply / proposes alternate with justification.

  3. Service + restock requirements

    Reference Section 4 SLA + cadence; require operator to attest in writing.

  4. Planogram framework

    Initial planogram proposal with allergen + healthier-option mix (per facility wellness policy); price ceilings noted.

  5. Commission / pricing model

    Operator proposes subsidy or revenue-share model; total cost to facility documented.

  6. Implementation + go-live plan

    T-30 / T-21 / T-14 / T-7 / T-0 milestones with owner accountability.

  7. References + capability evidence

    3+ industrial references; sample uptime report; sample QBR deck.
