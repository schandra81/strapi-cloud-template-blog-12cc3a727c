Vending Service-Call SLA Pack

Four-tier SLA, refund standards, telemetry spec, credit mechanism, monthly scorecard

Service responsiveness is the single most-cited host complaint in NAMA's annual customer survey. This pack codifies a four-tier SLA framework, with telemetry, refunds, and a monthly scorecard a capable operator can attest to in writing.

1. Four-tier SLA framework

Not every service event is the same urgency. The four-tier framework matches response time to business impact, keeping operator service cost sustainable while protecting host experience.

TierExamplesAcknowledge SLAResolve SLA
Tier 1 — CriticalMachine offline, cold-chain excursion, payment system down, security incident< 1 hour< 24 hours
Tier 2 — Stockout / Hot SKUTop-10 SKU sold out, planogram outage< 4 hours< 48 hours
Tier 3 — StandardSingle SKU stockout, slot jam, planogram refresh request< 24 hours< 5 business days
Tier 4 — CosmeticSignage refresh, wrap touch-up, cabinet cleaning< 5 business days< 14 business days

2. Telemetry spec — what enables the SLA

Without telemetry, an operator cannot meet a < 1-hour acknowledge SLA. The telemetry baseline below is non-negotiable for a Tier 1 program.

Hardware
Cellular modem (LTE Cat-M1 or NB-IoT) on every machine. Cantaloupe Seed, Nayax VPOS Touch, 365 Retail Markets ePort, AWM Smart Shelf.
Heartbeat interval
≤ 15 minutes. Sub-5-minute for cold-chain placements.
Monitored events
Online/offline, payment-system status, internal temperature (refrigerated), door-open, compressor short-cycle, bill-validator jam, motor stall.
Alert routing
Tier 1 events → operator on-call SMS + email + dashboard. Tier 2-4 events → ticketing queue with auto-priority.
Host visibility
Read-only dashboard or weekly digest with per-machine uptime, last-restock, ticket status.

3. Refund standards

Consumer refund flow is part of the SLA. Slow refunds undermine member trust and generate disproportionate complaints to host facilities team.

  1. Cashless refund

    Self-service via on-machine touchpad, operator app, or QR-code-to-refund. Auto-credit to original payment method within 24 hours of trigger.

  2. Cash refund

    Member submits refund slip with name + transaction time. Credit on next route visit (≤ 7 days) or check / Venmo dispatch (≤ 14 days).

  3. Refund audit

    Monthly refund report to host with reason codes (no-vend, stockout, jam, cold-chain, expired). Refund rate > 2% of transactions triggers planogram + machine quality review.

  4. Member transparency

    Refund-flow instructions on machine face, in member app, and in host onboarding email — three touchpoints minimum.

5. Monthly service scorecard template

Operator delivers this scorecard by 10th business day of the following month. Each metric is telemetry-backed.

Uptime %
Per machine, per month. Target ≥ 98% for snack/beverage; ≥ 99% for fresh/cold-chain.
Ticket counts by tier
Total tickets, with mean and 90th-percentile resolve time per tier.
SLA adherence %
% of tickets that met acknowledge SLA + % that met resolve SLA, separately.
Refund volume + reason codes
Total refunds, % of transactions, top reason.
Stockout incidents
Per machine, per SKU. Time-to-restock from telemetry alert.
Cold-chain excursions
Count, duration, root-cause, product-loss disposition.
Service-credit summary
Credits earned by host under section 4 clauses.

6. Site-side preparation that improves SLA outcomes

  • Service-access clearance documented at install (36 in front, 6 in sides + rear).
  • Cellular signal verified > -95 dBm at install; fallback Wi-Fi credentialed if signal marginal.
  • Single point of contact (POC) at host for after-hours access — name, mobile, badge access procedure.
  • Power circuit dedicated, surge-protected, with breaker labeled.
  • Site listed in operator dispatch system with directions, parking, loading-dock notes.
  • Spare key or access code stored with operator dispatch (with host facilities approval).

7. Contract clauses to require

  • Four-tier SLA defined as Section 1 above, with telemetry-backed evidence.
  • Monthly service scorecard delivered by 10th business day, signed.
  • Service-credit schedule per Section 4, with cap at 25% of month's net revenue.
  • Refund SLA — 24-hour cashless / 7-day cash — with audit log.
  • 30-day cure period before termination-for-cause on SLA breach pattern (3+ months below 90% adherence).
  • Audit rights — host can pull dispatch records and reconcile against scorecard quarterly.
