Gym Vending Installation Pack

RFP template, reference-check questions, healthy framework spec, contract terms

Adding vending to a gym is a 60-120 day process when run correctly. This pack walks through the four documents that drive the result — RFP, reference-check script, healthy-framework spec, and contract term sheet.

1. Scoping the placement — five questions before the RFP goes out

Operators bid the way the host scopes. Tighten these five answers first so the bids are comparable.

  1. Footprint + power

    Wall footprint, dedicated 120V/20A circuit availability, ceiling height ≥ 84 inches, ADA clear floor space (30 in × 48 in) available.

  2. Member count + peak

    Active members, peak-hour concurrent, class mix (drives recovery demand). Operators bid differently at 400 vs 1,200 members.

  3. Mix — beverage / snack / recovery / fresh

    Specify whether the placement is single combo, snack + beverage pair, or beverage + snack + recovery cooler trio.

  4. Cashless-only or cash + cashless

    Cashless-only reduces validator service calls 80% (NAMA 2024 study) but excludes members who pay cash.

  5. Branding requirements

    Standard operator wrap, custom-wrap, or full five-layer custom brand stack.

2. RFP template — what every operator must respond to

A consistent RFP makes responses comparable. Require operators to answer in the same format.

  • Company profile: years in business, fleet size, geographic coverage, references.
  • Equipment spec: make + model + ENERGY STAR 4.0 attestation + AHRI 810-2016 / SNAP refrigerant attestation per unit.
  • Telemetry stack: vendor, heartbeat interval, dashboard demo access.
  • Payment stack: PCI-DSS attestation, supported tenders (chip, contactless, Apple/Google Pay, RFID fob).
  • Planogram proposal: SKU list with per-SKU FALCPA allergen labels, ENERGY STAR + recyclable packaging where applicable.
  • Commission schedule: per-category commission %, payment cadence (monthly or quarterly), audit access.
  • Service SLA: Tier 1 / 2 / 3 definitions, acknowledgement + resolution targets.
  • Maintenance: refrigeration service schedule, condenser-coil cleaning cadence.
  • Insurance: GL, auto, workers comp limits with sample COI naming the gym as additional insured.
  • Term + termination: contract length, renewal terms, termination for cause / convenience, equipment removal.

3. Reference-check questions

Operators provide references; the host must call them. Use this script — 10 minutes per reference.

  1. Length of relationship + scale

    How long? How many machines? Single or multi-site?

  2. On-time service performance

    What is the actual restock-on-time rate? What is the service-ticket resolution time?

  3. Stockout frequency

    How often does a featured SKU stock out before the next restock?

  4. Commission accuracy + audit

    Do commission reports tie to telemetry? Have you ever found a discrepancy?

  5. Refund process

    How does the operator handle member refunds? How long does it take?

  6. Equipment quality + reliability

    How often do machines go offline? How fast does the operator respond?

  7. Account management

    Do you have a named account manager? How responsive?

  8. Contract experience

    Any surprise fees? Anything you wish you had negotiated differently?

  9. Would you renew?

    Open-ended; tells you more than the prior 8 combined.

5. Contract term sheet — the 12 clauses that matter

  1. Term

    1-3 years; longer for capex contribution. Auto-renew with 60-day notice window for non-renewal.

  2. Commission schedule

    Per-category %, payment cadence, audit access.

  3. Sales tax handling

    Whether commission is gross-of-tax or net-of-tax; specify state collection responsibility.

  4. SLA

    Tier 1 / 2 / 3 definitions with acknowledgement + resolution targets and credit / penalty if missed.

  5. Planogram approval

    Quarterly review with gym sign-off on SKU adds / changes.

  6. Healthy framework adherence

    Specific framework + minimum % compliance; right to terminate for sustained non-compliance.

  7. Equipment ownership + service

    Operator owns; warranty obligations; replacement on failure.

  8. Refund policy

    Member refund SLA; refund reporting to gym.

  9. Insurance

    GL $1M/$2M, auto $1M, WC statutory; certificate names gym as additional insured.

  10. Indemnification + limit of liability

    Mutual indemnification; LoL capped at greater of $X or 12 months commission.

  11. Termination

    Termination for cause (30-day cure) and for convenience (60-90 day notice).

  12. Data ownership

    Gym owns the transaction + member-tier data passed via integration; operator owns the operations data; both have access during the term.

7. Soft-launch checklist

  • Day 1: telemetry online, planogram loaded, signage + shelf talkers in place, refund flow tested with a $1 test purchase.
  • Week 1: front-desk script briefed; member feedback channel open; first telemetry summary reviewed.
  • Day 14: first commission report reviewed; reconciliation against telemetry.
  • Day 30: planogram tune based on telemetry velocity; healthy-framework compliance audit.
  • Day 60: first QBR with operator; SLA performance against targets; member NPS micro-survey.
  • Day 90: second planogram tune; consider expansion if performance hits targets.
