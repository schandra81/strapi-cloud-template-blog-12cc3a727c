Federal Wellness Vending Implementation Toolkit

Planogram template, subsidy structure, outcome measurement framework, and HHS-aligned audit checklist

This toolkit gives federal agency wellness leads, contracting officers, and facility coordinators the working documents to launch a wellness-vending program aligned with the HHS/CDC Food Service Guidelines for Federal Facilities. References pulled from HHS/GSA/DOD/USDA food service guidelines, FDA menu labeling (21 CFR 101.11), FALCPA, ADA Section 308, PCI-DSS 4.0, and CDC point-of-decision prompt guidance. Capability-framed throughout.

1. Wellness planogram template

2. Subsidy structure options

Federal placements typically apply one of three subsidy structures to encourage healthier-option purchase. The wellness office should document the chosen structure in the contract Schedule B and reconcile quarterly.

  1. Structure A — Price parity subsidy

    Agency wellness fund subsidizes BFY SKUs so the posted price is ≤ conventional comparator. Operator invoices monthly for the subsidy amount × units sold. Typical subsidy: $0.25-$0.75 per BFY transaction.

  2. Structure B — Free-first-purchase + recurring promo

    Agency funds a free-first-purchase code at launch and a recurring quarterly promo (e.g., 'BFY Friday' with $0.50 off). Operator tracks promo redemption and invoices the wellness fund.

  3. Structure C — Commission reinvestment

    Agency directs operator's commission share back into BFY product subsidy, signage, and point-of-decision prompts. Net cost to agency is zero; trade-off is no commission to the agency general fund.

3. Outcome measurement framework

FSG implementation should be measured against baseline. The framework below uses CDC-published evaluation approaches.

Metric 1 — BFY share of total transactions
Calculate monthly: (BFY transactions / total transactions). Target ≥ 50% by month 6; ≥ 60% by month 12. Source: operator telemetry.
Metric 2 — BFY share of facings
Audit at quarterly QBR: count BFY facings vs total. Target ≥ 50% per FSG; recommended 60%+.
Metric 3 — Average sodium per snack transaction
Calculate monthly: (sum of sodium per SKU × units sold) / total snack transactions. Target ≤ 200 mg average by month 12.
Metric 4 — Calorie-display compliance
Audit quarterly: 100% of SKUs display calorie information at the point of selection per FDA menu labeling rule (where operator has ≥ 20 placements).
Metric 5 — Employee satisfaction
Quarterly 5-question pulse: satisfaction (1-5), BFY availability (1-5), pricing (1-5), variety (1-5), open suggestion. Target ≥ 4.0 average by month 12.
Metric 6 — Refund rate
Operator refund report: < 2% of transactions. Spike > 2% triggers planogram or machine quality review.
Metric 7 — Uptime
Operator telemetry: ≥ 98% monthly. Federal placements typically include 24/7 access; off-hours uptime measured separately.

4. Point-of-decision prompt design

CDC and FSG guidance show that point-of-decision prompts increase BFY purchase by 5-15%. Use the prompt design framework below.

  • Shelf labels: green dot or 'Best Choice' icon at each FSG-aligned SKU; standardized across all machines.
  • Machine-face signage: 11x17 print with 'How to spot a Best Choice' graphic; mounted at adult eye level (60-64 in).
  • Calorie + sodium display: directly under each SKU's price; consistent format across all machines per FDA menu labeling.
  • Allergen summary: separate sign listing common allergens by SKU number per FALCPA.
  • Hydration prompt: 'Choose water — it's free at the fountain' sign near beverage machine; encourages no-cost choice.
  • Wellness program tie-in: QR code linking to agency wellness portal (where applicable) at each machine.

5. FSG audit checklist (annual)

  • BFY facings ≥ 50% (snack) and ≥ 50% (beverage) — count and photograph each machine
  • BFY SKUs meet thresholds: ≤ 200 cal, ≤ 230 mg sodium, ≤ 10 g added sugar (snack); ≤ 40 cal per 8 oz (beverage)
  • Calorie display visible at point of selection (per FDA menu labeling)
  • Allergen labeling per FALCPA on each SKU
  • ADA Section 308: operable parts 15-48 in AFF; ≤ 5 lbf operating force
  • Point-of-decision prompts present and standardized
  • Refrigerated units hold ≤ 40°F (FDA Food Code 3-501.16); telemetry log on file
  • Operator PCI-DSS 4.0 AOC / SAQ-B-IP current
  • Operator insurance COI current ($2M GL, $1M umbrella, additional-insured endorsement)
  • Quarterly QBR records retained in contract file (last 4 quarters)
  • Outcome metrics report from operator telemetry retained in wellness office file

6. Implementation timeline

  1. T-90: Wellness office + contracting officer scoping

    Confirm placement count, baseline planogram, agency wellness fund availability, subsidy structure choice.

  2. T-60: Operator coordination

    Operator submits FSG-aligned planogram proposal; wellness office reviews and approves; agency contracting officer confirms commission/subsidy schedule.

  3. T-30: Signage + POS prompts design

    Design + print machine-face signage, shelf labels, allergen summary. Wellness office reviews and approves.

  4. T-14: Calibration + photo documentation

    Operator deploys initial planogram and signage; wellness office walks each machine and documents with photos.

  5. T-0: Launch

    Free-first-purchase code active for 30 days; agency-wide email announcement; wellness portal QR codes live.

  6. T+30 / +60 / +90: Calibration

    Telemetry-driven planogram tune-up at 30/60/90 days; drop bottom-5 BFY SKUs that aren't moving; substitute with 5 new candidates.

  7. T+180: First FSG outcome report

    Wellness office presents 6-month outcome metrics to leadership; documents pivots for year 2.
