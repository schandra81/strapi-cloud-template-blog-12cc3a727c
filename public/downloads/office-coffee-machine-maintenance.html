Office Coffee Machine Maintenance Pack

Daily checklist, OCS contract scope, descaling cadence, water filtration spec, and quarterly PM documentation

This pack consolidates the maintenance practices that keep an office coffee program running at 98%+ uptime over a 5-7 year equipment life. It separates host-side daily tasks from operator-side preventive maintenance, gives water hardness and descaling math, and supplies the documentation an operator should attest to at every quarterly business review.

1. Daily host-side checklist (5 minutes)

These items take a designated workplace-experience or facilities steward 5 minutes per machine. Operator-side daily tasks are separate and listed in section 3.

  • Wipe drip tray + machine exterior with food-safe sanitizer (no abrasive cleaners)
  • Empty grounds bin if visible through transparent door (most superautomatics hold 60-80 pucks)
  • Top off bean hopper to 60-80% full (avoids stale beans at top + low-bean errors)
  • Top off cup + lid + condiment supply
  • Verify milk fridge temperature ≤ 40°F (digital thermometer log)
  • Run on-machine rinse cycle (typically a single-button prompt on Franke / Schaerer / WMF)
  • Log any error code or unusual behavior in shared service log (Slack channel, ticket system, or paper log)

2. Weekly host-side checklist (15 minutes)

  • Deep-clean milk system per manufacturer cycle (Franke A300/A600: programmed Hyclean cycle, 8-12 min)
  • Wipe and sanitize all touch surfaces (selection panel, dispense spouts, drip tray)
  • Empty + sanitize grounds bin + drip tray separately
  • Inspect water filter indicator (most cartridges have a date sticker or telemetry-reported gallon count)
  • Run on-machine descale-check cycle (machine will flag if descale is due)
  • Photograph or document any cosmetic damage and report to operator

4. Water filtration spec + hardness math

Water is the #1 cause of premature failure on bean-to-cup machines. Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) water standard targets: TDS 75-250 mg/L, hardness 17-85 mg/L as CaCO3 (1-5 grains), alkalinity 40-75 mg/L, chloride < 10 mg/L. Most US tap water exceeds hardness — filtration is mandatory.

Filter type
Carbon block + ion-exchange softener (BWT Bestmax Premium, Pentair Everpure 4FC-S, BUNN EQHP-25). Reverse osmosis with remineralization for premium tier or hardness > 250 ppm.
Sizing
Match filter to weekly volume. Bestmax Premium V: ~3,000 L (~12,000 cups) per cartridge. Replace at 6 months or volume threshold, whichever first.
Hardness measurement
Test inlet water quarterly with reagent strips (Hach 5-in-1) or digital meter (HM Digital TDS-EZ). Document on PM report.
Scale indicator
If descale alert fires more than once per 8 weeks at expected volume, filter is undersized or expired. Resize or replace.
Backflow + plumbing
Dedicated 1/4 in cold water line with shut-off valve + pressure regulator (40-80 psi) + backflow preventer per local plumbing code.

6. Equipment cycle counts + replacement triggers

Brew unit (bean-to-cup)
Replace at 30,000-50,000 cycles per manufacturer (Franke A300 ~ 40,000; Schaerer ~ 50,000). Telemetry tracks exact count.
Group head gaskets
Replace every 6-12 months or 15,000 shots, whichever first.
Grinder burrs
Steel burrs: 600-800 kg of beans. Ceramic burrs: 1,200-1,500 kg. Telemetry-tracked or weighed at PM.
Boiler descale chemistry
Manufacturer-approved descaler only (Cafiza / Urnex Dezcal / Franke-supplied). Citric acid 5-10% solution for stainless boilers; never vinegar (acetic acid pits brass + copper components).
End-of-life decision
Refurb economic when total ownership cost (refurb + 1 yr PM) < 50% of new equivalent. Most fleets refurb at year 5 and replace at year 7-8.

7. Service SLA tiers

  1. Tier 1 — machine offline, payment failure, leak

    <4 hour acknowledgement, <24 hour resolution. Loaner machine within 24 hours for primary brewer if not resolvable on-site.

  2. Tier 2 — single-drink failure, descale-required, dispense error

    Same-day acknowledgement, <48 hour resolution at next restock.

  3. Tier 3 — cosmetic, planogram, refresh request

    5-day acknowledgement, 14-day resolution.

8. Quarterly business review (QBR) documentation

Operator brings the following to every quarterly review. Host facilities + workplace-experience steward signs off.

  • Uptime report per machine per month over the quarter (telemetry-verified)
  • PM completion log with technician name, date, parts replaced
  • Water filter replacement receipts + descale completion certificates
  • Cycle count per brew unit + grinder + projected replacement window
  • Service ticket log with SLA adherence (acknowledged / resolved within tier)
  • Cost-per-cup actuals vs. bid; commodity pass-through reconciliation
  • Member feedback + satisfaction (NPS / smile-rating) trend
  • Action items + owner + due date for next quarter
This pack is operator-neutral. Use it to align internal facilities staff and as the documentation backbone for your operator's PM scope.
