Bean-to-Cup Office Coffee Playbook

Tier selection, supply quality, milk-system discipline, and operator SLA

Bean-to-cup machines (B2C) replace the office's pod-coffee or batch-brew with on-demand grind-and-brew espresso, drip, and milk-based drinks. They lift coffee NPS more than any other breakroom change at offices over 50 employees — when the four operational levers in this playbook are managed. This playbook codifies tier selection (commercial-grade vs. semi-commercial vs. office-prosumer), supply quality (bean grade + freshness), milk-system discipline (the #1 source of B2C complaints), and operator SLA. It maps to NSF 4 / NSF 169 sanitation standards, FDA food-contact equipment standards (21 CFR 175-178), and standard ADA Section 309.4 operating force.

1. Machine-tier selection

Three tiers cover virtually all office placements. Sizing is set by peak-hour drink count, not headcount. Plan for 1.2-1.6 drinks per employee per workday at full in-office occupancy.

2. Bean supply + freshness

  • Specialty-grade bean (SCA score ≥ 80 on the published Coffee Review scale) where possible; commercial-grade premium otherwise. Avoid blends with > 30% robusta unless explicitly chosen for body.
  • Roast date stamped on each bag; bag opened within 30 days of roast and consumed within 14 days of opening.
  • Bean hopper kept at 50-70% capacity (not full — older beans cycle to top). Topped up daily, not weekly.
  • Decaf hopper (where dual-hopper machine in use) supplied with comparable-quality decaf; rotation matches caffeinated consumption rate.
  • Operator publishes the named-origin or named-blend on the bean bag; not 'office blend' opaque label.
  • Annual bean review at QBR — taste, freshness, and supplier roast-date consistency.

3. Milk-system discipline — the #1 source of complaints

Milk-based drinks (latte, cappuccino, flat white) account for 35-50% of B2C volume at most offices. Milk-system failures (foam quality drop, off-taste, dispense errors) drive 60-70% of all B2C service tickets.

  1. Fresh-milk vs. powder

    Fresh-milk machines deliver materially better drink quality but require daily cleaning + refrigerated bulk supply. Powder-based machines are easier to maintain and acceptable at < 50-employee placements. Hybrid (fresh + powder) available in commercial-grade.

  2. Daily cleaning cycle

    Fresh-milk machines require automated rinse cycle multiple times per day and a full clean cycle daily. Operator field rep or designated host staff confirms cycle completion. Skipped cleaning surfaces as off-taste within 24 hours.

  3. Weekly deep clean

    Full disassembly + sanitize of milk frother / steam wand / lines. Operator field visit. NSF 4 / NSF 169 sanitation alignment.

  4. Refrigeration discipline

    Bulk milk container kept at 35-40°F continuously; temperature logged. Excursion outside range triggers same-day milk swap.

  5. Alt-milk support

    Oat, almond, soy support varies by machine. Confirm at tier selection; expect to add a secondary milk hopper or counter-side carafe for alt-milks at commercial-grade.

  6. Plant-based / allergen labeling

    Allergen signage on each drink type (dairy in lattes, soy in alt-milks, tree nuts in almond milk). FALCPA-aligned.

4. Water + descaling discipline

  • Filtered water supply — operator-supplied filter (3M, Everpure, BWT) sized to machine + water hardness; replacement cadence per spec (typically 6-12 months).
  • Water hardness tested at install + annually. Hard-water sites require additional descaling cadence or softener.
  • Automated descale alert on machine respected by operator field rep; descale completed within SLA.
  • Daily auto-rinse cycle confirmed; operator field rep flags any disabled or skipped cycle.
  • Drainage line clean + free-flowing; daily drip-tray empty + sanitize.

6. Pricing + subsidy posture

Office subsidy (most common)
Host absorbs full cost; drinks free to employees. Most common at 50-500 employee offices. Per-employee cost: 8-25 USD/month all-in at semi-commercial tier.
Subsidized pricing
Host pays operator + per-drink small charge to employees (0.25-0.75 USD). Maintains demand discipline; reduces waste.
Cost-recovery pricing
Drink priced at full cost (1.00-2.00 USD). Used where the breakroom serves contract / visitor traffic alongside employees.
Tier pricing
Free for employees; charged to visitors / contractors via badge / app rail. Requires identity integration.

7. Quarterly business review

  • Drink-count per machine per month over the quarter; peak-hour throughput vs. spec.
  • Drink-mix — espresso vs. drip vs. milk-based vs. specialty. Inform bean + milk supply forecasting.
  • Service-ticket volume by tier; SLA adherence %.
  • Milk-system cleaning compliance — daily auto-rinse + weekly deep-clean log.
  • Bean freshness audit — sampled bags' roast date vs. consumption date.
  • Water filter + descale log review.
  • Member NPS + feedback themes.
  • Recommendations — supply changes, planogram adjustments, equipment changes.

8. Pre-signing red flags

  • Operator declines to publish named bean origin / roast date.
  • No documented milk-system cleaning cadence or compliance log.
  • SLA proposed without per-tier acknowledgment + resolution targets.
  • Water filter program absent or 'as needed' rather than scheduled.
  • Single-tier machine offered regardless of office headcount.
  • No QBR cadence — program drifts on supply quality within 2 quarters.
This playbook is informational. Equipment selection benefits from a 90-day pilot at the proposed machine tier with a sample of the actual office drink mix.
