High-Traffic Airport Vending Pack

99.5% uptime, multi-lingual / multi-currency, hardened security, concession compliance

Airport vending operates under concession compliance (ACI-NA, ACDBE), TSA secured-area constraints, and 99.5%+ uptime expectations. This pack codifies the equipment, payment, telemetry, and compliance spec a capable operator can attest to.

1. Uptime + service architecture

Airport hosts measure machine uptime against published 99.5% concession-standard SLAs. The architecture below makes that achievable.

Telemetry
Sub-5-minute heartbeat (Cantaloupe Seed Pro, Nayax VPOS Touch, 365 Retail ePort 6). Dual-carrier cellular (LTE Cat-M1 primary, fallback) at airport placements.
On-call coverage
24/7 operator on-call with badged-staff dispatch authority for secure-area access; 90-minute on-site SLA airside, 60-minute SLA landside.
Mean time to repair
Tier 1 events resolved < 4 hours airside (badge + escort cycle); < 2 hours landside.
Restock cadence
Daily restock at high-traffic placements (>200 transactions/day); 2x daily at concourse anchors.

2. Multi-lingual + multi-currency UI

International gateway terminals require multilingual interfaces. Payment platforms (Nayax, Cantaloupe ePort Connect) support each capability natively.

Languages
Minimum English + Spanish at all US placements. International gateway add: Mandarin, Japanese, Korean, Arabic, French, German, Portuguese based on terminal demographics.
Display fallback
Icon-based UI with universal symbols (cup, plug, allergen) as backup to text.
Currencies accepted
USD plus contactless EMV (Visa, Mastercard, Amex, JCB, UnionPay, Discover, Diners). Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay, WeChat Pay, Alipay.
Foreign-card surcharge
Operator absorbs international interchange (~2.4-3.0%) into pricing, not surcharge-on-machine (concession contract typically prohibits surcharging).
Receipt
Email or SMS receipt option for travelers needing expense reimbursement; printed-receipt option where concession requires.

3. Hardened security spec

Airport placements face higher-than-average tamper, vandalism, and theft attempts. Equipment hardening reduces operator service cost and concession-breach risk.

Cabinet
Steel cabinet 14-gauge minimum; high-security T-handle lock (Medeco, Abloy) or electronic lock (Sargent Greenleaf). Anti-fishing device on coin/bill validator.
Glass
Tempered laminated glass (ANSI Z97.1) on glass-front coolers; impact-rated polycarbonate for high-risk concourse zones.
Bill validator
MEI VN-2000 / Cashcode SM with anti-stringing + anti-bleach detection; cassette auto-lock on tamper.
Anchoring
Floor-anchor bolts (3/8 in) into concrete; or J-bolt installer for unitized counter installs.
CCTV integration
Camera coverage from airport CCTV system documented in operator placement file; incident-response process tied to airport ops center.

4. Concession compliance — what operators must hold

  1. Concession agreement

    Operator party to airport concession agreement (or sub-concessionaire) with MAG / percentage rent structure documented.

  2. ACDBE participation

    Where airport receives federal funding (FAA AIP), Airport Concession Disadvantaged Business Enterprise plan applies per 49 CFR Part 23. Operator's ACDBE participation rate documented.

  3. TSA badging

    All field-service staff hold airport SIDA / AOA badge per TSA regulation 49 CFR 1542; fingerprint + STA clearance current.

  4. Insurance

    GL $5M, auto $2M, products $2M, workers comp per jurisdiction. Airport additional insured.

  5. Concession fees

    MAG (minimum annual guarantee) + percentage rent structure; settlement monthly with audited gross sales.

  6. Buy-local + healthy-vending mandates

    Some airports (e.g., LAX, SFO, JFK) mandate local product or healthy-vending percentages. Operator's planogram compliant + reportable.

7. Reporting + governance

Airport concession contracts require granular reporting. Capable operators automate via payment platform.

  • Daily transaction file (TSV / CSV / API) — transaction ID, machine ID, SKU, qty, gross sales, payment method, currency.
  • Monthly gross sales statement + percentage-rent calculation + remittance.
  • Monthly uptime report per machine; airport-defined SLA adherence %.
  • Quarterly concession review — sales trend, top SKUs, capex requests, concession-fee reconciliation.
  • Annual ACDBE participation report (where applicable).
  • Annual operator audit / inspection by airport concession office.
