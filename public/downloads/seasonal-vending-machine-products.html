Seasonal Vending Rotation Playbook

Four rotations, holiday overlays, telemetry-driven iteration

Operators that hold a static planogram for 12 months leave 8-15% of potential revenue on the table. This playbook codifies the four-rotation calendar, the holiday overlays that sit on top, and the telemetry-driven iteration discipline that keeps every rotation tuning toward sell-through, not preference.

2. Holiday + event overlays

On top of the four rotations, holiday + event overlays drive 2-6 week sub-cycles. Operator pre-positions for each overlay 4-6 weeks before the window opens.

  • Valentine's Day (Feb 7-14) — heart-themed chocolate + premium confection facings expanded
  • St. Patrick's Day (Mar 14-17) — green-themed + Irish-themed limited SKUs in 1-2 facings
  • Easter (week before through Easter Sunday) — Reese's egg, Cadbury egg, Peeps + spring confection
  • Cinco de Mayo (May 1-5) — Takis + spicy snack + Mexican-brand bump where regional fit
  • Memorial Day → July 4 → Labor Day — summer outdoor + hydration + meal-replacement
  • Back-to-school (mid-Aug through Sep 15) — lunchbox-format snack + breakfast bars
  • Halloween (Oct 25-31) — Halloween candy + spooky-flavor LTOs in 2-3 facings
  • Thanksgiving week + Christmas + New Year — comfort + holiday-baking flavor expansion

3. Planogram lever — flavor + format LTOs

Limited-time offers (LTOs) from the major snack + beverage brands (Frito-Lay, Mondelez, Hershey, Coca-Cola, Pepsi) drive seasonal velocity. Operator plans LTO allocation 8-12 weeks in advance against published manufacturer LTO calendars.

  1. LTO calendar import

    Operator pulls Frito-Lay + Mondelez + Hershey + Coca-Cola + Pepsi LTO calendars from distributor at start of each quarter; allocates LTO SKUs to 10-15% of facings during launch windows.

  2. LTO velocity test

    Each LTO tested on 20-30% of machines first 2-3 weeks; sell-through > 60% of facings/week earns expansion network-wide; under 30% returns to standard.

  3. LTO carry-over decision

    Top-performing LTOs from prior year (e.g., Pumpkin Spice variants from major brands) re-planned for matching window the following year; pattern repeats.

  4. Regional + local LTOs

    Regional brands' LTOs (Goldfish flavor, Takis flavor, regional beverage) added in 1-2 facings where local familiarity is established.

4. Telemetry-driven iteration

Telemetry (Cantaloupe Seed, Nayax, 365 Retail Markets, Crane Streamware) replaces planogram intuition with weekly sell-through data. Bottom-decile SKU rotation each quarter is the core discipline.

Weekly sell-through report
Per-machine + per-SKU sell-through report on Monday; bottom-10% SKUs flagged for action. Operator reviews + decides retain / swap / remove.
Stockout root-cause
Stockouts > 4 hr broken into (a) under-faced (planogram fix), (b) restock cadence (route fix), (c) supply (procurement fix). Telemetry differentiates.
Seasonality detection
Year-over-year velocity comparison detects emerging seasonality patterns; operator updates rotation plan for next year's matching window.
A/B planogram test
Where placement count permits, test rotation hypotheses on subset of machines (15-20%) before network rollout; statistically meaningful with 4+ weeks data.

5. Operational support structure

Restock cadence
Telemetry-driven, no fixed days. Pre-rotation restock window 1-2 weeks before each rotation launch to ensure new SKUs in place.
Service SLA — Tier 1
Offline, cold-chain, payment — <24 hr ack + <24 hr resolution.
Service SLA — Tier 2
Stockout + planogram refresh — 24-48 hr ack, 5-7 day resolution. Tightens to 24-72 hr during rotation launch windows.
Rotation roadmap
Annual rotation roadmap presented to host at start of fiscal year; quarterly business review covers prior rotation performance + upcoming rotation prep.

6. Compliance + labeling overlay

  • FDA 21 CFR §101.8 — calorie disclosure updated for every new SKU within 30 days of rotation launch
  • FALCPA — allergen disclosure updated for every new SKU; pre-launch QA on allergen labels before rotation goes live
  • USDA Smart Snacks (K-12 placements) — every seasonal SKU verified against 7 CFR §210.11 + Smart Snacks Product Calculator before launch
  • ADA + ANSI/NAMA — rotation planogram does not change reach-range or service-clearance compliance
  • NAMA FitPick — better-for-you marking applied consistently across seasonal SKUs where category fits
