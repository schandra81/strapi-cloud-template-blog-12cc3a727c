University Vending Contract Pack

Master agreement, per-placement addenda, governance framework, and AI cooler scope language

A university vending contract spans 5-10 years, multiple buildings, multiple campus card vendors, and an evolving technology mix (combo to AI cooler). This pack bundles the master agreement clause structure, per-placement addenda template, governance + change-management framework, and AI cooler scope language.

1. Master agreement — required clauses

Use the clauses below as the spine of the master services agreement (MSA). Each requires written operator commitment.

Term + renewal
Initial 5-year term with two 2-year renewal options at university election; price + commission re-opener at each renewal.
Termination for cause
Material breach of uptime SLA (≥ 3 months below 95%), security incident, brand-standard breach, audit failure (HHS share, ADA, dietitian audit). 30-day cure period for non-emergency; immediate for security.
Termination for convenience
90 days written notice from university; 180 days from operator. Transition obligations apply.
Commission structure
Tiered commission per placement volume (see addendum); quarterly true-up; minimum guarantee per placement.
Pricing + escalation
Operator publishes price list; annual escalation capped at CPI + 2%; university approves any item > 10% YoY increase.
Insurance + indemnity
$2M GL, $1M umbrella, cyber-liability $5M, food product liability $1M, additional-insured to university + system; mutual indemnity language.
Data + privacy
FERPA DPA exhibit; GLBA acknowledgment exhibit; PCI-DSS 4.0 AOC + SAQ schedule; SOC 2 Type II annually.
Sustainability + STARS
Operator commits to quarterly sustainability report aligned to AASHE STARS OP-8 + OP-9.
Accessibility + ADA
Operator commits to ADA Section 308 compliance per cooler; VPAT for digital interfaces; annual accessibility audit.
Transition out
On termination, operator continues service for 180 days; data export to university within 30 days; equipment removal within 60 days of final termination.

2. Per-placement addendum template

Each placement gets an addendum specifying scope, pricing, planogram, and operational details. Template covers the core fields below.

Placement ID + location
Building, room, floor, GPS, photo at install. Date of placement effective.
Equipment type + serial
AI cooler model + serial, vision-system version, payment-terminal version. Cabinet wrap version + brand-approval date.
Commission tier
Per-volume tier table; minimum guarantee floor; quarterly true-up methodology.
Planogram
Initial 60-80 SKU planogram attached; HHS-aligned share %; dietary attestation summary; refresh cadence.
Service SLAs
Tier-1/2/3 response + resolution times per placement risk class (e.g., athletics + library = high; back-office = medium).
Telemetry + reporting
Cellular or VLAN; data flow to campus reporting dashboard; per-placement velocity report cadence.
Payment + identity stack
Tender types accepted (campus card, Apple Pay, Google Pay, EMV); SDK version; refund flow.
Accessibility attestation
Operator ADA Section 308 attestation signed at placement install + annually.
Termination + transition (per placement)
30-day notice for placement-level termination; 60-day equipment removal.

3. Governance framework

Executive sponsor (university)
VP Auxiliary Services or VP Student Affairs; quarterly executive review; annual contract review.
Day-to-day owner (university)
Auxiliary services contract manager; routes change-requests, refund escalations, contract amendments.
Executive sponsor (operator)
Operator VP / Director of Higher Ed; quarterly executive review; annual contract review.
Day-to-day owner (operator)
Operator account manager; on-call for refund escalations + service tickets.
Joint operating committee
Monthly meeting: auxiliary services + dietitian + IT + facilities + sustainability + operator account manager. Reviews ticket queue + planogram + escalations.
Quarterly business review
Executive sponsors + JOC; reviews KPIs, uptime, refund, dietitian audit, STARS metrics, scope changes.
Annual strategic review
Renewal-readiness review; commission tier re-anchor; capital + technology refresh plan.

4. AI cooler scope language

AI cooler placements have additional contract scope beyond combo vending. Use the language below in master agreement + per-placement addendum.

Vision-system attestation
Operator attests to per-SKU vision-system accuracy ≥ 99%; weekly model-drift report; quarterly recalibration; mis-charge dispute window 24-48 hr.
Fresh + perishable handling
Cold-chain monitoring 35-40°F (refrigerated); temperature excursion alert ≤ 30 min; FALCPA labeling on every SKU; food product liability $1M.
Identity + privacy at cooler
Vision-data retention ≤ 30 days un-anonymized; hash-only thereafter; no facial-recognition profile; FERPA DPA covers campus-card SDK.
PCI scope
PAN never traverses university network; operator AOC + SAQ-B-IP; cooler on isolated VLAN with egress whitelist.
Refund automation
QR + SMS refund flow ≤ 24 hr credit-back to original tender; refund dashboard accessible to auxiliary services.
Brand-wrap + signage
Cabinet wrap submitted to campus brand standards; 14-21 day approval cycle; refresh on 24-month cycle or sooner if damaged.
Capital + refresh
Operator-funded equipment; 5-7 year capital refresh cycle; university option to fund cabinet wrap for higher commission tier.

6. Change-management process

  1. Change request submitted

    University or operator submits change request via shared change-management log; categorizes as new placement, planogram update, integration enhancement, or contract amendment.

  2. Joint operating committee review

    Reviewed at next monthly JOC; categorized as in-scope (no contract change), addendum-required (per-placement addendum), or amendment-required (master agreement amendment).

  3. Scoping + pricing

    Operator submits scope + pricing within 14 days; commission impact + capital impact clearly documented.

  4. Executive approval

    Executive sponsors approve amendment; auxiliary services contract manager + operator account manager execute.

  5. Implementation

    Per agreed scope + timeline; results reviewed at next QBR.

7. Compliance + audit schedule

  • Annual: SOC 2 Type II report refresh
  • Annual: PCI-DSS AOC + SAQ refresh
  • Annual: insurance certificate refresh
  • Annual: FERPA DPA + GLBA acknowledgment review
  • Semi-annual: campus dietitian audit (HHS alignment + allergen labeling)
  • Annual: ADA Section 308 accessibility audit per cooler
  • Quarterly: vision-system accuracy report
  • Quarterly: sustainability + STARS-aligned report
  • Annual: cybersecurity + pen-test report shareable under NDA
  • Annual: contract review meeting + scope reset

8. Termination + transition playbook

  • T-180 days: termination notice issued; transition plan kicks off
  • T-150 days: data export plan finalized; replacement operator selection workflow initiated
  • T-120 days: brand-wrap + signage transition planned
  • T-90 days: planogram + signage handoff to replacement operator; campus card SDK integration retest
  • T-60 days: equipment removal schedule confirmed
  • T-30 days: final data export + reconciliation + final commission true-up
  • T-0: termination effective; replacement operator live; final operator equipment removed
  • T+30 days: final invoice + commission reconciliation + service-credit settlement
  • T+60 days: contract close-out + records archived per university records-retention schedule
This pack is informational and not legal advice. University general counsel + procurement + auxiliary services + IT + privacy office should jointly review contract language and operator attestations before contract execution. Contract structure, commission tiers, and SLA service-credit amounts are negotiable and depend on campus scope and volume.
