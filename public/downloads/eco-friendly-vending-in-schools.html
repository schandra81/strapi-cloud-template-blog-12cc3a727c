2. USDA Smart Snacks compliance + healthier hydration

Smart Snacks in School (USDA Final Rule) sets nutrition standards for snacks + beverages sold to students during the school day. Eco-aligned programs typically exceed the floor.

Beverage limits (high school)
Water (any size), low-fat milk (≤ 12 oz), 100% juice (≤ 12 oz), other beverages ≤ 60 cal/12 oz or ≤ 40 cal/8 oz. No caffeine in elementary + middle.
Beverage limits (elementary + middle)
Water, low-fat milk, 100% juice only. No flavored milks above 8 oz, no caffeinated beverages.
Snack standards
≤ 200 cal/portion, ≤ 35% calories from fat, ≤ 10% saturated fat, 0 g trans fat, ≤ 200 mg sodium, ≤ 35% sugar by weight. Whole-grain-rich first ingredient OR ≥ ½ cup fruit/veg.
Hydration emphasis
Free filtered-water bottle-fill stations adjacent to vending — Elkay LZSTL8WSLK, Halsey Taylor HTHB-HVRGRN8K, similar. Cut beverage Scope 3 30-50% by shifting consumption to refillable bottles.

3. Telemetry + dynamic restock

Platform
Cantaloupe Seed Pro, Nayax Onyx, Vagabond. Cellular telemetry preferred; school Wi-Fi often blocks vending IoT VLANs.
Restock cadence
Telemetry-driven 1-3 visits/week per machine vs. fixed weekly. Cuts vehicle miles 30-50%.
Stockout SLA
≤ 8% machines stocked-out at any time. Hydration + Smart Snacks SKU stockouts trigger 24-hour resolution.
Reporting
Monthly utilization + emission factor per vend to district sustainability + nutrition services. Quarterly nutrition-compliance audit support.

4. Packaging + diversion

Schools are high-visibility laboratories for diversion. A clear three-stream sort adjacent to vending teaches lifelong recycling habits and supports STEAM curriculum integration.

Beverage packaging
≥ 70% recycled aluminum (cans), ≥ 50% rPET (bottles). Cartons (FSC-certified, recyclable curbside in most US districts).
Snack packaging
Push suppliers toward mono-material PE films + How2Recycle labeling. FSC paperboard for bar-format SKUs.
Bin infrastructure
Three-stream sorting at every machine: landfill / commingled recycle / compost. ClearStream + Toter + Rubbermaid in school-grade sizing. Photo-realistic signage at student eye-level.
Diversion target
≥ 60% diversion rate in mature programs; ≥ 80% on aluminum cans (least contamination).
Student stewardship
Optional sustainability-ambassador program — middle + high school students audit bins monthly. Integrates with NGSS HS-ESS3 curriculum.

6. Operator selection — school-specific criteria

  • Operator demonstrates current Smart Snacks compliance database with audit support
  • 100% ENERGY STAR equipment + 100% low-GWP refrigerant on all refrigerated machines on the school's account
  • Background-checked + fingerprinted restock staff per state code (CA: AB 1610, FL: 1012.32, TX: 22.083, NY varies by district)
  • Allergen labeling + cross-contact disclosure on every SKU; nut-aware planogram in K-5
  • Three-stream bin infrastructure included in operator scope
  • Telemetry + emission-factor data supplied to district sustainability + nutrition services
  • Local + district-of-attendance preference where applicable (e.g., Buy California, Buy Florida-Grown produce-based snacks)

8. Contract clause checklist

  • ENERGY STAR + R-290 attestation on every refrigerated machine
  • Smart Snacks compliance attestation with quarterly database refresh
  • Cellular telemetry standard; operator absorbs connectivity cost
  • Diversion infrastructure (bins + signage + collection) included in monthly fee
  • Annual ESG + nutrition compliance report to district
  • Background check + safety training on every employee accessing school grounds
  • Termination for convenience with 90-day notice in case of district policy change
