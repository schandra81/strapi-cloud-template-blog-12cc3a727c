Dealership Branded Vending Playbook

Four tiers, manufacturer alignment, co-marketed partnerships, and CSI integration

Branded vending in a dealership is a marketing surface as much as a refreshment program. The branding decision sits at the intersection of OEM brand standards, dealership-group identity, and beverage-brand co-marketing. This playbook frames the four common branding tiers, manufacturer alignment requirements, and the operator agreement structures that keep branded programs CSI-aligned and brand-compliant.

1. Four branding tiers

Dealership branded-vending programs fall into one of four tiers. Each tier has different cost, brand-control, and CSI impact characteristics.

2. OEM brand-standards alignment

OEMs publish customer-experience and brand-standards documentation that constrains how dealerships present third-party amenities. Confirm before signing a multi-year branded equipment agreement.

Brand guideline review
OEM brand center (e.g., Toyota Brand Site, Ford Brand Hub, BMW Group Brand Portal) publishes color palette, logo placement, allowed third-party brand co-presence, and signage requirements. Equipment skin design reviewed before fabrication.
OEM-sponsored programs
Some OEMs run vending or beverage programs (Mercedes-Benz Coffee Bar, Lexus Plus amenities) that pre-empt third-party operators. Confirm dealership is not already enrolled before contracting.
Multi-franchise groups
Group with multiple OEMs (e.g., Honda + Toyota + Ford) typically picks a neutral dealership-group brand rather than co-branding each location to its OEM. Avoids brand-conflict at the group level.
Annual brand-audit
OEM regional reps conduct annual brand-audit visits. Operator provides branded-machine photos and brand-standards attestation on request.

3. Co-marketed beverage-brand partnerships

  1. Coca-Cola Bottling Investments

    Local Coca-Cola bottler offers branded equipment, exclusive Coke-portfolio placement, and dedicated route service. Typical structure: free equipment + free install + dealership receives Coke-portfolio products at preferential pricing + 60-90 day product exclusivity. Confirm bottler territory and contract terms with local rep.

  2. PepsiCo Foodservice

    Similar structure to Coca-Cola, with Pepsi-portfolio brands (Pepsi, Mountain Dew, Gatorade, Aquafina, Starbucks RTD). Foodservice rep negotiates equipment + product + marketing co-funding.

  3. Red Bull or Monster

    Specialty energy-drink coolers branded by the energy company. Often paired with a vending machine for breadth. Branded coolers free but product mix locked to the sponsoring brand.

  4. OEM co-funding

    Some OEM regional offices co-fund branded amenities under brand-experience grants. Document offered grants and any matching dealer-spend requirement before signing.

4. Wrap design and fabrication

Wrap material
3M IJ180Cv3 cast-vinyl wrap with 3M 8520 overlaminate, rated 7+ year outdoor durability. UV-stable inks (Mimaki UJV or HP Latex) for color longevity. ANSI/CTA-2034 fabrication standards.
Color matching
Pantone-matched to OEM or dealership-group brand color. Operator provides color-proof for OEM/dealership sign-off before production.
Surface preparation
Cabinet surface cleaned with isopropyl alcohol and degreaser. Wrap applied by certified installer (3M Preferred Graphics Installer or equivalent).
Compliance markings
FCC, UL, and FDA Food Code compliance labels remain visible after wrap. ADA reach-range labels and accessibility markings preserved per ADA Section 308 and 309.

6. CSI and customer-experience integration

  • Branded machine signage matches OEM or dealership brand-experience standards and does not conflict with showroom signage.
  • Operator-contact information and refund process clearly visible on machine face per state vending regulation (most states require operator name, address, and phone number on each machine).
  • FALCPA allergen signage and FDA Food Code 3-501.16 cold-chain compliance markings remain visible after wrap.
  • ADA Section 308 reach-range markings preserved; ADA Section 703 high-contrast pricing display maintained.
  • Brand-standards photo-documentation submitted to OEM regional or dealership group during annual brand-audit cycle.
  • CSI verbatim feedback related to branded machine reviewed quarterly with operator; planogram or branding refresh triggered if recurring negative comments emerge.

7. Multi-rooftop group economics

Auto groups with 10+ rooftops can negotiate consolidated branded-vending programs that reduce per-location cost and produce a consistent customer experience.

Master service agreement
Single agreement covering all rooftops. Standardized SLA, branded equipment specification, and consolidated billing. Operator typically discounts 2-4% on commission share for master agreements.
Brand consistency across rooftops
Single wrap design and planogram refresh schedule across all locations. Reduces per-location design cost and ensures CSI auditors find consistent presentation.
Quarterly group business review
Operator presents to group VP-of-Operations quarterly: per-rooftop revenue, uptime, CSI verbatims, planogram refresh recommendations. Group benchmarks rooftops against each other.
OEM co-funding at scale
Group-level OEM co-funding typically larger than per-rooftop. Group brand-experience grants of $10K-$50K/year fund equipment, signage, and brand-standards refreshes.
This playbook is a working operational reference. Calibrate the branding tier, OEM alignment, and operator agreement to your group's brand position, OEM portfolio, and CSI program targets.
