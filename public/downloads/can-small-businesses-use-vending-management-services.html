Small-Business VMS Evaluation Pack

Operator shortlist criteria, qualification template, and contract checklist for SMB placements

Small-business placements (typically 25-200 employees, single site) sit below most regional VMS minimum-volume thresholds. This pack helps you qualify operators willing to serve SMB volumes, evaluate them against published industry SLA norms, and structure a contract that protects both sides without burdensome minimums.

1. SMB-fit operator shortlist criteria

Most regional and national VMS firms publish minimum-volume thresholds (commonly 100-200 employees per site or 30,000+ USD annual revenue per machine). Independent and small-regional operators typically serve below those thresholds.

Footprint match
Operator's route density within 25 miles of your site. Density drives restock cost; without density, operator either declines or charges a service fee.
Account-size mix
Confirm 40%+ of operator's book is sub-200-employee accounts. Operators whose book is dominated by large accounts often deprioritize SMBs at first stockout.
Equipment age policy
SMB placements often receive older equipment. Specify max age (e.g., ≤ 7 years) or 'card-reader-equipped' minimum in RFP.
Insurance + licensing
Operator carries 1M general liability + auto + product liability. Health-department permit for food/beverage in the jurisdiction.

2. Volume threshold + commission realism

SMB placements rarely justify commission. NAMA-published industry data suggests sub-100-employee single-machine placements typically generate 8,000-18,000 USD annual gross revenue. After cost of goods, route labor, and telemetry, operator gross margin is 12-22%.

3. Qualification questionnaire (to operator)

  1. Density + service window

    How many active accounts within 10 miles of our site? What is your typical restock day-of-week and time window for our zone?

  2. Minimum-volume threshold

    What is your minimum monthly gross per machine to keep the account active? What happens if we fall below — service fee, reduced cadence, or termination notice?

  3. Equipment + telemetry

    Will our machine be telemetry-enabled (Cantaloupe, Nayax, 365, or comparable)? Equipment year of manufacture? Card-reader rail coverage?

  4. Stockout response

    What is your SLA on a reported stockout? On a payment-system failure? On a machine offline?

  5. Planogram flexibility

    Will you accommodate per-quarter planogram refresh based on our employee feedback survey? How is sub-pack pricing set — operator discretion, fixed schedule, or agreed price list?

  6. Healthy-share + allergen

    What percentage of slots can be allocated to healthy SKUs (under 200 cal, under 230 mg sodium, under 35% calories from fat)? Allergen labeling on each SKU per FALCPA?

  7. Exit + transfer

    Notice period to terminate? Equipment removal cost? Any liquidated damages or buyout clause?

4. Contract checklist — SMB-protective terms

  • Term: 12 months initial, month-to-month after, 30-day mutual termination notice. Avoid 36+ month terms without exit ramps.
  • Service SLA in writing — uptime target (97%+), stockout resolution (24-48 hours), machine-offline resolution (24 hours).
  • Right to request equipment swap if uptime falls below 95% in any rolling 90-day window.
  • Price list reference (operator price list as of date) with annual escalator cap (CPI or 5%, whichever lower).
  • Quarterly business review with telemetry-derived data: per-SKU velocity, uptime, stockout count, refund rate.
  • Refund procedure: member-initiated app/SMS refund, cashless auto-credit within 24 hours, cash via slip + next-visit credit.
  • Liability + insurance: operator names host as additional insured; certificate on file.
  • Food safety + allergen: operator attests to FALCPA labeling and local health-department permit currency.
  • Data: host owns aggregate transaction data; operator may use de-identified data for benchmarking only.

5. Service-fee structures when commission won't work

Where account volume is below the operator's threshold, a service fee can keep service quality intact without forcing the operator to operate at a loss.

Monthly service fee
Fixed monthly amount (e.g., 75-200 USD) the host pays to maintain restock cadence regardless of sales volume. Common at sub-50-employee sites.
Subsidized pricing
Host pays operator the gap between member price (e.g., 0.50 coffee) and operator wholesale + margin floor. Used in white-glove or recruiting-tool placements.
Equipment lease
Host leases equipment from operator at a fixed monthly rate; host operates planogram independently or via 'reverse-vend' fill service.
Office coffee bundle
Combine coffee + snack + cold beverage into a single managed program; bundle volume often qualifies for full VMS service.

6. Operator-scoring rubric (100 points)

  • Density + service-window fit — 15 points.
  • Equipment age + telemetry rail coverage — 15 points.
  • Healthy-share + allergen capability — 10 points.
  • SLA terms (uptime, stockout, offline) in writing — 15 points.
  • Pricing transparency + escalator cap — 10 points.
  • Reference checks (3 SMB-sized accounts) — 15 points.
  • Insurance + licensing complete — 10 points.
  • Exit terms (notice period, removal cost) — 10 points.

7. Pre-signing red flags

  • Operator refuses telemetry on your machine — opaque velocity data.
  • Operator requires 36+ month term with no early-exit clause.
  • Operator declines to put SLA in writing.
  • Operator price list 'subject to change without notice' with no cap.
  • Operator references include zero accounts under 200 employees.
  • Operator declines additional-insured endorsement.
