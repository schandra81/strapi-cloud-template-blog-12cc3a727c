School Vending Fundraising Playbook

Smart Snacks checklist, RFP framework, sponsor agreement template, planogram by placement type, quarterly review template

School vending as a fundraising channel — for athletic programs, band, debate, PTA, booster clubs — works when the program respects USDA Smart Snacks (7 CFR §210.11) during school day, structures sponsorship + commission cleanly, and reports back to the booster organization quarterly. This playbook codifies the full program.

1. Smart Snacks compliance — checklist

Every SKU on a student-access machine during school day (midnight before school until 30 min after dismissal) must pass 7 CFR §210.11. Fundraising programs that ignore Smart Snacks lose state child-nutrition funding and trigger USDA audit findings.

  • Snack: ≤ 200 calories AND ≤ 200 mg sodium AND ≤ 35% calories from fat AND ≤ 10% calories from saturated fat AND 0g trans fat
  • Snack: ≤ 35% by weight sugar; first ingredient whole grain or fruit/vegetable/dairy/protein OR ≥ 10% DV of calcium/potassium/vit D/fiber
  • Entree: ≤ 350 calories AND ≤ 480 mg sodium AND same fat/sugar limits
  • Beverage K-5: water; milk ≤ 8 oz; 100% juice ≤ 8 oz
  • Beverage 6-8: water; milk ≤ 12 oz; 100% juice ≤ 12 oz
  • Beverage 9-12: water; milk ≤ 12 oz; 100% juice ≤ 12 oz; low/no-cal ≤ 20 oz; other ≤ 12 oz with ≤ 40 cal/8 oz
  • Caffeine — none in K-8; allowed in high school
  • Verification — operator maintains Smart Snacks Product Calculator results or USDA-aligned vendor attestation per SKU; on file with district nutrition services
  • After-school + faculty-only — Smart Snacks does not apply after dismissal + 30 min, or at faculty-only placements; operator + district may schedule planogram switch

2. RFP framework — fundraising vending

Athletic boosters + PTA + activity programs frequently issue their own vending RFPs separate from district master agreements. RFP should align to district master agreement language to avoid compliance drift.

  1. Section 1 — Scope + parties

    Booster organization (with district endorsement) as principal; operator as vendor. Define placements, machine count, hours, target install date.

  2. Section 2 — Smart Snacks compliance

    Per-SKU attestation required; right of district nutrition services to require SKU removal. Documentation retained per district master agreement.

  3. Section 3 — Equipment + telemetry

    ENERGY STAR refrigeration; cellular telemetry; cashless + prepaid integration; ADA Title III §305 + §308 compliance.

  4. Section 4 — Commission + revenue split

    Booster commission rate (typically 15-35% of gross); pay-out cadence (monthly); minimum guarantee (optional).

  5. Section 5 — Sponsorship rights

    Wrap + signage rights to sponsor (athletic program, local business); sponsor approval workflow; sponsor exclusivity (if any) per category.

  6. Section 6 — Operations + SLA

    Restock cadence, service SLA tiers, refund flow, academic calendar coordination.

  7. Section 7 — Reporting

    Monthly revenue + commission report; quarterly business review with booster + activity director + nutrition services.

  8. Section 8 — Insurance + compliance

    $2M GL + $1M auto + $1M product; booster + district named as additional insured. Annual certificate.

3. Sponsor agreement — template structure

Sponsor agreements pair a local business or category supplier with a fundraising program in exchange for branded wrap, exclusivity, or featured placement. Use this structure as the baseline.

  • Parties + term — booster organization + sponsor (named entity); 1-3 year primary with renewal
  • Sponsorship fee + structure — flat annual + per-transaction royalty + minimum guarantee
  • Branding rights — wrap design + placement + sign + on-screen logo on Class P equipment
  • Exclusivity — category exclusivity (beverage or snack) within named placements; non-exclusive across district
  • Product-placement rights — sponsor SKUs allocated specified % of facings (typically 20-50%); Smart Snacks compliance per district policy
  • Approval workflow — district + nutrition services + activity director approve final wrap + planogram
  • Termination — 90-day for convenience; immediate for compliance failure; cure period for material breach
  • Brand-conduct clause — sponsor's brand conduct off-program may trigger termination if it conflicts with district values policy

5. Operational support structure

Restock cadence
2-3 visits/week during school year. Pre-position for game-week + tournament-week surges. Suspended or minimal during summer + breaks unless after-school programs run.
Service SLA — Tier 1
Offline, cold-chain, payment — <24 hr ack + <24 hr resolution.
Service SLA — Tier 2
Stockout, planogram refresh — 24-48 hr ack, 5-7 day resolution.
Refund flow
Refund via app, on-machine touchpad, or front-office hand-off. Refund cost split per RFP (typically operator-borne up to 2%; booster-borne above).
Reporting
Monthly revenue + commission statement to booster treasurer + activity director; quarterly business review covers all metrics + planogram action items.

6. Quarterly review template

  • Total gross sales + commission earned by booster organization
  • Per-placement revenue + variance vs prior quarter + prior year
  • Smart Snacks compliance audit — per-SKU verification + any findings
  • Top-20 and bottom-20 SKUs with planogram action items
  • Service SLA adherence (Tier 1-3) + refund rate
  • Sponsor mix + sponsorship revenue contribution
  • Calendar look-ahead — events, fundraising drives, planogram surge plan
  • Action items + owner + due date, carried in QBR tracker
